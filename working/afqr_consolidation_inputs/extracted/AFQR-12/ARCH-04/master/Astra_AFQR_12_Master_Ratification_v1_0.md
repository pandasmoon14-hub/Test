## Part I — Executive decision

### Selected architecture

**Registered Typed Motivational–Behavioral State Architecture with Bounded Deliberation, Pluggable Plan Interfaces, Profiled Learning, and Bitemporal Continuity** (`RTMBS-BD-PPI-PL-BTC`)

### Central law

> No value, preference, need, drive, emotion, disposition, goal, intention, habit, plan, authored descriptor, learned policy, or model prediction selects or mutates authoritative behavior by itself. Every behavioral determination is a purpose-scoped, version-pinned evaluation over a frozen identity, epistemic, agency, control, relation, and world-state basis; every mutation is owner-prepared and AFQR-01 committed; plans remain non-authoritative until an action is separately validated and committed; historical motives, options, decisions, and updates are append-only.

### Confidence

**High.** The universal ownership, separation, lifecycle, visibility, replay, and escalation questions are resolved. Final source-local psychologies, formulas, detailed cognitive strategies, relationship mechanics, culture, reputation, and tactical AI remain registered downstream work.

### What it is

A federated architecture combining typed behavioral-subject references; distinct value, preference, need, drive, goal, intention, affect, disposition, habit, routine, plan, and learning records; bounded deliberation; multiple certified plan interfaces; family-specific continuity; audience-scoped private receipts; and deterministic replay.

### What it is not

It is not a freeform personality prompt, alignment system, universal utility function, BDI-only engine, behavior-tree-only engine, unrestricted planner, universal human psychology, diagnostic model, or model-authored NPC autonomy system.

### Research disposition

No second broad research pass is required before ratification. Narrow research is required when individual emotion, stress, culture, relationship, planning, learning, or source-local profiles are authored.

## Part V — Behavioral-subject and ownership model

A behavioral subject is a typed in-world entity or aggregate authorized to own one or more behavioral-state families. Eligible subjects include individual minds/actors, personas, collectives, institutions, swarms, artificial agents, familiars, proxies, automated systems, vehicles where a registered policy exists, and source-local metaphysical entities. Eligibility does not establish personhood, agency, consciousness, or emotions.

The architecture keeps `behavioral subject`, `epistemic subject`, `decision subject`, `action executor`, `institution`, `audience`, and `model consumer` separate. State is federated among value/preference, need/drive, goal/intention, affect/appraisal, disposition/persona, habit/routine, deliberation, planning, learning, continuity, institutional, and projection-validation owners. A coordinator can freeze inputs and collect owner fragments but cannot mutate any owner’s state.


## Part VI — Values, preferences, needs, and drives

Values are subject-specific evaluative stances; preferences are orderings or comparative tendencies; needs are typed maintenance requirements; drives are recurrent motivational pressures. None selects action by itself. Each record carries origin, endorsement, accessibility, persona/role scope, representation type, confidence or uncertainty, effective/recorded/logical time, conflict links, and update policy.

Allowed representations include categorical stances, ordinal levels, partial orders, lexicographic priorities, qualitative support/opposition, intervals, source-local structures, and explicitly unquantified states. Unlike representations are not silently converted. Public profession and private endorsement are distinct. Need urgency may narrow or influence deliberation only through a registered bridge; refusal to satisfy a need remains lawful.


## Part VII — Goals, commitments, and intentions

Goals have definitions and subject-owned instances. Origin is explicit: self-adopted, delegated, imposed, inherited, role-derived, emergent, source-local, or unresolved. Goal lifecycle uses orthogonal activation, commitment, progress, feasibility, conflict, visibility, and closure axes. Dormancy, suspension, blockage, invalidation, completion, abandonment, and supersession are not collapsed.

Goal adoption does not create an intention. Intention formation does not create an action commitment. Action commitment does not create an attempt or world mutation. Reconsideration and abandonment append receipts. Obligations and commands can influence or seed goal/intention candidates but remain separate governed or agency records.


## Part VIII — Emotion, mood, stress, and affect

The affect pipeline separates accessible trigger, appraisal, emotion candidate, committed episode, mood handoff, bodily/resource/condition handoff, behavioral influence, visible expression, and subject interpretation. The same event may yield different or no episodes under different profiles. An expression may be absent, suppressed, or fabricated.

No universal emotion taxonomy applies. Profiles may be discrete, dimensional, appraisal-based, bodily, computational, source-local, or explicitly inapplicable. Stress and morale are routed constructs rather than universal behavioral modifiers. Emotion can affect option access or evaluation only through registered bridges; it never directly commands action or proves personality.


## Part IX — Personality, dispositions, habits, and routines

A disposition is a context-qualified tendency with evidence, uncertainty, stability expectation, and change policy. Role persona, public persona, and private disposition are separate. Dispositions bias appraisal, option access, evaluation, style, persistence, or expression only through registered bridges. Contrary action remains representable.

Reflexes, instincts, habits, conditioned responses, trained procedures, scripts, routines, schedules, automated policies, and deliberate plans are distinct. Habit activation records cue, context, automaticity dimensions, inhibition, and conflict. Routines can be interrupted and schedules can become stale. Automatic behavior retains AFQR-11 action-origin evidence and does not imply consent or responsibility.


## Part X — Deliberation and choice

A deliberation context freezes decision subject, play-control contract, epistemic basis, agency/control basis, active values/goals/commitments, needs/drives, affect, dispositions, habits/routines, commands/authority, capabilities/affordances, logical time, attention constraints, planning budget, and policy versions.

Objectively possible, subject-perceived, registered, considered, excluded-before-consideration, hidden, and unrepresentable alternatives are separate. Supported evaluation profiles include rule-based, lexicographic, weighted multi-criteria, qualitative, threshold, satisficing, habit-first, duty-first, risk-sensitive, emotion-biased, source-local, and backend-RNG selection. Unlike profiles do not silently arbitrate each other. Lawful results include selection, deferral, refusal, tie, indecision, and unresolved conflict.


## Part XI — Planning

A common plan interface supports reactive policies, finite-state plans, behavior trees, goal-oriented plans, hierarchical task networks, schedules, projects, partial-order plans, contingencies, distributed plans, and source-local structures. Each binds goals, subject/collective, planner profile, belief basis, assumptions, dependencies, capabilities, interfaces, resources, risks, budget, monitoring triggers, and lifecycle.

Plans are not future truth and do not create capabilities, permissions, or actions. Draft, evaluation, adoption, activity, blockage, validity, repair, progress, and closure are orthogonal where required. Repair preserves declared continuity; replanning creates a materially new plan; abandonment, failure, completion, and supersession remain distinct.


## Part XII — Learning and adaptation

Learning begins from an experience, instruction, observation, or outcome record and applies a registered profile. It produces a bounded update candidate with evidence basis, target family, prior state, proposed delta, uncertainty, generalization scope, conflict checks, continuity effects, and quarantine status. Only the target owner may commit it through AFQR-01.

Reinforcement, punishment, conditioning, habituation, sensitization, imitation, instruction, skill learning, preference change, value change, trust handoff, threat learning, plan improvement, routine formation, extinction, source-local awakening, maladaptive learning, and institutional learning remain distinct. Learned policies never acquire unrestricted mutation, truth, or action authority.


## Part XIII — Behavioral continuity

Behavioral continuity is determined separately for values, preferences, needs, drives, goals, commitments, intentions, appraisals, emotion tendencies, current episodes, moods, dispositions, personas, habits, routines, scripts, plans, and learned policies. Identity continuity does not automatically carry any family.

Copies receive separate state references and may copy selected records as initial candidates. Fusion may retain conflict, reconcile, transform, or create new state. Fission may allocate, duplicate, transform, suspend, or leave state unresolved. Resurrection, reincarnation, possession, body replacement, autonomy grants, collective merges, and institutional succession use versioned family-specific continuity profiles.


## Part XIV — Collective and institutional behavior

Official goals, leadership goals, member goals, public mission, enacted policy, institutional routine, distributed plan, emergent tendency, and institutional learning are separate. Institutions need not be modeled as humanlike minds. Official adoption procedures and continuity records can preserve goals or routines across membership replacement, while member endorsement may remain absent.

Distributed plans identify contribution and coordination without inventing one author. Swarm or emergent behavior can receive an EmergentBehaviorReceipt describing a pattern without establishing collective intention, personhood, or responsibility.


## Part XV — Hidden motives and model projection

Private motives, values, conflicts, appraisals, plans, contingencies, excluded options, policy identities, and learning rules remain private records. Audience projections use audience-scoped opaque tokens and cannot expose hidden identifiers, counts, ordering, plan length, contingency number, policy names, retrieval ranking, duration, or stable cross-audience hashes.

Every model packet requires a `ModelBehaviorGroundingManifest`, claim-kind allowlist, grounding references, and cross-packet scope. Output validation rejects unsupported motive, emotion, intention, personality, plan, or choice claims. Prompt instructions alone are not a security boundary.

## Part XVII — Determination pipelines

### Motivation update

`trigger → freeze subject/epistemic basis → discover relevant values/needs/drives/commitments/roles/relations → registered appraisal or motivational profiles → non-mutating candidates → conflict detection → owner routing → AFQR-01 fragments → commit → private/visible projections`

### Deliberation

`decision trigger → decision subject/play-control resolution → freeze epistemic/agency/control/behavioral basis → perceived candidates → attention/option-access constraints → considered set → registered evaluation → conflicts → select/defer/refuse/unresolved → intention/action candidate → AFQR-03/02 → AFQR-01 commit`

### Planning

`goal/commitment trigger → freeze goal/belief basis → plan-interface selection → bounded candidate → validate capabilities/interfaces/dependencies/assumptions → hidden/uncertain prerequisites → risk/resource review → adopt/reject/defer/quarantine → monitor → repair/replan/suspend/abandon/complete`

### Emotion/appraisal

`accessible event/proposition → subject appraisal → emotion candidate → applicability → episode/no episode → optional mood/resource/memory/behavior influence handoffs → owner fragments → commit → expression projection`

### Learning

`experience/instruction → evidence/outcome basis → learning profile → bounded candidate → generalization check → conflict/continuity check → quarantine or owner fragments → commit → historical receipt`
