# ADR AFQR-13 — Social Relationships, Trust, Reputation, Status, Culture, Affiliation, and Group Dynamics

Status: Proposed for ratification

## Decision

Adopt **Registered Multiplex Social-State Architecture with Domain-Scoped Trust, Audience-Relative Reputation, Modular Culture–Norm Profiles, and Bitemporal Network Continuity** (`RMSSA-DT-ARR-MCNP-BNC`).

## Central law

No encounter, communication, repeated contact, governed relation, graph edge, public record, audience claim, social metric, donor descriptor, or model portrayal creates authoritative relationship, trust, reputation, status, affiliation, culture, norm, kinship, coalition, or social continuity by itself. Every social result is a typed, purpose- and audience-scoped, version-pinned determination over frozen identity, epistemic, agency, behavioral, governed-relation, and event bases; every mutation is owner-prepared and committed through AFQR-01; graph metrics and aggregates remain derived observations; historical social state and later corrections are append-only.

## Consequences

- Social state is federated and profile-scoped.
- Relationships are typed, directional, multiplex, and recognition-aware.
- Trust is domain-specific and distinct from reliance, affection, and loyalty.
- Reputation is audience-owned and proposition-scoped.
- Status, rank, prestige, office, authority, and deference are separate.
- Culture and norms are modular and cannot become personality stereotypes.
- Graph metrics remain derived observations.
- All transitions are append-only, hidden-safe, model-independent, and AFQR-01 committed.
