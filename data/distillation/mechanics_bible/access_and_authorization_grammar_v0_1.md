# Astra Access and Authorization Grammar v0.1

Derived from cross-donor consolidation after RHBF, Shadowrun, and Lancer.

## Scope of this file

This file owns Astra's **access and authorization grammar**.

It defines:
- what access law is,
- how access differs from competence, role identity, and resources,
- what kinds of gates Astra currently recognizes,
- how permissions, licenses, credentials, domains, ratings, and restricted channels are handled,
- where authorization-backed acquisition belongs,
- and what remains optional-profile support or donor-local retention.

It does **not** own:
- full capability doctrine,
- full Path doctrine,
- full credential-object schema,
- full platform/object schema,
- exact donor license tables,
- exact permit stacks,
- exact legal-state systems,
- exact Matrix/host architecture,
- exact domain rosters,
- or exact donor metaphysics of attunement, resonance, or access.

This is a grammar owner, not a catalog owner.

---

## Why this file exists

The first three donors establish that access is not decorative.

RHBF proves that Astra must support:
- Domain-gated learnability,
- Domain-gated lawful use,
- and progression that depends on access rather than raw class lock.

Shadowrun proves that Astra must also support:
- restricted channels,
- credential-backed permission,
- stateful access conditions,
- interface/native access differentiation,
- and acquisition that is lawful only if the actor has the right authorization state.

Lancer proves that Astra must further support:
- license-backed access,
- authorization-driven chassis/system acquisition,
- and progression where access bands are real build law rather than flavor.

The stable conclusion is:

> Access is a real Astra rules layer.  
> It is distinct from competence, distinct from Path, and distinct from raw resources.

## Core access law

A lawful Astra build or action may be constrained by **access state**.

Access state is the actor's current or acquired relationship to:
- a permission family,
- a restricted capability band,
- an authorized usage channel,
- a credential-governed domain,
- a license-backed acquisition ladder,
- or a comparable gate structure.

The current stable rule is:

> Competence answers whether an actor can perform.  
> Access answers whether the actor is allowed, attuned, licensed, connected, keyed, or otherwise lawfully positioned to perform, acquire, equip, invoke, install, or enter.

Access may therefore govern:
- learning,
- usage,
- equipping,
- installation,
- acquisition,
- traversal,
- activation,
- interface,
- and legality.

---

## Access is not the same as other build layers

### Access is not competence
An actor may know how to do something and still lack lawful access to do it.

Astra must preserve this distinction.

Examples of the distinction:
- a character may have the competence to handle advanced gear but lack permission to acquire or operate a restricted platform,
- a character may have strong magical capability but lack the required Domain or attunement channel for a specific expression,
- a character may be technically capable of entry or interface but lack the correct credential or live access state.

### Access is not Path
A Path may influence access, recommend access, or bundle starting access.

It does not own all access law.

A character's role identity must not absorb every permission structure.

### Access is not resources
Resources may help obtain access, maintain access, fake access, or expand access.

They do not automatically constitute access by themselves.

### Access is not object ownership
Owning a thing does not automatically grant lawful use, installation compatibility, or authorized activation.

Object-side compatibility remains distinct from actor-side authorization.

## Recognized access families

These are the current Astra-facing access families.

### 1. Domain access
This family governs lawful relationship to a discipline, domain, school, or permission-bearing field of use.

RHBF strongly validates this family.

Domain access may govern:
- learnability,
- usage,
- progression eligibility,
- lawful technique acquisition,
- or equivalent participation rights.

This family should not be treated as identical to role identity.

---

### 2. License-backed access
This family governs access through explicit authorization ladders, licensing bands, or progression-backed permission tiers.

Lancer strongly validates this family.

License-backed access may govern:
- chassis/frame access,
- advanced system acquisition,
- platform-side capability bands,
- specialized packages,
- or equivalent restricted acquisition families.

This family is lawful Astra grammar and should not be flattened into generic leveling alone.

---

### 3. Credential-backed access
This family governs access through recognized tokens of identity, permit, clearance, warrant, sponsorship, or equivalent institutional authorization.

Shadowrun strongly validates this family.

Credential-backed access may govern:
- entry,
- acquisition,
- legality,
- institutional trust,
- transaction permission,
- interface eligibility,
- or operation inside regulated structures.

The detailed asset records for such credentials do not belong here, but the grammar does.

---

### 4. Channel/state access
This family governs access through live state, resonance, attunement, connection, interface, synchronization, or other current-state conditions.

This family is broader than any one donor’s metaphysical framing.

It may govern:
- whether the actor is in the correct state to interface,
- whether a special channel is available,
- whether a restricted mode is currently active,
- or whether a donor-specific keyed condition has been met.

This family should remain broad and not collapse into one donor’s cosmology.

---

### 5. Compatibility/host access
This family governs whether a host, body, socket, frame, mount, substrate, or other receiving structure can lawfully accept an installed or linked capability.

This family sits at the edge of Batch A and Batch C:
- actor-side access state belongs here,
- object-side compatibility fields live in Batch C owners.

The grammar must acknowledge both sides without collapsing them into one file.

---

### 6. Situational / operational access
This family governs access that arises from current conditions rather than permanent build state.

It may depend on:
- location,
- sponsorship,
- operation phase,
- temporary authorization,
- live network condition,
- active escort,
- current mission state,
- or comparable situational factors.

This is lawful Astra grammar and prevents overfitting access into only permanent character sheets.

## Access outcomes

Astra currently needs to support at least these access outcomes:

### 1. Learnability gate
The actor may not learn the thing without access.

### 2. Usage gate
The actor may know or possess the thing but may not lawfully use it without access.

### 3. Equip/install gate
The actor may possess the object but may not equip or install it without access or compatibility.

### 4. Acquisition gate
The actor may desire the option but may not lawfully purchase, requisition, claim, or receive it without access.

### 5. Entry/traversal gate
The actor may not enter, traverse, or connect without access.

### 6. Operation gate
The actor may reach the system but still lack the live authorization or state needed to operate it.

### 7. Progression gate
The actor may not advance into a branch, license band, or specialization without access.

These outcomes are cross-donor-stable enough to preserve now.

## Authorization-backed acquisition

Some donors make acquisition itself a gated act.

Astra should therefore preserve a distinct rule:

> Not every desirable option is merely bought, learned, or found.  
> Some options are lawfully acquired only through authorization-backed pathways.

Authorization-backed acquisition may depend on:
- license rank,
- credential state,
- formal recognition,
- legal clearance,
- institutional sponsorship,
- Domain access,
- special channel eligibility,
- or compatible host state.

This matters especially because later donors may pressure:
- starship ownership,
- military-grade gear,
- interstellar passage,
- civic identity,
- platform deployment rights,
- and controlled magical or technical assets.

## Access-state structure

A lawful access state may have these components:

- **owner**: who or what the access belongs to
- **family**: domain / license / credential / state / compatibility / situational
- **scope**: what the access applies to
- **grade**: how broad, deep, or advanced the access is
- **duration**: permanent, temporary, operation-bound, consumable, revocable, or conditional
- **source**: where the access came from
- **revocation condition**: how access can be lost
- **interaction rules**: what it combines with or conflicts with

Not every donor will use all of these fields, but this structure is broad enough to host multiple access families.

## Build interaction rules

### Paths with access
A Path may grant, recommend, or bias access, but Path is not the sole owner of access law.

### Competence with access
Competence may be high while access is absent.
Competence may also be low while access exists.

Astra must preserve both possibilities.

### Resources with access
Resources may purchase, maintain, imitate, or pressure access.

They do not erase the distinction between having money and having permission.

### Credentials with access
Credentials often instantiate access but do not exhaust the whole grammar.
Some access is keyed, licensed, attuned, stateful, or situational rather than document-based.

### Operator/platform with access
The operator may hold the authorization while the platform provides the compatible host.
The platform may exist while the operator lacks the correct authorization.
Both sides must remain visible.

### Bonded or attuned objects with access
A bonded or attuned object may create a special access channel, but object attachment is not identical to general authorization law.

## What remains optional-profile support

The following are lawful recurring profiles, but not baseline universal access law:

- explicit access instruments as one strong profile,
- special access ratings distinct from primary attributes,
- permit/identity stacks with detailed legal-state modeling,
- single donor-specific resonance or attunement cosmologies,
- mission-only temporary authorization structures as the dominant access model,
- access-by-upkeep/accounting as a full doctrine family,
- support-tier access maintenance,
- exact restricted-channel numerical scales.

These should be supported, but not universalized.

## Current Astra-facing summary

Astra now recognizes access as a stable rules layer that may govern:
- learning,
- usage,
- equipping,
- installation,
- acquisition,
- entry,
- traversal,
- operation,
- and progression.

It is neither mere flavor nor merely a side effect of Path, competence, or wealth.

The final rule is simple:

> If a donor makes permission, attunement, licensing, clearance, keyed state, or lawful channel matter, Astra must preserve that as access law rather than flattening it into something else.
