# Astra Harvest and Treasures v0.1

## Scope and ownership

This file owns the cross-donor grammar for **resource recovery**, **post-encounter extraction**, **site and environment yields**, and **progression-significant treasure objects**.

It does **not** own full item catalogs, exact donor formulas, exact reserve taxonomies, or exact donor-local payout tables.

After RHBF and Shadowrun, Astra must support more than a slain-creature loot loop. Resource and treasure doctrine must survive both:

- **organic ecologies** such as beasts, remains, ruins, shrines, fungi, fossils, and anomalous growths,
- and **manufactured ecologies** such as installed assets, credential objects, rated devices, host-linked systems, and recoverable platform components.

The Astra-stable outcome is therefore: **resource sources are plural, outputs are typed, and some recovered objects matter for advancement, installation, growth, access, or transformation rather than sale value alone.**

---

## Core doctrine

### 1. Resources do not come only from slain actors

Astra rejects a "monsters drop loot" model.

Lawful resource sources include:

- **actor source** — beasts, adversaries, support entities, summoned/compiled entities, biological or synthetic actors,
- **site source** — ruins, labs, shrines, caches, battlefields, wrecks, vaults, extraction zones,
- **environment source** — mineral seams, botanical growths, fungal colonies, storms, irradiated or tainted regions, living terrain,
- **anomaly source** — unstable nodes, essence-rich tears, resonant fields, temporal distortions, relic wakes,
- **object source** — credential assets, devices, relics, installed systems, containers, broken or recoverable tools,
- **mixed source** — any combination of the above.

Every recovery event should identify its source type explicitly.

### 2. Recovered outputs must be typed

Recovered outputs should not enter Astra as generic loot mush.

A lawful recovery result should state at least:

- **source type**,
- **output type**,
- **condition or quality state**,
- **handling or extraction requirement** if any,
- **likely downstream use**.

### 3. Treasure and resource are related but not identical

Astra distinguishes between:

- **materials** — inputs consumed or processed by later systems,
- **salvage** — recoverable components or partial-value remains,
- **treasure objects** — discrete finds with direct value, utility, or progression relevance,
- **progression-significant resource objects** — finds whose primary importance is advancement, transformation, access, bonding, growth, or installation,
- **credential assets** — permits, licenses, keys, clearances, authorizations, or rated entitlements,
- **growth inputs** — typed feeds or evolution triggers used by bonded-growth objects, installations, or threshold systems.

Not every valuable recovery is treasure in the same sense, and not every treasure is primarily a sale good.

## Recovery source ontology

### Actor-based recovery

Actor-based recovery includes ordinary post-defeat harvest, dismantling, capture extraction, residue collection, and post-breakdown salvage from biological, anomalous, synthetic, or mixed actors.

Typical actor-linked outputs may include:

- tissue, blood, hide, bone, shell, organ, gland, venom, coating,
- core, shard, residue, node, essence-bearing remains,
- device fragments, installed components, interface hardware, mount pieces,
- identifiable trait-bearing parts,
- bonded or keyed remains.

### Site and environment recovery

Site and environment recovery is fully lawful and should be supported as first-class doctrine.

Typical site/environment outputs may include:

- botanical treasures,
- mineral treasures,
- anomalous growths,
- fossil or ruin extractions,
- shrine or node residues,
- environmental catalysts,
- rare reagents,
- wreckage salvage,
- cached or embedded assets.

This doctrine is important because RHBF proved extraction must extend beyond dead actors, and Shadowrun proved recoverable value can come from objects, systems, and environments rather than corpses or creature remains alone.

### Object and manufactured recovery

Object recovery includes devices, credentials, installed systems, augment packages, removable modules, access instruments, encoded carriers, damaged platforms, and recoverable subcomponents.

These are lawful Batch C outputs even when they are not framed as treasure in the fantasy sense.

## Typed output grammar

Astra should treat recovered outputs through typed families rather than donor-specific name lists.

### Material families

Examples of lawful material/output families include:

- biological materials,
- mineral materials,
- anomalous materials,
- energetic or resonant residues,
- synthetic or manufactured components,
- credential or authorization assets,
- relic-linked growth inputs,
- installable modules or subassemblies,
- medicinal or ritual reagents,
- upgrade or repair inputs.

A donor may supply exact names and subtypes, but Astra owns the family-level grammar.

### Quality and state fields

Recovered outputs may also carry state fields such as:

- intact,
- degraded,
- volatile,
- keyed,
- contaminated,
- unstable,
- bonded,
- licensed,
- traced,
- dormant,
- active.

These are Astra-stable as schema fields even when exact donor terminology varies.

## Treasure doctrine

### Treasure classes

Astra should support at least the following treasure classes:

- **ordinary value treasure** — primarily convertible to value or trade leverage,
- **utility treasure** — immediately usable objects or consumables,
- **progression-significant treasure** — objects whose main importance is growth, bonding, threshold crossing, installation, or transformation,
- **access treasure** — keys, seals, permits, clearances, warrants, passes, or other access-bearing objects,
- **schema-bearing treasure** — relics, credential assets, installables, or similar objects that require a real record rather than a one-line value tag.

### Natural treasures

**Natural Treasure** is preserved as an Astra-stable concept.

A Natural Treasure is a progression-significant or otherwise exceptional resource object whose origin is environmental, ecological, anomalous, or naturally emergent rather than primarily manufactured.

What Astra promotes here is the concept, not RHBF's exact reserve payload.

Promoted now:

- natural treasure as a meaningful object class,
- natural treasure as potentially progression-significant,
- natural treasure as lawful input to growth, transformation, relic development, ritual, or access processes.

Not promoted yet:

- exact `Essence / Arako / Radiance` semantics,
- any exact reserve-triplet baseline,
- exact donor-local yield tables.

## Post-recovery use channels

Recovered outputs may lawfully feed:

- relic or bonded-growth development,
- crafting and fabrication,
- repair and restoration,
- medicine and remediation,
- ritual or technique support,
- augmentation or installation procedures,
- platform upkeep,
- access fulfillment or credential checks,
- faction contracts and service obligations,
- advancement, threshold, or transformation systems.

Astra should preserve the existence of these use channels without forcing every donor to use all of them.

---

## Cross-donor retention and non-promotion rules

### Promoted Astra-stable grammar

- resource-source ontology broader than slain creatures,
- typed output grammar,
- treasure classes broader than sale-value objects,
- natural treasure as a meaningful progression-significant class,
- support for both organic and manufactured recovery ecologies,
- support for credential/access-bearing recoverables,
- downstream use channels that include growth, installation, repair, and access.

### Retained donor-local or provisional

- exact RHBF reserve semantics,
- exact RHBF harvest names and yield tables,
- exact Shadowrun accounting tables, market logic, or itemized device lists,
- exact donor-local formulas for extraction difficulty or payout,
- exact trophy, threat, or donor rarity scales unless later promoted.
