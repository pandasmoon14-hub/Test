# Astra Ascension — Paths and Techniques Packet v0.1 (Cross-Donor Revised)

## Scope of this packet

This packet preserves Astra's path-and-technique layer while correcting RHBF-overfit after cross-donor comparison against Shadowrun.

It owns:
- path-facing role packages,
- domain-gated learnability,
- technique-facing access and activation grammar,
- the distinction between ordinary competence and codified active expression,
- and the current live examples for path-linked power expression.

It does **not** claim that all Astra build differentiation reduces to paths plus techniques alone.

## Layered Build Law

Paths are one major Astra build layer, not the sole owner of all build identity.

A lawful Astra character may be shaped by several layers at once, including:
- body / origin structure,
- culture / background structure,
- path / profession / role package,
- access channels or permissions,
- capabilities and specializations,
- resources, contacts, or credentials,
- bonded objects,
- augmentations or installed systems,
- and donor-local special ratings or interface states.

This means a path-facing packet must remain compatible with donors where build pressure also comes from licenses, interface channels, augment overlays, social capital, or other non-path layers.

## Paths

A Path defines one major route by which an Ascendant begins and organizes growth.

A Path may grant or influence:
- recommended Attribute priorities,
- starting Domains,
- starting Resistances,
- ordinary trained capabilities,
- initial techniques or equivalent active expressions,
- and a distinctive doctrine of growth.

Paths remain lawful and important in Astra, but they are not the only possible owner of all meaningful differentiation.

## Ordinary Competence Versus Codified Expression

Skills and capabilities are not automatically techniques.

Astra distinguishes:
- **ordinary competence** — trained practical capability,
- **gated access** — permissions, channels, licenses, bonds, install states, or equivalent lawful access structures,
- **codified active expression** — techniques, spells, maneuvers, rituals, powers, complex forms, commands, or equivalent active objects.

RHBF pressures Astra toward skills-as-powers in some cases. Shadowrun pressures Astra in the opposite direction by proving that broad practical skill families should remain capabilities rather than being flattened into technique objects.

The stable Astra rule is:
- practical disciplines stay capabilities,
- codified active expressions stay ability objects,
- and access law decides what can be learned, equipped, installed, activated, or lawfully sustained.

## Domains and Access Law

Domains remain a strong Astra feature.

Domains are not decorative. They are capability keys and permission structures that help determine what an Ascendant may legitimately learn or activate.

A technique or equivalent active expression may list one or more Domains. An Ascendant may use that expression if they possess the required access state, domain, license, bond, rating, install state, attunement, or other lawful permission structure specified by the record.

This means Astra preserves **domain-gated learnability** while remaining broad enough to host donors that also require:
- licenses,
- interface states,
- bonded permissions,
- augmentation-linked permissions,
- credentials,
- or donor-local special access ratings.

## Technique Capacity Doctrine

Astra owns the **grammar** of capacity, access, readiness, and activation.

Donors may realize that grammar through different lawful profiles, including:
- fixed slot structures,
- prepared or readied limits,
- learned-known limits,
- rating-gated access,
- install-linked capacity tracks,
- bonded-object capacity tracks,
- stance-bound selections,
- or other donor-local architectures.

Astra therefore does **not** treat one donor's exact capacity realization as universal law.

### What Astra owns

Astra owns the following stable questions:
- What kind of expression is this?
- What access is required?
- How many may be readied, sustained, installed, equipped, or actively carried?
- What activates it?
- What refreshes it?
- What conflicts with other active expressions?
- What build layer grants or modifies it?

### What donors may vary

Donors may vary:
- exact slot counts,
- exact readied totals,
- exact rating ladders,
- exact install ceilings,
- exact replacement rules,
- exact refresh cadence,
- and exact object names for capacity structure.

## Donor-Local Capacity Profiles

### RHBF-style fixed slot profile

The RHBF `Crown / Arm / Stride / Core` topology remains a lawful donor-local retained profile.
It is mechanically coherent and useful for RHBF conversion fidelity, but it is **not** promoted here as universal Astra baseline law.

### Shadowrun-style access-channel pressure

Shadowrun proves that Astra must also host lawful build pressures such as:
- special access ratings,
- interface-native channels,
- augmentation-linked permissions,
- role packages not reducible to one Path,
- and active expressions that live in separate owners rather than ordinary technique slots.

Those pressures are valid. They should not be flattened into ordinary technique monoculture.

## Technique Families and Expression Types

Astra still benefits from strong active-expression classification, but the classification must remain broad enough for multiple donor families.

Useful first-pass families include:
- **Combat Expression** — directly offensive or contest-facing action objects,
- **Movement Expression** — reposition, traversal, mobility, escape, or route-changing objects,
- **Utility Expression** — sensing, shielding, support, restoration, manipulation, control, or state-altering objects,
- **Peak / Crown Expression** — rare or decisive effects carried by special capacity law,
- **Persistent or Installed Expression** — effects sustained by install, bond, stance, channel, or ongoing state rather than ordinary one-off activation.

These are Astra-facing families, not a claim that every donor uses the same exact names or capacity shape.

## Cadence and Refresh

Cadence remains a valid Astra-facing concern, but exact cadence ladders are not universalized from one donor.

Astra can host lawful cadence structures such as:
- freely reusable expressions,
- encounter-limited expressions,
- day-limited expressions,
- recharge or refresh expressions,
- install-persistent expressions,
- and donor-local special refresh rules.

The stable Astra concern is **refresh law**, not one donor's exact vocabulary.

## Path-Facing Capability Guidance

As a live standard:
- a Path may still grant ordinary trained capabilities,
- Background or equivalent layers may add more,
- and later advancement may deepen competence or widen access.

If a donor places heavy pressure on non-path build layers, those layers remain lawful owners of meaningful starting differentiation.

Examples include:
- contacts and social capital,
- starting resources,
- credentials and clearance,
- augmentation overlays,
- interface-native capacity,
- bonded-object privilege,
- and donor-local reserve or tactical-economy pressures.

## Technique Record Format

Every Astra technique or equivalent codified active expression should record, at minimum:

**Name**  
**Expression Type**  
**Required Access**  
**Domain or Equivalent Permission Key**  
**Capacity / Readiness Law**  
**Cadence / Refresh Law**  
**Action / Activation**  
**Range**  
**Target / Area**  
**Effect**  
**Sustain / Conflict Notes**  
**Upgrade / Growth Notes**

This format keeps Astra stable across donors that differ sharply in how they grant, ready, and activate power.

## Current cross-donor stance

Promoted here as Astra-stable:
- Paths remain a lawful and important build layer.
- Domain-gated learnability remains strong Astra grammar.
- Astra owns capacity, access, readiness, and activation grammar.
- Ordinary competence is not identical to techniques.
- Donors may lawfully pressure additional layers such as licenses, augment channels, interface states, resources, contacts, or bonded permissions.

Retained donor-local or optional:
- exact RHBF slot topology,
- exact donor-specific cadence ladders,
- exact special access ratings,
- exact donor tactical-reserve economies,
- and any build logic that depends on one donor's unique package stack or naming scheme.
