# Astra Transit and Travel Infrastructure v0.1

Derived from cross-donor consolidation after RHBF, Shadowrun, Lancer, and Starfinder.

## Scope of this file

This file owns Astra's **transit and travel infrastructure doctrine**.

It defines:
- what transit is,
- what travel infrastructure is,
- how movement between nodes becomes meaningful procedure,
- how travel differs from ordinary scene movement,
- how travel interacts with access law, operational anchors, platforms, hazards, standing/scrutiny/trace, and opportunity structures,
- what travel families Astra currently recognizes,
- and what remains optional-profile support or donor-local retention.

It does **not** own:
- full operation-loop doctrine,
- full platform object schema,
- full starship/vehicle schema,
- exact donor travel cosmologies,
- exact navigation minigames,
- exact fuel economies,
- exact jump or Drift rules,
- exact trade-route tables,
- exact ship combat turns,
- or exact world-generation systems.

This is a procedure and infrastructure owner, not a donor travel catalog and not a platform-stat file.

---

## Why this file exists

The donor set now proves that Astra cannot treat travel as a trivial transition.

RHBF proves that Astra must support:
- explicit access-linked travel,
- operational movement between hubs, trials, realms, and sites,
- dangerous traversal,
- and movement as a meaningful part of the world loop.

Shadowrun proves that Astra must support:
- secure and insecure routes,
- constrained movement through institutional or technical scrutiny,
- layered approach/infiltration/traversal,
- and travel shaped by attention, legality, and exposure.

Lancer proves that Astra must support:
- deployment structure,
- mission entry and extraction,
- operational repositioning,
- and travel/deployment as procedure rather than flavor text.

Starfinder proves that Astra must support:
- stations, ports, worlds, and frontier nodes,
- world-to-world and interstellar travel,
- shared mobile anchors,
- and larger-scale travel infrastructure without collapsing all movement into one donor cosmology.

The stable conclusion is:

> Transit is a real Astra procedure family.  
> Travel infrastructure is a real Astra support doctrine.  
> Neither should be reduced to "the party goes there."

## Core transit law

A **transit event** is movement between meaningful nodes, zones, sites, or scales of operation where:
- access matters,
- timing matters,
- route conditions matter,
- support or infrastructure matters,
- danger or exposure matters,
- or destination entry conditions materially affect play.

Astra should treat transit as procedure when movement meaningfully shapes:
- risk,
- access,
- timing,
- resource burden,
- exposure,
- sponsorship,
- opportunity,
- or operational viability.

The stable rule is:

> Travel is not procedure merely because distance exists.  
> Travel becomes procedure when route, infrastructure, access, danger, or consequence materially affect what can happen.

---

## Travel is not the same as other doctrine owners

### Transit is not the full world-operation loop
The world-operation loop owns:
- sponsor/hook,
- planning,
- broader operation structure,
- debrief,
- downtime,
- and recurring campaign flow.

Transit doctrine owns the movement layer inside or between those phases.

### Transit is not ordinary tactical movement
This file does not own:
- room-scale positioning,
- turn-by-turn movement,
- combat map geometry,
- or shell-level positioning.

That remains with tactical shell doctrine and related scene owners.

### Transit is not platform schema
A ship, vehicle, frame, or route system may enable transit.
Their object-side records belong elsewhere.

This file owns how those things matter procedurally in travel.

### Transit is not access law
Access law answers whether an actor is allowed, keyed, licensed, or authorized.

Transit doctrine answers how movement works when access becomes relevant to routes, entry, boarding, passage, or arrival.

### Transit is not node doctrine by itself
Nodes such as cities, stations, ports, worlds, gates, or strongholds matter here only insofar as they shape movement between them.

## Core travel families

These are the current Astra-facing transit families.

### 1. Local transit
Movement within one settlement, district, station, site cluster, or comparable short-horizon environment where:
- checkpoints,
- traffic,
- security posture,
- route choice,
- timing,
- or environmental conditions
may matter.

### 2. Regional transit
Movement across a broader region, wilderness, sector, or route network where:
- support points,
- hazards,
- pursuit,
- route denial,
- and timing pressure
may matter.

### 3. Site-to-site expeditionary transit
Movement between meaningful operation sites where:
- danger,
- setup,
- extraction planning,
- staging,
- and access
matter.

This is strongly validated by RHBF and Starfinder.

### 4. Deployment transit
Movement into an operation, conflict zone, or target area where:
- insertion,
- staging,
- deployment timing,
- or route compromise
matter.

Lancer strongly validates this family.

### 5. Vehicle/platform-enabled transit
Movement materially shaped by a transport, vehicle, starship, carrier platform, or other platform-side enabler.

This is now baseline-supportable after Lancer and Starfinder.

### 6. World-to-world transit
Movement between worlds, planets, major habitats, or equivalent separated macro-nodes.

Starfinder strongly validates this family.

### 7. Interstellar or macro-network transit
Movement through a larger-scale network where infrastructure, passage regime, and route structure matter.

Starfinder validates this strongly enough to preserve the family, though exact travel cosmology remains donor-local.

### 8. Restricted or credential-gated transit
Movement where:
- permits,
- licenses,
- routes,
- seals,
- manifests,
- docking rights,
- or keyed-state conditions
matter.

RHBF, Shadowrun, and Starfinder all reinforce this family differently.

## Travel infrastructure doctrine

Astra now needs a stable concept of **travel infrastructure**.

Travel infrastructure includes:
- ports,
- stations,
- gates,
- checkpoints,
- roads,
- tunnels,
- route networks,
- relays,
- docking facilities,
- border systems,
- jump or passage systems,
- convoy lanes,
- authorized corridors,
- and comparable support/constraint structures.

Travel infrastructure matters because it shapes:
- where movement is possible,
- how movement is verified,
- how movement is delayed,
- how movement is supported,
- and what risks attach to passage.

The stable rule is:

> A route is not only distance.  
> A route is also an infrastructure relationship.

## Operational nodes and transit

Transit doctrine depends on **operational nodes** but does not own them.

Nodes relevant to transit may include:
- cities,
- capitals,
- settlements,
- strongholds,
- stations,
- ports,
- colonies,
- frontier habitats,
- ruins,
- gates,
- docking points,
- staging grounds,
- route waypoints,
- and extraction points.

Astra should preserve the distinction between:
- the node as a place/object/support point,
- and transit as the movement procedure between nodes.

## Required travel questions

When travel matters procedurally, Astra should ask at least these questions:

### 1. What is the origin node?
Where is movement beginning from, and what support or restriction applies there?

### 2. What is the destination node?
What must be reached, boarded, entered, or crossed into?

### 3. What is the route family?
Is this local, regional, expeditionary, deployment, vehicle/platform-enabled, world-to-world, or interstellar transit?

### 4. What access conditions apply?
Does movement require:
- permission,
- credential assets,
- a seal,
- a route key,
- a docking right,
- a legal state,
- a license,
- or a current authorization state?

### 5. What infrastructure shapes the route?
What ports, checkpoints, corridors, relays, roads, gates, routes, or networks matter?

### 6. What pressure exists?
What hazards, delays, scrutiny, pursuit, scarcity, environmental conditions, or political barriers affect movement?

### 7. What changes on arrival?
What must be true to enter, deploy, dock, land, pass inspection, or otherwise convert transit into arrival?

## Travel pressure families

Astra should currently support at least these travel pressure families.

### 1. Access pressure
Movement is limited by:
- permission,
- route status,
- legal recognition,
- sealed passage,
- docking rights,
- or keyed-state requirements.

### 2. Hazard pressure
Movement is shaped by:
- weather,
- hostile environment,
- vacuum,
- dangerous terrain,
- route instability,
- anomaly,
- or other world-facing danger.

### 3. Exposure pressure
Movement creates:
- scrutiny,
- trace,
- witness chains,
- technical residue,
- or route recognition risk.

### 4. Delay pressure
Movement is shaped by:
- checkpoints,
- congestion,
- route instability,
- damaged infrastructure,
- timing windows,
- interception,
- or long travel chains.

### 5. Support pressure
Movement depends on:
- resupply,
- refit,
- staging,
- escort,
- safe harbor,
- docking,
- maintenance,
- or available anchor points.

### 6. Claimant pressure
Movement is shaped by:
- border control,
- faction territory,
- blockade,
- disputed claims,
- tolls,
- sponsorship,
- or restricted passage regimes.

## Transit outcomes

Transit procedure may end in more than successful arrival.

A lawful travel outcome may be:
- clean arrival,
- delayed arrival,
- compromised arrival,
- denied arrival,
- diverted arrival,
- tracked arrival,
- forced reroute,
- costly passage,
- partial extraction,
- or aborted transit.

The stable rule is:

> Travel should be able to fail, degrade, expose, or redirect without always becoming a generic combat scene.

## Transit and other Astra doctrines

### With access and authorization grammar
Transit may require:
- access state,
- credentials,
- licenses,
- route permissions,
- seals,
- or docking rights.

Transit doctrine should not absorb those objects or states, but it must visibly depend on them where relevant.

### With credential/access assets
Some travel families are materially shaped by:
- passes,
- manifests,
- route keys,
- transit seals,
- docking authorizations,
- or identity-bearing objects.

These assets belong elsewhere, but their procedural role must be visible here.

### With standing/scrutiny/trace
Travel often changes consequence layers:
- clean transit may preserve low scrutiny,
- repeated route use may build trace,
- denied or challenged passage may affect standing,
- unsafe transit may force new exposure.

### With operational anchors
Travel often begins from or ends at an Operational Anchor.
A mobile anchor may itself be the transit enabler.

### With tactical shells
Transit may lead into:
- insertion shells,
- extraction shells,
- escort shells,
- interception shells,
- or corridor-control shells.

Transit doctrine should acknowledge the handoff without becoming shell doctrine.

### With platform and installable asset grammar
Ships, vehicles, docking systems, route devices, and related enablers belong to object-side doctrine.
Transit owns the procedure of passage, not the object record.

## Travel scale doctrine

Astra should now visibly preserve multiple movement scales.

### Personal/local scale
Movement inside one local area or node.

### Regional scale
Movement across a larger contiguous area or route network.

### Expeditionary scale
Movement between meaningful operational sites.

### Platform-supported scale
Movement enabled by a specific transport or platform.

### World-to-world scale
Movement across major separated macro-nodes.

### Interstellar scale
Movement across larger travel networks where passage regime, infrastructure, and macro-route law matter.

No single donor’s cosmology or route engine should dominate these scales by default.

Astra should support these as donor-local or optional profiles, not as universal baseline.

## Design rules for Astra-facing transit

1. Travel should become procedure only when route/infrastructure/access/pressure materially matter.
2. Origin, destination, and route family should be visible.
3. Travel infrastructure should be treated as real support/constraint structure.
4. Access-linked movement should remain distinct from ordinary movement.
5. Travel should be able to create exposure, delay, denial, or reroute.
6. Movement scales should remain plural rather than one-size-fits-all.
7. Platforms may enable transit without owning transit procedure.
8. No one donor cosmology should become Astra’s default travel law.

Astra now recognizes transit and travel infrastructure as a stable procedural doctrine family.

It supports:
- multiple travel scales,
- node-to-node movement as meaningful procedure,
- infrastructure-shaped routes,
- access-linked passage,

- and larger world-to-world movement without committing to one donor cosmology.

> If movement meaningfully depends on route, infrastructure, access, danger, exposure, or arrival conditions, Astra should treat it as transit procedure rather than as an invisible transition.
