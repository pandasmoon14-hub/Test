# Astra Creature Taxonomy v0.1

## Scope and ownership

This file is the Astra-facing owner for **mechanical classification doctrine** inside Batch C.

It does not own every creature, platform, entity, or asset record by itself. It owns the rule that **classification must do mechanical work** and the grammar that lets unlike donor ecologies share one lawful taxonomy layer.

This file therefore sits below the live creatures-and-bound-relics packet and beside narrower owners such as harvest and treasures, bound relics, and any later augmentation or platform schema files.

Its purpose is to remain broad enough to host pressure from:
- RHBF-style creature families and thresholded beasts,
- Shadowrun-style devices, platforms, support entities, and manufactured actors,
- and later donor families such as vehicles, mechs, starships, summon ecologies, swarms, divine beings, horrors, or civic infrastructure entities.

## Cross-donor design law

Astra canonizes **classification grammar** before it canonizes donor-default posture bundles.

That means:
- family, category, class, lineage, platform type, or equivalent grouping must do mechanical work,
- classification is not flavor-only metadata,
- exact donor-default immunity, vulnerability, or recovery packages are not promoted automatically,
- and exact donor naming, exact donor threat formulas, and exact donor ecology assumptions remain donor-local unless later donor pressure supports promotion.

## Mechanical taxonomy principle

Every serious creature, entity, platform, or support actor should carry a meaningful classification field.

A mechanical classification can imply one or more of the following:
- likely resistance or integrity posture,
- likely condition interaction,
- likely mobility or footprint assumptions,
- likely morale, instinct, protocol, or control behavior,
- likely environmental or network dependencies,
- likely harvest, salvage, extraction, or residue outputs,
- likely compatibility with crafting, installation, feeding, refining, or research loops,
- likely access or authority assumptions,
- likely faction, territorial, or institutional role.

The point is not that every classification must imply every one of these. The point is that classification must help the system predict behavior, aftermath, and lawful interaction.

## Stable classification fields

At minimum, an Astra-facing record should be able to carry:

- **Primary Classification** — the broad mechanical family or category,
- **Subtype / Pattern** — the narrower donor-local or setting-local identity,
- **Ecology Layer** — organic, anomalous, manufactured, institutional, mixed, or equivalent,
- **Threshold State** — baseline, elevated, ascendant, host-linked, autonomous, temporary, etc. where relevant,
- **Interaction Tags** — optional tags for later file owners such as harvest, salvage, installation, feeding, containment, credential-bearing, remote-control, or faction-claim pressure.

Not every donor needs every field in the same way. The important part is that Astra maintains a stable comparison grammar across unlike sources.

## Seed classifications

The following seed classifications are lawful Astra-facing examples, not an exhaustive universal roster.

### Organic and once-living classifications

- **Beast**: baseline fauna, predators, territorial ecologies, pack logic, instinct-driven actors
- **Ascendant Beast**: beast that has crossed a threshold of selfhood, authority, or transformative capacity
- **Plant**: root, spore, growth, entanglement, terrain interaction, cyclical spread
- **Undead**: death-bound persistence, unusual restoration logic, identity or will residue
- **Engineered Hybrid**: bred, altered, or otherwise intentionally modified living actor

### Anomalous or extra-ordinary classifications

- **Aberrant**: distortion, instability, paradox pressure, mutation, unpredictability
- **Elemental**: typed-energy embodiment, environment-linked manifestation, field-shaping presence
- **Astral**: dream, planar, void, oath, memory, or cosmic-adjacent manifestation
- **Summoned / Invoked Entity**: brought into the field by procedure, rite, channel, or command layer
- **Compiled / Pattern-Bound Entity**: assembled from code, resonance, ritual pattern, shard logic, or similar structured substrate

### Manufactured or installed classifications

- **Construct**: resilience, built logic, selective immunity, artificial maintenance, system-based behavior
- **Platform**: a record that exists as a physical or technical host distinct from its operator or controller
- **Support Platform**: drone, turret, relay, deployable, node, remote support frame, or similar subordinate system
- **Manufactured Security Actor**: defense unit, patrol frame, sentry shell, barrier rig, or equivalent autonomous or semi-autonomous actor
- **Credentialed Asset**: a record whose core value is access, permission, authorization, or legal/technical identity rather than raw combat function

### Institutional or mixed classifications

- **Territorial / Civic Entity**: site-bound, polity-bound, shrine-bound, guild-bound, or infrastructure-linked actor
- **Mixed Ecology Entity**: any actor that spans biological, anomalous, and manufactured features without collapsing cleanly into one donor family

These seed classifications are meant to show breadth. They are not yet a closed master list.

## Family and category posture doctrine

Classification should predict **likely posture**, but Astra does not yet promote one donor’s exact posture package as universal law.

A useful posture model can track tendencies such as:
- offense bias,
- defense or integrity bias,
- mobility bias,
- control or disruption bias,
- concealment or signal bias,
- environmental dependency,
- aftermath or output bias.

This allows classification to carry real system meaning without freezing exact immunity/vulnerability bundles too early.

### Promotion rule

Promoted now:
- family/category as active mechanical classifier,
- posture as a lawful implied field,
- aftermath/output expectations linked to classification.

Not promoted yet:
- exact donor-default resistance packages,
- exact donor-default condition bundles,
- exact family immunity/vulnerability defaults,
- exact Threat Level formulas tied to one donor.

## Ecology layers

Astra taxonomy must survive more than one ecology model.

### Organic ecology

Includes beasts, plants, altered organisms, predators, brood structures, and other living or once-living ecologies.

Typical pressures:
- territorial logic,
- harvestable residue,
- family-linked aftermath,
- thresholded growth,
- environmental dependency.

### Anomalous ecology

Includes astral beings, anomalies, dream-entities, invoked entities, paradox actors, oath residues, and other non-ordinary manifestations.

Typical pressures:
- unusual manifestation rules,
- non-biological or unstable aftermath,
- containment or banishment interactions,
- symbolic or metaphysical dependencies.

### Manufactured ecology

Includes constructs, augment-linked systems, devices, platforms, support units, rated technical actors, and other intentionally produced records.

Typical pressures:
- system failure and integrity logic,
- salvage rather than biological harvest,
- operator/platform separation,
- host-cost, install, or credential interaction,
- access-law and authorization dependence.

### Mixed ecology

Some donors will produce actors that blend two or more ecologies.

Astra should allow such entries to carry a primary classification plus interaction tags rather than forcing them into a false single-family certainty.

## Thresholded actors

Astra needs a lawful place for actors that cross a threshold of identity, authority, complexity, or self-direction.

An **Ascendant Beast** is one important example, but the doctrine is broader than beasts alone.

A thresholded actor may be:
- a beast that develops strategic selfhood,
- a construct that acquires command autonomy,
- a summoned entity that stabilizes beyond ordinary duration,
- a territorial anomaly that becomes a political or factional actor,
- or another record that becomes more than an ordinary instance of its baseline family.

### Threshold signs

An actor is a candidate for thresholded status when several of the following are true:
- it develops named or repeated system-level behaviors,
- it reshapes local ecology, territory, or procedure around itself,
- it acquires bargaining, command, or institutional significance,
- its remains, salvage, or residue are unusually identity-bearing,
- it changes category expectations without fully leaving its baseline classification behind.

Threshold state should be representable without forcing every high-tier actor into the same donor story.

## Taxonomy and record stability

Creature and entity taxonomy should remain compatible with stable record structure.

A useful Astra record can pair classification doctrine with fields such as:
- role,
- function,
- threat signal,
- defenses or integrity posture,
- mobility/access mode,
- stealth/detection or signal posture,
- actions,
- systems/traits/permissions,
- aftermath outputs,
- operator/platform linkage where relevant.

Classification should help explain the record, not replace it.

## Taxonomy and aftermath

Classification should help predict what kind of aftermath logic applies.

Possible aftermath implications include:
- biological harvest,
- structural salvage,
- anomalous extraction,
- credential seizure,
- identity-bearing residue,
- platform recovery,
- feed-compatible inputs for bonded-growth objects,
- research, study, or refinement channels.

This is where taxonomy links directly to harvest and treasures without being swallowed by that file.

## Donor-local retention rule

The following remain donor-local or provisional unless later donors reinforce them:
- exact RHBF family default posture bundles,
- exact RHBF threat and reserve-linked family assumptions,
- exact Shadowrun device/accounting tables,
- exact summoned/compiled category vocabularies,
- exact donor catalog names,
- exact threat formulas or progression math tied to one donor.

## Current Astra-facing promotion stance

### Promote now

- classification as active mechanical doctrine,
- family/category/class doing real work,
- broad ecology support across organic, anomalous, manufactured, and mixed entities,
- threshold-state support for actors that outgrow baseline roles,
- taxonomy-linked aftermath expectations,
- posture grammar without exact default packages.

### Optional profiles

- Ascendant Beast as one special thresholded actor profile,
- compiled or pattern-bound entities as one support-entity profile,
- credential-bearing assets as one classification-heavy profile,
- platform-linked support actors as one manufactured-ecology profile.

### Not promoted yet

- any exact donor-default family immunity/vulnerability package,
- any exact donor-default condition posture bundle,
- any exact donor-specific category formula or threat table.

## Design law summary

Astra creature taxonomy exists to make classification **predictive**.

It should tell the system what kinds of defenses, conditions, aftermaths, dependencies, and operational interactions are likely.

It should not quietly turn one donor’s family packages into hidden universal law.
