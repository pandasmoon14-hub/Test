# Astra Bound Relics v0.1

## Scope and ownership
This file owns Astra's cross-donor grammar for **bound-growth objects**: owner-linked relics, bonded implements, bonded weapons, bonded foci, or similar objects whose development is tied to a bearer relationship and a structured growth loop.

This file does **not** own all special equipment, all magical items, all augmentations, or all installed systems.

In particular, this file must remain distinct from:
- ordinary wondrous or exceptional items,
- installed augmentations and host-cost systems,
- rated devices and credential assets,
- platforms, vehicles, frames, and other operator-distinct assets,
- donor-local sentience, personality, or artifact cosmology assumptions.

## Core identity
A Bound Relic is an object class defined by four Astra-stable properties:

- **bearer linkage**: the object is tied to one bearer, one claimant, or one controlled bearer set,
- **growth track**: the object can change over time through lawful inputs rather than remaining static,
- **trait-bearing development**: the object gains, unlocks, or intensifies structured properties,
- **typed progression pressure**: the growth loop depends on what is fed, bound, accomplished, endured, claimed, or otherwise lawfully integrated.

Astra accepts this grammar as baseline. It does **not** accept any one donor's exact growth math as universal law.

## Cross-donor stance
RHBF strongly validates bound-growth through resonant feeding, typed relic traits, and progression-significant materials. Shadowrun pressures Astra in the opposite direction by proving that many high-value object classes are **installed, rated, licensed, or credentialed** without being bound-growth relics.

Therefore Astra distinguishes:
- **Bound Relics**: owner-linked objects with bearer-bound growth grammar,
- **Installed Systems / Augmentations**: host-linked systems governed by installation law rather than relic-growth law,
- **Rated Devices / Platforms**: asset classes governed by rating, licensing, mounts, interface, or operator procedure rather than bearer-growth law.

This distinction is Astra-stable after RHBF + Shadowrun.

## Core properties
Bound Relics may have some or all of the following:

- owner-bound or claimant-bound state,
- bearer resonance or compatibility requirements,
- growth milestones or thresholds,
- trait slots, trait bands, or thresholded trait unlocks,
- persistence beyond ordinary wear,
- heightened repair, recovery, or reconstitution rules,
- optional personality-bearing, memory-bearing, or semi-sentient presentation.

### Non-baseline note
Astra does **not** require Bound Relics to be sentient, weapon-shaped, or personality-bearing. Those are lawful donor-local or optional-profile variants, not universal defaults.

## Growth law
Astra owns the grammar of bound-growth, not one fixed donor loop.

A lawful Bound Relic growth loop contains these fields:
- **bond state**: how the bearer link is established or maintained,
- **accepted input types**: what kinds of lawful feed, offering, integration, proof, or component inputs matter,
- **growth thresholds**: when development checks or advancement events occur,
- **trait acquisition law**: how new traits or improvements are selected, rolled, unlocked, or earned,
- **stability / strain law**: what limits, risks, backlash, or saturation pressures apply,
- **maintenance / recovery law**: how the relic is restored, repaired, or reattuned if damaged or disrupted.

A donor may realize this through resonant feeding, milestone claims, bonded attunement thresholds, trait purchases, sacrifice loops, affinity tracks, or other lawful structures.

## Progression loop
Astra supports the following general progression pattern:

encounter / operation / recovery -> acquire typed input -> lawful integration or feeding -> threshold check -> gain, intensify, or transform one or more relic traits

This is the cross-donor-stable loop.

### Donor-local note
RHBF's exact sequence of harvested components, refined materials, and exact trait-cap math remains a lawful donor profile, but it is **not** Astra baseline.

## Input types
A Bound Relic may grow from one or more typed input categories such as:
- harvested biological components,
- refined materials,
- natural treasures,
- anomalous residues,
- oath or claim proofs,
- bearer milestones,
- operational trophies,
- site-linked or event-linked resonance,
- credentialed or licensed feed equivalents when a donor supports them.

Astra owns the existence of typed inputs. Exact donor names, exact tables, and exact feed recipes remain donor-local unless later canonized.

## Relic Trait law
Relic Traits are structured properties gained through the relic's growth track.

Astra-stable trait law:
- traits must be tied to lawful growth inputs, thresholds, or bearer-state changes,
- traits should be typed and recorded rather than handled as vague flavor,
- traits may add capability, reshape operation, alter affinity, or unlock further evolution,
- traits may be intensified, replaced, refined, or branched depending on donor profile.

Traits may reflect:
- source material type,
- creature or family-derived pressure,
- site or anomaly origin,
- bearer behavior or achievements,
- covenant, oath, or claimant state,
- or other lawful donor-supported feed vectors.

### Non-promotion note
RHBF's exact trait cap, exact bearer bonus math, and exact component naming stay donor-local.

## Bound-state variants
Astra should support multiple lawful bound-growth profiles, including:
- weapon-shaped bound relics,
- focus or implement relics,
- defensive relics,
- companion-adjacent relics,
- utility or traversal relics,
- non-weapon heirloom or seal relics.

This keeps the file broad enough to survive later donors without treating the living-weapon profile as the only real form.

## Distinction from adjacent object classes
### Bound Relics vs ordinary items
Ordinary rare or wondrous items may be powerful, but they do not become Bound Relics unless they satisfy bearer linkage plus lawful growth-track criteria.

### Bound Relics vs augmentations
Installed augmentations change the host through installation and host-cost law. They may evolve in some donors, but they are not automatically Bound Relics unless they also meet bearer-bound object-growth criteria.

### Bound Relics vs rated devices / platforms
A rated device, platform, drone, frame, or credential asset may be critical, persistent, or personalized, but that does not make it a Bound Relic. Those classes are governed primarily by rating, license, mount, install, or platform procedure law.

## Promotion posture after RHBF + Shadowrun
### Astra-stable now
- bound-growth object grammar,
- bearer linkage as a lawful object state,
- typed input / feed vectors,
- trait-bearing development,
- distinction between bound relics and non-bound asset classes.

### Optional profiles
- weapon-shaped bound relic dominance,
- personality-bearing or semi-sentient relic presentation,
- thresholded bonded heirloom / seal variants,
- tightly milestone-locked trait acquisition.

### Donor-local retained
- exact RHBF growth math,
- exact trait caps,
- exact bearer bonus ladders,
- exact component names,
- exact relic personality assumptions,
- exact donor-specific feed formulas.

## Record guidance
A Bound Relic record should ideally capture:
- name,
- class / subtype,
- bearer-link state,
- accepted input types,
- current growth state,
- trait list,
- thresholds or advancement triggers,
- maintenance / recovery notes,
- donor-local profile notes where relevant.
