# Astra Ascension — Activity Engine Packet v0.1

## Why Ascendants Move

Ascendants do not grow by meditation alone. They grow by exposure, conflict, extraction, discovery, contract, and claim.

The Astra activity loop is built around four recurring structures:

- **Nexus Hubs**
- **Transit Seals**
- **Ascension Trials**
- **Hunting Realms**

These create the practical rhythm of advancement.

## Core Loop

A standard Astra cycle looks like this:

1. Return to a Nexus Hub
2. Gather rumors, contracts, warnings, and faction pressure
3. Acquire or spend Transit Seals
4. Enter an Ascension Trial, Hunting Realm, or Expedition Site
5. Survive, claim, harvest, or extract
6. Return to Stronghold Phase
7. Convert gains into future capability
8. Trigger new rivalry, pressure, or opportunity

If a campaign is missing one of these beats, it will usually feel flat.

## Nexus Hubs

A Nexus Hub is a repeatable operations center.
It is where Ascendants:
- take contracts
- buy or barter for specialized supplies
- negotiate with factions
- access Technique Repositories
- process captured information
- arrange travel
- convert short-term victories into long-term leverage

A Hub is not just a city. It is a **campaign control point**.

### Common Nexus Hub actions

When characters return to a Nexus Hub, they usually choose 2–4 actions:
- take a faction contract
- sell or process harvested materials
- buy, trade, or earn a Transit Seal
- repair, upgrade, or feed a Bound Relic
- research a Trial or Realm
- recruit help or make a political approach
- access a Repository to learn, trade, or verify a Technique
- resolve a Pressure Event

### Pressure at a Hub

A hub should never be inert.
On each significant return, the Guide should introduce at least one:
- contract
- threat warning
- faction demand
- market distortion
- rumor of a Trial opening
- travel disruption
- new claim on a Realm or site

That keeps the hub from becoming a static shop menu.

## Transit Seals

Transit Seals are access keys.
They grant lawful, illicit, contingent, or opportunistic movement into places of value.

Transit Seals may open access to:
- Ascension Trials
- Hunting Realms
- sealed ruins
- faction enclaves
- resource sites
- emergency extraction routes
- temporary event locations

### How characters acquire Transit Seals

Transit Seals can be:
- purchased
- granted by factions
- won in a Trial
- scavenged from defeated rivals
- assembled from fragments
- copied at risk
- issued as temporary warrants or licenses

### How Transit Seals create play

Transit Seals matter because they:
- gate opportunity
- create competition
- let factions control access
- create reasons to negotiate, raid, or cooperate
- turn travel into a strategic choice instead of a handwave

## Ascension Trials

An Ascension Trial is a discrete challenge node built around claim, selection, and reward.
A Trial is not just a dungeon and not just a fight. It is a **designed test under pressure**.

Every Trial should answer six questions:

1. **How is it accessed?**
   - open call
   - discovered seal
   - faction invitation
   - emergency rupture
   - inherited claim

2. **Where is it located?**
   - ruin
   - mountain shrine
   - dead city chamber
   - floating site
   - buried complex
   - anomaly zone

3. **Who else wants it?**
   - rival Ascendants
   - faction agents
   - local predators
   - claim-jumpers
   - guardians

4. **What is being tested?**
   - combat
   - endurance
   - perception
   - command
   - purity
   - adaptation
   - sacrifice
   - technique mastery

5. **What is the reward?**
   - Transit Seals
   - Techniques
   - relic growth material
   - faction standing
   - rare resources
   - world authority
   - secret knowledge

6. **What is the cost of failure?**
   - injury
   - exposure
   - wasted opportunity
   - faction retaliation
   - corruption
   - sealed losses
   - rival advancement

### Running a Trial

A Trial usually has three beats:
- **Approach**: reach it, decipher it, or survive entry pressure
- **Core Test**: face the defining challenge
- **Claim**: secure the reward while under pressure from rivals, guardians, or consequences

That structure keeps Trials distinct from general exploration.

## Hunting Realms

A Hunting Realm is a repeatable progression space where danger and harvest are both expected.

Unlike a Trial, a Realm is not primarily a one-time test.
It is a **renewable pressure environment**.

A Hunting Realm should define:
- threat band
- travel difficulty
- encounter rate
- environmental hazards
- common prey
- elite presences
- harvest outputs
- retreat or extraction conditions

### What Hunting Realms are for

Use Hunting Realms when characters want to:
- train under pressure
- hunt specific components
- test a new Technique loadout
- gather relic-feed materials
- clear repeated faction interference
- escort or patrol
- establish control over a dangerous region

### Realm rhythm

A Realm run usually follows:
1. entry and navigation
2. hazard or tracking phase
3. encounter or prey acquisition
4. harvest or extraction
5. escalation roll for one more risk or reward
6. exit, retreat, or overcommitment

That makes Realms distinct from Trials:
- Trials are claim-focused and discrete
- Realms are repeatable and yield-focused

## Expedition Sites

Some opportunities are not cleanly Trials or Realms.
Use an Expedition Site when the characters are entering a hostile, valuable, or unstable place with a limited objective.

An Expedition Site usually emphasizes:
- tactical pathing
- environmental risk
- timed extraction
- discovery pressure
- limited-return opportunities

Examples:
- a collapsing forge complex
- a sealed vault
- a battlefield grave engine
- a contaminated relic chamber

## Faction Pressure

Factions are not background wallpaper.
They create pressure, access, obstruction, sponsorship, and retaliation.

A faction may:
- issue contracts
- control Seal access
- contest a Trial claim
- tax a Hub market
- sponsor Stronghold growth
- blacklist enemies
- raid harvest lanes
- manipulate rumors and information
- embargo Repositories or Gates

### Faction pressure in practice

At least one faction should be affecting the characters every cycle through:
- demands
- rewards
- leverage offers
- surveillance
- sabotage
- alliance opportunities

A faction matters only if it changes player choices.

## Stronghold Phase

Between expeditions, Ascendants enter Stronghold Phase.

This is the phase where gains become infrastructure.

During Stronghold Phase, characters may:
- heal and recover
- stabilize allies
- build or repair defenses
- upgrade a Gate Array
- expand or secure a Technique Repository
- refine harvested materials
- craft or improve gear
- feed a Bound Relic
- resolve internal events
- pay faction obligations
- assign personnel or retainers
- convert temporary gains into persistent assets

### Stronghold development categories

A Stronghold usually grows along four axes:
- **Defense**: walls, wards, response assets, hidden exits
- **Transit**: gates, routes, beacons, access control
- **Knowledge**: repositories, archives, maps, analysts
- **Production**: workshops, refining sites, storage, medical support

### Why Stronghold Phase matters

Without Stronghold Phase, progression stays personal and disposable.
With it, progression becomes civilizational, strategic, and worth defending.

## Pressure Events

Between or during cycles, the Guide should introduce Pressure Events.
These are events that force a choice rather than just add color.

Examples:
- a Hub market crash
- a Trial opening with short notice
- a faction ultimatum
- an elite beast migration into a Realm
- a Gate failure
- a Repository theft
- a rival claim on harvested territory
- a corruption leak in a Stronghold district

A good Pressure Event changes what the players thought they were about to do.

## Reward Types

The activity engine should repeatedly pay out in:
- raw resources
- harvested materials
- Technique access
- Transit Seals
- faction standing
- infrastructure growth
- relic-feed components
- territory influence
- political leverage

If rewards are only money or only lore, the loop weakens quickly.

## Distinction Summary

Use a **Nexus Hub** when characters need leverage, access, trade, politics, and preparation.

Use a **Transit Seal** when access should be strategic instead of free.

Use an **Ascension Trial** when you want a discrete, claim-centered challenge with a clear reward.

Use a **Hunting Realm** when you want repeatable progression, component hunting, and controlled danger.

Use an **Expedition Site** when the goal is a tactical or unstable objective that is not well modeled as a Trial or Realm.

Use **Stronghold Phase** when you want gains to become persistent assets instead of evaporating between scenes.
