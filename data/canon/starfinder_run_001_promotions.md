# Starfinder Run 001 Promotions

Run ID: `starfinder_prod_run_001`  
Purpose: `Authoritative promotion ledger derived from the Starfinder run after merge review and integration.`

This file records what Starfinder pressures Astra to promote, what should remain optional-profile support, and what should not be promoted at this stage.

---

## Baseline grammar

### 1. Theme/background-like identity as a real build layer
Starfinder strongly reinforces that a theme/background-like identity layer is a real build owner rather than decorative flavor.

This now sits alongside other identity/build layers and may shape:
- starting profile,
- competency emphasis,
- ongoing identity,
- and future ranked development.

### 2. Competence distinct from codified active expression
Starfinder strongly reinforces the distinction between:
- skills/competencies,
- and codified active expressions such as class features, spells, or similar objects.

This is reinforcement of already-stable doctrine.

### 3. Feats as specialization overlays
Starfinder strongly reinforces that feats belong in specialization-overlay space rather than being swallowed by Path, skill, or Technique doctrine.

### 4. Broadened Operational Anchor doctrine
Starfinder reinforces that operational anchors may include:
- ships,
- stations,
- ports,
- colonies,
- frontier worlds,
- and exploratory organizations.

### 5. Exploration as structured campaign engine
The donor strongly validates exploration/site-chain/world-chain procedure as a first-class campaign engine.

### 6. Operational nodes at civic/interstellar scale
Stations, ports, worlds, and frontier habitats are real procedural nodes.

### 7. Travel-access interplay
Travel is a real procedure family shaped by access, danger, and infrastructure.

### 8. Larger-scale sponsor/claimant faction logic
Organizations operating across worlds or large civic structures are a strong reinforcement for faction-pressure doctrine.

### 9. Starship as a major platform family
Starfinder is the strongest donor so far for validating starships as major platform-side schema owners.

### 10. Vehicle-family schema breadth
The donor strongly reinforces platform doctrine broader than mechs alone.

### 11. Mixed object ecology
Technology, magic, hybrid, installable, and platform-side object families coexist strongly enough to reinforce mixed-ecology doctrine.

### 12. Augmentation as installed body-facing asset
Starfinder strongly reinforces installed body-facing asset doctrine.

### 13. Rated system/computer object family
The donor strongly validates object classes whose significance lies in system/rating/state logic and access interaction.

### 14. Broadened entity schema
Starfinder reinforces entity doctrine capable of hosting mixed organic/technological/science-fantasy threats.

## Optional profiles

### 1. Resolve as reserve-family support
Preserve reserve-family significance.
Do not universalize Starfinder’s exact formula.

### 2. SP/HP-style split durability
Preserve split recoverable/deeper durability as optional-profile support rather than baseline law.

### 3. Shared starship campaign baseline
A campaign centered on one shared starship is a strong recurring profile, but not universal.

### 4. Ship-as-home-base / mobile base-operations profile
Lawful recurring support, not mandatory cadence.

### 5. Crew-role starship procedure family
Differentiated shipboard roles are clearly lawful, but exact role menus remain profile-level.

### 6. Starship-scene conflict family
A lawful scene family, but not universal baseline procedure.

### 7. Interstellar expedition cadence
A strong recurring profile, but not universal law.

### 8. Powered armor bridge family
Important pressure between gear, installable doctrine, and stronger platform doctrine; final placement remains open.

### 9. Shared persistent ship ownership
Lawful campaign/support profile, not universal assumption.

## Explicit refusals

### 1. Do not let Starfinder’s exact cosmology become Astra baseline
The donor’s interstellar setting pressure is useful, but exact Drift framing and Pact Worlds assumptions are not Astra universal law.

### 2. Do not let exact ship math become Astra platform law
Starships are promoted as a family; exact BP/tier and subsystem math are not.

### 3. Do not let theme become mandatory universal terminology
The layer is promoted; the donor word is not mandatory Astra terminology.

### 4. Do not collapse computers/rated systems into pure procedure
Their object-side importance is real and should stay visible.

### 5. Do not collapse mixed object ecologies back into single-source item assumptions
Starfinder strongly reinforces Astra’s mixed-object ecology.
