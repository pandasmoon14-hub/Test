## Authority
This file is the authoritative doctrine file for **Astra progression-axis architecture**.

It defines:
- what qualifies as a progression axis,
- the relationship between Astra's **primary ascension ladder** and its **supporting progression axes**,
- vertical and horizontal growth doctrine,
- dependency, gating, and eligibility relationships between axes,
- unlock cadence and scaling-policy doctrine,
- progression currencies, thresholds, and state markers,
- temporary versus persistent advancement distinctions,
- multi-frame, platform, and infrastructure advancement logic,
- and donor mapping rules for single-axis and multi-axis systems.

It does **not** define:
- final path, archetype, or profession package contents,
- final kinform, heritage, or background grants,
- chargen procedure,
- the detailed contents of future advancement tables,
- the complete ability-upgrade catalog,
- full gear compendium progression,
- or later canon presentation text beyond the structural requirements of progression itself.

If any later Batch A file appears to contradict this file on the **classification, ownership, relationship, or lawful mapping of advancement structures**, this file governs unless deliberately revised.

## 1. Purpose

Astra requires an advancement architecture that is both:
- **conversion-stable** across many donor systems,
- and **setting-coherent** as a recognizable Astra-native progression model.

This file exists to prevent two equal and opposite failures:
- **ladder collapse**, where every donor progression structure is forcibly flattened into one monolithic level track,
- and **progression fragmentation**, where Astra has so many unrelated advancement lanes that it ceases to feel like a coherent setting and starts feeling like a filing cabinet.

Astra therefore uses a two-layer progression model:
- an underlying **axis architecture**,
- and a canonical **primary ascension presentation ladder**.

The axis architecture preserves conversion flexibility.
The primary ladder preserves Astra's public-facing progression identity.

This file defines both, and the lawful relationship between them.

## 3. Core Progression Principles

### 3.1 Progression is axis-based, not ladder-monolithic
Astra does not treat all advancement as one undifferentiated “level.”

Advancement may occur across multiple lawful axes at once, including but not limited to:
- overall ascendant stature,
- inner refinement,
- lineage or heritage activation,
- path or profession mastery,
- capability development,
- relic, implant, platform, or infrastructure growth,
- and social or institutional authority where relevant.

### 3.2 Astra preserves a canonical progression spine
Astra may preserve a central public-facing progression spine without assuming that all advancement belongs only to that spine.

This spine is called the **Primary Ascension Axis** and is presented through the **Primary Ascension Ladder**.

### 3.3 Supporting axes are real, not decorative
Supporting progression axes are not flavor notes or hidden sidebars.
They are lawful advancement lanes that may:
- broaden options,
- deepen specialization,
- unlock thresholds,
- gate main-axis movement,
- reinforce major breakthroughs,
- or represent meaningful growth that does not immediately raise the main ladder.

### 3.4 Donor systems may map directly or indirectly
A donor progression structure may map:
- directly into Astra's primary ascension ladder,
- primarily into one or more supporting axes,
- or into a mixed normalized model where supporting-axis movement later informs overall primary-axis placement.

Astra must support both direct conversion and normalization.

### 3.5 Progression architecture is broader than raw power
Advancement may concern:
- potency,
- access,
- breadth,
- refinement,
- stability,
- mastery,
- integration,
- infrastructure,
- authority,
- or lawful conversion between those states.

A donor system does not need to increase broad attributes or raw damage numbers in order to be a real progression system.

### 3.6 Not every donor activates every axis
Astra defines a broad advancement architecture, but donor mappings should only activate the lanes that are actually relevant.

A donor with no lineage logic does not need forced lineage progression.
A donor with no gear maturation does not need fake relic ladders.
A donor with no cultivation interiority does not need invented inner refinement lore.

### 3.7 Progression state and progression currency are not the same thing
Astra distinguishes:
- **progression currency** — what is spent, earned, accumulated, or otherwise required,
- **progression state** — where an actor currently stands on an axis,
- **progression threshold** — what conditions must be met for movement,
- **progression outcome** — the actual resulting advancement.

No advancement file should collapse these into one smiling noun like “level.”

## 4. What Qualifies as a Progression Axis

### 4.1 Definition
A **progression axis** is a durable lane of lawful advancement that tracks meaningful change over time and can alter one or more of the following:
- potency,
- access,
- breadth,
- refinement,
- integration,
- mastery,
- authority,
- platform capability,
- or advancement eligibility relative to other axes.

### 4.2 What a progression axis is not
A progression axis is not merely:
- a temporary buff,
- a one-scene transformation,
- a consumable resource pool,
- a condition track,
- a single item effect,
- or a narrative label with no mechanical continuity.

Such things may interact with progression, but they are not automatically progression axes.

### 4.3 Axis admission rule
A candidate advancement lane should be treated as a true progression axis only if it is:
- durable across time,
- meaningful to long-term actor development,
- capable of lawful state change,
- and distinct enough from other existing axes that collapsing it would hide important structure.

If those conditions are not met, the construct should usually remain:
- a subsystem detail,
- a source-local growth track,
- or a derived expression of another axis rather than a new core axis.

## 5. The Primary Ascension Axis and Ladder

### 5.1 Function
Astra uses a canonical **Primary Ascension Axis** to represent overall integrated ascendant stature.

This axis is the setting's public-facing progression spine. It is not identical to every supporting advancement structure, but it is the central ladder by which Astra presents broad ascendant rise.

### 5.2 Structural model
The default Astra presentation model preserves a structured major-stage progression ladder comparable in complexity to RHBF's multi-rank, multi-level pattern.

Accordingly, Astra recognizes a **five-stage major ascension ladder with ten internal steps per major stage** as its canonical primary presentation structure unless later canonical doctrine deliberately revises that frame.

This file governs the structural pattern, not permanent donor-derived naming.
Final ladder names may be stabilized later by canonical lexicon or sourcebook doctrine.

### 5.3 What the primary axis means
The Primary Ascension Axis represents **overall integrated ascendant development**.

It is not merely:
- raw combat level,
- a class level,
- a bloodline tier,
- a relic level,
- or a soul-refinement gauge in isolation.

Rather, it is the lawful public-facing measure of where an actor stands in Astra's broad ascendant order after relevant supporting axes, thresholds, and eligibility conditions are considered.

### 5.4 What the primary axis does not erase
Movement on the primary axis does not make supporting axes unreal.

An actor may:
- advance horizontally without rising in major stage,
- deepen one or more supporting axes without immediate primary-ladder ascent,
- meet some but not all requirements for main-axis movement,
- or gain substantial local power without immediately changing overall ascendant stature.

### 5.5 Presentation rule
Astra may preserve a satisfying major-stage progression identity without forcing every donor mechanic into that single ladder.

That is the doctrinal compromise that keeps Astra coherent without turning it into donor imitation.

## 6. Vertical vs Horizontal Growth

### 6.1 Vertical growth
**Vertical growth** increases ceiling, potency, order-of-play relevance, threshold access, or overall development tier.

Vertical growth commonly appears as:
- major-stage ascent,
- internal step increase within a major ladder,
- higher-order refinement,
- stronger platform tier,
- or passage into content gated by a more advanced developmental state.

### 6.2 Horizontal growth
**Horizontal growth** broadens options, coverage, specialization spread, operational reach, or lawful flexibility without necessarily changing the actor's overall ascension stage.

Horizontal growth commonly appears as:
- new capabilities,
- additional ability branches,
- broader access rights,
- profession breadth,
- item suite diversification,
- supplemental specializations,
- or supporting-axis enrichment.

### 6.3 Hybrid growth
Some advancement steps are **hybrid**. They deepen potency and broaden options at once.

Astra permits hybrid growth, but doctrine should still identify which component is vertical, which is horizontal, and which axis actually owns the change.

### 6.4 Anti-collapse rule
Horizontal growth is not “fake progression,” and vertical growth is not the only lawful form of advancement.

A donor with heavy feat webs, profession branches, relic integration, or capability breadth still has real progression even if its raw ladder is shallow.

## 7. Major Axis Families

### 7.1 Primary ascension axis
The main presentation ladder representing overall integrated ascendant stature.

### 7.2 Inner refinement axis
Advancement tied to internal cultivation, soul refinement, reservoir quality, psychic depth, core purification, spiritual stabilization, or analogous inward growth.

### 7.3 Lineage / heritage / inheritance axis
Advancement tied to bloodline awakening, ancestral resonance, inherited pattern activation, bonded legacy, genomic unlock, or equivalent persistent inherited structures.

### 7.4 Path / profession / role-mastery axis
Advancement tied to class-like, path-like, archetype-like, profession-like, doctrine-school-like, or role-mastery structures.

### 7.5 Capability and ability-development axis
Advancement tied to learning new capabilities, mastering techniques, broadening operational scope, refining specializations, or lawfully upgrading ability families.

### 7.6 Gear / relic / implant / platform axis
Advancement tied to living weapons, relic attunement depth, implant maturity, suit integration, mech frame upgrade, vehicle growth, ship systems, drone suites, or other platform-linked development.

### 7.7 Social / institutional / faction axis
Advancement tied to rank, office, clearance, authority, sect position, faction standing, command capacity, legal privileges, or similar institutional ladders where donor systems meaningfully support them.

### 7.8 Infrastructure / network / capital axis
Advancement tied to site development, sanctum maturation, colony growth, domain control, infrastructure unlocks, or networked systems that meaningfully alter an actor's practical developmental state.

### 7.9 Minor or donor-local axes
Astra allows donor-local advancement lanes that do not rise to core-axis status, provided they are mapped lawfully and do not pollute the core architecture.

## 8. Dependency, Gating, and Synchronization Relationships

### 8.1 Independence
Some axes may advance independently.
Movement on one does not automatically require movement on another.

### 8.2 Prerequisite gating
One axis may require thresholds in another before lawful advancement occurs.

This is the structural lesson Astra preserves from RHBF-style multi-rank dependence without inheriting donor specifics as hidden law.

### 8.3 Reinforcement
Progress on one axis may strengthen, accelerate, stabilize, or qualify progress on another without strictly gating it.

### 8.4 Synchronization
Some systems may require approximate parity or synchronized movement across two or more axes.

### 8.5 Partial gating
An axis may require only selected thresholds, branches, milestones, or states from another axis rather than full co-equal advancement.

### 8.6 Eligibility logic
Astra recognizes **Ascension Eligibility Logic** as the lawful doctrine determining when movement on the primary ascension axis is allowed, blocked, accelerated, delayed, or modified by supporting-axis state.

Eligibility logic may reference:
- supporting-axis thresholds,
- access requirements,
- rare events,
- trial completion,
- refinement state,
- item/platform integration,
- or other lawful prerequisites.

### 8.7 Anti-collapse rule
Supporting axes may gate or reinforce primary-axis advancement without being collapsed into it.

If a donor system requires multiple developmental components before a breakthrough, Astra should preserve that structure rather than flattening every prerequisite into “more level.”

## 9. Unlock Cadence Models

### 9.1 Definition
**Unlock cadence** is the lawful pattern by which new options, thresholds, functions, branches, or statuses become available over time.

### 9.2 Common cadence forms
Astra supports at least the following cadence models:
- regular incremental cadence,
- milestone cadence,
- threshold cadence,
- branch-choice cadence,
- external-acquisition cadence,
- training/research cadence,
- event-triggered cadence,
- and item/platform-attunement cadence.

### 9.3 Major-stage cadence
The primary ascension ladder may combine:
- internal step cadence within a stage,
- and major threshold cadence between stages.

This preserves a RHBF-like sense of internal climb plus major breakthrough without requiring all supporting axes to mirror that same rhythm.

### 9.4 Donor normalization rule
When a donor cadence does not map directly to Astra's public ladder, Astra should normalize the donor's cadence into the relevant supporting axes first, and only then determine whether primary-axis movement is warranted.

## 10. Scaling Policy Models

### 10.1 Definition
**Scaling policy** determines how effectiveness, thresholds, or granted functions change as progression advances.

### 10.2 Supported scaling models
Astra recognizes at least the following lawful scaling models:
- automatic scaling,
- invested scaling,
- threshold scaling,
- upgrade-based scaling,
- item/platform scaling,
- branch scaling,
- and fixed non-scaling effects.

### 10.3 Ownership rule
The file that owns a subsystem later determines its exact content, but this file defines the lawful policy classes under which such scaling may occur.

### 10.4 Anti-assumption rule
Advancement along one axis does not automatically scale every other subsystem equally.

Some things:
- rise with the primary axis,
- some require separate investment,
- some scale only through branch development,
- and some remain fixed unless specifically upgraded.

## 11. Persistent vs Temporary Advancement States

### 11.1 Persistent advancement
Persistent advancement changes the actor's long-term lawful developmental state.

This is the primary concern of `batchA_10`.

### 11.2 Temporary progression-like states
Astra recognizes temporary progression-like states such as:
- unstable breakthroughs,
- battle transformations,
- temporary awakenings,
- trial boons,
- session-limited modes,
- emergency overdrives,
- or borrowed ascendant states.

These may mimic advancement, but they are not automatically persistent axis movement.

### 11.3 Interaction rule
Temporary states may:
- reference progression thresholds,
- simulate higher-axis behavior,
- or create future advancement consequences,
without becoming permanent advancement unless another rule explicitly converts them.

## 12. Multi-Frame, Platform, and Infrastructure Progression

### 12.1 Multi-frame doctrine
An actor may possess multiple progression-relevant frames.

Examples include:
- pilot and mech,
- summoner and summon network,
- body and relic-shell,
- ship and crew doctrine,
- caster and bonded entity,
- operator and drone lattice.

### 12.2 Frame-specific advancement
Different frames may progress along different axes at different rates.

Astra therefore permits:
- actor growth distinct from platform growth,
- platform growth distinct from path mastery,
- and companion or network growth distinct from both.

### 12.3 Owner versus platform growth
A relic maturing, a vehicle frame upgrading, or a capital structure expanding is not automatically identical to character advancement, even if it contributes to overall power and may influence primary-axis eligibility.

### 12.4 Shared and external progression
Some advancement may be:
- crew-bound,
- institution-bound,
- site-bound,
- network-bound,
- lineage-bound,
- or otherwise externalized beyond one actor's body.

Astra allows such progression, but it must still be classified lawfully by axis and ownership.

## 13. Progression Currencies, Thresholds, and State Markers

### 13.1 Progression currency
A **progression currency** is something earned, accumulated, refined, spent, or otherwise required in order to move on an axis.

Examples may include:
- experience-like value,
- refinement substrate,
- lineage resonance,
- reputation,
- relic charge-depth,
- training credit,
- trial completions,
- or donor-local equivalents.

### 13.2 Progression threshold
A **progression threshold** is a condition that must be met before lawful advancement occurs.

Thresholds may include:
- numeric minimums,
- required milestones,
- branch completion,
- access requirements,
- trial completion,
- support-axis parity,
- or source-local triggers.

### 13.3 Progression state marker
A **progression state marker** records where the actor currently stands on an axis.

A state marker is not automatically the same thing as the currency that enabled movement to that state.

### 13.4 Currency-state separation rule
Astra must not collapse progression currency, threshold, and resulting state into one label.

This rule exists specifically to prevent donor progression from being mistranslated into fake simplicity.

### 13.5 Asynchronous advancement
Axes may advance unevenly.
Astra allows lawful reconciliation of asynchronous progress rather than assuming that all actors or all axes rise in perfect lockstep.

This matters for:
- multi-axis donors,
- absent-participant catch-up logic,
- gear/platform development,
- and systems where side axes lag or surge relative to the main ladder.

## 14. Donor Mapping Rules

### 14.1 Single-axis donors
A donor with one dominant ladder may map:
- directly into the primary ascension axis,
- or into a supporting axis that later informs primary-axis normalization,
depending on what the donor ladder actually measures.

### 14.2 Multi-axis donors
A donor with multiple advancement lanes should preserve those lanes wherever they remain structurally meaningful.
Astra should not flatten them merely for convenience.

### 14.3 Cultivation donors
Cultivation donors may distribute advancement across:
- primary ascension state,
- inner refinement,
- lineage awakening,
- rare technique acquisition,
- and breakthrough eligibility.

No one of those automatically owns the rest.

### 14.4 Class/package donors
Class, archetype, doctrine-school, or profession donors may map mostly into role-mastery axes, with only partial or normalized translation into the primary ascension ladder.

### 14.5 Feat/web and specialization donors
Donors built around webs, branches, talents, specializations, permissions, or modular picks often map heavily into horizontal and supporting-axis growth.
They should not be treated as “no progression” merely because they lack a dramatic central rank title.

### 14.6 Gear/platform donors
Gear-heavy and platform-heavy donors may map significant advancement into relic, implant, suit, vehicle, ship, or infrastructure axes. Those axes may influence overall ascendant stature without being identical to it.

### 14.7 Tag-, permission-, and narrative-state donors
Astra recognizes that some systems advance through tags, permissions, moves, relationships, authority shifts, or narrative-state growth rather than classic stat ladders.
Such systems still map lawfully into Astra progression architecture if their advancement is durable and mechanically meaningful.

### 14.8 Normalization rule
When direct one-to-one mapping would distort donor structure, Astra should:
1. classify donor advancement into the relevant axis or axes,
2. preserve the donor's real dependency logic,
3. determine whether primary-axis advancement is directly warranted,
4. and only then produce an Astra-native normalized presentation.

### 14.9 Anti-overfit rule
Astra may preserve a structured major-stage progression model comparable in complexity to RHBF's five-rank, ten-level frame, but donor systems must not be forcibly flattened into that ladder if their logic is better represented through multiple axes.
