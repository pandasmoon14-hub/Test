# batchA_11b_culture_background_and_pre_ascendant_life_framework

## 1. Purpose and authority

This file defines Astra doctrine for the **learned / social / formative side** of identity architecture. It governs culture, homeland or formative environment, upbringing, factional or institutional imprint, profession-before-adventure, and episodic formative event layers. It exists to prevent donor systems from collapsing learned identity, social formation, profession, factional relation, and dramatic life events into one bundled background object.

This file is authoritative for:
- culture doctrine
- homeland / formative environment doctrine
- upbringing and pre-Ascendant-life doctrine
- factional or institutional imprint doctrine
- profession-before-adventure doctrine
- episodic formative event-layer doctrine
- grant-boundary law for learned / social / formative identity layers

This file is **not** authoritative for:
- kinform
- embodiment or substrate
- heritage / inherited-origin doctrine
- current path / archetype / profession package law
- chargen order
- unrestricted access law
- major active ability suites
- primary progression ladders
- current faction command authority

Those belong elsewhere in Batch A.

## 2. Scope boundaries

This file covers:
- culture as learned worldview, norms, and identity practice
- homeland or formative environment as place-shaped familiarity and environmental formation
- upbringing as social formation before current adventuring role
- factional or institutional imprint as formative exposure rather than automatic current membership
- profession-before-adventure as prior social or work role
- episodic formative event layers
- mixed, displaced, plural, and nonstandard social formation
- what learned / social / formative layers may grant
- what learned / social / formative layers may not grant by default
- donor mapping rules for background-, culture-, homeland-, or upbringing-shaped constructs

This file does not cover:
- biology, kinform, or embodiment
- inherited heritage and lineage logic
- current path / class / archetype / doctrine school packages
- current role authority
- chargen procedure
- full access law
- full capability law
- full progression law

## 3. Core doctrine principles

### 3.1 Identity layers are separated by function

Learned, social, and formative identity layers are not allowed to absorb the jobs of body / inheritance layers, current path packages, current authority structures, or progression systems merely because a donor bundles them together.

### 3.2 Culture is not homeland

Culture and homeland may overlap, but they are not the same category.

- **Culture** is learned norms, values, language habits, etiquette, customs, social expectations, and identity practice.
- **Homeland / formative environment** is terrain, settlement pattern, climate, infrastructure, regional rhythms, and place-shaped familiarity.

A donor construct that bundles both must be split.

### 3.3 Upbringing is not profession and profession-before-adventure is not current path

How an actor was raised is not identical to what work they performed, and neither of those is automatically the same as the actor’s current adventuring role package.

### 3.4 Formative affiliation is not current authority

Being guild-raised, court-raised, monastery-raised, academy-trained, hospital-formed, or underworld-shaped does not automatically grant:
- current membership
- current command authority
- current faction rank
- unrestricted institutional access
- present political loyalty

### 3.5 Event-layer history is overlay, not identity superclass

Episodic formative events may matter greatly, but they do not replace culture, homeland, upbringing, factional imprint, or profession layers by default.

### 3.6 No single learned or social layer may silently replace the others

No donor construct may use one background-like object to silently stand in for all of the following at once:
- culture
- homeland / environment
- upbringing
- factional imprint
- profession-before-adventure
- event-history
- current faction membership
- current path package

## 4. Learned / social / formative layer split

This file distinguishes six related but non-identical layers.

### 4.1 Culture

**Culture** is the learned layer of norms, values, language practice, etiquette, customary behavior, symbols, and identity habits.

Culture answers questions such as:
- what social expectations shaped this actor
- what language and etiquette practices are normal to them
- what worldview habits or identity practices were learned
- what customs, taboos, or social codes are familiar

### 4.2 Homeland or formative environment

**Homeland / formative environment** is the place-shaped layer of terrain, settlement, climate, infrastructure, region, and environmental familiarity.

It answers questions such as:
- what kind of place formed this actor
- what environmental assumptions are normal to them
- what regional habits, terrain, or infrastructure are familiar
- what settlement rhythms or travel patterns shaped them

### 4.3 Upbringing / pre-Ascendant social formation

**Upbringing** is the layer of how the actor was raised or socially formed before their current role state.

It answers questions such as:
- what social mode of formation shaped the actor
- what household, communal, institutional, or informal structures raised them
- what expectations, routines, and formative disciplines were normal

### 4.4 Factional or institutional imprint

**Factional / institutional imprint** is formative exposure to a guild, court, monastery, archive, criminal network, hospital, academy, trade house, civic office, military structure, or similar institution.

It answers questions such as:
- what institution shaped the actor’s formation
- what procedures, habits, contacts, or obligations derive from that formation
- what traces of affiliation remain even if current membership does not

### 4.5 Profession-before-adventure

**Profession-before-adventure** is the actor’s prior social or work role before current adventuring / operative / ascendant identity took precedence.

It answers questions such as:
- what the actor used to do
- what practical work identity they held
- what labor, trade, or vocational networks or habits shaped them

### 4.6 Event-layer formative history

**Event-layer formative history** is the episodic overlay of major past events that affected the actor without automatically redefining the whole identity stack.

Examples include:
- war survivor
- political hostage
- disaster witness
- exile
- plague survivor
- escaped experiment subject
- miraculous rescue recipient
- trial survivor
- famous scandal participant

These are overlays, not default replacements for the persistent layers above.

## 5. Persistent layers versus episodic event layers

The following are **persistent learned / social identity layers**:
- culture
- homeland / formative environment
- upbringing
- factional / institutional imprint
- profession-before-adventure

The following are **episodic or overlay layers**:
- one major formative incident
- one traumatic episode
- one turning-point survival event
- one scandal, disaster, rite, exile, or crisis history

Event-layer entries may grant:
- hooks
- scars
- obligations
- narrow contacts
- reputation traces
- narrow familiarities
- source-specific baggage

They do **not** redefine the rest of the identity stack by default.

## 6. Formative affiliation versus current affiliation and authority

Formative institutional imprint may produce:
- familiarity
- procedural knowledge
- contacts
- obligations
- reputation traces
- limited recognition
- social expectations
- lingering loyalties or tensions

It does **not** automatically grant:
- current membership
- current command authority
- current faction rank
- unrestricted access
- current office or jurisdiction
- present doctrinal leadership

Examples Astra must preserve:
- guild-raised ≠ currently guild-ranked
- court-raised ≠ currently noble authority
- monastery-trained ≠ currently ordained or empowered
- academy-formed ≠ currently licensed
- underworld-shaped ≠ currently criminal command
- institution-built ≠ currently institution-controlled

## 7. Mixed, plural, displaced, and nonstandard social formation

### 7.1 Mixed and plural culture

An actor may lawfully possess:
- mixed culture
- adopted culture
- dual language worlds
- conflicting etiquette formations
- overlapping identity practices
- partial or layered cultural belonging

### 7.2 Displaced and transplanted formation

An actor may have been formed far from inherited origin or far from current place of life. Astra recognizes:
- exile
- diaspora formation
- offworld upbringing
- institutional relocation
- refugee formation
- fostered upbringing
- adopted homeland identity
- culturally displaced development

### 7.3 Non-biological and nonstandard social formation

Constructs, AI shells, symbiotes, relic-born beings, clones, grown vessels, bonded composites, and other nonstandard entities may still lawfully possess:
- culture
- etiquette
- institutional imprint
- profession-like prehistory
- homeland familiarity
- event-layer history

No conversion may assume ordinary biological childhood as the only valid formation model.

## 8. Grant-boundary law

### 8.1 Learned / social / formative layers may grant

These layers may lawfully grant:
- language
- literacy
- etiquette
- codes and social conventions
- cultural fluency
- homeland or regional familiarity
- narrow practical know-how
- institutional familiarity
- starting contacts
- obligations
- debts
- reputation traces
- pre-existing work networks
- minor persistent tags or hooks
- narrow environmental familiarity
- procedural familiarity tied to prior formation

### 8.2 Learned / social / formative layers may not grant by default

These layers may **not** grant by default:
- full path packages
- full current profession packages
- major active ability suites
- unrestricted access law
- whole progression ladders
- major stat rewrites
- current command authority
- current faction rank
- complete doctrine schools
- full package-scale combat suites
- unrestricted institutional permissions

Any donor construct that tries to do these jobs must be split across the proper Astra layers.

## 9. Donor mapping rules

When converting donor culture / background / upbringing / homeland / affiliation material, the converter must ask:

1. Is this describing learned norms, values, customs, language practice, or etiquette?  
   - If yes, map toward **culture**.

2. Is this describing terrain, settlement pattern, climate, region, infrastructure, or place-shaped familiarity?  
   - If yes, map toward **homeland / formative environment**.

3. Is this describing how the actor was raised or socially formed?  
   - If yes, map toward **upbringing**.

4. Is this describing formative exposure to an institution, faction, order, court, guild, or network?  
   - If yes, map toward **factional / institutional imprint**, not automatic current membership.

5. Is this describing prior work identity or pre-adventuring vocation?  
   - If yes, map toward **profession-before-adventure**.

6. Is this describing a major event or turning-point history?  
   - If yes, map toward **event-layer formative history**.

7. Is this donor object doing more than one of those jobs?  
   - If yes, split it. Do not preserve the bundle out of convenience.
