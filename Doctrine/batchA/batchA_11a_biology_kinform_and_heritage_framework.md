# batchA_11a_biology_kinform_and_heritage_framework

## 1. Purpose and authority

This file defines Astra doctrine for the **body / inheritance side** of identity architecture. It governs kinform, embodiment, substrate, inherited imprint, heritage, ancestry-adjacent origin, and related body- or inheritance-bound traits. It exists to prevent donor systems from collapsing biology, culture, profession, faction, trauma, and destiny into one bundled origin object.

This file is authoritative for:
- kinform doctrine
- embodiment and substrate doctrine
- heritage / inherited-origin doctrine
- mutable, hybrid, discontinuous, and non-biological body-origin handling
- grant-boundary law for body / inheritance layers

This file is **not** authoritative for:
- culture
- homeland
- upbringing
- profession-before-adventure
- current faction membership
- chargen order
- path or profession packages
- full active ability suites
- progression ladders

Those belong elsewhere in Batch A.

## 2. Scope boundaries

This file covers:
- kinform as body-plan / substrate category
- heritage as inherited or origin-bound imprint
- the relationship between current embodiment, inherited embodiment, and prior embodiment
- hybrid, mixed, synthetic, grown, cloned, relic-born, altered, and otherwise nonstandard origin models
- what body / inheritance layers may grant
- what body / inheritance layers may not grant by default
- donor mapping rules for ancestry-, species-, bloodline-, shell-, chassis-, or origin-body constructs

This file does not cover:
- culture and learned worldview
- formative environment
- background events
- institutional upbringing
- profession-before-adventure
- current affiliation law
- package selection or character-creation procedure

## 3. Core doctrine principles

### 3.1 Identity layers are separated by function

Body / inheritance layers are not allowed to absorb the jobs of learned / social / formative layers merely because a donor bundles them together.

### 3.2 Kinform is not culture

A body-plan, substrate, or embodied form does not automatically define learned worldview, social norms, language practice, profession, or faction relation.

### 3.3 Heritage is not profession or destiny

Inherited origin, ancestral resonance, bloodline, symbolic lineage, or manufactured origin does not automatically grant a full current role package, a path package, a major active ability suite, or a full progression ladder.

### 3.4 No single body-origin layer may silently replace the others

No donor construct may use one label to silently stand in for all of the following at once:
- body-plan
- inherited imprint
- culture
- homeland
- upbringing
- faction relation
- profession-before-adventure
- current role

### 3.5 Form may be mutable, discontinuous, or layered

Astra recognizes that current form, inherited form, and prior form may differ. A character may possess:
- a current embodied state
- an inherited or originating body logic
- prior embodied states
- altered shells
- temporary forms
- secondary or alternate frames

This is structural doctrine. It does not import any single donor cosmology as universal law.

### 3.6 Heritage does not require bloodline and kinform does not require flesh

Astra must support non-biological, non-familial, synthetic, grown, cloned, relic-born, constructed, symbiotic, vessel-based, or otherwise atypical origins without forcing them into false bloodline logic.

## 4. Body / inheritance layer split

This file distinguishes two related but non-identical layers.

### 4.1 Kinform

**Kinform** is the category that describes an actor’s body-plan, substrate, embodiment model, or structural mode of being.

Kinform answers questions such as:
- what kind of body or substrate is this
- what environmental assumptions or constraints apply
- what movement / sensory / morphology tendencies are structurally normal
- what platform or loadout compatibilities matter
- what embodiment-linked limitations exist

Kinform may be biological, synthetic, altered, hybrid, distributed, vessel-based, spiritualized, or otherwise unusual.

### 4.2 Heritage

**Heritage** is the inherited, originating, ancestral, lineage-bound, or origin-imprinted layer that explains what persistent inherited pattern, resonance, affinity, origin mark, or source-bound trait accompanies the actor.

Heritage answers questions such as:
- what inherited or origin-bound imprint persists
- what ancestral, symbolic, genealogical, designed, or source-linked pattern exists
- what latent affinities or marks derive from origin
- what long-duration inherited hooks or tags apply

Heritage is not automatically identical to kinform.

## 5. Kinform doctrine

Kinform may include one or more of the following:
- biological body-plan
- synthetic shell type
- altered substrate
- hybrid morphology
- vessel or frame type
- symbiotic embodiment class
- distributed or remote-operant embodiment model
- multi-frame embodiment relation

Kinform is allowed to define:
- morphology-linked movement tendencies
- body-size or scale assumptions where relevant
- environmental compatibility or strain
- sensory tendencies
- substrate-linked compatibility
- morphological limitations
- body-plan-relevant loadout or interface notes

Kinform is not allowed by default to define:
- culture
- language package
- professional training
- current institutional membership
- path package
- unrestricted access law
- large active ability suites
- whole advancement ladders

## 6. Heritage doctrine

Heritage may include one or more of the following:
- ancestral inheritance
- bloodline-adjacent imprint
- symbolic lineage
- designed origin pattern
- constructed-source imprint
- relic-linked origin mark
- clone-template inheritance
- awakened source inheritance
- bonded-source inheritance

Heritage is allowed to define:
- inherited affinities
- latent marks
- minor persistent tags
- origin-bound hooks
- restricted lineage interactions
- dormant or conditional origin traits
- lawful ties to later lineage/heritage progression architecture

Heritage is not allowed by default to define:
- full class/path/profession package
- unrestricted access to all source-related mechanics
- major active ability suites
- complete social worldview
- major stat rewrites
- full progression systems by itself

## 7. Mixed, hybrid, and discontinuous handling

### 7.1 Mixed kinform

An actor may lawfully possess mixed or layered kinform logic. This may include:
- hybrid biological ancestry
- synthetic-biological blending
- altered or grafted embodiment
- shell replacement
- multiple coexisting frames
- temporary alternate embodiment

### 7.2 Mixed heritage

An actor may lawfully possess:
- multiple inherited lines
- partial inheritance
- blended ancestral resonance
- discontinuous lineage
- artificial or recovered heritage
- symbolic rather than biological inheritance

### 7.3 Discontinuous embodiment

Astra recognizes actors whose current body is not the same as their originating body. This may arise from:
- transfer
- rebirth
- shell replacement
- possession or inhabitation
- synthetic migration
- reconstructed embodiment
- relic or vessel inhabitation
- severe transformative alteration

In such cases, kinform and heritage must remain separately classifiable.

## 8. Non-biological and non-familial origins

Astra explicitly supports origins such as:
- constructs
- clones
- symbiotes
- grown vessels
- AI shells
- relic-born entities
- engineered lineages
- manufactured cadres
- altered substrates
- bonded composite beings

No conversion may force these into flesh-biological or blood-family assumptions unless the donor genuinely requires that structure.

## 9. Persistent layers versus event layers

Kinform and heritage are **persistent identity layers**.

They are not the same as episodic formative events such as:
- war survivor
- hostage
- exile
- disaster witness
- survivor of a rite
- veteran of a campaign
- escaped experiment subject

Those belong to event-layer or background-layer handling elsewhere unless the event permanently altered kinform or heritage in a way doctrine recognizes as persistent.

## 10. Formative affiliation versus current affiliation

Body / inheritance layers may reference origin-linked affiliations or design provenance, but they do not automatically determine current allegiance, current faction status, or current institutional membership.

Examples of distinctions Astra must preserve:
- raised by a court ≠ currently serving that court
- built by an institution ≠ currently owned by that institution
- descended from a lineage ≠ currently aligned with its politics
- designed for a purpose ≠ currently living by that purpose

## 11. Grant-boundary law

### 11.1 Body / inheritance layers may grant

These layers may lawfully grant:
- traits
- affinities
- environmental compatibility
- morphology-linked tendencies
- substrate-linked constraints
- minor persistent tags or hooks
- origin-bound contacts or obligations where directly relevant
- limited sensory or bodily distinctions
- lawful links to later lineage / heritage progression
- origin-relevant knowledge or familiarity in narrow scope

### 11.2 Body / inheritance layers may not grant by default

These layers may **not** grant by default:
- full path packages
- full profession packages
- major active ability suites
- unrestricted access law
- complete doctrine schools
- whole progression ladders
- major stat rewrites
- broad social worldview packages
- whole cultural identity
- current faction rank or command authority

Any donor construct that tries to do these jobs must be split across the proper Astra layers.

## 12. Donor mapping rules

When converting donor ancestry / species / bloodline / shell / chassis / lineage material, the converter must ask:

1. Is this describing body-plan, substrate, or embodiment?  
   - If yes, map toward **kinform**.

2. Is this describing inherited imprint, origin-bound resonance, lineage, source-mark, or ancestral logic?  
   - If yes, map toward **heritage**.

3. Is this describing worldview, customs, language, norms, or social identity?  
   - If yes, do **not** leave it here. Route it to culture/background handling.

4. Is this describing profession, training, faction membership, or current role?  
   - If yes, do **not** leave it here. Route it downstream.

5. Is this one donor object doing more than one of those jobs?  
   - If yes, split it. Do not preserve the bundle out of convenience.
