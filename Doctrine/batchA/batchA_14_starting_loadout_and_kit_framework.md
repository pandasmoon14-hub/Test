# batchA_14_starting_loadout_and_kit_framework

## 1. Purpose and authority

This file defines Astra doctrine for **starter material support**: the legal framework by which a new character begins play with lawful loadout, kit access, item state, and starter support.

Its purpose is to prevent donor systems from collapsing:
- starter support model,
- starter entitlement,
- starter legality,
- starter availability,
- starter readiness,
- and item state

into one oversimplified shopping step.

This file is authoritative for:
- starter support models
- starter kit categories
- package-linked loadout influence
- starter entitlement / legality / availability distinctions
- starter readiness doctrine
- starter item-state doctrine
- minimal and conversion-valid starting loadout doctrine
- nonstandard holder and bond/platform starter-kit doctrine

This file is **not** authoritative for:
- the full equipment catalog
- advanced crafting
- long-form economy systems
- comprehensive trade-good lists
- complete ammo and upkeep systems
- full relic, cyberware, biotech, or vehicle rules
- full access law
- full progression law
- loadout procedure outside starter-entry logic

Those belong elsewhere in Batch A.

## 2. Scope boundaries

This file covers:
- how a new Astra character lawfully begins with material support
- what starter support models exist
- what kit categories are recognized at starter scale
- how packages influence starter kits without owning the gear layer
- how legality, availability, readiness, and item state are checked
- how starter consumables and basic replenishment expectations are handled
- how bond/platform, companion, and nonstandard holders begin with lawful gear support
- how minimal, partial, inherited, salvaged, issued, and conversion-valid starts remain lawful

This file does not cover:

## 3. Core starter-loadout principles

### 3.1 Starting loadout is an entry framework into the gear layer

Starter loadout introduces lawful material support.
It does not replace the broader gear layer.

### 3.2 Starter support is not one universal shopping model

Astra recognizes multiple lawful support models.
No single donor economy model becomes universal law by default.

### 3.3 Entitlement, legality, availability, readiness, and item state are distinct

A character may be entitled to begin with a category of gear without automatically:
- being legally permitted to use every item in that category
- having every item in that category available
- being ready to use every item immediately
- having the item in pristine or fully operational state

### 3.4 Starter ownership is not the same as starter readiness

Owning, inheriting, being issued, bonding to, or being assigned an item is not identical to:
- attunement
- installation
- fueling
- calibration
- stabilization
- command access
- safe operation
- deployment readiness

### 3.5 Starter state must be declared, not invented

A valid starting loadout may be:
- complete
- minimal
- partial
- inherited
- issued
- salvaged
- damaged
- bonded
- platform-bound
- conversion-valid only

provided that the state is declared rather than silently idealized.

### 3.6 Starter-loadout procedure does not bypass other doctrine

Starter-loadout procedure may authorize entry into gear categories, but it does not bypass:
- access law from `batchA_06_access_tags_permissions_and_proficiency_gates.md`
- compatibility law from `batchA_04` and `batchA_11a`
- progression appropriateness from `batchA_10_progression_axes.md`
- package law from `batchA_12_path_archetype_and_profession_framework.md`
- scenario scarcity assumptions

## 4. Starter support models

Astra recognizes multiple lawful support models for starting material support.

### 4.1 Direct currency budget

The character begins with a defined starter budget that may be exchanged for lawful starter items.

### 4.2 Package-issued starter support

The character begins with package-linked starter issue, recommendations, or lawful bundle templates shaped by current package structure.

### 4.3 Profession-issued starter support

The character begins with vocation-linked tools, instruments, work kits, or procedural equipment associated with current profession-shaped play.

### 4.4 Faction or institution issue

The character begins with items issued by a faction, sect, court, militia, archive, hospital, guild, crew, or other institutional body.

### 4.5 Bond / platform issue

The character begins with bonded, companion-centered, platform-centered, mech-linked, drone-linked, relic-linked, ship-linked, or similar support.

### 4.6 Inherited or carried-in support

The character begins with heirlooms, inherited tools, legacy gear, relics, salvaged continuity items, or pre-existing possessions.

### 4.7 Scavenged or minimalist support

The character begins with survival-grade, improvised, prison-break, shipwreck, refugee, or otherwise severely reduced material support.

### 4.8 Mixed support model

A lawful starter state may combine multiple models so long as the resulting loadout remains valid under the rest of this file.

## 5. Starter kit categories

Astra recognizes at least the following starter kit categories.

### 5.1 Primary armament kit
Weapons, offensive tools, attack platforms, or other primary means of causing or threatening harm.

### 5.2 Defensive kit
Armor, shielding, guard-support, protective wear, barrier tools, or equivalent defensive materials.

### 5.3 Basic field kit
General utility equipment, travel gear, carrying solutions, light survival gear, and common operational necessities.

### 5.4 Professional / tool kit
Trade tools, field instruments, diagnostic tools, artisan gear, medical gear, survey tools, or occupation-linked equipment.

### 5.5 Travel / survival kit
Rations, weather support, mobility aids, navigation basics, shelter support, wilderness gear, or hardship supplies.

### 5.6 Ritual / esoteric kit
Foci, reagents, circles, seals, implements, reliquaries, symbolic tools, or doctrinal materials for esoteric practice.

### 5.7 Tech / implant / biotech kit
Devices, installation support, calibration tools, biotech housings, implant maintenance gear, interface gear, or linked consumables.

### 5.8 Bond / platform kit
Companion harnesses, beast support gear, drone frames, mech startup components, shipboard operational gear, control rigs, relic housings, summon anchors, or equivalent bonded/platform support.

### 5.9 Social / presentation kit
Ceremonial wear, formal tools, insignia, office tools, negotiation materials, identity displays, or setting-appropriate presentation materials.

## 6. Package-linked loadout influence

Packages may influence starter loadout through:
- entitlement to certain kit categories
- recommendation of certain loadout patterns
- lawful starter bundle templates
- package-linked readiness assumptions
- package-linked support channels
- package-linked bond/platform requirements

Packages do **not** own the full gear layer.
They do not override access law, compatibility law, or scarcity law merely by thematic association.

## 7. Starter entitlement vs legality vs availability

### 7.1 Starter entitlement

**Entitlement** answers:
- what categories or types of support the character is allowed to begin with under their package, profession, faction, scenario, or support model

### 7.2 Starter legality

**Legality** answers:
- whether the character can lawfully possess, equip, invoke, install, attune, or operate the specific starting item under current Astra doctrine

Legality must check against:
- access restrictions
- compatibility requirements
- package limitations
- progression appropriateness
- holder or platform requirements

### 7.3 Starter availability

**Availability** answers:
- whether the starter context actually provides the item or support in that scenario

Availability depends on:
- scarcity assumptions
- support model
- faction or institutional issue
- scenario conditions
- source-local setting assumptions

A character may be entitled to a kit category without every desired item in that category being available at start.

## 8. Starter readiness and item state

### 8.1 Readiness states

A starting item or kit may be:
- ready
- present but not yet attuned
- present but uninstalled
- present but unfueled
- present but uncalibrated
- present but unstable
- present but locked
- present but inactive
- present but restricted pending access resolution
- present but platform-bound rather than personally deployed

### 8.2 Item states

A starting item may be:
- pristine
- standard issue
- worn
- salvaged
- damaged
- improvised
- heirloom
- ceremonial
- black-market
- bonded
- mission-loaned
- partially functional

### 8.3 State declaration law

A starter item’s state must be declared when it materially affects:
- legality
- readiness
- operational reliability
- narrative burden
- conversion fidelity
- platform dependence

## 9. Scarcity and scenario assumptions

Starter loadout must be interpreted through scenario assumptions.

A lawful starter scenario may be:
- abundant market start
- standard institutional issue
- frontier scarce
- refugee / displaced
- prison-break minimal
- shipwreck / loss-based
- pilgrimage-poor
- ritual-rich but material-poor
- high-tech provisioned
- bond/platform dependent
- post-disaster salvage-driven

Scarcity or scenario assumptions may reduce, reshape, or redirect starter availability without invalidating the character record.

## 10. Consumables and replenishment expectations

Starter material support includes not only durable gear, but also starter-scale consumable support where relevant.

This may include:
- ammunition
- charges
- reagents
- med supplies
- feed
- batteries
- rations
- ritual materials
- maintenance supplies
- stabilizers
- calibration packs

This file does not define long-term economy law.
It only recognizes that consumables may be part of lawful starter support.

## 11. Nonstandard holders, bond/platform kits, and multi-frame starts

Astra explicitly supports starter loadouts for:
- bond-centered characters
- companion-centered characters
- beast-linked packages
- mech-linked actors
- drone or AI-linked actors
- relic symbiosis holders
- summoned-framework users
- multi-frame actors
- shipboard or vehicle-centered roles
- other platform-dependent holders

Starter support for such characters may include:
- personal gear
- companion gear
- shared platform gear
- control infrastructure
- frame-start equipment
- linked consumables
- support housings
- harnesses, anchors, or interfaces

These are not edge cases.
They are lawful starter categories.

## 12. Minimal, partial, and conversion-valid starting loadouts

### 12.1 Minimal viable loadout

A character may be play-valid with a minimal viable loadout if:
- the scenario permits it
- core function is still lawful
- missing ideal gear is explicitly acknowledged rather than invented

### 12.2 Partial starter loadout

A partial starter loadout may remain lawful for conversion or reconstruction if:
- missing items are flagged
- support model is declared
- no false readiness is assumed
- the record is marked appropriately

### 12.3 Conversion-valid starter state

A converted donor record may be starter-valid for conversion even when:
- exact items are unknown
- only category-level support is preserved
- inherited or issued status is known but exact catalog entries are not
- readiness or item state remains unresolved but explicitly flagged

## 13. Donor mapping rules

When converting donor starting gear or starter support, the converter must ask:

1. What starter support model is the donor actually using?  
   - Currency, issue, inheritance, salvage, bond/platform, mixed, or something else?

2. What starter kit categories are actually present?  
   - Do not mistake all starting support for generic shopping inventory.

3. What does the character have a lawful starter entitlement to?  
   - Separate this from what they can legally use.

4. What is lawful under access, compatibility, and progression doctrine?  
   - Do not bypass other files.

5. What is actually available in the donor start state?  
   - Market abundance is not universal.

6. What is the readiness state of the item?  
   - Owned is not the same as ready.

7. What is the item state?  
   - Pristine, issued, heirloom, damaged, salvaged, bonded, loaned, etc.

8. Is the loadout personal, shared, platform-bound, or companion-bound?  
   - Classify this explicitly.

9. Is the donor record complete, minimal, partial, or conversion-valid only?  
   - Mark that state rather than inventing a perfect starter kit.
