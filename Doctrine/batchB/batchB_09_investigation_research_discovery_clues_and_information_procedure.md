# batchB_09_investigation_research_discovery_clues_and_information_procedure.md

## Purpose

This file defines Astra Ascension’s doctrine for **information pressure under procedural play**.

Its role is to provide Astra with a **universal information-procedure layer** capable of absorbing donor systems built around searching, studying, researching, interviewing, reading archives, analyzing evidence, verifying claims, monitoring targets, handling false or contradictory information, and converting discoveries into actionable leads without collapsing those functions into a donor-shaped mystery chapter.

This file is not merely a clue-finding section.
It is the procedure layer for:
- information acquisition,
- information interpretation,
- information verification,
- information contradiction,
- clue handling,
- lead generation,
- research pressure,
- investigative time and exposure,
- and conversion of information into actionable next steps.

In Astra terms, this file governs how:
- information is discovered,
- information is recognized as meaningful,
- information is interpreted,
- information is confirmed, disputed, or falsified,
- different information sources behave procedurally,
- scene investigation differs from project investigation,
- inquiry and research produce forward motion,
- and investigation hands off cleanly into travel, social procedure, stealth, hazards, and later scenario content.

## Core design stance

Astra must be able to absorb donor systems where information pressure is expressed as:
- searching a room for traces,
- examining a body or artifact,
- reading or decoding a document,
- questioning a witness,
- following rumors through a hub,
- consulting libraries, records, or databases,
- extracting data from technical or magical systems,
- reconstructing an event from partial evidence,
- validating or disproving a claim,
- conducting surveillance,
- building a longer investigative project over time,
- and converting raw facts into leads, answers, and operational choices.

Astra must therefore treat investigation as a doctrine layer rather than as a narrow mystery minigame.

B09 is not responsible for writing adventures.
It is responsible for giving Astra a stable grammar for **how information behaves procedurally**.

## Core doctrine terms

### Information pressure
**Information pressure** is any procedural state in which progress, safety, leverage, planning, or understanding depends on acquiring, interpreting, verifying, or acting on information.

### Information state
An **information state** is the current procedural condition of a fact, clue, theory, record, testimony, or signal.
Information state tracks whether the information has been noticed, understood, verified, disputed, or rendered actionable.

### Source channel
A **source channel** is the immediate form through which information is encountered.
Examples include traces, witnesses, records, sensor readouts, rumors, bodies, artifacts, magical residues, institutional files, or environmental patterns.

### Information environment
An **information environment** is the broader procedural context in which information is obtained.
Examples include a field site, archive, rumor market, hostile institution, technical system, occult ruin, or deceptive scene.

### Question
A **question** is the procedural problem an investigation is trying to answer.
Question doctrine exists so investigation is oriented toward actionable uncertainty rather than generic “looking around.”

### Clue
A **clue** is an information-bearing element that indicates, suggests, reveals, or points beyond itself.
A clue may be direct, indirect, partial, misleading, planted, or interpreted incorrectly.

### Lead
A **lead** is an actionable output produced by information.
A lead points toward a next source, destination, contact, contradiction, danger, opportunity, or procedure.

### Answer
An **answer** is information that resolves a defined question at an actionable level.
An answer may still be partial, provisional, or vulnerable to later contradiction.

### Hypothesis
A **hypothesis** is a provisional explanatory model derived from one or more clues or answers.
A hypothesis is not automatically true.

### Conclusion
A **conclusion** is a stabilized interpretation of available information.
A conclusion may still be incomplete, wrong, or later overturned.

### Verification
**Verification** is the procedure by which information is corroborated, tested, cross-checked, authenticated, or challenged.

### Monitoring
**Monitoring** is sustained observation or information collection over time, often to detect change, pattern, timing, movement, or escalation.

### Provenance
**Provenance** is the traceable origin and handling chain of information.
Provenance affects credibility, contamination risk, and interpretive weight.

### Falsifiability
**Falsifiability** is the degree to which a claim, clue, or conclusion can be tested, disproved, or meaningfully challenged.

## Doctrine commitments

### 1. Information state is the spine of the file
Astra must not treat investigation as a binary “found clue / did not find clue” procedure.
The primary procedural question is the **state of the information** and how it changes over time.

### 2. Information sources must remain plural
Astra must not assume all investigations are room searches or library checks.
The framework must support environmental evidence, witnesses, rumors, records, archives, readouts, occult traces, institutional holdings, hostile misinformation, and mixed-source investigations.

### 3. Clue, lead, answer, hypothesis, and conclusion must remain distinct
A clue is not automatically a conclusion.
A lead is not automatically an answer.
A conclusion is not automatically true.
These distinctions are mandatory.

### 4. Investigation must preserve forward motion
Conversion must preserve donor information flow.
Astra must not normalize investigation into brittle one-roll chokepoints unless the donor construct explicitly depends on fragility.

### 5. Verification matters
A successful information procedure must not automatically produce clean truth.
Astra must support disputed evidence, planted clues, forged records, contradictory testimony, partial corroboration, and biased sources.

### 6. Investigation may be immediate or extended
Astra must support both scene investigation and project investigation.
Some information problems resolve quickly.
Others require sustained work across time, access, cost, and risk.

### 7. Investigation produces action, not only lore
The purpose of information procedure is not merely to reveal facts.
It is also to create actionable next states: destinations, contacts, contradictions, vulnerabilities, warnings, timelines, and procedural opportunities.

### 8. Donor pressure must be preserved
Astra must not flatten investigation-heavy, rumor-heavy, archive-heavy, forensics-heavy, espionage-heavy, horror-heavy, or research-heavy systems into generic skill checks.
Astra must also not inflate lightweight information models into overbuilt conspiracy engines where the donor does not require them.

## Information-state doctrine

### Core rule
Information state is the central procedural measure of investigation.
Information state tracks whether a fact or source has merely been encountered, whether it has been understood, whether it has been verified, and whether it can support action.

### Information-state ladder
Astra recognizes at least the following information states.

#### Unnoticed
The relevant information has not yet entered procedural play for the investigating side.
It may exist in the environment, in a source, or in a system without being recognized.

#### Hinted
The investigating side has some indication that relevant information exists, but lacks a concrete actionable clue.
A hint creates pressure or suspicion without yet producing a stable lead.

#### Discovered
The information-bearing element has been found or encountered.
Discovery does not guarantee understanding.

#### Lead-bearing
The information now points toward a meaningful next step.
The lead may indicate a person, place, archive, contradiction, weakness, route, timer, or follow-up procedure.

#### Interpreted
The information has been meaningfully read, contextualized, or translated into a working understanding.
Interpretation may still be wrong.

#### Corroborated
The information has been meaningfully supported by additional evidence, trusted cross-checks, or confirming procedure.
Corroboration increases reliability but does not create absolute truth.

#### Disputed
The information remains contested, contradictory, suspect, or under challenge.
Disputed information still matters procedurally.
It may support hypotheses without supporting firm conclusions.

#### Falsified
The information, claim, or conclusion has been shown to be false, planted, corrupted, forged, misread, or otherwise invalid in its prior form.
Falsified information may still reveal other leads.

#### Operationally actionable
The information is sufficiently stable, relevant, or urgent to support meaningful next action even if it remains incomplete.
Actionability does not require total certainty.

### State transitions
Information may move forward, stall, regress, branch, or split across states.
A discovered clue may remain uninterpreted.
A corroborated lead may still produce a false conclusion.
A falsified record may reveal a new source channel or hostile actor.

## Source-channel doctrine

### Core rule
Astra must treat information source channels as plural and distinct.
Different source channels create different investigative procedures, risks, and verification needs.

### Core source-channel families
Astra recognizes at least the following source-channel families.

#### Scene evidence
Physical traces, hidden compartments, disturbed spaces, residues, patterns, wounds, tool marks, bloodstains, fragments, or environmental anomalies.

#### Witness testimony
Statements, memories, testimony, interrogatives, reports, confessions, hearsay, or recollections from persons or sapient entities.

#### Rumor and gossip
Diffuse, low-certainty, socially transmitted information gathered through communities, hubs, factions, public chatter, marketplaces, or informal networks.

#### Records and archives
Books, ledgers, reports, registries, maps, logs, files, codices, libraries, institutional records, data vaults, and stored documentation.

#### Technical or magical readouts
Sensor returns, diagnostic outputs, surveillance feeds, thaumic scans, runic traces, machine logs, magical residue interpretation, or black-box data.

#### Forensic traces
Evidence requiring reconstruction or analysis, including sequence inference, chemical or magical profiling, timeline reconstruction, contamination analysis, and cause attribution.

#### Historical or doctrinal lore
Known traditions, learned models, sect records, theological interpretation, specialist expertise, cultural memory, or established knowledge bodies.

#### Anomalous echoes
Dream residues, psychic impressions, prophetic bleed-through, cursed impressions, temporal echoes, spirit messages, warped-site information, or other nonstandard channels.

### Source-channel rule
A source channel defines what kind of procedure is appropriate, what verification might be needed, and what kind of uncertainty is natural to the source.

## Information-environment doctrine

### Core rule
Information is not encountered only through channels.
It is also shaped by the **environment** in which the information lives.

### Core information-environment families
Astra recognizes at least the following information environments.

#### Field site
Ruins, crime scenes, lairs, chambers, laboratories, battlefields, shrines, wilderness sites, ship compartments, or other directly inspectable places.

#### Archive
Libraries, repositories, records offices, secured vaults, long-term memory institutions, legal archives, and historical storage systems.

#### Institution
Guilds, sects, ministries, corps, temples, bureaucracies, schools, military offices, and other structures that gate information through policy or hierarchy.

#### Market or rumor hub
Cities, ports, caravan hubs, underworld exchanges, gossip networks, taverns, plazas, trade nexuses, and social-information marketplaces.

#### Witness network
Communities of people whose combined partial knowledge forms the real information environment.

#### Technical system
Sensor grids, networked devices, machine records, monitoring arrays, laboratories, scanner webs, AI-assisted repositories, or data systems.

#### Occult or anomalous site
Places where information is embedded in ritual, symbol, residue, prophecy, dream logic, cursed architecture, or unstable metaphysical traces.

#### Hostile or deceptive information environment
Environments where sources are compromised, planted, trapped, surveilled, corrupted, altered, forged, self-protective, or designed to mislead.

### Environment rule
The information environment shapes access cost, source reliability, exposure risk, and what procedure families are viable.

## Question doctrine

### Core rule
Investigation procedure must be question-oriented.
Characters do not investigate “in general.”
They investigate toward uncertainties that matter.

### Common question classes
Astra recognizes at least the following question classes.
- **What is here?**
- **What happened here?**
- **Who did this?**
- **What does this mean?**
- **Where does this lead?**
- **What is missing?**
- **What is false?**
- **What can be exploited?**
- **What happens next if nothing changes?**
- **Who knows more?**
- **What source must be accessed next?**

### Question rule
A procedure may produce facts without answering the original question.
That is acceptable if it produces a new actionable lead or a better-framed question.

## Procedure-family doctrine

### Search
**Search** is the field procedure for locating overlooked, hidden, subtle, or non-obvious information within an immediate environment or object space.
Search primarily changes information from unnoticed to discovered.

### Study
**Study** is close examination of a specific source, clue, object, body, text, image, pattern, artifact, or record to extract meaning or operational detail.
Study primarily changes information from discovered to interpreted.

### Research
**Research** is extended access to stored, organized, or searchable knowledge over time.
Research often depends on archives, institutions, technical systems, or specialized source access.

### Inquiry
**Inquiry** is information acquisition through people, groups, institutions, intermediaries, or social-source networks.
Inquiry does not automatically become social conflict.
It becomes B10 procedure only when leverage, resistance, bargaining, confession pressure, or influence dynamics dominate.

### Analysis
**Analysis** is the procedure of comparing, sorting, reconstructing, decoding, and interpreting multiple information elements.
Analysis may produce hypotheses, timeline models, pattern recognition, or causal understanding.

### Correlation
**Correlation** is a specialized analytical subfamily that connects separate clues, records, testimonies, or signals into a shared pattern.
Correlation is useful when no single clue is decisive but the combined pattern matters.

### Verification
**Verification** is the procedure of checking whether information is reliable, authentic, consistent, or false.
Verification includes corroboration, authentication, contradiction testing, chain-of-custody checking, source comparison, replication, and challenge testing.

### Monitoring
**Monitoring** is sustained observation or data collection across time.
Monitoring is used when the question depends on change, timing, repetition, movement, contact networks, ritual cycles, traffic, patrols, transmissions, or slow-developing patterns.

## Clue, lead, answer, hypothesis, and conclusion doctrine

### Core rule
These categories must remain procedurally distinct.
They serve different functions in Astra’s information grammar.

### Clue
A clue is an information-bearing element.
It does not need to solve anything by itself.

### Lead
A lead is a directional output that creates forward motion.
A lead points toward another source, place, risk, contradiction, contact, or procedure.

### Answer
An answer resolves a concrete question at a usable level.
An answer may still be incomplete.

### Hypothesis
A hypothesis is a provisional explanatory model built from available information.
It remains open to verification or falsification.

### Conclusion
A conclusion is a stabilized interpretation the investigating side is presently willing to act upon.
A conclusion may still be wrong.

### Separation rule
A single clue may generate multiple leads.
Multiple clues may support one hypothesis.
A conclusion may emerge before full verification.
A false clue may still reveal a real hostile actor or pressure source.

## Certainty, provenance, contradiction, and falsifiability

### Core rule
Information must carry reliability context.
Astra must not assume that every successful investigative step yields unambiguous truth.

### Certainty classes
Astra supports at least the following certainty classes.
- **directly observed**
- **inferred**
- **probable**
- **corroborated**
- **contested**
- **uncorroborated**
- **deceptive or hostile**
- **unknown reliability**

### Provenance classes
Astra supports at least the following provenance classes.
- **scene-origin**
- **witness-origin**
- **institution-origin**
- **archive-origin**
- **technical-origin**
- **occult-origin**
- **anonymous or obscured origin**
- **compromised origin**

### Contradiction doctrine
Contradictory information is procedurally meaningful.
It should not be discarded automatically.
Contradiction may indicate bias, forgery, partial truth, timeline mismatch, planted evidence, multiple actors, hidden layers, or a bad conclusion.

### Falsifiability doctrine
Investigation should support meaningful challenge and testing when the donor construct does so.
Some information cannot yet be verified.
Some information can be disproven quickly.
Some information remains operationally useful even without final verification.

## Anti-chokepoint and information-flow invariants

### Core invariant
Conversion must preserve information flow.
Astra must not let a single brittle miss, one exact skill label, or one unrepeatable roll collapse the only forward path unless the donor explicitly depends on that fragility.

### Mandatory forward-information doctrine
Astra distinguishes at least the following information-output classes.

#### Mandatory forward information
Information that must keep the procedure moving.
If this is withheld, the scene or project stalls illegitimately.

#### Optional depth information
Information that enriches context, raises understanding, or broadens the picture without being required for forward motion.

#### Bonus leverage information
Information that creates advantage, shortcuts, protection, bargaining leverage, tactical edge, or safer procedure.

#### Misleading or hostile information
Information that diverts, traps, distorts, or complicates the procedure.
This should create meaningful challenge, not arbitrary dead ends.

### Redundancy rule
If the donor clearly supports multiple plausible ways to reach the next lead, Astra should preserve that multiplicity rather than compressing everything into one gate.

## Time, cost, exposure, and investigation pressure

### Core rule
Information acquisition is often not free.
B09 must support investigative commitment across time, access, risk, and attention.

### Investigation pressure classes
Astra supports at least the following pressure classes.
- **time cost**
- **attention commitment**
- **opportunity cost**
- **access cost**
- **institutional cost**
- **risk or exposure**
- **heat, suspicion, or alarm generation**
- **resource consumption**
- **fatigue or cognitive burden where relevant**

### Pressure rule
A procedure may succeed at finding information while still increasing risk, delay, alert state, or dependency on further access.

## Scene investigation vs project investigation

### Scene investigation
**Scene investigation** is immediate and localized.
It concerns a bounded place, source, witness, object, or short information exchange.
Examples include searching a chamber, examining a corpse, reading a note, checking a terminal, or interviewing one witness.

### Project investigation
**Project investigation** is extended and cumulative.
It concerns patterns, networks, conspiracies, histories, timelines, archives, route systems, or hidden structures that require repeated effort across time.
Examples include mapping a cult network, decoding an archive, proving a conspiracy, identifying the origin of an outbreak, or locating a hidden vault through multiple leads.

### Project rule
Some donor systems resolve information in one step.
Others track extended inquiry over clocks, milestones, progress segments, or recurring actions.
Astra must support both without forcing every investigation into the same scale.

## Lead ecology and actionable outputs

### Core rule
B09 does not own plot hooks as narrative objects.
It owns **lead ecology** as procedure.
A lead-bearing output is the Astra-native form by which information becomes action.

### Lead-bearing output families
A lead may point toward:
- a **next destination**,
- a **next source**,
- a **next risk**,
- a **next question**,
- a **next contact**,
- a **next opportunity**,
- a **next contradiction to resolve**,
- a **next weakness to exploit**,
- a **new timer or escalation**, 
- a **hidden layer revealed**,
- or a **new information environment opened**.

### Lead rule
A procedure that does not yield a final answer may still be fully successful if it yields a meaningful lead-bearing output.

## Lawful incomplete mapping

When a donor construct does not fully specify its information logic, Astra may classify it as:
- **directly mapped**,
- **normalized**,
- **source-channel assigned**,
- **environment assigned**,
- **question-class assigned**,
- **project-class assigned**,
- **placeholder-classified**,
- or **quarantined pending later doctrine**.

Astra must not invent false certainty where donor investigation is partial, atmospheric, genre-coded, or intentionally ambiguous.

## Anti-hallucination rules

### 1. Do not collapse all information play into clue finding
Investigation includes search, study, research, inquiry, analysis, verification, and monitoring.

### 2. Do not collapse discovery into understanding
Finding a clue is not the same as interpreting it.

### 3. Do not collapse interpretation into truth
A conclusion can be actionable without being final.

### 4. Do not collapse leads into answers
A lead points forward.
It does not necessarily resolve the current question.

### 5. Do not collapse all source channels into room search
Archives, rumors, witnesses, records, sensors, residues, and hostile information systems require different handling.

### 6. Do not collapse contradiction into failure
Conflicting information is a meaningful investigative state, not merely a dead result.

### 7. Do not collapse project investigation into one scene roll
Longer inquiries may require repeated access, sustained monitoring, extended research, or cumulative progress.

### 8. Do not let B09 absorb adjacent files
If the problem is mostly uncertain perception, hand off to B08.
If the problem is mostly social leverage or confession pressure, hand off to B10.
If the problem is mostly routefinding or travel-state tracking, hand off to B06.
If the problem is mostly environmental-source interpretation, hand off to B07.
If the problem is mostly trap, puzzle, or scenario schema, reserve it for Batch C.
