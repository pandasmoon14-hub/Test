## 11. LLM non-authority rules

RT-007 prohibits the LLM from:

- mutating relationship state;
- changing faction standing;
- deciding NPC/faction beliefs as truth;
- creating actor knowledge records;
- committing obligations, favors, debts, reputation, or institutional consequences;
- inventing faction actions as backend events;
- treating dialogue as durable memory authority;
- treating summaries as relationship state;
- revealing hidden agendas;
- creating recurring factions or contacts without provenance;
- bypassing backend validation/reviewer approval.

Dialogue may produce a claim candidate only, not authoritative truth. The LLM may propose non-authoritative phrasing, ask clarifying questions, or narrate backend-approved social/faction facts supplied by a future authorized context packet. It cannot be the authority for relationship, faction, actor-knowledge, obligation, reputation, institutional, hidden-agenda, recurrence, persistence, or validation state.

## 12. Context-packet and narration handoff expectations

Social/faction state must remain backend-owned, persistent, auditable, and redacted through RT-005. Future RT-007 context packets must be backend-produced after command legality, relationship/faction/knowledge validation, hidden-information redaction, provenance/recurrence review, and reviewer/validator gates. Packets must distinguish backend truth, actor beliefs, faction beliefs, visible attitudes, known standing, authorized rumors, unverified dialogue claims, hidden agendas, unavailable contacts, pending obligations, allowed narration emphasis, prohibited inference, and audit/event identifiers.

Recurring factions, NPCs, and contacts require RT-008 provenance/recurrence controls. Narration may describe only backend-approved social/faction facts, visible attitudes, known standing, or authorized rumors. Narration must not mutate relationship state, change standing, create actor memory, commit faction action, reveal hidden agendas, convert dialogue into durable truth, or treat summaries as persistent relationship state.

This scaffold does not create a context-packet compiler, packet schema, relationship schema, actor-knowledge database, dialogue memory system, validator, generator, persistence writer, command IR, or narration generator.
