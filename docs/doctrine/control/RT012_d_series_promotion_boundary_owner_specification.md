## 4. Authority model

- Authority hierarchy controls promotion. Current doctrine and future canon outrank D-series/native-design pressure.
- D-series/native-design source packs are design pressure and evidence, not authority by default.
- Converted content is evidence, not canon.
- Generated content is backend/provenance pressure only, not canon or sourcebook authority by default.
- Examples and training samples are not authority.
- Live-play narration and summaries are not authority, memory truth, promotion evidence, or canon.
- Reviewer/backend governance must authorize promotion before any D-series/native-design material can become doctrine, sourcebook, canon, runtime, training, or live-play authority.
- RT-012 owns routing and quarantine boundaries, not final promotion implementation.
- Future canon/sourcebook processes must own accepted promotion, if separately authorized.
- The LLM may summarize or compare approved source-pack material only inside bounded context and may not promote, rewrite authority, decide canon, authorize sourcebook inclusion, or route around reviewer decisions.

Authority hierarchy by layer:

- RT-001 owns command/action lifecycle, legality, declaration, rejection/quarantine, and event/state commitment boundaries for any D-series/native-design material that proposes command or action procedure.
- RT-002 owns costs, rewards, losses, consequences, resource pressure, economy pressure, and recovery math for any material that proposes resource or economy implications.
- RT-003 owns combat, hazard, damage, injury, healing, recovery, mitigation, exposure, conditions, and active-threat boundaries for any material that proposes combat or hazard implications.
- RT-004 owns abilities, effects, skills, techniques, prerequisites, targeting, cooldowns, scaling, capabilities, and item/relic/implant/vehicle effect handoffs for any material that proposes capability implications.
- RT-005 owns context-packet projection, hidden information, redaction, visibility, claims, rumors, known facts, false claims, and narrator fact-set boundaries for any material that proposes information visibility implications.
- RT-006 owns missions, rewards, clues, hidden truths, objectives, branches, penalties, scenarios, and investigation implications for any material that proposes mission or scenario implications.
- RT-007 owns social, faction, reputation, actor-knowledge, relationship, witness, institutional, rumor, contact, obligation, and belief-state boundaries for any material that proposes social implications.
- RT-008 owns generated-content provenance, recurrence, source-local generated material, durable eligibility, and generator-library implications for any material that proposes generated-content implications.
- RT-009 owns RNG, tables, oracles, random dependencies, seed/replay, and result-domain boundaries for any material that proposes random-output implications.
- RT-010 owns inventory, items, gear, relics, implants, vehicles, platforms, companions, cargo, custody, ownership, repair, salvage, crafting, requisition, and asset state boundaries for any material that proposes asset implications.
- RT-011 owns validation/readiness governance for any material that proposes validation or readiness implications.

## 6. Promotion-boundary contract

Planning-level promotion-boundary requirements:

- D-series/native-design material may propose a design claim but cannot become authority by existence.
- Source-pack material is pressure, not doctrine.
- Source-pack material is not canon.
- Source-pack material is not sourcebook content.
- Source-pack material is not training data unless separately authorized.
- Native-design claims that affect mechanics must route to the relevant RT owner before implementation can even be considered.
- Native-design claims that affect canon/sourcebook content must route to future canon/sourcebook governance before inclusion can be considered.
- Conflicts with current doctrine, RT owner specs, future canon, or future sourcebook material must be flagged and reviewed.
- Repeated use in play, examples, summaries, generated content, or conversion output does not promote content.
- Unpromoted design pressure must remain rejected, quarantined, or source-local pressure until separately reviewed.

This contract does not define final promotion procedure, reviewer workflow, canon record schema, sourcebook record schema, training policy, or runtime implementation.
