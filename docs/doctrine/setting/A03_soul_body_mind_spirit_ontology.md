# A03 Soul / Body / Mind / Spirit Ontology

## 1. Purpose and status
- File ID: A03
- Layer: 1_setting_spine
- Phase: 1A
- Status: draft
- Purpose: Define Astra doctrine-level ontology boundaries for body, soul, mind, and spirit; identity continuity; personhood classifications; and persistence classifications without importing donor metaphysics as Astra default law.

## 2. What this file owns
- Doctrine-level classification grammar for body component, mind component, soul component, and spirit component.
- Identity continuity classification grammar across embodied, disembodied, artificial/constructed, copied, forked, hosted, possessed, bonded, and symbiotic cases.
- Doctrine-level handling for uploaded identity and persistence-related continuity claims.
- Source-local identity construct, quarantine posture, and escalation posture for unresolved ontology pressure.

- Specific cyberware assets.

- Afterlife planes.

## 4. Required definitions
- **Body component**: materially embodied substrate classification used for ontology mapping without assigning numeric mechanics.
- **Mind component**: cognition/mentality component classification covering memory, reasoning, and perspective carriers.
- **Soul component**: continuity-bearing metaphysical component classification used where donor sources assert soul-like identity properties.
- **Spirit component**: non-body, non-mind metaphysical agency component classification where sources assert spirit-like agency.
- **Identity anchor**: the doctrine-level reference basis used to evaluate whether continuity claims point to the same identity subject.
- **Continuity claim**: a claim that identity remains the same across transitions, transfer, death/persistence pressure, or embodiment changes.
- **Discontinuity event**: an event or transition classification indicating potential break, split, merge, remnant persistence, or unresolved identity continuity.
- **Embodied personhood**: personhood classification where the identity anchor is mapped through a body component context.
- **Disembodied personhood**: personhood classification where identity claims persist without a currently embodied body component context.
- **Artificial/constructed personhood**: personhood classification for artificial, constructed, or synthetic entities, including AI personhood candidates.
- **Uploaded identity**: identity claim associated with transfer into a non-original substrate that requires explicit continuity outcome classification.
- **Copied/forked identity**: identity claim where copying or branching produces multiple continuity candidates requiring explicit fork classification.
- **Hosted identity**: identity claim where identity is carried or expressed through a host context without automatic continuity conclusions.
- **Possessed identity**: identity claim in possession contexts where controlling agency and host relation must be classified explicitly.
- **Bonded identity**: identity claim involving stable bond-linked identity relation across components or entities.
- **Symbiotic identity**: identity claim involving reciprocal co-dependent identity relation requiring non-collapsing classification.
- **Reincarnated/returned identity claim**: persistence claim asserting return continuity across death/persistence boundaries.
- **Restored/reconstructed identity claim**: persistence claim asserting continuity after restoration or reconstruction operations.
- **Undead/persistent remnant identity claim**: persistence claim asserting residual or remnant continuity after major discontinuity pressure.
- **Source-local identity construct**: donor identity construct retained with provenance and local scope only.
- **Quarantined identity construct**: unresolved identity construct held outside active Astra defaults pending later review.
- **Escalated identity problem**: contradiction requiring formal downstream doctrine resolution.

### Continuity outcome grammar
- **Continuous identity**: continuity claim classified as directly continuous with no doctrine-level interruption.
- **Interrupted but continuous identity**: continuity claim classified as interrupted in process but still continuous by doctrine criteria.
- **Reconstructed identity**: continuity claim classified through reconstruction and not treated as automatically continuous.
- **Copied identity**: continuity claim classified as copy-derived and distinct from singular continuous identity by default.
- **Forked identity**: continuity claim classified as branch-derived with multiple continuity candidates.
- **Merged identity**: continuity claim classified where multiple prior anchors contribute to one merged outcome.
- **Hosted identity**: continuity claim classified where identity persists via host context.
- **Possessed identity**: continuity claim classified where possession relation is central to continuity outcome.
- **Bonded/symbiotic identity**: continuity claim classified where bonded or symbiotic relation is central.
- **Remnant identity**: continuity claim classified as remnant persistence rather than full direct continuity.
- **Apparent continuity only**: continuity-like presentation without doctrine-level continuity confirmation.
- **Source-local only**: continuity handling retained as donor-local construct only.
- **Quarantined pending later doctrine**: continuity handling held in quarantine until later doctrine resolution.
- **Escalated contradiction**: continuity handling escalated due to unresolved contradiction.

## 5. Core doctrine rules
- A03 defines ontology classification grammar and boundaries only; it does not define runtime mechanics.
- A03 remains `A03_soul_body_mind_spirit_ontology.md` and is not renamed.
- K01 remains separate and lexicon governance is not moved into A03.
- A03 is doctrine draft guidance and is not current.
- A03 does not define lore canon and does not promote donor ontology into Astra default law.
- A03 may reference A01 only for source-local afterlife, plane, or persistence pressure classification context.
- A03 may reference A02 only when identity, embodiment, augmentation, or persistence depends on a source-field relation.

## 6. Conversion mapping rules
- **Direct ontology mapping**: donor concept maps to an existing Astra ontology component class without semantic distortion.
- **Normalized ontology mapping**: donor concept maps after explicit normalization preserving provenance and boundary notes.
- **Continuity outcome mapping**: continuity claims are classified using the continuity outcome grammar before downstream usage.
- **Source-local retained identity construct**: donor identity metaphysics retained for local conversion continuity without Astra-canon authority.
- **Quarantined identity construct pending later doctrine**: unresolved identity metaphysics isolated pending later doctrine/canon review.
- **Escalated identity contradiction**: contradiction or unclassifiable identity metaphysics escalated for formal doctrine resolution.

## 7. Source-local handling
- Donor soul/body/mind/spirit metaphysics can be retained as source-local records.
- Source-local records preserve donor provenance but do not become Astra ontology canon.
- Repeated donor pressure can produce canon candidates only through later K-layer/canon review.
- Unclassifiable identity metaphysics are quarantined or escalated, not normalized by invention.
- Donor proper nouns remain source-local unless canon promotion accepts them later.
- Source-local soul or identity terms do not become accepted lexicon terms through A03.

## 8. Donor pressure absorbed
- Pressure to import donor soul laws as universal Astra truth.
- Pressure to treat AI strictly as equipment regardless of personhood evidence.
- Pressure to collapse copied/forked/uploaded identity cases into single unquestioned continuity outcomes.
- Pressure to force donor possession/bonding/symbiosis metaphysics into global defaults.
- Pressure to equate extraction success with canon adoption.

## 9. Hard refusals / rejected imports
- D&D soul or afterlife mechanics are not Astra default ontology law.
- Shadowrun essence/astral assumptions are not Astra default ontology law.
- Donor resurrection-return models are not Astra default ontology law.
- Contradictory continuity claims are not silently merged by invention.
- Donor identity proper nouns are not accepted lexicon terms by A03.
