# D16-03 — Encounter Composition, Objectives, Mixed Opposition, and Noncombat Threats

Status: `doctrine-pack`
Version: `v0.1`
Layer: D16 / operational procedure doctrine
Primary scope: Opposition, creature, encounter, and hazard construction
Authority posture: Doctrine-facing and conversion-facing. Not final runtime schema, not final statblock schema, not encounter-balance math.

D16 is part of the D12–D18 operational procedure layer. It assumes D00–D15 are active doctrine dependencies and must preserve their owner boundaries.


## Purpose

Encounter composition assembles multiple threats into an encounter-facing situation without importing donor encounter budgets, CR math, tactical-role formulas, random encounter tables, or combat-only assumptions.

## Objective–Pressure–Composition Model

An encounter-facing situation is built from:

```text
1. Encounter purpose
2. Primary pressure
3. Opposition elements
4. Role distribution
5. Objective structure
6. Environment / context pressure
7. Stakes and state-delta targets
8. Readiness and owner-file handoffs
```

## Encounter purpose

Every encounter should identify a procedural purpose: block route, guard resource, test intruders, create cost, force choice, delay party, reveal pressure, escalate faction response, threaten harm, threaten exposure, create negotiation, trigger pursuit, consume time, protect secret, contest claim, deny access, defend site, collapse environment, advance source-local script, or another bounded purpose.

Purpose prevents “monsters appear and fight” from becoming default.

## Primary pressure

Every encounter declares one primary pressure and any secondary pressures. Primary pressure may be harm, control, route denial, resource drain, time pressure, social pressure, legal pressure, information denial, hazard exposure, corruption/instability, object or platform disruption, territorial pressure, domain pressure, or source-local pressure.

## Opposition elements

Each independent opposition element with its own function, pressure, or owner-file handoff receives its own Threat Profile.

Elements may include creature profile, NPC/rival profile, social antagonist, hazard, environmental threat, trap-like pressure, security system, vehicle/ship/mech/platform threat, swarm/horde, elite/boss, faction agent, legal authority, object system, summon/companion under opposition role, or source-local entity.

## Role distribution

Role distribution clarifies how elements interact: guardian + hazard source, ambusher + route blocker, negotiator + legal pressure, boss + objective holders, swarm + area denial, platform threat + crew hazard, social blocker + rumor pressure, pursuer + terrain pressure, controller + resource drain.

Balanced role distribution is not required. Clarity is required.

## Objective structure

Objective structures include single_objective, competing_objectives, layered_objectives, escape_objective, defense_objective, negotiation_objective, exposure_objective, resource_objective, and source-local objective.

Not all encounters are meant to be defeated. Some must be survived, bypassed, negotiated, delayed, escaped, exposed, transformed, or routed into another procedure.

## Context pressure

D16 may include terrain, visibility, weather/exposure, crowd, legal jurisdiction, faction territory, domain alignment, site instability, platform state, resource scarcity, hidden information, time limit, or source-local environment as context pressure. D14 may identify context. D16 composes opposition inside it. Owner files define actual effects.

## Stakes and state-delta targets

Encounters identify likely state-delta targets: actor condition, injury/harm, resource state, object/platform state, route access, map-state, relationship standing, faction pressure, law status, information state, territory/domain posture, salvage/reward claim, project status, campaign pressure, or source-local state.

## Mixed opposition

Mixed opposition is first-class. D16 supports combat + hazard, social + legal, stealth + platform, faction + encounter, hazard + resource, boss + objective, and other combinations.

Mixed opposition must declare primary pressure, secondary pressures, primary D12 cadence profile, owner-file handoffs, independent profiles, context pressure, and source-local boundaries.

## Noncombat opposition

Noncombat opposition is first-class. It includes legal blockade, social exclusion, reputation trap, institutional denial, misinformation campaign, ritual lock, security protocol, resource scarcity, environmental exposure, territorial claim, surveillance net, platform malfunction, diplomatic obstacle, and source-local barrier.

It must not be reduced to “make a skill check” unless D02/D05 and scene context justify it.

## Balance posture

D16 does not define encounter budgets, CR conversion, expected-party-level math, threat-point systems, or quantitative difficulty. It uses qualitative composition review and owner-file readiness.
