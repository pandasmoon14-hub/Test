# D16-00 — Opposition, Creature, Encounter, and Hazard Construction Owner Boundaries

Status: `doctrine-pack`
Version: `v0.1`
Layer: D16 / operational procedure doctrine
Primary scope: Opposition, creature, encounter, and hazard construction
Authority posture: Doctrine-facing and conversion-facing. Not final runtime schema, not final statblock schema, not encounter-balance math.

D16 is part of the D12–D18 operational procedure layer. It assumes D00–D15 are active doctrine dependencies and must preserve their owner boundaries.


## Purpose

D16 defines how Astra constructs opposition as usable encounter-facing pressure across creatures, rivals, hazards, social antagonists, security systems, swarms, bosses, platform enemies, summons, companions acting in opposition, and institutional proxies.

D16 does not merely build monsters. It builds anything that resists, endangers, contests, obstructs, pursues, negotiates against, or pressures a player-facing actor or goal.

## Core doctrine question

How does Astra construct opposition, creatures, rivals, hazards, encounters, bosses, swarms, platform enemies, social antagonists, and noncombat threats across genre and scale without importing donor monster math, CR systems, encounter budgets, statblock structures, or combat action economies as Astra defaults?

## Primary architecture

D16 uses a **Threat Role–Pressure–Profile Architecture**.

```text
opposition trigger or source construct
  -> threat function identification
  -> opposition scale and substrate review
  -> role / objective / behavior posture
  -> pressure profile construction
  -> owner-file handoffs for actor, object, harm, ability, resource, world-state, economy
  -> encounter composition or source-local retention
  -> quarantine or escalation if doctrine is missing
```

## What D16 owns

D16 owns opposition construction procedure, creature/NPC encounter-facing profile construction, rival and antagonist construction, social antagonist construction, hazard construction as encounter-facing pressure, environmental threat construction, boss/elite/swarm/minion/horde/group pressure construction, summon/companion opposition handling, vehicle/ship/mech/platform enemy construction as opposition-facing pressure, morale and retreat posture as opposition behavior pressure, objectives, tactical role definition, threat posture, resistance and vulnerability routing, scaling doctrine, encounter composition, noncombat opposition construction, bestiary conversion pressure, source-local opposition systems, and quarantine/escalation for unsupported opposition math.

## What D16 does not own

D16 does not own D02 resolution math; D03 resource pools, recharge, overdraw, or economy; D04 advancement or breakthrough gates; D05 full skill/method taxonomy; D06 Technique, Principle, oath, domain expression, or power construction; D07 harm math, damage-family taxonomy, injury, corruption, or condition rules; D08 actor personhood, body/form-state, substrate, companion identity, or creature ontology; D09 object, relic, implant, platform, vehicle, ship, mech, tool, or weapon state; D10 world-state, faction-state, hidden truth, relationships, law, or information-state; D11 narration and presentation; D12 encounter cadence and action windows; D13 project procedure; D14 exploration/travel/site-entry trigger procedure; D15 faction/institution operation; D17 economy/reward/loot/requisition; D18 campaign pacing, arc escalation, and long-horizon threat continuity; final runtime implementation; or final creature/hazard/encounter schema.

## Binding anti-drift controls

Donor stat fields are evidence only. D16 maps function, pressure, scale, role, objective, posture, and owner-file handoff. It does not define final math.

Combat is not the default opposition form. Social, legal, institutional, object/system, platform, environmental, hazard, information, terrain, corruption, resource, and noncombat opposition are first-class.

D16 may identify harm pressure, resistance pressure, vulnerability pressure, immunity pressure, and bypass pressure, but D07 and other owner files define exact mechanics.

D16 constructs encounter-facing opposition. D08 owns actor substrate, body/form-state, personhood, companion identity, summon identity, control boundaries, AI, spirits, clones, and creature ontology.

D16 may construct platform/object opposition pressure. D09 owns object, gear, weapon, armor, vehicle, ship, mech, tool, implant, platform, module, hardpoint, security-system, and object-state doctrine.

D16 may create conflict posture or pressure. D12 owns immediate cadence, D15 owns institutional operation, D17 owns economy/reward handling, and D18 owns campaign-scale continuity.

## Donor-family pressures

D20/class-level donors pressure D16 toward CR, level-based balance, AC, HP, attack bonuses, saves, legendary actions, lair actions, monster types, spell lists, treasure, and encounter budgets.

OSR donors pressure D16 toward sparse statblocks, morale, reaction rolls, wandering monsters, high lethality, treasure incentives, and rulings-heavy traps.

Tactical/mech donors pressure D16 toward roles, templates, grids, initiative systems, cover, range bands, hardpoints, action points, heat, armor, and battlefield control.

Narrative/tag donors pressure D16 toward threats, fronts, aspects, tags, clocks, narrative permissions, and antagonist drives.

Horror/investigation donors pressure D16 toward unknowable entities, clue-gated threats, hidden vulnerabilities, pursuit pressure, and impossible opposition.

Cyberpunk/skill-pool donors pressure D16 toward security teams, drones, hackers, corporate response, cyberware threats, surveillance, heat, and tactical gear.

Vehicle/ship/platform donors pressure D16 toward platform-scale enemies, crew roles, hull states, hardpoints, sensors, fuel, maneuvers, and scale mismatch.

Cultivation/LitRPG donors pressure D16 toward tiered beasts, realms, aura pressure, domain suppression, sect rivals, breakthrough guardians, elite bosses, resource guardians, and scaling pressure.

Faction/domain donors pressure D16 toward armies, institutions, sanctions, legal threats, propaganda, territory pressure, patrols, and organized retaliation.

Bestiary-heavy donors pressure D16 toward exact stat conversion, monster family taxonomies, special abilities, immunity/resistance lists, encounter ecology, and loot tables.

## Lawful outcomes

Every donor opposition construct must receive one lawful outcome: direct Astra mapping, normalized Astra mapping, source-local retained construct, quarantined construct pending later doctrine, or escalated doctrine problem.

# D16-01 — Opposition Anatomy, Threat Function, Scale, Role, Objective, and Posture

Status: `doctrine-pack`
Version: `v0.1`
Layer: D16 / operational procedure doctrine
Primary scope: Opposition, creature, encounter, and hazard construction
Authority posture: Doctrine-facing and conversion-facing. Not final runtime schema, not final statblock schema, not encounter-balance math.

D16 is part of the D12–D18 operational procedure layer. It assumes D00–D15 are active doctrine dependencies and must preserve their owner boundaries.


## Purpose

D16 opposition anatomy defines what must be known before a creature, rival, hazard, social antagonist, platform enemy, swarm, boss, institutional proxy, or mixed threat can be used as Astra opposition.

## Opposition anatomy

D16 uses ten construction elements:

```text
1. Opposition Source
2. Threat Function
3. Opposition Scale
4. Threat Role
5. Objective
6. Threat Posture
7. Pressure Profile
8. Constraint / Vulnerability / Resistance Routing
9. Owner-File Handoffs
10. Source-Local Boundary
```

## 1. Opposition Source

Opposition Source identifies what kind of construct is being used as opposition. It may be a creature, NPC, rival, social antagonist, hazard, environmental threat, security system, trap-like pressure, object system, vehicle, ship, mech, platform, swarm, horde, elite, boss, summon, companion under opposition role, faction agent, institutional proxy, legal pressure, territorial pressure, source-local entity, or mixed source.

The source routes ownership but does not decide mechanics by itself.

## 2. Threat Function

Threat Function identifies what the opposition does procedurally before any donor stat, label, type, CR, or level is considered.

Threat functions include harm, capture, delay, guard, pursue, ambush, control_area, deny_access, separate_party, drain_resource, inflict_condition, create_exposure, deceive, misdirect, social_block, legal_block, extract_information, protect_target, summon_support, escalate_pressure, corrupt, test_intruders, force_choice, damage_object, disrupt_platform, alter_terrain, and source-local function.

## 3. Opposition Scale

Scale describes operational level, not power by itself.

D16 supports individual, pair_or_small_group, group, swarm, horde, elite, boss, environmental, site, object_system, platform, vehicle_ship_mech, institutional_proxy, territorial, regional, source_local, and mixed scale.

A boss does not automatically mean solo monster math. A swarm does not automatically mean area damage. A platform does not automatically mean a vehicle statblock.

## 4. Threat Role

Threat Role describes the job the opposition performs inside the encounter or pressure situation.

Roles include striker, defender, controller, skirmisher, support, artillery, lurker, leader, guardian, pursuer, ambusher, negotiator, investigator, social_blocker, legal_blocker, hazard_source, terrain_shaper, platform_threat, objective_holder, summoner, disruptor, buffer, debuffer, and source-local role.

These are functional roles and do not grant donor-style abilities by themselves.

## 5. Objective

Opposition should have an objective when capable of directed behavior or when pressure has a defined purpose.

Objectives include kill, capture, drive_away, delay, guard, warn_others, escape, protect_target, protect_territory, extract_resource, extract_information, enforce_law, test_intruders, negotiate, mislead, corrupt, feed, survive, complete_ritual, hold_position, buy_time, trigger_alarm, damage_asset, block_route, and source-local objective.

Hazards may have functional objectives such as separating the party or blocking a route without implying intent.

## 6. Threat Posture

Threat Posture describes current behavior state: unaware, watching, warning, probing, negotiating, guarding, hostile, hunting, ambushing, pursuing, defending, retreating, desperate, controlled, corrupted, confused, suppressed, dormant, triggered, source-local posture, or mixed.

Posture affects whether D12 enters focused scene, structured exchange, chase, conflict, hazard cadence, negotiation, or another scene procedure.

## 7. Pressure Profile

Every opposition construct identifies a pressure profile.

Pressure families include harm_pressure, control_pressure, mobility_pressure, area_denial, resource_pressure, time_pressure, social_pressure, legal_pressure, fear_or_dread_pressure, corruption_pressure, condition_pressure, exposure_pressure, terrain_pressure, information_pressure, object_pressure, platform_pressure, domain_pressure, summon_pressure, reinforcement_pressure, and source_local_pressure.

D16 defines the profile. Owner files define final consequences.

## 8. Constraint / Vulnerability / Resistance Routing

D16 may identify constraints, vulnerabilities, resistances, immunities, protections, thresholds, weaknesses, morale limits, or access conditions. It must not import donor resistance math.

Exact mechanics route to D07 for harm interactions, D08 for actor/substrate interactions, D09 for object/platform protection, D06 for domain/Technique interaction, D10 for hidden weakness/world truth, and D11 for presentation.

## 9. Owner-file handoffs

Every opposition profile identifies owner-file handoffs. D16 must explicitly route uncertainty to D02, resource drain to D03, method implications to D05, power expression to D06, harm to D07, actor questions to D08, object/platform state to D09, world/faction truth to D10/D15, presentation to D11, cadence to D12, exploration trigger to D14, reward/economy to D17, and recurring threat continuity to D18 as applicable.

## 10. Source-local boundary

Source-local opposition may preserve donor-specific creatures, bosses, hazards, faction patrols, morale procedures, encounter scripts, and platform enemies only with explicit boundaries and rejected-import notes.

Source-local retention does not promote the construct to Astra canon.

# D16-02 — Threat Profile Construction, Capability Pressure, Scaling, and Encounter-Facing Readiness

Status: `doctrine-pack`
Version: `v0.1`
Layer: D16 / operational procedure doctrine
Primary scope: Opposition, creature, encounter, and hazard construction
Authority posture: Doctrine-facing and conversion-facing. Not final runtime schema, not final statblock schema, not encounter-balance math.

D16 is part of the D12–D18 operational procedure layer. It assumes D00–D15 are active doctrine dependencies and must preserve their owner boundaries.


## Purpose

D16 threat profile construction turns an opposition construct into a usable, auditable encounter-facing profile without adopting donor statblocks, challenge ratings, level math, action economy, hit points, armor values, or encounter budgets.

## Eight-checkpoint construction procedure

```text
1. Evidence and source checkpoint
2. Threat function checkpoint
3. Scale and role checkpoint
4. Objective and posture checkpoint
5. Capability pressure checkpoint
6. Constraint / resistance / vulnerability checkpoint
7. Threat weight and context checkpoint
8. Encounter-facing readiness checkpoint
```

## 1. Evidence and source checkpoint

Evidence may come from bestiary statblocks, NPC profiles, hazard entries, trap procedures, encounter text, adventure nodes, faction response, patrol tables, random encounter tables, vehicle/ship/mech records, platform systems, summon/companion entries, social antagonists, legal or institutional pressure, or source-local lore.

Donor fields are evidence, not Astra mechanics.

## 2. Threat function checkpoint

Define what the opposition does before form or donor label.

A CR monster may become close-range harm and area-denial pressure. A corporate investigator may become information-pressure and legal-pressure opposition. A collapsing bridge may become mobility-pressure and separation hazard. An interdiction ship may become platform-pressure, route-denial, and resource-drain opposition.

## 3. Scale and role checkpoint

Assign scale and role from D16-01. Scale answers what level of procedure the opposition occupies. Role answers what job the opposition performs.

## 4. Objective and posture checkpoint

Define what the opposition is attempting or what procedural purpose it serves. D16 rejects “opposition fights until destroyed” as universal default.

## 5. Capability pressure checkpoint

Capability pressure identifies what the opposition can bring to bear without becoming a final power list.

Capability families include direct_harm_capability, area_control_capability, mobility_capability, defense_or_survival_capability, support_capability, summon_or_reinforcement_capability, social_leverage_capability, legal_or_institutional_capability, information_capability, stealth_or_ambush_capability, resource_drain_capability, corruption_or_instability_capability, object_disruption_capability, platform_system_capability, terrain_or_environment_capability, domain_or_power_pressure_capability, and source_local_capability.

D06 owns Techniques, Principles, oaths, domains, and power expression. D07 owns harm/effect consequences. D08 owns actor substrate. D09 owns object and platform systems. D10/D15 own institutional and world-state implications.

## 6. Constraint / resistance / vulnerability checkpoint

Identify what limits or modifies opposition: territorial binding, command source, limited senses, required environment, host body, legal authority, route binding, unstable system, morale limit, source-local taboo, resistance pressure, vulnerability pressure, immunity pressure, protection, threshold, or bypass condition.

D16 may identify the pressure but must not invent exact immunity, damage, armor, or bypass mechanics without owner support.

## 7. Threat Weight and context checkpoint

D16 uses qualitative Threat Weight bands:

```text
incidental
minor
standard
major
severe
dominant
apex / source-local
```

Threat Weight is a construction and review signal, not CR, level, balance math, encounter budget, XP value, or universal difficulty rating.

Threat Weight depends on actor capability, location tier, PCR pressure, domain alignment, numbers, environment, objective, preparedness, resource state, hidden information, source-local rules, owner-file support, and campaign role.

## 8. Encounter-facing readiness checkpoint

Profiles receive readiness states:

```text
ready_for_scene_use
ready_with_owner_file_handoffs
source_local_ready
requires_schema_support
requires_harm_support
requires_actor_support
requires_object_or_platform_support
requires_world_state_support
requires_economy_or_reward_support
quarantined_pending_doctrine_or_evidence
escalated_owner_file_problem
```

Unsupported opposition must not be used as if complete.

# D16-03 — Encounter Composition, Objectives, Mixed Opposition, and Noncombat Threats

Status: `doctrine-pack`
Version: `v0.1`
Layer: D16 / operational procedure doctrine
Primary scope: Opposition, creature, encounter, and hazard construction
Authority posture: Doctrine-facing and conversion-facing. Not final runtime schema, not final statblock schema, not encounter-balance math.

D16 is part of the D12–D18 operational procedure layer. It assumes D00–D15 are active doctrine dependencies and must preserve their owner boundaries.


## Purpose

Encounter composition assembles multiple threats into an encounter-facing situation without importing donor encounter budgets, CR math, tactical-role formulas, random encounter tables, or combat-only assumptions.

## Objective–Pressure–Composition Model

An encounter-facing situation is built from:

```text
1. Encounter purpose
2. Primary pressure
3. Opposition elements
4. Role distribution
5. Objective structure
6. Environment / context pressure
7. Stakes and state-delta targets
8. Readiness and owner-file handoffs
```

## Encounter purpose

Every encounter should identify a procedural purpose: block route, guard resource, test intruders, create cost, force choice, delay party, reveal pressure, escalate faction response, threaten harm, threaten exposure, create negotiation, trigger pursuit, consume time, protect secret, contest claim, deny access, defend site, collapse environment, advance source-local script, or another bounded purpose.

Purpose prevents “monsters appear and fight” from becoming default.

## Primary pressure

Every encounter declares one primary pressure and any secondary pressures. Primary pressure may be harm, control, route denial, resource drain, time pressure, social pressure, legal pressure, information denial, hazard exposure, corruption/instability, object or platform disruption, territorial pressure, domain pressure, or source-local pressure.

## Opposition elements

Each independent opposition element with its own function, pressure, or owner-file handoff receives its own Threat Profile.

Elements may include creature profile, NPC/rival profile, social antagonist, hazard, environmental threat, trap-like pressure, security system, vehicle/ship/mech/platform threat, swarm/horde, elite/boss, faction agent, legal authority, object system, summon/companion under opposition role, or source-local entity.

## Role distribution

Role distribution clarifies how elements interact: guardian + hazard source, ambusher + route blocker, negotiator + legal pressure, boss + objective holders, swarm + area denial, platform threat + crew hazard, social blocker + rumor pressure, pursuer + terrain pressure, controller + resource drain.

Balanced role distribution is not required. Clarity is required.

## Objective structure

Objective structures include single_objective, competing_objectives, layered_objectives, escape_objective, defense_objective, negotiation_objective, exposure_objective, resource_objective, and source-local objective.

Not all encounters are meant to be defeated. Some must be survived, bypassed, negotiated, delayed, escaped, exposed, transformed, or routed into another procedure.

## Context pressure

D16 may include terrain, visibility, weather/exposure, crowd, legal jurisdiction, faction territory, domain alignment, site instability, platform state, resource scarcity, hidden information, time limit, or source-local environment as context pressure. D14 may identify context. D16 composes opposition inside it. Owner files define actual effects.

## Stakes and state-delta targets

Encounters identify likely state-delta targets: actor condition, injury/harm, resource state, object/platform state, route access, map-state, relationship standing, faction pressure, law status, information state, territory/domain posture, salvage/reward claim, project status, campaign pressure, or source-local state.

## Mixed opposition

Mixed opposition is first-class. D16 supports combat + hazard, social + legal, stealth + platform, faction + encounter, hazard + resource, boss + objective, and other combinations.

Mixed opposition must declare primary pressure, secondary pressures, primary D12 cadence profile, owner-file handoffs, independent profiles, context pressure, and source-local boundaries.

## Noncombat opposition

Noncombat opposition is first-class. It includes legal blockade, social exclusion, reputation trap, institutional denial, misinformation campaign, ritual lock, security protocol, resource scarcity, environmental exposure, territorial claim, surveillance net, platform malfunction, diplomatic obstacle, and source-local barrier.

It must not be reduced to “make a skill check” unless D02/D05 and scene context justify it.

## Balance posture

D16 does not define encounter budgets, CR conversion, expected-party-level math, threat-point systems, or quantitative difficulty. It uses qualitative composition review and owner-file readiness.

# D16-05 — Scaling, Morale, Behavior, Recurrence, and Encounter Readiness

Status: `doctrine-pack`
Version: `v0.1`
Layer: D16 / operational procedure doctrine
Primary scope: Opposition, creature, encounter, and hazard construction
Authority posture: Doctrine-facing and conversion-facing. Not final runtime schema, not final statblock schema, not encounter-balance math.

D16 is part of the D12–D18 operational procedure layer. It assumes D00–D15 are active doctrine dependencies and must preserve their owner boundaries.


## Purpose

D16 qualitative scaling and behavior-readiness doctrine governs threat weight review, contextual scaling, opposition behavior, morale, retreat, recurrence, and readiness without importing numerical balance or donor morale tables.

## Qualitative Scaling and Behavior State Model

D16 uses six controls:

```text
1. Threat Weight Review
2. Context Scaling Review
3. Behavior / Morale / Retreat Posture
4. Recurrence and Continuity Handling
5. Encounter Readiness Gates
6. Owner-File Escalation Rules
```

## Threat Weight Review

Threat Weight uses the bands incidental, minor, standard, major, severe, dominant, and apex/source-local.

Review considers current actor capability, number of opposition elements, primary pressure, secondary pressures, location tier, environmental PCR, domain alignment, resource state, surprise/preparedness, route/site constraints, objective difficulty, hidden information, support/reinforcement, resistance/vulnerability pressure, platform/object support, institutional backing, and source-local context.

Threat Weight is not fairness, CR, level, party-level math, XP, or encounter budget.

## Context Scaling Review

D16 does not scale by party level or donor challenge rating. It asks what context the opposition is designed for, what makes it more or less dangerous, what owner files support its pressure, and whether the opposition is meant to be fought, bypassed, negotiated, survived, delayed, studied, escaped, or transformed.

Scaling can adjust objective, numbers, posture, preparedness, environment, support, reinforcement, pressure profile, constraints, vulnerabilities, time pressure, access conditions, and source-local limits. It must not invent HP, damage, armor, action counts, or CR equivalents.

## Behavior / Morale / Retreat Posture

Behavior states include unaware, watching, probing, warning, negotiating, holding_position, pressing_attack, seeking_capture, protecting_target, calling_support, retreating, routing, surrendering, hiding, regrouping, escalating, deescalating, controlled, frenzied, bound_by_command, and source-local.

Opposition may change behavior when objective is achieved or impossible, leader is lost, support is cut off, route is blocked, hidden vulnerability is exposed, cost becomes too high, legal authority is challenged, social leverage collapses, resource state changes, environment shifts, D10/D15 pressure changes, or source-local trigger occurs.

Morale is persistence pressure, not a universal score or table. It may be irrelevant, fragile, ordinary, disciplined, fanatical, programmed, bound, desperate, territorial, professional, or source-local.

Donor morale checks or tables may remain source-local, but do not become Astra baseline.

## Recurrence and Continuity

Recurring opposition may be a rival, faction agent, hunt threat, boss survivor, legal adversary, social antagonist, territorial guardian, institutional proxy, platform threat, persistent hazard, or source-local recurring threat.

D16 must not preserve recurring opposition by fiat. Recurrence requires continuity support: escape route, survival condition, institutional replacement, source-local return condition, unresolved pressure, offscreen continuity support, hazard persistence, platform repair/rebuild path, or D18 campaign arc handoff.

D10 records persistent state. D15 owns institutional continuity when factional. D18 owns long-horizon campaign continuity and arc pacing.

## Encounter Readiness Gates

Before use, D16 checks whether source is identified, threat function is clear, scale and role are declared, objective and posture are defined where relevant, pressure profile is clear, constraints/vulnerabilities/resistances are routed, owner-file handoffs are explicit, source-local boundaries are recorded, donor math assumptions are rejected or preserved as evidence, encounter purpose is clear, primary pressure is declared, state-delta targets are routable, and readiness state is assigned.

# D16-06 — Source-Local Bestiary Systems, Donor Opposition Mapping, Quarantine, and Escalation

Status: `doctrine-pack`
Version: `v0.1`
Layer: D16 / operational procedure doctrine
Primary scope: Opposition, creature, encounter, and hazard construction
Authority posture: Doctrine-facing and conversion-facing. Not final runtime schema, not final statblock schema, not encounter-balance math.

D16 is part of the D12–D18 operational procedure layer. It assumes D00–D15 are active doctrine dependencies and must preserve their owner boundaries.


## Purpose

D16 donor opposition mapping defines how Astra converts donor creatures, statblocks, hazards, traps, NPC rivals, bosses, encounter budgets, swarm systems, morale rules, vehicle enemies, platform threats, and social/legal opposition without allowing donor opposition math or statblock structure to become Astra law.

## Functional donor opposition mapping ladder

```text
1. Identify the donor opposition construct.
2. Identify the donor evidence type.
3. Identify the threat function.
4. Identify the pressure profile.
5. Identify the scale and role.
6. Identify objective and posture if applicable.
7. Identify donor math, stat fields, action economy, resistance rules, morale rules, loot, and encounter assumptions.
8. Identify owner files affected.
9. Decide lawful handling:
   - direct Astra mapping;
   - normalized Astra mapping;
   - source-local retained construct;
   - quarantine;
   - escalation.
10. Record rejected donor assumptions.
```

## Direct Astra mapping

Use direct mapping only when the donor opposition construct already matches D16 grammar without unsupported math or hidden donor systems.

Example: a guard patrol that denies access, warns others, and delays intruders may map to institutional proxy / faction agent opposition with those threat functions, D15 institutional handoff, D12 encounter cadence, and D16 threat construction.

## Normalized Astra mapping

Use normalized mapping when donor function survives but donor math is stripped.

A CR monster becomes a creature/monster Threat Profile with function, scale, role, objective, posture, pressure, owner-file handoffs, and Threat Weight review. CR does not become Astra math.

A trap DC and damage entry becomes hazard pressure with trigger, function, pressure family, owner-file routing, and readiness state. DC and damage dice remain donor evidence unless adopted by proper owner files.

A boss phase script becomes dominant threat pressure, source-local script if bounded, or escalation if repeated pressure reveals missing doctrine.

A vehicle enemy becomes platform pressure with D09/D12/D17 handoffs. Hull points, hardpoints, speed, and crew actions are not adopted automatically.

## Source-local retained constructs

Use source-local retention for named monster families, source-specific boss phases, local dungeon wandering monster procedures, donor morale tables used inside one adventure, unique ship combat profiles, realm-specific guardian hazards, specific security rating systems, special cult encounter tables, donor cosmology ecology, proper-noun apex entities, and similar bounded systems.

Retained constructs require explicit boundaries and rejected-import notes.

## Quarantine triggers

Quarantine donor opposition systems when extraction damage collapses statblock fields, table rows are missing, sidecars are missing, encounter boundaries are unclear, creature/hazard/object boundaries are unclear, harm/damage families are unsupported, condition/effect is unsupported, actor/substrate/personhood assumptions are unsupported, object/platform mechanics are unsupported, power/domain expression is unsupported, action economy is unsupported, resistance/vulnerability mechanics are unsupported, morale/reaction procedure is unsupported, encounter budget or CR conversion is unsupported, loot/reward/economy linkage is unsupported, social combat/legal opposition is unsupported, donor cosmology or monster taxonomy is unsupported, source-local boundary is unclear, state mutation is unclear, or owner-file support is missing.

## Escalation triggers

Escalate repeated or high-impact missing doctrine, such as need for normalized creature threat-band doctrine, tactical role-based pressure doctrine, controlled set-piece threat doctrine, platform-opposition interface doctrine, impossible/unknowable threat doctrine, social antagonist construction refinement, morale/reaction persistence-pressure refinement, or encounter table governance.

## Rejected donor assumptions

D16 rejects unsupported CR as Astra threat math, level as opposition scaling law, HP as Astra vitality, armor class as Astra defense, attack bonus as Astra accuracy math, damage dice as Astra harm math, saving throws as Astra resistance procedure, action economy as Astra cadence, legendary actions as boss default, lair actions as site default, minion rules as default group handling, swarm rules as default horde handling, morale tables as universal behavior, reaction rolls as universal social posture, creature type as metaphysical canon, immunity/resistance lists as final mechanics, trap DCs as Astra difficulty law, hazard damage as Astra harm law, encounter budgets as balance law, loot tables as reward doctrine, monster ecology as canon, vehicle hull points as platform harm law, hardpoints as universal platform schema, NPC class levels as Astra build law, and social statblocks as social truth.

## Table-like, budget-like, and clock-like systems

Statblocks, tables, clocks, phases, budgets, and ratings are evidence, not authority. They route by function to D16, D12, D07, D08, D09, D10, D15, D17, D18, source-local retention, quarantine, or escalation.
