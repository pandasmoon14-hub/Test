## Purpose

This file defines how travel and exploration create pressure without making random encounters, survival attrition, travel rations, hex hazards, or scripted triggers universal law.

## Pressure Trigger and Owner Handoff Model

Travel or exploration pressure may arise only from a lawful trigger: posture, route state, environmental PCR, known hazard, owner-supported hidden pressure, D10 unresolved pressure, opposition movement, resource scarcity, site design, source-local procedure, prior state delta, failed/partial D02 result, acceleration/unsafe method, or owner-file clock/pressure queue.

Travel pressure is not automatic.

## Pressure families

Families include resource_pressure, exposure_pressure, hazard_pressure, encounter_pressure, navigation_pressure, map_state_pressure, ambush_exposure, social_or_legal_pressure, faction_or_territory_pressure, object_or_platform_pressure, hidden_state_pressure, time_pressure, and source_local_pressure.

## Resource pressure

Examples: supplies, fuel, power, heat/strain, light, air/environment support, ritual stability, medicine, mount endurance, companion burden, crew attention, tool durability, platform maintenance, time, favors, permits, source-local resources. Handoffs: D03, D09, D17, D18.

## Exposure pressure

Examples: fatigue, weather exposure, corruption exposure, toxic exposure, hostile atmosphere, spiritual/domain pressure, affliction risk, radiation-like hazard, psychic or reality distortion, source-local exposure. Handoffs: D07, D08, D03, D11.

## Hazard pressure

Examples: unstable terrain, trap signs, anomaly, falling debris, flooding, fire, cold, storm, corruption field, dimensional instability, security systems, minefield, platform malfunction. Handoffs: D07, D09, D10, D12, D16. D14 owns approach/trigger handling, not hazard construction.

## Encounter pressure

Examples: patrol contact, creature sign, rival movement, faction checkpoint, law enforcement, ambush opportunity, wandering opposition, distress signal, ship contact, social confrontation. Handoffs: D08, D10, D12, D16, D11. D14 identifies contact pressure; it does not build encounter math.

## Navigation and map-state pressure

Examples: lost route, false trail, map contradiction, missing landmark, sensor spoof, realm distortion, shifting site, unreliable guide, hidden route, blocked path, compromised map. Handoffs: D02, D05, D10, D11.

## Social/legal/faction pressure

Examples: checkpoint scrutiny, permit demand, restricted district, sect territory, guild-controlled road, corporate surveillance, boundary violation, reputation exposure, smuggling route, rumor spread. Handoffs: D10, D15, D17, D11.

## Object/platform pressure

Examples: vehicle wear, ship system strain, sensor overload, tool damage, mount exhaustion, gear loss, cargo instability, platform exposure, fuel stress, module malfunction, map-tool corruption. Handoffs: D09, D17, D12, D13, D16.

## Time pressure

Examples: arrival delay, missed window, patrol rotation changes, storm advances, ritual deadline approaches, faction response escalates, resource spoils, site changes, campaign clock advances, source-local countdown. Handoffs: D10, D15, D18, D13.

## Random encounters and tables

Random encounters, patrol tables, weather tables, oracle results, route events, and procedural encounter checks are valid only when supported by source-local procedure, converted source boundary, later procedural tool, runtime/world-state module using validated tables, scenario design, or owner-file state. They are not default D14 law and must not bypass D10, D11, D16, D17, source-local boundaries, or lawful outcome taxonomy.

## Transition handoffs

Travel may transition to D12 focused scene or structured exchange, D13 Project, D15 faction operation, D16 encounter/hazard construction, D17 acquisition/requisition procedure, D18 campaign-scale travel/time skip, source-local procedure, quarantine, or escalation. Every transition records why it occurred and which owner files are invoked.
