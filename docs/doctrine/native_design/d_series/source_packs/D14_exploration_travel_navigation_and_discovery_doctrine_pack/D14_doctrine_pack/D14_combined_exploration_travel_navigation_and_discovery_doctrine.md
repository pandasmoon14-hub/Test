## Locked architecture

D14 uses the **Posture–Route–Discovery Architecture**:

```text
travel or exploration intent
  -> posture declaration
  -> route / area selection
  -> known-state review
  -> risk and PCR assessment
  -> movement or exploration interval
  -> discovery / hazard / encounter / transition check
  -> map-state update
  -> owner-file handoff
  -> continue, enter site, shift cadence, or end travel
```

## Core locks

Travel posture is first-class and is not a generic bonus. Routes are functional paths/vectors with knowledge states. D14 owns discovery procedure and classification; D10 owns actual world-state/information-state; D11 owns presentation. Party-facing map-state is distinct from actual truth. Environmental PCR is a travel/exploration pressure input. Donor travel systems map, source-local retain, quarantine, or escalate by function rather than label.

## Not-final-schema warning

All records in this pack are **not final schema**. They are doctrine-facing and conversion-facing control shapes. Runtime Gate B, Batch C map/location schema doctrine, or later sourcebook consolidation may replace or formalize them.

# D14-01 — Travel Posture, Route Selection, and Known-State Review

Status: doctrine-draft / Phase 1 native doctrine continuation  
Version: `0.1.0-d14-generated`  
Generated: 2026-06-02  
Layer: D14 operational procedure doctrine  
Owner: Astra doctrine architecture / exploration and discovery procedure layer

## Purpose

This file defines the front end of travel and exploration.

## Five-checkpoint front end

```text
1. Travel / exploration intent checkpoint
2. Posture checkpoint
3. Route / area checkpoint
4. Known-state review checkpoint
5. Risk and handoff checkpoint
```

## Intent checkpoint

Intent must be clear enough to classify the procedure as route travel, area exploration, site approach, site entry, pursuit/evasion, scouting, mapping, resource search, platform navigation, realm crossing, or source-local exploration. Broad intent should be clarified rather than converted into a generic travel roll.

## Posture checkpoint

Travel posture is a declaration of priority, not a generic bonus. Posture changes what is easier, harder, visible, missed, exposed, delayed, protected, or handed off.

Core posture families: speed, safety, stealth, scouting, mapping, tracking, foraging/resource search, concealment, diplomatic approach, survey/analysis, ritual sensing, sensor sweep, platform caution, resource conservation, pursuit, evasion, combat readiness, social blending, and source-local posture.

Examples: speed may improve progress but increase hazard exposure; stealth may reduce detection but slow progress; mapping may improve future route certainty but increase exposure; sensor sweep may reveal contacts but create emissions; ritual sensing may detect domain pressure but risk false resonance or hidden attention.

## Route / area checkpoint

D14 supports both route declarations and area declarations. A route is a path, vector, corridor, lane, trail, road, district sequence, jump line, platform route, realm crossing, or approach path. An area is a region, district, ruin, site, wilderness, platform section, city sector, plane-local region, battlefield, or unknown search space.

Routes are functional, not necessarily geometric. Route states include known, partially_known, suspected, rumored, hidden, false, blocked, dangerous, contested, watched, restricted, unstable, source_local, and unmapped. A route may carry multiple states.

## Known-state review checkpoint

Known-state categories: confirmed_known, party_believes, rumored, suspected, mapped_but_unverified, false_or_compromised, hidden_from_party, protected_hidden, and source_local. This prevents rumors, player guesses, map notes, sensor readings, generated table output, or donor boxed text from becoming world truth.

D10 owns actual truth. D11 owns safe presentation. D14 owns the review procedure.

## Risk and handoff checkpoint

D14 identifies likely owner-file handoffs before travel proceeds: D02 for uncertainty; D03 for resource pressure; D05 for methods; D06 for Techniques/domains; D07 for exposure/harm; D08 for actor substrate; D09 for maps/tools/platforms; D10 for world-state and hidden information; D11 for presentation; D12 for active-scene transition; D13 for long Projects; D15 for institutional territory; D16 for later hazards/opposition; D17 for supplies/licenses/scarcity; D18 for long journeys and time skips.

## Anti-drift rules

Posture is not a flat modifier. Route geometry is not required unless geometry matters. Party-facing known-state is not actual truth. Protected hidden state is not revealed during setup.

# D14-02 — Movement Intervals, Travel Pressure, Navigation, and Route Progress

Status: doctrine-draft / Phase 1 native doctrine continuation

Layer: D14 operational procedure doctrine

## Purpose

This file defines what happens during travel or exploration after intent, posture, route/area, known-state, and owner-file risks are identified.

D14 uses flexible **Travel / Exploration Intervals**, not universal travel turns.

## Travel / Exploration Interval

An interval is a bounded unit of movement or exploration during which posture, route, known-state, environmental pressure, and external risk can change. It may represent a momentary approach, scene segment, site segment, district leg, wilderness leg, watch period, ship/platform vector, stealth movement segment, site survey pass, realm crossing stage, search sweep, or source-local travel turn.

## Seven interval checkpoints

```text
1. Interval scale checkpoint
2. Navigation / orientation checkpoint
3. Posture pressure checkpoint
4. Environmental PCR checkpoint
5. Resource / exposure checkpoint
6. Discovery / hazard / encounter checkpoint
7. Progress and transition checkpoint
```

## Interval scale

The interval scale is context-driven. D14 does not set universal durations. A donor watch, turn, day, jump, phase, or segment is not Astra law unless source-local or owner-file supported.

## Navigation / orientation

D14 checks navigation only when direction, route, position, pursuit, map accuracy, or destination sense is uncertain, contested, hidden, distorted, false, dangerous, pursuit-sensitive, or otherwise risky. If there is no uncertainty, no roll is required. D02 owns resolution. D05 owns method relevance.

## Posture pressure

The declared posture modifies exposure, visibility, blind spots, delay, discovery opportunities, and owner-file handoffs. It is not a generic bonus system.

## Environmental PCR

Environmental PCR may include location_tier, location_tags, domain_alignment, opposition_modifier, hidden_cosmic_modifier, environmental_pressure, and source_local_modifiers. D14 uses PCR to shape route difficulty, risk, distortion, visibility, cost, domain alignment, and hidden pressure. D02 owns resolution, D10 owns truth, D11 owns presentation, and hidden Pneuma remains protected.

## Resource / exposure

Travel may create fatigue, supplies, fuel, power drain, heat/strain, ritual stability, equipment wear, companion burden, platform maintenance, injury aggravation, corruption exposure, legal scrutiny, faction trace, weather/terrain exposure, or source-local attrition. D14 identifies triggers. D03, D07, D08, D09, D10, D17, and D18 own actual state changes.

## Discovery / hazard / encounter

Each interval may produce route progress, map-state improvement, landmarks, clues, resource signs, hidden route hints, hazard exposure, environmental shifts, ambush risk, patrol contact, faction presence, object/salvage opportunities, site entrance, lost route, false route confirmation, pressure escalation, or no notable event.

Discovery, hazard, or encounter triggers require support from route pressure, posture, PCR, owner-file state, source-local procedure, prior state, or scenario design. Random encounters are not default D14 law.

## Progress states

Route progress states include advanced, advanced_with_cost, advanced_with_discovery, advanced_with_exposure, delayed, misdirected, lost, blocked, hazard_triggered, encounter_triggered, site_entry_triggered, map_state_updated, pressure_escalated, transitioned, quarantined, and escalated.

## Anti-drift rules

Do not use fixed travel turns by default. Do not check navigation every interval unless risk supports it. Do not roll random encounters by default. Do not let PCR expose hidden cosmic truth. Do not let D14 define resource loss, harm, fuel economy, weather damage, or encounter math.

# D14-03 — Discovery, Scouting, Mapping, and Information-State Boundaries

Status: doctrine-draft / Phase 1 native doctrine continuation  
Version: `0.1.0-d14-generated`  
Generated: 2026-06-02  
Layer: D14 operational procedure doctrine  
Owner: Astra doctrine architecture / exploration and discovery procedure layer

## Purpose

This file defines how travel and exploration create discovery opportunities and map-state updates while preserving D10 world-state authority and D11 hidden-state presentation boundaries.

## Discovery Opportunity Model

A Discovery Opportunity is a lawful chance for exploration to surface party-facing information, route-state, map-state, hazard-state, site-state, or owner-file pressure. A discovery is not automatically actual world truth.

## Discovery sources

Discovery opportunities may arise from posture, navigation, scouting, mapping, tracking, survey/analysis, ritual sensing, sensor sweep, social blending, resource search, environmental PCR, interval outcomes, site approach, D10 unresolved pressure, source-local table/procedure, scenario design, failed or partial movement, hazard proximity, faction/opposition movement, or object/platform sensor interaction.

## Discovery categories

Categories include route_discovery, location_discovery, site_entry_discovery, hazard_discovery, resource_discovery, clue_or_lead_discovery, faction_presence_discovery, opposition_trace_discovery, environmental_condition_discovery, object_or_salvage_discovery, map_correction, false_route_exposure, hidden_boundary_hint, and source_local_discovery.

D14 classifies. D10 decides actual truth. D11 presents safely.

## Discovery confidence

Confidence states: confirmed, high_confidence, partial, ambiguous, rumored, suspected, false_or_compromised, protected_hidden, and source_local. Confirmed discovery requires owner-file support. A good roll does not reveal protected truth if the truth is protected.

## Scouting

Scouting is a focused attempt to reduce uncertainty before full commitment. It may identify route safety, hazard signs, ambush risk, patrol patterns, site entrances, environmental PCR signals, resource signs, faction presence, object/salvage opportunities, map errors, hidden boundary hints, or need for further investigation.

Scouting does not automatically reveal all hidden truth. It may produce partial, ambiguous, or false information if the route is protected, distorted, deceptive, source-local, or beyond the actor's method.

## Mapping

Mapping converts movement and discovery into party-facing spatial or relational knowledge. D14 supports relational, route-based, zone-based, node-based, coordinate-based, platform-based, and source-local maps. Hexes and grids are optional/source-local forms, not universal law.

Map-state categories: known, surveyed, partially_mapped, suspected, rumored, false, compromised, hidden, blocked, unreachable, and source_local. A map-state update may improve, degrade, contradict, confirm, invalidate, or complicate party knowledge.

## Three-layer boundary

```text
actual truth — owned by D10
party-facing known-state/map-state — procedurally updated by D14
presentation — owned by D11
```

Hidden Pneuma, hidden cosmic modifiers, concealed factions, secret routes, GM-facing map facts, source-local secrets, and unrevealed world-state remain protected unless lawful procedure and D11 presentation permit surfacing.

## Anti-drift rules

Do not reduce discovery to perception checks. Do not let maps become truth. Do not let procedural tables generate canon truth without owner support. Do not let player-authored discoveries bypass D10. Do not leak hidden state through scouting, sensors, or ritual sensing.

## Purpose

This file defines Exploration Context Profiles and special transition tools for site entry, watches, and ambush exposure. Profiles are routing/procedure templates, not full subsystems.

## Profile structure

Each profile identifies movement logic, route forms, posture pressures, discovery types, hazard/encounter triggers, map-state concerns, watch/ambush exposure concerns, resource/exposure pressures, owner-file handoffs, source-local risks, and quarantine/escalation triggers.

## Core profiles

### Urban / Social-Dense

Used for cities, districts, ports, markets, stations, arcologies, courts, sect compounds, slums, campuses, corporate zones, and settlements. Movement logic: access, legality, visibility, social blending, surveillance, restricted areas, contacts, faction territory, transport networks, checkpoints. Handoffs: D05, D09, D10, D11, D15, D17. Do not import city encounter tables, crime systems, social-status systems, or faction clocks as universal law.

### Wilderness / Open-Region

Used for forests, deserts, mountains, tundra, oceans, plains, wastelands, jungles, and frontiers. Movement logic: route finding, weather/terrain pressure, supplies, exposure, landmarks, tracking, foraging, visibility, ambush space, environmental PCR. Handoffs: D03, D05, D07, D10, D16, D17. Do not universalize hexes, foraging, travel pace, random encounters, or watch shifts.

### Site / Dungeon / Interior

Used for ruins, buildings, dungeons, ships, bases, caves, labs, temples, tombs, vaults, bunkers, and facilities. Movement logic: entry control, room/node relation, visibility, sound, traps/hazards, keyed areas, locked paths, hidden doors, site memory, map uncertainty. Handoffs: D09, D10, D12, D16, D17. Do not import dungeon turns, keyed-room reveal assumptions, trap procedures, or wandering monster checks.

### Space / Vehicle / Platform

Used for ships, vehicles, mechs, stations, derelicts, convoys, fleets, star lanes, debris fields, and mobile bases. Movement logic: vectors, sensors, crew roles, fuel/power pressure, system integrity, platform scale, external hazards, docking/approach, instruments, communications. Handoffs: D03, D08, D09, D10, D12, D16, D17. Do not collapse platform movement into actor movement or import ship-turn, fuel, hardpoint, grid, or tactical-frame law.

### Dimensional / Realm / Threshold

Used for planes, realms, spirit roads, pocket worlds, gates, unstable zones, dream spaces, cultivation territories, domain regions, and mythic thresholds. Movement logic: access, domain alignment, stability, resonance, threshold conditions, identity pressure, route truth instability, hidden cosmic pressure, source-local metaphysics. Handoffs: D03, D06, D07, D08, D10, D11. Do not import donor cosmology, planar law, tribulation travel, astral travel, or realm metaphysics.

### Hazardous / Anomalous Zone

Used for radiation-like zones, corrupted regions, magical storms, hostile biomes, broken reality spaces, battlefield aftermath, cursed terrain, unstable ruins, and environmental anomaly zones. Movement logic: exposure management, hazard pulses, safe path discovery, protective posture, depletion, sensor/ritual distortion, environmental PCR, escalating instability. Handoffs: D03, D07, D09, D10, D12, D16. D14 must not define final exposure math or damage families.

### Pursuit / Evasion

Used when travel occurs under chase, escape, hunt, tailing, interception, or tracking pressure. Movement logic: distance/position pressure, route choice under time, concealment, speed versus safety, tracking signs, ambush reversal, transition into active chase cadence. Handoffs: D02, D05, D07, D10, D12, D16. D14 handles route/exploration under pursuit; D12 owns active chase cadence.

## Site Entry

Site Entry is the transition from route/area movement into a bounded location, structure, region, platform, node, or scene space. It may trigger known-state review, entry posture, map-state update, hazard scan, ambush exposure, D12 transition, D10 pressure, D11 presentation, or source-local procedure.

Site Entry is not automatic boxed-text reveal. It does not reveal all room facts, trigger every trap, or start an encounter unless owner-file state, posture, route pressure, scenario design, or source-local procedure supports it.

## Watches

A Watch is a posture or role assignment for monitoring danger, route-state, environment, or hidden pressure during a travel/exploration interval. Watch types include guard, scout, sensor, ritual, social, rear, platform systems, faction/surveillance, and source-local watch. It is not a fixed universal shift system.

## Ambush Exposure

Ambush Exposure is the risk that movement, posture, route, visibility, opposition, or hidden pressure creates an opportunity for hostile or hazardous action before the party controls the scene. It is not automatic surprise. D12 handles sequencing transition; D02 handles uncertainty.

# D14-06 — Source-Local Travel, Donor Exploration Mapping, Quarantine, and Escalation

Status: doctrine-draft / Phase 1 native doctrine continuation  
Version: `0.1.0-d14-generated`  
Generated: 2026-06-02  
Layer: D14 operational procedure doctrine  
Owner: Astra doctrine architecture / exploration and discovery procedure layer

## Purpose

This file defines how Astra converts donor travel, exploration, navigation, map, encounter, watch, pursuit, route, survival, space-travel, realm-travel, and site-entry systems without allowing donor procedure to become universal Astra law.

## Functional donor exploration mapping ladder

```text
1. Identify the donor procedure's function.
2. Identify the spatial model it assumes.
3. Identify the timing model it assumes.
4. Identify what state it changes or reveals.
5. Identify what risks, resources, encounters, discoveries, or transitions it controls.
6. Identify which Astra owner files are affected.
7. Decide lawful handling: direct mapping, normalized mapping, source-local retention, quarantine, or escalation.
8. Record rejected donor assumptions.
```

The donor label is never enough.

## Direct mapping

Use direct mapping only when the donor procedure already fits D14 without importing donor-specific spatial, timing, resource, encounter, or metaphysical assumptions.

## Normalized mapping examples

A hexcrawl route becomes functional route/area exploration, not universal hex law. A dungeon turn becomes a source-local or normalized interval, not Astra's default exploration clock. A random encounter table becomes source-local table pressure or owner-file-supported encounter pressure, not automatic travel law. A watch shift becomes a watch role, not fixed timekeeping. A space jump becomes route/vector travel with D09/D17/D10 handoffs, not universal fuel or jump law. A planar crossing becomes dimensional/threshold exploration with PCR and source-local metaphysics control, not Astra cosmology.

## Source-local retention

Use source-local retention when a donor travel/exploration procedure is useful inside a converted source but unsafe to generalize: a dungeon's torch-and-wandering-monster procedure, keyed hex map, named realm's gate sequence, local city patrol table, source-specific ship-jump route, desert survival procedure, haunted village escalation table, solo oracle site-generation table, or sect territory crossing law.

The retained procedure must have a boundary and cannot become general Astra exploration doctrine through polish, repetition, or familiarity.

## Quarantine triggers

Quarantine when safe mapping is blocked by extraction damage, missing map/diagram sidecar, unclear keyed relation, unsupported table structure, embedded action economy, unsupported supply/encumbrance/resource law, unsupported vehicle/ship/platform procedure, unsupported realm/cosmology assumption, unsupported faction/law/patrol procedure, unclear hidden-state boundary, unclear source-local boundary, unclear mutation, missing D16 hazard/opposition support, missing D17 supply/economy support, or missing D18 campaign-travel support.

## Escalation triggers

Escalate repeated or high-impact pressure for route-network doctrine, crewed navigation/sensor doctrine, threshold-access doctrine, controlled exploration-pressure intervals, surveillance/patrol-state procedure, map-state schema or visual-sidecar doctrine, procedural table governance, or long-horizon journey pacing.

## Rejected imports

Reject unsupported donor assumptions: hexes as universal space units, grids as universal exploration, dungeon turns as default cadence, random encounters as default pressure, wandering monsters as default site pressure, fixed travel pace tables, fixed watch shifts, automatic ration/supply attrition, foraging as universal procedure, weather tables as travel law, room-entry reveal assumptions, trap detection by default passive rule, player-authored map facts as world truth, space fuel/jump/sensor rules, ship turns, planar or realm cosmology, cultivation territory travel law, urban heat clocks, and source-local route tables as general doctrine.

## Clock-like and table-like systems

Clock-like and table-like systems route by function to D14, D10, D12, D13, D15, D16, D17, D18, source-local retention, quarantine, or escalation. A table is a procedure shape, not authority.

D14 is integration-ready only if travel/exploration intent, posture, route/area, known-state review, environmental PCR, movement intervals, discovery, map-state updates, pressure, site entry, watch/ambush exposure, source-local boundaries, and owner-file handoffs are all explicit when relevant.

D02 owns uncertainty resolution. D03 owns resource pools and overdraw. D05 owns methods. D06 owns Techniques/domains. D07 owns harm/exposure. D08 owns actor substrate. ## Source-local boundary checklist

A source-local exploration system requires source/conversion boundary, retained procedure purpose, preserved elements, stripped/rejected elements, owner-file handoffs, hidden-state protections, non-generalization statement, and canon-review requirement before broader reuse.

## Quarantine checklist

Quarantine when extraction damage blocks map/procedure/table understanding; map or sidecar is missing; route relation cannot be recovered; donor timing/spatial/resource/encounter assumptions cannot be stripped; hidden-state or source-local boundary is unclear; procedure depends on missing D16/D17/D18 doctrine; or state mutation is unclear.

## Escalation checklist

Escalate repeated or high-impact pressure for route-network doctrine, crewed navigation/sensor doctrine, threshold-access doctrine, controlled exploration-pressure intervals, surveillance/patrol-state procedure, map-state schema or visual-sidecar doctrine, procedural table governance, or long-horizon journey pacing.

## Anti-drift rules

Do not universalize hexes, grids, nodes, zones, or maps. Do not make random encounters default travel pressure. Do not make dungeon turns, watch shifts, or travel days universal timing law. Do not treat travel posture as a generic bonus. Do not treat party-facing maps as actual world truth. Do not reveal protected hidden truth through scouting, mapping, sensors, tracking, or ritual sensing. Do not import donor realm, planar, space, ship, platform, fuel, or jump metaphysics. Do not make foraging, supply attrition, weather checks, or survival procedures universal. Do not let room-entry procedure automatically reveal all relevant facts. Do not make ambush exposure equivalent to automatic surprise. Do not let source-local exploration procedures become Astra law through repetition alone. Do not let D14 become D16 encounter construction, D17 supply/economy doctrine, or D18 campaign travel pacing. Do not treat D14 record shapes as final runtime or map schemas.

## DDR register

### DDR-D14-001 — Primary architecture

Decision: D14 uses Posture–Route–Discovery Architecture. Rationale: it supports many exploration families without adopting a donor travel system. Risk: it may be too generic; profiles and records mitigate this.

### DDR-D14-002 — Posture

Decision: travel posture is first-class and affects visibility, exposure, opportunity, blind spots, and handoffs, not generic bonuses.

### DDR-D14-003 — Route and map-state

Decision: routes are functional and party-facing map-state is distinct from actual truth.

### DDR-D14-004 — Discovery

Decision: D14 owns discovery opportunities/categories; D10 owns truth; D11 owns presentation.

### DDR-D14-005 — Intervals and pressure

Decision: D14 uses flexible intervals and pressure trigger handoffs. Random encounters are not default law.

### DDR-D14-006 — Context profiles

Decision: D14 uses Exploration Context Profiles, not separate full travel systems.

### DDR-D14-007 — Donor exploration mapping

Decision: donor systems map by function, not label.

### DDR-D14-008 — Records

Decision: D14 includes lightweight not-final-schema records for conversion and future runtime handoff.

## Acceptance criteria

D14 is acceptable when it can create travel/exploration procedure from movement intent; classify posture without generic bonuses; handle route and area declarations; distinguish party-facing known-state from actual truth; use environmental PCR without taking D02/D10/D11 authority; resolve intervals without universal turns; support scouting, mapping, discovery, and map-state without truth leakage; support urban, wilderness, site/interior, space/platform, dimensional/realm, hazardous/anomalous, and pursuit/evasion contexts; handle site entry as transition; handle watch and ambush exposure without automatic surprise; classify pressure by family and route it to owner files; permit random tables only when supported; map donor procedures by function; preserve bounded source-local systems; quarantine unsupported systems; escalate repeated/high-impact gaps; and route consequences without stealing ownership.
