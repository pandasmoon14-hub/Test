## Purpose

This file defines how travel and exploration create discovery opportunities and map-state updates while preserving D10 world-state authority and D11 hidden-state presentation boundaries.

## Discovery Opportunity Model

A Discovery Opportunity is a lawful chance for exploration to surface party-facing information, route-state, map-state, hazard-state, site-state, or owner-file pressure. A discovery is not automatically actual world truth.

## Discovery sources

Discovery opportunities may arise from posture, navigation, scouting, mapping, tracking, survey/analysis, ritual sensing, sensor sweep, social blending, resource search, environmental PCR, interval outcomes, site approach, D10 unresolved pressure, source-local table/procedure, scenario design, failed or partial movement, hazard proximity, faction/opposition movement, or object/platform sensor interaction.

## Discovery categories

Categories include route_discovery, location_discovery, site_entry_discovery, hazard_discovery, resource_discovery, clue_or_lead_discovery, faction_presence_discovery, opposition_trace_discovery, environmental_condition_discovery, object_or_salvage_discovery, map_correction, false_route_exposure, hidden_boundary_hint, and source_local_discovery.

D14 classifies. D10 decides actual truth. D11 presents safely.

## Discovery confidence

Confidence states: confirmed, high_confidence, partial, ambiguous, rumored, suspected, false_or_compromised, protected_hidden, and source_local. Confirmed discovery requires owner-file support. A good roll does not reveal protected truth if the truth is protected.

## Scouting

Scouting is a focused attempt to reduce uncertainty before full commitment. It may identify route safety, hazard signs, ambush risk, patrol patterns, site entrances, environmental PCR signals, resource signs, faction presence, object/salvage opportunities, map errors, hidden boundary hints, or need for further investigation.

Scouting does not automatically reveal all hidden truth. It may produce partial, ambiguous, or false information if the route is protected, distorted, deceptive, source-local, or beyond the actor's method.

## Mapping

Mapping converts movement and discovery into party-facing spatial or relational knowledge. D14 supports relational, route-based, zone-based, node-based, coordinate-based, platform-based, and source-local maps. Hexes and grids are optional/source-local forms, not universal law.

Map-state categories: known, surveyed, partially_mapped, suspected, rumored, false, compromised, hidden, blocked, unreachable, and source_local. A map-state update may improve, degrade, contradict, confirm, invalidate, or complicate party knowledge.

## Three-layer boundary

```text
actual truth — owned by D10
party-facing known-state/map-state — procedurally updated by D14
presentation — owned by D11
```

Hidden Pneuma, hidden cosmic modifiers, concealed factions, secret routes, GM-facing map facts, source-local secrets, and unrevealed world-state remain protected unless lawful procedure and D11 presentation permit surfacing.

## Anti-drift rules

Do not reduce discovery to perception checks. Do not let maps become truth. Do not let procedural tables generate canon truth without owner support. Do not let player-authored discoveries bypass D10. Do not leak hidden state through scouting, sensors, or ritual sensing.
