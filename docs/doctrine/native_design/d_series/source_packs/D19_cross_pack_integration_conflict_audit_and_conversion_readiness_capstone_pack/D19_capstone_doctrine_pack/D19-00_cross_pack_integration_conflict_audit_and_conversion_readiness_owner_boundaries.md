# D19-00 — Cross-Pack Integration, Conflict Audit, and Conversion-Readiness Owner Boundaries

> **Status:** Phase 1 capstone doctrine. This file is a control-layer artifact for Astra Ascension doctrine integration. It is not final sourcebook prose, not a live-play GM adapter, not a runtime/backend schema, and not a donor conversion output. Any record shapes in this pack are lightweight doctrine-facing / conversion-facing / audit-facing shapes only and may be replaced or formalized later by Batch C schema doctrine, Runtime Gate B, canon consolidation, or playtest calibration.


## Core question

How do D00–D18 operate as one coherent Astra doctrine system, with clear owner boundaries, lawful handoffs, source-local containment, quarantine/escalation discipline, record-shape governance, deferred-schema awareness, and conversion-readiness controls, without becoming a new megafile or inventing new doctrine that should belong to an owner pack?

D19 asks:

```text
Do the D00–D18 files agree?
Do their owner boundaries hold?
Do their handoffs work?
Do their record shapes align?
Do their lawful outcomes match?
Do their deferred questions have a known future home?
Can a mixed donor construct route through the stack without improvisation?
Can this doctrine stack survive 200–400 donor sources without collapse?
```

2. Cross-pack handoff matrix.

4. Lawful outcome consistency audit.
5. Deferred doctrine / schema / math ledger.
6. Mixed donor-routing stress tests.

## D19 anti-drift rules

- Do not let D19 become an ordinary doctrine domain.
- Do not let D19 overwrite owner-file doctrine.
- Do not solve deferred math, schemas, canon, runtime, or live-play behavior.
- Do not turn audit records into runtime schemas.
- Do not treat stress tests as conversions.
- Do not make repo integration a canon-promotion event.
- Do not let cross-pack clarity collapse lawful owner boundaries.

## Acceptance criteria

D19-00 is accepted if it clearly establishes that D19 is a capstone control pack, identifies what it owns, identifies what it must not own, defines its eight capstone functions, and prevents the capstone from becoming a megafile or new gameplay subsystem.
