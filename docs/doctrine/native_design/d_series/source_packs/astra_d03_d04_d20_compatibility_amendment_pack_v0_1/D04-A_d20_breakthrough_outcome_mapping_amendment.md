# D04-A D20 Breakthrough Outcome Mapping Amendment

Version: v0.1  
Status: Compatibility amendment  
Applies to: D04 Advancement, Breakthrough, Transformation, and Progression doctrine pack  
Reason: D02 changed to locked d20-first resolution with Astra five-state margin outcomes.

## Purpose

This amendment replaces the older D04 breakthrough mapping that referenced pre-final D02 lane names and older margin breakpoints.

This is not a regeneration of D04. It is a narrow compatibility override for D04 sections that mapped breakthrough results to obsolete D02 labels.

## Compatibility problem

D04 contains older D02 mapping language such as:

- `Critical Success`;
- `Exceptional Success`;
- `Clean Success`;
- `Partial Result`;
- `Mixed Result`;
- `Failure with Consequence`;
- `Severe Failure`;
- `Critical Failure`.

It also contains older margin breakpoints such as:

- `Margin +5`;
- `Margin 0 to +4`;
- `Margin -4 to -7`;
- `Margin -8 or worse`.

Those labels and breakpoints are superseded.

## Locked D02 outcome ladder

D04 must now read D02 results through this locked ladder:

| D02 outcome | Margin guide |
|---|---:|
| Ascendant | +10 or higher |
| Clear | 0 to +9 |
| Fractured | -1 to -4 |
| Receded | -5 to -9 |
| Sundered | -10 or lower |

D02 also defines:

- natural 20 as a critical opportunity, not automatic impossible success;
- natural 1 as a complication flag, not automatic failure.

## Replacement breakthrough mapping

Where D04 maps breakthrough, evolution, transformation, proof, advancement risk, threshold crossing, cultivation jump, class/path evolution, or discontinuous progression outcome to the older D02 lanes, replace that mapping with the table below.

| D02 result | D04 breakthrough interpretation |
|---|---|
| Natural 20, action vector lawful | Critical opportunity: may add a lawful breakthrough rider, stabilize the payload, reveal a rare option, soften danger, improve integration quality, or expose the next lawful requirement. Not automatic impossible success. |
| Ascendant (+10 or higher) | Exceptional lawful breakthrough: enhanced, reinforced, rare, defining, cleanly integrated, or strongly stabilized payload if proof, tier, route, resource, and identity posture permit. |
| Clear (0 to +9) | Clean breakthrough: intended payload lands; declared cost applies; normal integration and owner-file deltas occur. |
| Fractured (-1 to -4) | Partial or scarred breakthrough: useful result with complication, instability, extra cost, reduced payload, altered mark, drift, flaw, debt, or scar. |
| Receded (-5 to -9) | Failed or receding breakthrough: desired payload does not land cleanly; partial residue, blocked threshold, wasted catalyst, exposed flaw, instability, or consequence occurs. |
| Sundered (-10 or lower) | Severe breakthrough rupture: shatter, corruption, inversion, crippling, carrier burn, route damage, identity fracture, catastrophic instability, or lethal consequence only if the declared risk posture and owner files permit it. |
| Natural 1 | Complication flag: cannot produce the best clean result; may worsen cost, instability, misalignment, corruption risk, catalyst waste, route strain, public scandal, or integration flaw within the declared risk posture. Not automatic total failure if margin and doctrine do not support it. |

## D04-specific interpretation rules

### 1. Natural 20 cannot bypass advancement gates

A natural 20 does not ignore:

- missing proof;
- missing threshold eligibility;
- absent catalyst;
- incompatible route;
- invalid transformation vector;
- blocked attribute lattice;
- source-local hard prohibition;
- missing owner-file authorization.

If a breakthrough attempt is impossible, a natural 20 may reveal the lawful path, missing proof, required catalyst, safer timing, or hidden incompatibility.

### 2. Natural 1 cannot erase overwhelming preparation by itself

A natural 1 is a complication flag. If the final result and preparation still support success, the result may become:

- Clear with flaw;
- Fractured success;
- successful but costly integration;
- breakthrough with scar;
- breakthrough with instability;
- source-local complication.

It is not automatic total failure.

### 3. Fractured is the key transformation-risk band

Fractured should be the primary home for:

- success at cost;
- partial transformation;
- flawed evolution;
- scarred advancement;
- unstable new capacity;
- imperfect integration;
- social/legal/faction consequence;
- object/catalyst loss;
- route strain;
- hidden future pressure.

### 4. Sundered requires proportionality

Sundered can be severe, but D04 should not use it as arbitrary punishment.

Severe breakthrough consequences require support from:

- declared risk;
- unsafe method;
- unstable resource;
- source-local danger;
- D07 harm/corruption doctrine;
- D08 actor/form substrate doctrine;
- D09 object/catalyst doctrine;
- D10 public/world consequence doctrine.

## Source-local boundary

Donor breakthrough, evolution, class-up, level-up, bloodline awakening, cultivation tribulation, skill evolution, monster evolution, cybernetic humanity-loss, sanity mutation, and transformation subsystems may be:

- directly mapped to D04/D02;
- normalized into D04/D02 outcome grammar;
- retained source-locally;
- quarantined;
- escalated.

Do not import donor advancement result tables as universal Astra breakthrough rules.

## Acceptance criteria

D04 is compatible with locked D02 when:

1. old D02 lane names are no longer treated as active result categories;
2. old margin bands are replaced by the locked D02 five-state ladder;
3. natural 20 is critical opportunity, not impossible breakthrough override;
4. natural 1 is complication flag, not automatic total collapse;
5. Fractured, Receded, and Sundered are used with proportionality and declared-risk logic;
6. D04 continues to own advancement gates and breakthrough doctrine;
7. D02 continues to own resolution.
