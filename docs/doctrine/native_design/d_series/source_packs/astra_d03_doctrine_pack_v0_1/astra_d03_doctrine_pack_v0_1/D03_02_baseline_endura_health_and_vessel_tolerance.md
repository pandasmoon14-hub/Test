# Astra Ascension — D03-02 Baseline Endura, Health, and Vessel Tolerance

Status: design-draft / accepted architecture

File ID: D03-02  
Version: v0.1  
Date: 2026-05-29

---

## 1. Purpose

This file defines baseline embodiment and Vessel Power Economy tolerance.

It clarifies that Health and Endura are baseline systems for physically embodied entities and do not count against the standard three integrated Power Economy limit.

---

## 2. Core principle

Astra separates embodied survival, embodied exertion, and trained power integration.

A mundane actor can exist, travel, labor, hunt, fight, flee, rest, and overexert without possessing a non-baseline Power Economy. A character starting mundane is valid and should remain fully supported.

---

## 3. Health / Vital State

Health or Vital State represents bodily harm, survival condition, injury pressure, impairment, and death-risk interface.

D03 acknowledges Health/Vital State but does not fully own injury doctrine.

### D03 health boundaries

D03 may reference Health when:

- overdraw causes bodily harm;
- failed economy use causes injury;
- Endura depletion creates fatigue or collapse risk;
- expression inscription affects the Vessel;
- Sangua or Vitia routes spend or damage life state;
- recovery converts between resource states and bodily harm.

D03 must not define:

- final damage math;
- injury severity tables;
- death procedure;
- healing procedure;
- combat harm resolution;
- full condition taxonomy.

Those belong primarily to D07.

---

## 4. Endura

Endura is baseline embodied exertion.

Endura covers:

- travel fatigue;
- labor;
- carrying burden;
- overexertion;
- endurance pressure;
- chase/stress movement;
- mundane combat exertion;
- physical recovery buffer;
- breath and stamina interaction;
- environmental exhaustion;
- long-duration effort.

Endura is present in physically embodied entities by default unless the entity lacks a normal physical Vessel.

Endura does not count against the three integrated non-baseline Power Economy tolerance.

---

## 5. Entities without physical Vessels

Entities without standard physical Vessels may not have Endura in the same form.

Examples:

- spirits;
- echoes;
- ghosts;
- artificial intelligences;
- distributed networks;
- summoned constructs;
- platform minds;
- dream entities;
- domain-bound intelligences.

These entities may later use equivalents such as:

- cohesion;
- manifestation stability;
- anchor integrity;
- signal coherence;
- phantasmal persistence;
- platform integrity;
- network load;
- domain tether.

D08 owns the actor/entity-specific model. D03 only records that Endura is baseline for physically embodied actors and that nonphysical equivalents require later doctrine.

---

## 6. Mundane starts

A character may start mundane.

A mundane embodied character has:

- Health/Vital State;
- Endura;
- mundane competencies, professions, knowledge, equipment, relationships, and world-state hooks;
- no non-baseline Power Reservoir unless later unlocked.

Mundane characters are not locked out of later power development. They may later gain Power Economy access through:

- training;
- teacher/apprenticeship;
- relic;
- exposure;
- pact or entity contact;
- crisis awakening;
- observation;
- copying;
- theft;
- experimentation;
- research;
- faction support;
- domain discovery;
- inscription craft;
- breakthrough;
- source-local event.

---

## 7. Power Economy access states

D03 distinguishes access from integration.

| State | Meaning | Counts against three-economy limit? |
|---|---|---|
| Baseline | Health/Endura embodiment systems. | No |
| External access | Actor can interact with economy through relic, tool, site, ally, network, ritual, or temporary source. | No, unless internalized |
| Temporary channel | Actor can channel an economy for a limited event or context. | Usually no |
| Studied route | Actor understands enough to pursue access but cannot yet use reliably. | No |
| Integrated economy | Economy is stabilized inside the Vessel/build. | Yes |
| Fused economy | Two or more economies form a confluence Expression. | Depends on integrated roles |
| Overlimit economy | Economy is integrated beyond tolerance or poorly stabilized. | Yes, with pressure |

---

## 8. Soft three-economy Vessel limit

A standard Vessel can stably integrate up to three non-baseline Power Economies.

The default limit applies only to integrated economies, not to Endura, Health, external access, or purely theoretical knowledge.

### Accepted rule

- External access does not count unless internalized.
- Exceeding the Vessel limit is allowed.
- Exceeding the limit creates overlimit pressure unless stabilized.
- The standalone temporal economy is removed.
- No economy is antagonist-only.

---

## 9. Overlimit pressure

Integrating a fourth or additional non-baseline economy without adequate support creates overlimit pressure.

Possible overlimit effects:

- adverse roll posture on economy use;
- increased cost;
- increased backlash chance;
- fusion instability;
- threshold pressure;
- Vitia contamination;
- attribute strain;
- identity drift;
- economy interference;
- forced economy dormancy;
- degraded recovery;
- external trace;
- Vessel fracture;
- requirement for a stabilizer economy;
- requirement for relic, implant, oath, anchor, domain site, or path architecture.

Overlimit pressure is not automatic denial. It is a cost/risk state.

---

## 10. Upgrading Vessel tolerance

A Vessel may increase stable economy tolerance through:

- Vessel breakthrough;
- Integrity breakthrough;
- Animus breakthrough;
- specific path architecture;
- domain stabilization;
- Scrypta containment;
- Isotra harmonization;
- Aethra anchoring;
- Reticula external support;
- relic or implant scaffolding;
- long-term training;
- successful controlled fusion;
- rare source-local transformation.

D04 and D06 will define the advancement and access mechanics.

---

## 11. Degrading Vessel tolerance

A Vessel may lose economy tolerance through:

- Vitia contamination;
- failed fusion;
- severe backlash;
- critical failure during overlimit use;
- identity fracture;
- Animus injury;
- Vessel trauma;
- hostile domain exposure;
- severance ritual;
- overload;
- corrupted breakthrough;
- repeated misuse;
- incompatible economy stacking;
- expression slot collapse.

---

## 12. Conversion implications

Donor systems that treat stamina, HP, mana, fatigue, or class resources as one system should be decomposed.

- Health-like constructs route to D07/D03 interface.
- Stamina/endurance/fatigue route to Endura if baseline exertion.
- Supernatural resource systems route to non-baseline Power Economies.
- External relic/site/faction power does not automatically become internal integration.
- Donor multiclass/resource stacking does not bypass the three-economy tolerance model.
