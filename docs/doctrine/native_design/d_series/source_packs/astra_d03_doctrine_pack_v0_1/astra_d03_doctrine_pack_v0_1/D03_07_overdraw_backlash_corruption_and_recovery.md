## 1. Purpose

This file defines D03's skeleton for overdraw, overlimit pressure, instability, backlash, corruption/Vitia, and recovery.

Exact numerical thresholds and tables are deferred. This file exists to preserve ownership boundaries and ensure D03 does not leave cost consequences vague.

---

## 2. Core principle

Power costs something.

Astra allows characters to push beyond safe limits, but pushing converts cost into risk, instability, backlash, corruption, injury, carrier damage, trace, threshold pressure, or world consequence.

---

## 3. Cost channels

A cost channel is where the price of an action lands.

| Channel | Function |
|---|---|
| Spend | Resource/partition consumed immediately. |
| Reserve / occupancy | Capacity locked while effect is maintained. |
| Strain | Temporary pressure on body, mind, tool, platform, relationship, or domain. |
| Instability | Dangerous accumulated load from power use, overreach, corruption, resonance, or backlash. |
| Backlash | Triggered consequence from failed, overcharged, hostile, or critical use. |
| Exposure / trace | Actor becomes visible, trackable, suspicious, or easier to target. |
| Injury / condition | Cost lands on Health/Vital State or condition. D07 details this. |
| Debt / obligation | Cost deferred into social, factional, spiritual, institutional, or pact pressure. |
| Material / tool strain | Cost lands on items, tools, weapons, relics, vehicles, implants, platforms. D09 details this. |
| Threshold pressure | Action pressures an attribute/breakthrough/advancement track. D04 details this. |
| Opportunity cost | Time, route, position, clue, market chance, faction window, tempo lost. |
| Carrier damage | Expression slot/carrier is strained, degraded, sealed, corrupted, or transformed. |

---

## 4. Overdraw

Overdraw occurs when an actor attempts to pay more than a safe available partition, carrier, economy, or reservoir can support.

Overdraw may be voluntary or forced.

Overdraw can produce:

- increased effect;
- improved posture;
- expanded scope;
- emergency activation;
- preserved Expression under pressure;
- temporary access to unaffordable cost;
- severe instability or backlash.

Overdraw must never be free.

---

## 5. Overlimit pressure

Overlimit pressure occurs when a Vessel integrates more non-baseline Power Economies than its current tolerance supports.

Overlimit differs from overdraw:

- overdraw is spending beyond safe capacity;
- overlimit is structural integration beyond safe tolerance.

Overlimit may cause:

- economy interference;
- partition leakage;
- slot degradation;
- adverse roll posture;
- higher cost floor;
- recovery penalty;
- Vitia contamination;
- identity drift;
- threshold pressure;
- forced dormancy;
- unstable confluence;
- Vessel fracture.

---

## 6. Instability

Instability is accumulated dangerous load.

Instability is not the same as backlash or corruption.

- Instability is the warning/load state.
- Backlash is the triggered event.
- Corruption/Vitia is lasting alteration or contamination.

Instability sources:

- overdraw;
- overlimit integration;
- hostile Power Context Register;
- critical failure;
- unsafe Expression attempt;
- failed fusion;
- unstable Expression use;
- mismatched carrier;
- repeated risky replacement;
- incompatible economy interaction;
- Vitia contamination;
- environmental or domain pressure.

Instability may decay, discharge, stabilize, convert, or trigger backlash depending on recovery and context.

---

## 7. Backlash

Backlash is a triggered consequence.

Backlash may occur on:

- Critical Failure;
- Severe Failure / Consequence;
- Failure with Consequence if risk profile includes backlash;
- overdraw;
- unsafe Expression attempt;
- overlimit use;
- hostile domain interaction;
- failed recovery;
- failed expression replacement;
- failed fusion or re-authoring.

Backlash payloads may include:

- Health harm;
- Endura drain;
- partition burn;
- carrier strain;
- slot degradation;
- instability spike;
- Vitia contamination;
- false read;
- tool/relic damage;
- external attention;
- trace;
- threshold pressure;
- world-state reaction.

Exact backlash tables are deferred.

---

## 8. Corruption and Vitia

Vitia is the named Power Economy associated with corruptive/tainted/degenerative power.

Corruption is the broader lasting alteration state that may arise from Vitia or other sources.

Sources of corruption:

- Vitia use;
- Vacuus interaction;
- hostile domain exposure;
- failed overdraw;
- critical failure in forbidden expression;
- corrupted relic;
- entity contact;
- overlimit integration;
- repeated unsafe replacement;
- source-local contamination.

Corruption may affect:

- Vessel structure;
- Animus;
- Integrity;
- expression carriers;
- slot conditions;
- Power Economy compatibility;
- recovery;
- appearance;
- social/faction standing;
- Pneuma-adjacent hidden cosmic pressure if later doctrine allows.

D03 defines corruption interface; detailed corruption effects may require D04, D07, D10, and source-local doctrine.

## 9. Recovery categories

Recovery is contextual, not a universal full reset.

| Recovery type | Function |
|---|---|
| Scene recovery | Small refresh after immediate pressure ends. |
| Rest recovery | Larger refresh through sleep, meditation, downtime, maintenance, treatment. |
| Domain recovery | Recovery improved in aligned locations, planes, sanctums, workshops, networks, or social contexts. |
| Action recovery | Recovery earned by specific actions: breathing, stabilizing, repairing, grounding, bargaining, venting. |
| Milestone recovery | Refresh after objective, threshold, breakthrough, or arc event. |
| Cost-conversion recovery | Recover one lane by accepting pressure in another lane. |
| External recovery | Healing, repair, medicine, ritual, technology, allies, faction support, consumables. |
| Purification | Removes or reduces Vitia/corruption/contamination at cost. |
| Recalibration | Repairs partition, carrier, Scrypta, tool, relic, network, implant, or slot alignment. |
| Release/discharge | Safely vents built-up instability through controlled method. |

---

## 10. Recovery should preserve pressure

D03 rejects universal automatic full recovery as a default.

Recovery should preserve meaningful campaign consequences.

A character may recover Endura quickly but need downtime to repair carrier damage.

A partition may recover after rest but slot degradation may remain.

Instability may decay, but corruption may require purification, acceptance, transformation, or long-term consequence.

---

## 11. Cost conversion

Astra may allow one cost channel to be paid by another.

Examples:

- restore partition by accepting instability;
- sustain expression by burning Endura;
- avoid Health harm by damaging a carrier;
- reduce backlash by spending Isotra partition;
- stabilize overlimit economy by binding it to a relic;
- recover Volith through decisive triumph or emotional equilibrium;
- recover Calora by cooling or venting heat into environment;
- recover Reticula through network repair;
- recover Scrypta through recalibration.

Exact conversion rates are deferred.

---

## 12. Conversion implications

Donor stress, heat, corruption, fatigue, spell slots, cooldowns, charges, wounds, rage, ki, faith, luck, and similar resources should be mapped by function:

- spend;
- reserve;
- overdraw;
- instability;
- backlash;
- corruption;
- recovery;
- carrier damage;
- expression burden;
- source-local cost.

Donor recovery cycles are evidence only.
