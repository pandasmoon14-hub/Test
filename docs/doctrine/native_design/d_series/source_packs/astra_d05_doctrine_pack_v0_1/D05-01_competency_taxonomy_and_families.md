# D05-01 Competency Taxonomy and Families

**Astra Ascension Doctrine Pack:** D05 — Competency, Profession, Knowledge, Training, Research, and Synthesis  
**Version:** v0.1  
**Generated:** 2026-05-31  
**Status:** Design doctrine generated from accepted D05 decisions.  
**Scope rule:** D05 defines capability substrate. It does not authorize final Expression mechanics, Power Economy math, Path/Class/Dao identity, breakthrough outcomes, item evolution, companion evolution, faction authority, territory control, canon sourcebook text, runtime database schema, or live-play GM behavior.

## Design question

What types of competencies exist in Astra, how are they ranked, how are they grouped, and how does D05 prevent competency taxonomy from becoming either a tiny skill list or an unbounded class system?

## Competency

A Competency is any learned, practiced, studied, embodied, or socially transmitted capability that affects what an actor can perceive, understand, attempt, improve, teach, research, formalize, or synthesize. It can influence advantage, modifiers, cost, consequence, information yield, time, risk posture, action vectors, Development Pick targets, proof, research, Expression refinement, route pressure, and teaching authority. It does not automatically grant an Expression, Path/Class/Dao, Power Economy, title, faction rank, item ability, or supernatural authority.

## Skill

A Skill is a narrower practiced capability: Swordsmanship, Stealth, Tracking, Lockwork, Metallurgy, Herbalism, Surgery, Navigation, Diplomacy, Beast Handling, Formation Reading, Relic Appraisal. It answers what the actor can do reliably through practice. Skills affect checks directly when relevance is validated.

## Profession

A Profession is a life-pattern competency. It is broader than a skill because it includes repeated labor, tools, social function, routines, economic role, problem-solving habits, material familiarity, and proof generation.

Examples include Blacksmith, Fisher, Baker, Hunter, Farmer, Alchemist, Navigator, Archivist, Medic, Soldier, Scribe, Merchant, Engineer, Exorcist, Beastkeeper, Pilot, Cook, Teacher, Sailor, Miner, Mason, Cartographer.

A profession can generate proof records, social access, tool access, workspace access, project access, knowledge fields, profession-specific action vectors, route seeds, title seeds, Awakening vectors, research questions, Development Pick targets, apprenticeship relations, and standing within institutions. A profession is not automatically Path/Class/Dao. It may create route pressure that D04 and D06 later evaluate.

## Knowledge Field

A Knowledge Field is stored understanding. It may be practical, scholarly, occult, technical, cultural, linguistic, ecological, historical, tactical, medical, legal, or metaphysical.

Examples include Ancient Languages, Local Law, Monster Anatomy, Ship Systems, Planar Weather, Herbal Taxonomy, Cultivation Theory, Religious Doctrine, Faction History, Relic Inscriptions, Material Science, Trade Law, Machine Architecture, Spirit Taxonomy.

Knowledge supports identification, assessment, crafting, dialogue, hidden-mechanic discovery, research speed, route recognition, and false-assumption avoidance. Knowledge does not author world truth. It gives an actor a basis for memory, inference, comparison, interpretation, and action.

## Training Track / Project

A Training Track is a practice route for improving a competency or preparing a future capability. When meaningful enough to track, it becomes a Training Project. Training may produce rank progress, method seeds, specializations, proof, safer risk posture, access vectors, route pressure, and research questions.

## Research Track

A Research Track is a structured attempt to turn uncertainty into usable knowledge, theory, prototype, blueprint, recipe, method, candidate, or lawful action vector. It begins from a question or desired outcome and identifies current basis, missing basis, research mode, input needs, risk posture, output class, and owner-file handoff. It does not grant final powers directly.

## Mastery Rank

Mastery Rank is deeper command beyond ordinary reliability. It affects reliability, cost efficiency, teaching ability, information yield, reduced backlash, improvisation within a known domain, false-method recognition, synthesis support, method creation, proof weight, and route pressure. Mastery is not just a bigger modifier.

## Specialization

A Specialization is a narrow branch inside a competency: Half-swording, Pattern-Welded Blades, Battlefield Surgery, Storm Routes, Bloodline Relic Appraisal, Hostage Negotiation, Drone Swarms. It is stronger inside scope but does not replace parent competency.

## Method

A Method is a repeatable application of competency below Technique or Expression: Hammer Rhythm, Depth Reading, Silent Step Pattern, Three-Point Brace, Ash-Scent Lure, Breath Counting, Lens-and-Needle Inspection, Saltwater Line Casting. A Method can later become a training form, profession signature, Technique candidate, Expression candidate, route seed, research output, or teachable form.

## Adjacent structures not owned by D05

A Technique is a formalized capability that may be route-linked, trained, invoked, refined, or taught as a defined move or procedure. D06 generally owns Techniques, with D03 involved if cost/resource mechanics exist.

An Expression is a retained or activated power-capability governed by D03/D06. D05 may support Expression research, training, and candidates.

Path/Class/Dao is a formal route identity or advancement structure owned by D06. D05 may create route pressure.

## Competency families

D05 uses broad competency families as grouping and routing categories, not a final universal skill list.

- Bodily: athletics, lifting, climbing, swimming, balance, breath discipline, manual labor, endurance practice.
- Martial: weapons, unarmed fighting, defense, battlefield reading, tactics, styles, combat drills.
- Stealth / Mobility: concealment, infiltration, silent movement, escape, pursuit, parkour, evasion.
- Survival / Exploration: tracking, navigation, foraging, weather reading, wilderness craft, route finding.
- Craft / Trade: blacksmithing, cooking, tailoring, alchemy, carpentry, engineering, masonry, repair, farming, fishing, mining, brewing.
- Technical / Scientific: devices, machinery, computation, medicine, chemistry, ship systems, energy systems, platform diagnostics.
- Scholarly / Lore: history, languages, law, cosmology, archives, religion, genealogy, philosophy, taxonomy.
- Social / Cultural: persuasion, etiquette, performance, deception, negotiation, leadership, empathy, streetwise knowledge.
- Sensory / Investigative: perception, assessment, forensics, appraisal, pattern reading, hidden-state detection.
- Esoteric / Occult: relics, inscriptions, spirits, domains, Power Economy theory, ritual structures.
- Command / Strategic: leadership, logistics, formation, governance, war planning, crew coordination.
- Creative / Artistic: music, poetry, painting, design, architecture, dance, symbolic performance.
- Relational / Care: teaching, caregiving, animal handling, companion training, therapy, mentorship.
- Interface / Platform: mechs, ships, networks, drones, vehicles, AI systems, implants, platforms, exosystems.
- Source-local: campaign-specific, donor-specific, culture-specific, realm-specific, faction-specific, or conversion-local competencies.

## Dual rank model

Competencies use player-facing qualitative ranks with backend progress support. Working rank names are provisional.

| Rank | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Unfamiliar | No meaningful training. |
| Familiar | Basic exposure; can recognize simple cases. |
| Novice | Can perform under favorable conditions. |
| Practiced | Reliable at ordinary use. |
| Adept | Handles pressure and variation. |
| Expert | Solves difficult cases and can teach basics. |
| Master | Can improvise, refine, and train others deeply. |
| Grandmaster | Can create methods, influence routes, and generate rare proof. |
| Transcendent | Extreme/capstone-like competency state; rare and requires D04/D06 interaction if advancement-relevant. |

Transcendent competency is not Ascension, not Tier 10, and not a standalone route. It is a D05 record state for extreme mastery pressure. D04 and D06 must validate it when it becomes advancement-relevant.

## Visibility states

Known, Latent, Discovered, Active Training, Dormant, Degraded, Source-local, and Hidden are valid visibility states. Hidden or latent states must surface through action, assessment, research, training, teacher feedback, consequence, or proof. They must not become arbitrary punishment.

## Guardrails

Competency families are not final character-sheet categories. Rank names are provisional. Professions are powerful but not classes. Knowledge does not author facts. Methods do not bypass Techniques or Expressions. D05 produces candidates and pressure, not final cross-system authorization.
