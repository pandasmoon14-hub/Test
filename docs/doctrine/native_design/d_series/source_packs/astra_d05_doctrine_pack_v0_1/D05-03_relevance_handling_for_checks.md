# D05-03 Relevance Handling for Checks

**Astra Ascension Doctrine Pack:** D05 — Competency, Profession, Knowledge, Training, Research, and Synthesis  
**Version:** v0.1  
**Generated:** 2026-05-31  
**Status:** Design doctrine generated from accepted D05 decisions.

It does not authorize final Expression mechanics, Power Economy math, Path/Class/Dao identity, breakthrough outcomes, item evolution, companion evolution, faction authority, territory control, canon sourcebook text, runtime database schema, or live-play GM behavior.

## Design question

How does Astra decide whether a competency affects a check, what kind of effect it provides, and how does the system prevent unlimited stacking while keeping professions, knowledge, preparation, and methods meaningful?

## Core relevance law

Competencies affect checks only through validated relevance. Relevance has type, strength, and effect. One competency may provide the primary roll posture benefit, while additional relevant competencies may provide secondary effects. Relevance can alter success chance, cost, consequence, information, access, proof, training, research, and route pressure. Invalid and harmful relevance must be possible. D02 owns final d20 math. D05 owns relevance classification.

## Categories

- **Direct:** central to the action. Examples: Blacksmithing for forge quality, Surgery for wounds, Navigation for route plotting, Swordsmanship for sword combat. May grant advantage, modifier, reduced cost, improved information, access vector, or proof.
- **Supporting:** helps one dimension but is not central. Examples: Metallurgy supporting relic appraisal, Hunting supporting tracking, Cooking supporting herb identification by smell. May grant a small modifier, narrow information, reduced time, or setup advantage.
- **Contextual:** relevant due to target, place, tool, culture, profession, history, or situation. Fisher knows knots, water, bait, patience; Baker knows ovens, heat gradients, fermentation, flour dust. May reveal hidden options, reduce risk, or generate proof.
- **Interpretive:** helps understand results rather than perform the action. Insight probes a relic; Blacksmithing interprets tempering and stress. May reveal tags, reduce false leads, or create research tracks.
- **Preparatory:** applied before the roll through setup, research, planning, tools, or practice. May lower difficulty, grant advantage, reduce cost/consequence, or improve risk posture.
- **Recovery:** reduces fallout after failure. Acrobatics reduces fall injury; Medicine reduces wound severity; Survival reduces exposure. It does not turn failure into success.
- **Access:** determines whether the action can be attempted safely or at all. Ancient Language allows reading inscriptions; Pilot allows platform operation; Relic Appraisal allows non-destructive inspection. If missing, classify as requires training, research, tools, unsafe, source-local, or blocked.
- **Harmful:** prior training misleads the actor. Mundane metallurgy fails on living stone; mundane medicine misdiagnoses a spiritual wound. Requires mismatch evidence and should not punish creativity arbitrarily.
- **Invalid:** claimed relevance does not apply. No benefit.

## Strength bands

| Strength | Meaning | Typical effect |
|---|---|---|
| None | No useful relationship. | No benefit. |
| Weak | Marginal relationship. | Hint or minor information. |
| Moderate | Helps one dimension. | Small modifier, cost/time reduction, information. |
| Strong | Central or highly applicable. | Advantage or meaningful modifier. |
| Decisive | Makes action possible or transforms result. | Access, strong output, safer posture, proof. |

## Primary roll posture limit

Only one competency may provide advantage, disadvantage cancellation, major modifier, or major difficulty adjustment for a single check. Other relevant competencies may provide secondary effects such as information expansion, hidden tag reveal, reduced cost, consequence reduction, misread reduction, research/project creation, proof generation, training progress, route pressure, safer posture, or material requirement discovery.

## Procedure

1. State attempted action.
2. Identify core demand.
3. Identify target and context.
4. Identify claimed competencies.
5. Classify each claim by type.
6. Assign strength.
7. Choose one primary roll posture effect.
8. Assign secondary effects.
9. Identify affected costs/consequences.
10. Identify proof/training/research/route-pressure output.
11. Commit record only if it matters long term.

## No arbitrary refusal

A relevance failure should produce a classification: invalid, insufficient, requires preparation, requires access, requires research, requires training, harmful due to mismatch, or blocked by owner file. It should not be used as arbitrary refusal.

## Examples

Two characters inspect a sword relic. A character with no relevant competency can inspect visible features but lacks deep vectors. A blacksmith with Metallurgy and Relic Appraisal may use Blacksmithing or Metallurgy as primary relevance. Relic Appraisal expands hidden tags. Hammer Rhythm may identify forged stress. Tool familiarity may reduce handling risk. Output might be abnormal forging pattern, material warning, research question, D09 handoff, or proof.

A mundane doctor treating a spiritual wound may have harmfully relevant Medicine if the wound structure does not match their assumptions. The result may be wrong treatment, delayed diagnosis, or research/consultation requirement. This reflects mismatch, not punishment.

A baker does not get universal wolf-killing bonuses. Baking becomes contextually relevant when ash, smoke, scent, heat, bait, hearth defense, and food behavior matter.

## Record threshold

Ordinary relevance support need not be durable. Create durable Relevance Records when relevance affects proof, training, research, route pressure, major consequence, action-vector legality, hidden-state interpretation, or future Development Pick eligibility.
