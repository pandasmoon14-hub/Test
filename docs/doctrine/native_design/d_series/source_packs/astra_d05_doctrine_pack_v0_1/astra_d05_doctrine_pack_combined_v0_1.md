# D05-00 Competency Architecture Overview

**Astra Ascension Doctrine Pack:** D05 — Competency, Profession, Knowledge, Training, Research, and Synthesis

**Status:** Design doctrine generated from accepted D05 decisions.

## Design question

What is a competency in Astra Ascension, what does D05 own, and how does the competency layer support checks, professions, research, training, proof, methods, synthesis, and future runtime state without becoming a hidden class or power system?

## Working answer

Astra uses a Layered Competency Architecture. Competency is the umbrella learned-capability substrate. It includes Skills, Professions, Knowledge Fields, Training Tracks, Research Tracks, Mastery Ranks, Specializations, Methods, Synthesis structures, and Teaching Authority.

Competency may affect what an actor can attempt; how a check is modified; what information a result produces; what costs or consequences change; what training paths are available; what research questions can be pursued; what methods can form; what proof can be generated; what route pressure can emerge; and what owner-file handoffs are required.

Competency does not authorize final powers or route identity.

## D05 ownership

D05 owns the learned-capability layer. It defines the difference between skill, profession, background, knowledge, method, training, research, mastery, specialization, synthesis, and teaching. It defines how these structures affect checks through relevance, how they improve through training, how they investigate unknowns through research, how mastery produces methods and route pressure, and how nonclass advancement tracks route to owner files.

## D05 non-ownership

D05 does not own final authorization for D02 roll math; D03 Power Economies, Expressions, carrier math, cost, recharge, burden, or confluence; D04 proof sufficiency, Development Pick validation, Awakening Packages, tier rewards, breakthrough outcomes, capstone, or Ascension Access; D06 Path/Class/Dao identity, formal Techniques, Domains, concept authority, branches, or route fusion; D07 harm/corruption/mutation/recovery; D08 companions, spirits, AI, summons, actor-state changes, and bonds; D09 items, relics, platforms, vehicles, tools, external anchors; or D10 factions, reputation, territory, law, and world-state.

D05 may create support records, proof evidence, research outputs, candidates, and pressure for these systems. It does not resolve them alone.

## File-level doctrine law

D05 defines what an actor can practice, know, train, research, specialize, synthesize, teach, and use as capability substrate. D05 may create proof, route pressure, method seeds, research outputs, and owner-file candidates. D05 does not authorize final Expressions, Path/Class/Dao identity, Power Economy mechanics, breakthrough outcomes, item evolution, companion evolution, faction rank, territory authority, or world-state changes.

## Relationship to D02

D02 owns action resolution and d20 outcome structure. D05 owns how competencies become relevant to checks. D05 may tell D02 that a competency is directly relevant, that a profession has contextual relevance, that a knowledge field improves interpretation, that a method creates access relevance, or that prior training is harmful in context. D05 does not reopen D02 roll margins or outcome names.

## Relationship to D03

D03 owns Power Economies and Expressions. D05 supports D03 by recording training that improves Power Economy handling, research that creates Expression candidates, synthesis that suggests confluence or re-authoring, and methods that become Expression candidates. D05 cannot finalize Expression mechanics.

## Relationship to D04

D04 owns proof, advancement gates, Development Picks, Awakening, breakthrough, capstone, and Ascension Access. D05 feeds D04 through profession proof, training proof, research proof, mastery proof, method proof, synthesis proof, route pressure, and advancement-track evidence. D05 cannot decide proof sufficiency or grant advancement eligibility alone.

## Relationship to D06

D06 owns Path/Class/Dao, formal Techniques, Domains, conceptual authority, route identity, and route fusion. D05 can create route pressure, Technique candidates, profession-route seeds, method-to-technique handoffs, mastery evidence, research evidence, and synthesis evidence. D05 cannot formalize Path/Class/Dao identity.

## Relationship to D07-D10

D05 records support for later systems. D07 receives harmful methods, corrupted training, mutation pressure, injury recovery training, and stability risks. D08 receives companion handling, teaching, care, bond training, AI interface support, and nonstandard actor learning. D09 receives tool use, craft training, relic research, platform operation, externalized methods, and item-bound handoffs. D10 receives profession standing, institutional training, faction education, reputation-adjacent proof, teaching lineages, and world-facing professional effects.

## Layered model

D05's internal structure is: Competency, Skill, Profession, Knowledge Field, Training Track/Project, Research Track, Mastery, Specialization, Method, Synthesis, and Teaching Authority. These are capability substrates and records, not automatic powers.

## Corpus-scale justification

Astra must absorb a large donor corpus containing skill lists, broad domains, backgrounds, professions, crafting skills, knowledge systems, training systems, research clocks, martial arts, spell creation, power customization, titles, faction ranks, pet bonds, item refinement, territory growth, cultivation organs, divine progressions, cybernetic synchronization, and platform tracks. D05 must not flatten all of these into skills. It must route them lawfully.

## Required downstream amendments

D03 must later incorporate competency-supported Expression research, Power Economy training tracks, carrier/reservoir advancement-track recognition, methods and synthesis as Expression candidates, and D05 Research Tracks as re-authoring inputs.

D04 must later incorporate D05 records as proof sources, D05-owned Advancement Tracks, Development Pick targeting of competencies/training/research/methods/mastery/synthesis, profession and research proof in Awakening, and mastery/research/synthesis proof in Capstone.

D06 should not begin until those passes occur or are explicitly deferred.

# D05-01 Competency Taxonomy and Families

**Astra Ascension Doctrine Pack:** D05 — Competency, Profession, Knowledge, Training, Research, and Synthesis  
**Version:** v0.1  
**Generated:** 2026-05-31  
**Status:** Design doctrine generated from accepted D05 decisions.  
**Scope rule:** D05 defines capability substrate. It does not authorize final Expression mechanics, Power Economy math, Path/Class/Dao identity, breakthrough outcomes, item evolution, companion evolution, faction authority, territory control, canon sourcebook text, runtime database schema, or live-play GM behavior.

## Design question

What types of competencies exist in Astra, how are they ranked, how are they grouped, and how does D05 prevent competency taxonomy from becoming either a tiny skill list or an unbounded class system?

## Competency

A Competency is any learned, practiced, studied, embodied, or socially transmitted capability that affects what an actor can perceive, understand, attempt, improve, teach, research, formalize, or synthesize. It can influence advantage, modifiers, cost, consequence, information yield, time, risk posture, action vectors, Development Pick targets, proof, research, Expression refinement, route pressure, and teaching authority. It does not automatically grant an Expression, Path/Class/Dao, Power Economy, title, faction rank, item ability, or supernatural authority.

## Skill

A Skill is a narrower practiced capability: Swordsmanship, Stealth, Tracking, Lockwork, Metallurgy, Herbalism, Surgery, Navigation, Diplomacy, Beast Handling, Formation Reading, Relic Appraisal. It answers what the actor can do reliably through practice. Skills affect checks directly when relevance is validated.

## Profession

A Profession is a life-pattern competency. It is broader than a skill because it includes repeated labor, tools, social function, routines, economic role, problem-solving habits, material familiarity, and proof generation.

Examples include Blacksmith, Fisher, Baker, Hunter, Farmer, Alchemist, Navigator, Archivist, Medic, Soldier, Scribe, Merchant, Engineer, Exorcist, Beastkeeper, Pilot, Cook, Teacher, Sailor, Miner, Mason, Cartographer.

A profession can generate proof records, social access, tool access, workspace access, project access, knowledge fields, profession-specific action vectors, route seeds, title seeds, Awakening vectors, research questions, Development Pick targets, apprenticeship relations, and standing within institutions. A profession is not automatically Path/Class/Dao. It may create route pressure that D04 and D06 later evaluate.

## Knowledge Field

A Knowledge Field is stored understanding. It may be practical, scholarly, occult, technical, cultural, linguistic, ecological, historical, tactical, medical, legal, or metaphysical.

Examples include Ancient Languages, Local Law, Monster Anatomy, Ship Systems, Planar Weather, Herbal Taxonomy, Cultivation Theory, Religious Doctrine, Faction History, Relic Inscriptions, Material Science, Trade Law, Machine Architecture, Spirit Taxonomy.

Knowledge supports identification, assessment, crafting, dialogue, hidden-mechanic discovery, research speed, route recognition, and false-assumption avoidance. Knowledge does not author world truth. It gives an actor a basis for memory, inference, comparison, interpretation, and action.

## Training Track / Project

A Training Track is a practice route for improving a competency or preparing a future capability. When meaningful enough to track, it becomes a Training Project. Training may produce rank progress, method seeds, specializations, proof, safer risk posture, access vectors, route pressure, and research questions.

## Research Track

A Research Track is a structured attempt to turn uncertainty into usable knowledge, theory, prototype, blueprint, recipe, method, candidate, or lawful action vector. It begins from a question or desired outcome and identifies current basis, missing basis, research mode, input needs, risk posture, output class, and owner-file handoff. It does not grant final powers directly.

## Mastery Rank

Mastery Rank is deeper command beyond ordinary reliability. It affects reliability, cost efficiency, teaching ability, information yield, reduced backlash, improvisation within a known domain, false-method recognition, synthesis support, method creation, proof weight, and route pressure. Mastery is not just a bigger modifier.

## Specialization

A Specialization is a narrow branch inside a competency: Half-swording, Pattern-Welded Blades, Battlefield Surgery, Storm Routes, Bloodline Relic Appraisal, Hostage Negotiation, Drone Swarms. It is stronger inside scope but does not replace parent competency.

## Method

A Method is a repeatable application of competency below Technique or Expression: Hammer Rhythm, Depth Reading, Silent Step Pattern, Three-Point Brace, Ash-Scent Lure, Breath Counting, Lens-and-Needle Inspection, Saltwater Line Casting. A Method can later become a training form, profession signature, Technique candidate, Expression candidate, route seed, research output, or teachable form.

## Adjacent structures not owned by D05

A Technique is a formalized capability that may be route-linked, trained, invoked, refined, or taught as a defined move or procedure. D06 generally owns Techniques, with D03 involved if cost/resource mechanics exist.

An Expression is a retained or activated power-capability governed by D03/D06. D05 may support Expression research, training, and candidates.

Path/Class/Dao is a formal route identity or advancement structure owned by D06. D05 may create route pressure.

## Competency families

D05 uses broad competency families as grouping and routing categories, not a final universal skill list.

- Bodily: athletics, lifting, climbing, swimming, balance, breath discipline, manual labor, endurance practice.
- Martial: weapons, unarmed fighting, defense, battlefield reading, tactics, styles, combat drills.
- Stealth / Mobility: concealment, infiltration, silent movement, escape, pursuit, parkour, evasion.
- Survival / Exploration: tracking, navigation, foraging, weather reading, wilderness craft, route finding.
- Craft / Trade: blacksmithing, cooking, tailoring, alchemy, carpentry, engineering, masonry, repair, farming, fishing, mining, brewing.
- Technical / Scientific: devices, machinery, computation, medicine, chemistry, ship systems, energy systems, platform diagnostics.
- Scholarly / Lore: history, languages, law, cosmology, archives, religion, genealogy, philosophy, taxonomy.
- Social / Cultural: persuasion, etiquette, performance, deception, negotiation, leadership, empathy, streetwise knowledge.
- Sensory / Investigative: perception, assessment, forensics, appraisal, pattern reading, hidden-state detection.
- Esoteric / Occult: relics, inscriptions, spirits, domains, Power Economy theory, ritual structures.
- Command / Strategic: leadership, logistics, formation, governance, war planning, crew coordination.
- Creative / Artistic: music, poetry, painting, design, architecture, dance, symbolic performance.
- Relational / Care: teaching, caregiving, animal handling, companion training, therapy, mentorship.
- Interface / Platform: mechs, ships, networks, drones, vehicles, AI systems, implants, platforms, exosystems.
- Source-local: campaign-specific, donor-specific, culture-specific, realm-specific, faction-specific, or conversion-local competencies.

## Dual rank model

Competencies use player-facing qualitative ranks with backend progress support. Working rank names are provisional.

| Rank | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Unfamiliar | No meaningful training. |
| Familiar | Basic exposure; can recognize simple cases. |
| Novice | Can perform under favorable conditions. |
| Practiced | Reliable at ordinary use. |
| Adept | Handles pressure and variation. |
| Expert | Solves difficult cases and can teach basics. |
| Master | Can improvise, refine, and train others deeply. |
| Grandmaster | Can create methods, influence routes, and generate rare proof. |
| Transcendent | Extreme/capstone-like competency state; rare and requires D04/D06 interaction if advancement-relevant. |

Transcendent competency is not Ascension, not Tier 10, and not a standalone route. It is a D05 record state for extreme mastery pressure. D04 and D06 must validate it when it becomes advancement-relevant.

## Visibility states

Known, Latent, Discovered, Active Training, Dormant, Degraded, Source-local, and Hidden are valid visibility states. Hidden or latent states must surface through action, assessment, research, training, teacher feedback, consequence, or proof. They must not become arbitrary punishment.

## Guardrails

Competency families are not final character-sheet categories. Rank names are provisional. Professions are powerful but not classes. Knowledge does not author facts. Methods do not bypass Techniques or Expressions. D05 produces candidates and pressure, not final cross-system authorization.

# D05-02 Boundary Rules and Owner Handoffs

**Astra Ascension Doctrine Pack:** D05 — Competency, Profession, Knowledge, Training, Research, and Synthesis  
**Version:** v0.1  
**Generated:** 2026-05-31  
**Status:** Design doctrine generated from accepted D05 decisions.  
**Scope rule:** D05 defines capability substrate. It does not authorize final Expression mechanics, Power Economy math, Path/Class/Dao identity, breakthrough outcomes, item evolution, companion evolution, faction authority, territory control, canon sourcebook text, runtime database schema, or live-play GM behavior.

## Design question

How does Astra distinguish Skills, Professions, Backgrounds, Knowledge Fields, Methods, Techniques, Expressions, Training Tracks, Research Tracks, Advancement Tracks, and Path/Class/Dao structures so donor packaging does not decide ownership?

## Core boundary law

Skills are narrow practiced capabilities. Professions are life-pattern competencies. Backgrounds are origin contexts that may seed competencies. Knowledge Fields support understanding and inference but do not author world truth. Methods are repeatable competency applications below Techniques. Techniques and Expressions hand off to D06/D03 when they gain formal activation, cost, effect, route, or power structure. Training improves known or discoverable capability. Research converts uncertainty into theory, prototype, blueprint, or lawful action vector. Competency may create route pressure but not route identity.

## Boundary rules

### Skill

A Skill is a bounded practiced capability. It may affect roll posture, modifiers, information yield, action-vector legality, cost, consequence, training eligibility, proof records, and method creation. It should not by itself grant route identity, supernatural effect, technique tree, or Path/Class/Dao package.

### Profession

A Profession is a practiced life pattern made of skills, tools, routines, social role, problem-solving habits, material familiarity, and proof generation. It can generate proof clusters, Awakening vectors, route pressure, Development Pick targets, tool access, recipe/project access, social standing, apprenticeships, methods, title seeds, and faction or market access. It becomes Path/Class/Dao pressure only when D04 proof and D06 route formalization support it.

### Background

A Background describes origin context. It may seed starting competencies, contacts, tools, debts, languages, reputation, social access, and proof seeds. It does not replace active competency. A character from a fishing village is not automatically a practiced fisher unless the record supports practice.

### Knowledge Field

A Knowledge Field is understanding, not fact authority. It supports identification, assessment, research, crafting, dialogue, interpretation, hidden-mechanic discovery, route recognition, and false-assumption avoidance. A character with ancient-law knowledge may interpret legal relics or infer institutional patterns; they cannot declare a matching empire exists unless D10 or source-local context supports it.

### Method

A Method is a repeatable application of competency. It can become a training form, research output, profession signature, route seed, Technique candidate, or Expression candidate. It is not automatically a Technique or Expression.

### Technique

A Technique is more formalized than a Method. Once it has defined activation, effect, cost, scope, risk, or route relation, it begins handing off to D06 and sometimes D03. D05 owns training support, not formal Technique authority.

### Expression

An Expression is a retained, activated, or formalized power-capability governed by D03/D06. D05 supports research, training, safe practice, known-but-not-inscribed understanding, refinement requirements, and lawful action-vector formation. It does not authorize final Expression mechanics.

### Training Track

A Training Track improves an existing or discoverable capability through practice. It may output competency progress, method refinement, proof, reliability, safer posture, Development Pick eligibility, rank increase, or teaching eligibility. It does not answer unknown questions; Research does.

### Research Track

A Research Track investigates something not yet fully known. It may output theory, blueprint, recipe, prototype, failed hypothesis, lawful action vector, warning, requirement list, route possibility, Expression candidate, project requirement, or source-local limitation. It does not automatically grant the desired result.

### Competency vs Advancement Track

A Competency is a learned-capability structure. An Advancement Track is any durable growth lane. Some tracks are D05-owned competencies; many route to D03, D04, D06, D07, D08, D09, D10, source-local handling, or escalation.

### Route pressure, not route identity

Competency can create route pressure but not route identity. D05 records the competency basis. D04 records proof. D06 formalizes route identity.

## Owner handoff table

| Construct function | Owner | D05 role |
|---|---|---|
| Practiced action | D05 | Skill/competency |
| Life-pattern vocation | D05 | Profession |
| Stored understanding | D05 | Knowledge Field |
| Practice improvement | D05 | Training Project |
| Unknown-to-structure work | D05 | Research Track |
| Repeatable competency application | D05 | Method |
| Formal move/procedure with route/cost/effect | D06/D03 | Candidate and training support |
| Retained/activated power | D03/D06 | Research/refinement support |
| Formal ascendant identity | D06 | Route pressure and candidate support |
| Proof/title/achievement/level/tier | D04 | Competency evidence |
| Energy/reservoir/carrier | D03 | Training/research support |
| Injury/corruption/mutation | D07 | Recovery/harmful evidence |
| Companion/pet/spirit/AI bond | D08 | Handling/care/training support |
| Item/relic/tool/platform growth | D09 | Use/craft/research support |
| Faction/reputation/territory | D10 | Profession/social evidence |

## Conversion tests

If donor material improves practiced ability, map to Skill or Competency. If it describes job, vocation, service history, trade, or life role, map to Profession or Background depending on active practice. If it describes origin context, map to Background. If it describes stored understanding, map to Knowledge Field. If it describes learning over time, map to Training Track. If it describes discovering formulas, methods, blueprints, or theories, map to Research Track. If it has activation, cost, effect, scope, resource, or power behavior, hand off to D03/D06. If it grants formal identity, archetype, class feature, domain authority, or path structure, hand off to D06. If it handles level, title, proof, milestone, or breakthrough, hand off to D04.

## Examples

Hammer Rhythm as D05 Method reduces cracking when forging difficult metal. Hammer Rhythm as D06 Technique candidate becomes a formal forge art. Hammer Rhythm as D03 Expression candidate uses an economy to alter metal behavior beyond ordinary craft.

Fisher is a Profession. Depth Reading is a Method. Impossible-line research may be a Research Track. Repeated proof creates D04 Awakening pressure. D06 may later formalize Far Fisher. No D05 record automatically creates the route.

# D05-03 Relevance Handling for Checks

**Astra Ascension Doctrine Pack:** D05 — Competency, Profession, Knowledge, Training, Research, and Synthesis  
**Version:** v0.1  
**Generated:** 2026-05-31  
**Status:** Design doctrine generated from accepted D05 decisions.  
**Scope rule:** D05 defines capability substrate. It does not authorize final Expression mechanics, Power Economy math, Path/Class/Dao identity, breakthrough outcomes, item evolution, companion evolution, faction authority, territory control, canon sourcebook text, runtime database schema, or live-play GM behavior.

## Design question

How does Astra decide whether a competency affects a check, what kind of effect it provides, and how does the system prevent unlimited stacking while keeping professions, knowledge, preparation, and methods meaningful?

## Core relevance law

Competencies affect checks only through validated relevance. Relevance has type, strength, and effect. One competency may provide the primary roll posture benefit, while additional relevant competencies may provide secondary effects. Relevance can alter success chance, cost, consequence, information, access, proof, training, research, and route pressure. Invalid and harmful relevance must be possible. D02 owns final d20 math. D05 owns relevance classification.

## Categories

- **Direct:** central to the action. Examples: Blacksmithing for forge quality, Surgery for wounds, Navigation for route plotting, Swordsmanship for sword combat. May grant advantage, modifier, reduced cost, improved information, access vector, or proof.
- **Supporting:** helps one dimension but is not central. Examples: Metallurgy supporting relic appraisal, Hunting supporting tracking, Cooking supporting herb identification by smell. May grant a small modifier, narrow information, reduced time, or setup advantage.
- **Contextual:** relevant due to target, place, tool, culture, profession, history, or situation. Fisher knows knots, water, bait, patience; Baker knows ovens, heat gradients, fermentation, flour dust. May reveal hidden options, reduce risk, or generate proof.
- **Interpretive:** helps understand results rather than perform the action. Insight probes a relic; Blacksmithing interprets tempering and stress. May reveal tags, reduce false leads, or create research tracks.
- **Preparatory:** applied before the roll through setup, research, planning, tools, or practice. May lower difficulty, grant advantage, reduce cost/consequence, or improve risk posture.
- **Recovery:** reduces fallout after failure. Acrobatics reduces fall injury; Medicine reduces wound severity; Survival reduces exposure. It does not turn failure into success.
- **Access:** determines whether the action can be attempted safely or at all. Ancient Language allows reading inscriptions; Pilot allows platform operation; Relic Appraisal allows non-destructive inspection. If missing, classify as requires training, research, tools, unsafe, source-local, or blocked.
- **Harmful:** prior training misleads the actor. Mundane metallurgy fails on living stone; mundane medicine misdiagnoses a spiritual wound. Requires mismatch evidence and should not punish creativity arbitrarily.
- **Invalid:** claimed relevance does not apply. No benefit.

## Strength bands

| Strength | Meaning | Typical effect |
|---|---|---|
| None | No useful relationship. | No benefit. |
| Weak | Marginal relationship. | Hint or minor information. |
| Moderate | Helps one dimension. | Small modifier, cost/time reduction, information. |
| Strong | Central or highly applicable. | Advantage or meaningful modifier. |
| Decisive | Makes action possible or transforms result. | Access, strong output, safer posture, proof. |

## Primary roll posture limit

Only one competency may provide advantage, disadvantage cancellation, major modifier, or major difficulty adjustment for a single check. Other relevant competencies may provide secondary effects such as information expansion, hidden tag reveal, reduced cost, consequence reduction, misread reduction, research/project creation, proof generation, training progress, route pressure, safer posture, or material requirement discovery.

## Procedure

1. State attempted action.
2. Identify core demand.
3. Identify target and context.
4. Identify claimed competencies.
5. Classify each claim by type.
6. Assign strength.
7. Choose one primary roll posture effect.
8. Assign secondary effects.
9. Identify affected costs/consequences.
10. Identify proof/training/research/route-pressure output.
11. Commit record only if it matters long term.

## No arbitrary refusal

A relevance failure should produce a classification: invalid, insufficient, requires preparation, requires access, requires research, requires training, harmful due to mismatch, or blocked by owner file. It should not be used as arbitrary refusal.

## Examples

Two characters inspect a sword relic. A character with no relevant competency can inspect visible features but lacks deep vectors. A blacksmith with Metallurgy and Relic Appraisal may use Blacksmithing or Metallurgy as primary relevance. Relic Appraisal expands hidden tags. Hammer Rhythm may identify forged stress. Tool familiarity may reduce handling risk. Output might be abnormal forging pattern, material warning, research question, D09 handoff, or proof.

A mundane doctor treating a spiritual wound may have harmfully relevant Medicine if the wound structure does not match their assumptions. The result may be wrong treatment, delayed diagnosis, or research/consultation requirement. This reflects mismatch, not punishment.

A baker does not get universal wolf-killing bonuses. Baking becomes contextually relevant when ash, smoke, scent, heat, bait, hearth defense, and food behavior matter.

## Record threshold

Ordinary relevance support need not be durable. Create durable Relevance Records when relevance affects proof, training, research, route pressure, major consequence, action-vector legality, hidden-state interpretation, or future Development Pick eligibility.

# D05-04 Training, Practice, Teachers, and Downtime

**Astra Ascension Doctrine Pack:** D05 — Competency, Profession, Knowledge, Training, Research, and Synthesis  
**Version:** v0.1  
**Generated:** 2026-05-31  
**Status:** Design doctrine generated from accepted D05 decisions.  
**Scope rule:** D05 defines capability substrate. It does not authorize final Expression mechanics, Power Economy math, Path/Class/Dao identity, breakthrough outcomes, item evolution, companion evolution, faction authority, territory control, canon sourcebook text, runtime database schema, or live-play GM behavior.

## Design question

How do Astra competencies improve through practice, field use, teachers, and downtime without becoming grind, automatic purchase, or teacher-vendor advancement?

## Core training law

Competency growth occurs through validated practice, feedback, time, pressure, tools, environment, instruction, and proof. Training Projects structure meaningful improvement. Low-rank growth may arise from ordinary use and modest downtime, but higher-rank, dangerous, specialization, method-forming, route-pressuring, or cross-system growth requires structured support. Teachers improve access, safety, feedback, and method transmission, but do not bypass proof. Downtime feeds concrete training targets. Training may progress, stall, deform, degrade, or transform into research or breakthrough pressure.

## Training Project Model

A Training Project is a structured effort to improve a known or discoverable capability through practice. It tracks target, current rank, target output, training mode, access basis, inputs, progress state, risk state, output, proof links, and owner handoff.

Training Projects are required for meaningful, high-rank, dangerous, specialization, method-forming, route-pressuring, degraded, source-local, or cross-system improvement. They are not required for trivial practice, low-stakes maintenance, flavor training, or ordinary minor use.

## Training modes

- **Self-practice:** low ranks, familiar skills, basic drills, maintenance. Slow at high rank and may reinforce bad habits.
- **Field practice:** meaningful use under consequence. Stronger proof, higher risk.
- **Guided instruction:** teacher, manual, school, AI, spirit, faction, simulation, or relic provides correction and access.
- **Apprenticeship:** long-term embedded professional or institutional learning. Strong for professions, tools, recipes, social standing, proof, and route pressure.
- **Pressure training:** deliberate difficulty, scarcity, opposition, exhaustion, instability, or environment pressure. Strong proof and danger.
- **Corrective training:** repairs degraded, harmful, outdated, blocked, corrupted, or mislearned competency.
- **Cross-training:** one competency trains through another. Produces supporting relevance, method seeds, or synthesis pressure, not full rank transfer.
- **Immersive training:** learning by living inside environment, faction, ship, realm, or culture. Produces contextual competency.
- **Simulation / controlled scenario:** sparring, drills, dreamspace, virtual systems, memory palaces, or constructed hazards. Safer but often less proof-rich.
- **Source-local training:** bounded to local culture, faction, realm, relic, institution, or campaign mechanism.

## Training outputs

Training can produce rank progress, rank increase, method seed, method refinement, specialization seed, specialization rank, proof record, route pressure, access vector, research question, risk reduction, cost reduction, harmful habit, degradation repair, and teacher authority.

## Access gates

Training may require exposure, teacher access, tool access, material access, safe environment, live pressure, knowledge basis, physical capacity, cognitive capacity, Power Economy access, route access, social access, or source-local access. Access gates should create projects, warnings, or alternatives rather than only denial.

## Teachers

Teachers may be people, factions, schools, manuals, AI tutors, spirits, relics, simulations, memory records, platforms, territories, or source-local entities.

Teacher levels: Example, Peer, Instructor, Expert Teacher, Master Teacher, Lineage Holder, Impossible Teacher. Teachers provide access, feedback, safety, correction, methods, and acceleration. They do not automatically grant ranks unless the target is trivial or already overprepared.

## Downtime

Downtime feeds Training Projects; it is not independent rank-purchase currency. A downtime declaration should have a target, mode, time, tools/materials, teacher/environment, risk state, and intended output.

Bad: “I train.” Good: “I spend downtime restoring Practiced Blacksmithing after forge injury using guild tools and master critique.”

## Rank advancement posture

| Improvement | Required posture |
|---|---|
| Unfamiliar -> Familiar | Exposure, basic instruction, simple practice. |
| Familiar -> Novice | Short training or repeated safe use. |
| Novice -> Practiced | Repetition, feedback, ordinary field use. |
| Practiced -> Adept | Deliberate training plus variation or pressure. |
| Adept -> Expert | Difficult problems, correction, proof, specialization. |
| Expert -> Master | Major proof, teaching, innovation, pressure, rare challenge. |
| Master -> Grandmaster | Defining achievement, method creation, route pressure, rare proof. |
| Grandmaster -> Transcendent | Capstone-like proof and D04/D06 interaction. |

## Failure states

Training can become stalled, mislearned, harmful habit, overstrained, degraded, corrupted, source-local locked, false mastery, breakthrough pressure, or research conversion.

## Relationship to D04, D03, D06

D05 records training evidence. D04 decides proof sufficiency. D03 validates energy, carrier, resource, and Expression effects. D06 validates route/Technique effects.

Practice in a yard is training progress. Defending villagers with the practiced cut is field proof. Teaching the cut is mastery proof. Creating a new method is method proof. Shaping life around it is route pressure.

## Design question

How does Astra handle research, invention, experimentation, theorycraft, reverse-engineering, and player-desired outcomes that are not currently legal actions?

## Core research law

Research begins with a question or desired outcome and identifies current basis, missing basis, research mode, inputs, risk posture, output class, and owner-file handoff. Research may produce theory, warning, prototype, method seed, lawful action vector, Expression candidate, Training Track, route pressure, proof, source-local result, quarantine, or escalation. Research creates lawful paths forward; it does not grant unsupported powers.

## No unsupported power declaration

A desired outcome may begin a Research Track, but it does not become a rollable action or playable mechanic until a lawful action vector and owner-file validation exist. If a character lacks a sound method, compulsion route, relevant Expression, relic, teacher, entity contact, proof, Power Economy basis, or route access, “I speak a word that kills or compels” is not a valid power roll. It may become research, training, access pursuit, dangerous temptation, or mundane action depending on context.

## Research Track anatomy

A Research Track should track research question, desired outcome, current lawful basis, missing basis, research mode, inputs, risk posture, output class, owner handoff, progress state, proof links, and validation state.

## Modes

- **Study:** books, teachers, archives, memory records, inscriptions, datasets, maps, codices, oral tradition.
- **Experimentation:** hypothesis testing; may create prototype, failed hypothesis, instability, method seed, risk discovery, proof.
- **Reverse-engineering:** existing relic, technique, inscription, machine, formation, creature ability, enemy method, or source-local system. Understanding is not copying rights.
- **Observation:** watching event, actor, environment, faction, domain, ritual, machine, or technique.
- **Prototype development:** preliminary method, tool, Expression, recipe, formation, protocol, or procedure.
- **Field testing:** real conditions; stronger proof and higher consequence.
- **Consultation:** teacher, expert, faction, spirit, AI, relic, archive, domain, source-local authority.
- **Simulation:** controlled scenario, dreamspace, virtual system, memory palace, lab model.
- **Meditation / Comprehension:** contemplation, breath, inner work, symbolic reasoning, spiritual practice, sustained attention.
- **Source-local research:** bounded mechanism; output stays source-local unless promoted.

## Outputs

Research may produce theory, requirement list, warning, failed hypothesis, prototype, method seed, blueprint, recipe, protocol, lawful action vector, Training Track, Expression candidate, Technique candidate, route pressure, proof record, hidden tag reveal, source-local retention, quarantine, escalation, dangerous temptation, or corrupted result.

## Gates

Basic research requires question, target, relevant competency, time, information source or testable phenomenon. Advanced research may require knowledge field, tools/lab/workshop/archive, materials, teacher/reference, safe test conditions, proof basis, project time, and access to target phenomenon. Dangerous research requires warning state, risk posture, containment plan, recovery plan, cost review, owner-file handoff, and explicit player acceptance when appropriate. Final playable outputs require owner validation, tier authorization, proof sufficiency if advancement-relevant, cost/burden validation, scope limits, safety/consequence record, and source-local/canon status.

## Desired-outcome classification

Classify unavailable desired outcomes as currently lawful, lawful with cost/risk, requires preparation, requires research, requires training, requires access, requires proof, requires owner validation, source-local only, dangerous temptation, blocked, or quarantined.

## Failure states

Research can become stalled, false theory, incomplete theory, dangerous prototype, contaminated result, source-local lock, misleading analogy, material failure, owner-file block, ethical/social obstacle, breakthrough pressure, or harm event. Failure should create state, not nothing.

## Proof

Research can generate proof of varying weight. Common texts and speculation are low proof. Difficult study, working prototype, and controlled risk are moderate proof. Field-testing under danger, solving a unique problem, surviving a dangerous experiment, or changing a profession/route is strong proof. Research changing identity, route, world-state, Power Economy relation, actor state, or capstone direction may be defining proof. D04 owns sufficiency.

## Example — Earthen Wisp to Wisp of Steel

The research question is whether an earth-material control Expression can be re-authored into a steel-pressure construct. Basis includes known Earthen Wisp, Mistra, Pressa, earth/material competency, metallurgy, proof/training. Missing basis may include confluence safety, steel behavior under Pressa, cost partition, carrier burden, and compatibility. Outputs may include theory, reduced-scope prototype, metallurgy training requirement, burden warning, D03 validation request, D04 proof after testing, and D06 technique pressure. D05 does not grant Wisp of Steel.

## Example — mundane seeker

A level 5 mundane fisher/farmer/hunter seeks ascendant possibility. D05 can open research into routes from practiced life, using profession proof, survival events, rumors, desire, consultation, observation, field testing, study. Outputs may include Awakening vector candidates, teacher lead, relic/site lead, profession transmutation project, proof targets, route pressure, and soft-cap warning.

## Owner validation

# D05-06 Mastery, Specialization, and Methods

**Astra Ascension Doctrine Pack:** D05 — Competency, Profession, Knowledge, Training, Research, and Synthesis  
**Version:** v0.1  
**Generated:** 2026-05-31  
**Status:** Design doctrine generated from accepted D05 decisions.  
**Scope rule:** D05 defines capability substrate. It does not authorize final Expression mechanics, Power Economy math, Path/Class/Dao identity, breakthrough outcomes, item evolution, companion evolution, faction authority, territory control, canon sourcebook text, runtime database schema, or live-play GM behavior.

## Design question

What happens when a competency deepens beyond ordinary use, and how does it become mastery, specialization, method, teachable form, route pressure, Technique candidate, or Expression candidate?

## Core mastery law

Mastery is deep competency that can create formalization options. It may produce specializations, methods, teachable forms, proof weight, research acceleration, teaching authority, route pressure, and D03/D06 handoff candidates. Specializations remain bounded sub-competencies. Methods exist as Seed, Practiced, Formal, or Teachable states and may degrade, mutate, corrupt, externalize, or remain source-local. D05 methods do not become Techniques, Expressions, or Path/Class/Dao structures without owner-file validation.

## Mastery and Grandmastery

Mastery is deep command of a competency such that the actor can adapt it under pressure, teach principles, diagnose failure, create refinements, and generate proof. A Master blacksmith identifies hidden forging patterns, diagnoses blade failure, creates specialized methods, teaches apprentices, reduces waste, preserves tools, recognizes impossible materials, and generates proof.

Grandmastery is where competency can become world-facing, route-pressuring, or structurally transformative. A Grandmaster may create new methods, found a school, define a style, create rare proof, create lineages, support Awakening or capstone proof, and generate D06 route pressure under D04 review.

Transcendent competency is a record state for extreme mastery pressure. D05 records it; D04/D06 validate it.

## Specialization

A Specialization is a narrow branch within a competency. It is stronger inside scope and does not replace parent competency. Examples include Half-swording, Living Metal, Planar Rivers, Spirit-Wound Triage, Void Routes, Criminal Mediation, Cursed Relics, Mech Docking. It may affect relevance, information, cost, consequence, training speed, method access, and proof quality inside its scope.

## Method states

| State | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Method Seed | Pattern emerging but not reliable. |
| Practiced Method | Actor can repeat under favorable conditions. |
| Formal Method | Defined trigger, use case, limits, risk, training path. |
| Teachable Method | Actor can transmit under proper conditions. |

Methods may be stable, strained, degraded, corrupted, mutated, obsolete, externalized, or source-local.

## Method creation threshold

A Method Record is created only when a pattern is repeated, trained, proof-bearing, teachable, risky, source-local, owner-handoff relevant, or mechanically distinct enough to matter beyond a single scene. Not every habit becomes a method.

## Method vs Technique vs Expression

A Method remains D05-owned when it is a practiced application of competency with ordinary or bounded effect. A Technique begins D06 handoff when it becomes a formal move, maneuver, art, route structure, or defined capability with progression implications. An Expression begins D03/D06 handoff when it becomes retained or activated power-capability with cost, carrier, burden, Power Economy use, inscription, or ascendant effect.

## Examples

Hammer Rhythm as D05 Method helps reduce cracking when forging difficult metal. As D06 Technique candidate it becomes formal forge art. As D03 Expression candidate it uses Zi, Mistra, Scrypta, Pressa, Calora, or another economy to alter metal beyond ordinary craft.

Depth Reading as D05 Method lets a Fisher interpret hidden motion, hazards, prey behavior, or unnatural currents. D06 becomes involved if it becomes a route action; D03 if it becomes powered by Tellura/Sympathis/Zi; D04 if it supports Far Fisher proof.

Ash-Scent Lure as D05 Method uses smoke, ash, fat, flour, hunger, and pack behavior to influence mundane pack movement. D08 becomes involved if creature influence/control begins; D06 if it becomes compulsion or route method; D03 if powered; D04 if tied to Wolfbane proof.

## Method formalization triggers

Repetition, pressure, teaching, research, specialization, proof, tool/anchor dependency, breakthrough, route pressure, Expression pressure, and source-local pressure can formalize methods.

## Mastery benefits

Mastery may produce reliability, relevance upgrade, cost efficiency, consequence control, information depth, method creation, specialization access, teaching authority, research acceleration, proof weight, and route pressure. It does not automatically grant free Expressions, unrestricted action declarations, automatic Path/Class/Dao identity, tier bypass, capstone effects, or universal advantage.

## Teaching authority

Teaching authority begins at high competency and improves through mastery, proof, method formalization, and transmission experience. It may require sufficient rank, communication ability, compatible learner, time, tools/environment, method clarity, proof of use, and source-local access. Not everyone who knows a competency can teach it well.

## Method record doctrine shape

A future method record should preserve method name, parent competencies, state, use case, conditions/tools, effect summary, limits, risks, relevance profile, proof/training/research links, handoff candidates, visibility state, and validation state. This is doctrine shape, not final schema.

# D05-07 Synthesis and Multi-Competency Interaction

**Astra Ascension Doctrine Pack:** D05 — Competency, Profession, Knowledge, Training, Research, and Synthesis  
**Version:** v0.1  
**Generated:** 2026-05-31  
**Status:** Design doctrine generated from accepted D05 decisions.  
**Scope rule:** D05 defines capability substrate. It does not authorize final Expression mechanics, Power Economy math, Path/Class/Dao identity, breakthrough outcomes, item evolution, companion evolution, faction authority, territory control, canon sourcebook text, runtime database schema, or live-play GM behavior.

## Design question

How should Astra handle multiple competencies interacting in one action, project, method, research track, profession pattern, or route-pressure event?

## Core synthesis law

Synthesis is a structured interaction between multiple capability sources. It does not stack roll bonuses. One source provides primary roll posture; other sources may provide secondary effects. Durable synthesis requires evidence, repetition, practice, research, proof, meaningful use, or validation. Synthesis may create methods, specializations, research tracks, proof clusters, teaching forms, action vectors, route pressure, Expression or Technique candidates, harmful states, source-local states, or owner-file handoffs. Any unusual synthesis is routed by function and owner-file impact rather than rejected for not appearing on a predefined list.

## Synthesis is not stacking

D05 restricts primary roll posture to one source. Synthesis is about structured outputs, not bonus accumulation. It should not become hidden stacking.

## Interaction types

- **Support synthesis:** multiple competencies support one action without durable method.
- **Interpretive synthesis:** competencies combine to understand something none could interpret alone.
- **Method synthesis:** repeated interaction creates a D05 Method.
- **Profession synthesis:** a profession absorbs knowledge/proof/method from another life-pattern.
- **Research synthesis:** competencies make a Research Track possible.
- **Tool synthesis:** competency becomes bound to tool, relic, platform, workshop, AI, or anchor.
- **Teaching synthesis:** mastered competency plus method becomes teachable.
- **Route-pressure synthesis:** competencies plus proof point toward route.
- **Expression-candidate synthesis:** competencies and methods plus Power Economy access create Expression candidate.
- **Harmful synthesis:** false analogies, incompatible assumptions, or unstable interaction create risk.

## States

Incidental, Emerging, Recorded, Practiced, Formalized, Teachable, Externalized, Unstable, Harmful, Source-local, Escalated.

## Durability gate

A synthesis becomes durable only when supported by functional interaction, evidence, repetition, practice, research, proof, meaningful use, pressure, teaching, or owner-file validation. A one-time clever description may create incidental support but not automatically create a Synthesis Record.

## Functional relationship test

Before recording synthesis, ask what components are interacting, whether the relationship is functional or decorative, what new output exists, whether it stays in D05, whether owner handoff is needed, whether it is stable/unstable/harmful/source-local/escalated, and what state delta should be recorded.

## Valid D05 outputs

Secondary relevance, improved information yield, reduced consequence, training progress, research question, research track, method seed, practiced method, specialization seed, proof record, profession signature, teaching form, route pressure, action vector, source-local note, owner-file handoff.

## Invalid final outputs without owner validation

Expression, Technique, Path/Class/Dao, Power Economy change, tier authorization, capstone transformation, item growth, companion growth, faction rank, territory authority, corruption purification.

## Anti-abuse rule

Synthesis is not automatic because two competencies sound related. Blacksmithing + Swordsmanship may synthesize around blade balance, maintenance, edge behavior, or anti-armor design. It does not automatically grant superior combat damage in every fight. Cooking + Alchemy may synthesize around extraction, fermentation, dosage, and heat control. It does not automatically create every potion recipe.

## Any-eventuality routing

For unusual synthesis, classify by function. What components interact? What changes? What output is proposed? Which owner file would break if mishandled? Does it create proof, training, research, method, specialization, or route pressure? Does it touch energy, route, harm, actor, item, faction, territory, world-state, or source-local rules? Which owner validates?

## Examples

Sword relic: Blacksmithing primary; Metallurgy and Relic Appraisal secondary; Hammer Rhythm interprets forged stress. Outputs may include interpretive synthesis, research question, proof, and D09 handoff.

Wisp of Steel: Earthen Wisp knowledge + Mistra + Pressa + Metallurgy creates research synthesis. D03 validates cost, partition, burden, confluence. D06 validates route technique if relevant.

Baker-wolf-slayer: Baking + smoke/ash + wolf proof + survival creates profession synthesis, Ash-Scent Lure method seed, proof cluster, route pressure, and title seed. D04/D06/D08/D03 handoffs may apply.

Pilot and AI platform: Platform Operation + AI Interface + Tactical Insight + Neural Link creates tool/platform synthesis. D09/D08 handoff; harmful synthesis possible if assumptions mismatch.

## Synthesis record doctrine shape

A Synthesis Record should preserve name, actor, state, components, interaction type, functional relationship, primary effect, secondary effects, limits, risks, proof/training/research outputs, owner handoffs, and validation state. Doctrine shape only; final schema deferred.

# D05-09 Records, UI, and Runtime Handoff

## Design question

What must Astra record so competencies, professions, training, research, methods, synthesis, and relevance remain usable without excessive bookkeeping or layer collapse?

## Core record law

D05 uses a tiered record model. Ordinary competency use may remain ephemeral. Competency interactions become durable when they affect long-term state, proof, training, research, methods, synthesis, visibility, route pressure, teaching authority, degradation, source-local status, Development Pick eligibility, or owner-file handoff. ## Record tiers

- **Ephemeral:** temporary check support; not normally preserved. Use for ordinary minor skill use, flavor-only support, failed relevance claims without consequence, trivial practice.
- **Session-relevant:** preserved during current play when it may matter soon. Use when relevance changed a roll, consequence was reduced, assessment revealed important information, training/research made progress, a method seed might be emerging, claimed competency was harmful, source-local competency appeared, or future proof may depend on it.
- **Durable:** actor/character state record. Required when long-term capability, proof, training, research, method, synthesis, visibility, route pressure, teaching, degradation, source-local status, Development Pick eligibility, or owner handoff changes.

## Durable thresholds
