# D08-09 Integration Checklists and DDR Register

## Purpose

This file is the control ledger for the D08 doctrine pack. It consolidates accepted decisions, validates owner boundaries, provides checklists, and lists risk safeguards that must be preserved during conversion, canon consolidation, and later runtime design.

## Accepted decision register

### D08-LASA — Layered Actor-State Architecture

D08 uses Layered Actor-State Architecture. Actor is the broadest technical term. Character, creature, companion, summon, spirit, AI, swarm, avatar, anchor-bound entity, possession-adjacent entity, and source-local entity are categories or states beneath Actor. Actor records use separable layers. Actor continuity is tracked separately from body, memory, route, and form-state. Varied agency/control states are supported. Companion status is a bond/relation interface. Not every actor uses full player-character advancement.

### D08-CPA — Continuity, Personhood, and Agency

D08 uses Layered Continuity Model. Continuity anchors include body, memory, identity, agency, soul/spirit, route, carrier, bond, social recognition, and source-local rule. Continuity states include continuous, altered, partitioned, copied, inhabited, replaced, severed, merged, fragmentary, dormant, source-local, and escalated. Personhood, cognition, agency/control, and playability grade are separate. Possession-adjacent states are supported.

### D08-BKF — Body, Kinform, Form-State, and Nonhuman Physiology

D08 uses Layered Body / Kinform / Form-State Model. Body, kinform, species, ancestry, and form-state are distinct. Broad body substrates are supported. Kinform does not determine culture, morality, route, profession, faction, language, social status, Power Economy, or advancement. Form-states and dual-state bodies are tracked when relevant. Source-local templates normalize by function.

### D08-COMP — Companions, Bonds, Mounts, Pets, and Familiars

Companion is a bonded actor interface. Ally, companion, summon, and item/anchor are distinct constructs that may overlap. Bond types, voluntariness, depth, agency/personhood, advancement posture, permissions, and limits are tracked. Source-local companion systems normalize by function.

### D08-BEAM — Summons, Constructs, Spirits, AI, and Bound Entities

D08 uses Bound Entity Actor Model. Summoned, constructed, AI, spirit, or bound entities become actors only when persistence, agency, personhood, bond, harm, memory, route relevance, or world consequence justifies it. Persistence, sustainment, dismissal, breach, duration, escape, anchor dependency, contract terms, and source-local limits are tracked when relevant. D08 actor-state remains separate from D09 and D10 ownership.

### D08-DIST — Swarms, Hives, Colonies, and Distributed Actors

D08 uses Distributed Actor Model. Distributed actors may be single collective actors, collectives with key nodes, many actors with shared links, companion swarms, platform networks, territory-embedded actors, source-local constructs, or escalated cases. Unit/node tracking is scalable. Cohesion is formal. Unit loss and cohesion harm are distinct from ordinary injury. Collective cognition, personhood, agency, and continuity are separate. D10 owns distributed groups as societies, factions, territories, settlements, populations, economies, and world-state structures.

### D08-EVO — Actor Advancement, Evolution, and Route Interface

D08 uses Governed Actor Advancement and Evolution Interface. Advancement postures include non-advancing, full, limited, track, evolutionary, bond, route-bearing, training, construct/upgrade, distributed growth, source-local, and escalated. Advancement, evolution, transformation, and conversion are distinct. D08 records actor-state outputs while owner files validate their domains. Evolution requires affordances and constraints.

### D08-SLC — Source-local Actor and Creature Conversion

D08 uses Functional Actor Conversion Routing. Donor actor taxonomy is decomposed by function. Every donor actor construct receives a lawful outcome. Donor ancestry/species/race packages, monsters, NPC templates, companions, summons, constructs, spirits, AI, swarms, undead, and transformation systems normalize by function. Source-local boundary records are required. High-risk donor assumptions are blocked.

### D08-RUNTIME — Runtime Records, UI, and Owner Handoffs

D08 uses Tiered Actor Record Model. Record depth grades include ephemeral entity, simplified actor, functional actor, persistent actor, major actor, companion-grade actor, route-bearing actor, source-local actor, and escalated actor. Record families are actor-state header, continuity/personhood/agency, body/kinform/form-state, companion/bond, summon/construct/bound entity, distributed actor, actor advancement/evolution, and source-local actor conversion. Durable records require threshold justification. Visibility states and UI grouping are supported. Actor records may promote, demote, retire, or retain history.

## D08 ownership checklist

D08 owns actor-state, actor categories, record depth, continuity, personhood, cognition, agency/control, possession-adjacent actor-state, body substrate, kinform, form-state, nonhuman physiology interface, companion/bond substrate, summon/construct/spirit/AI/bound entity actor-state, distributed actor structure, actor advancement posture, actor evolution outputs at actor-state layer, source-local actor conversion routing, runtime record families, and actor UI grouping guidance.

D08 does not own D03 Power Economy, D04 advancement proof, D05 training, D06 Routes, D07 harm, D09 items/platforms, D10 social/world-state, D11 narration, final database schema, or donor taxonomy as canon.

## Checklists

### Actor-state checklist

Ask whether the construct is actor, effect, item, hazard, faction, environment, route feature, source-local construct, or mixed; whether it needs persistence; whether it can act, receive harm, hold memory, bond, have personhood, advance/evolve, use body/form-state, require source-local boundary, require handoff, and what record depth is justified.

### Continuity / personhood / agency checklist

Assign continuity state and anchors. Check whether body, memory, identity, agency, soul/spirit, route, carrier, bond, and social recognition agree or conflict. Assign personhood, cognition, agency/control, possession-adjacent state, and playability/control grade.

### Body / kinform / form-state checklist

Identify substrate, kinform, species relevance, ancestry, form-state, physiology, senses, movement, maintenance, vulnerabilities, resistances, D03 carrier interface, D07 harm/recovery, D06 route access, D10 recognition, and source-local template.

### Companion / bond checklist

Distinguish ally, companion, summon, item/anchor, or overlap. Record bond type, voluntariness, depth grade, agency/personhood, permissions, limits, advancement, D07 harm, D03 shared power, D06 route integration, D09 gear/anchor, and D10 social recognition.

### Summon / construct / spirit / AI / bound entity checklist

Classify entity category, actor promotion, persistence, agency/control, personhood, sustainment, dismissal, breach, anchor destruction, escape/rebellion/negotiation/memory/bond potential, D09 ownership, D10 recognition, and source-local boundary.

### Swarm / hive / colony / distributed actor checklist

Classify distributed form, significant units/nodes, core/queen/command/memory/carrier/platform nodes, cohesion state, unit loss vs injury, memory-node damage, split/merge/copy/dissolution, personhood/cognition/agency, D10 social/territory ownership, D09 hardware, and D07 cohesion harm.

### Actor advancement / evolution checklist

Assign advancement posture, identify advancement/evolution/transformation/conversion, trigger, process phase, domain, scale, direction, reversibility, origin, actor-state outputs, affordances, constraints, and handoffs to D03/D04/D05/D06/D07/D09/D10.

### Source-local actor conversion checklist

Identify donor label, construct type, function, D08 actor-state parts, D03/D04/D05/D06/D07/D09/D10 parts, rejected assumptions, retained rules, prohibited generalizations, promotion requirements, and lawful outcome.

### Runtime / UI checklist

Assign record depth, record families, durable threshold, visibility state, UI group, simplification, promotion, demotion, retirement, history retention, owner handoff states, and source-local visibility.

## Pre-generation risk queue preserved

- D08 must not become universal entity owner.
- Every actor must not become a full character sheet.
- Minor actors must remain promotable.
- Body, kinform, ancestry, species, and culture must stay separated.
- Continuity cannot become body-only, memory-only, or soul-only.
- Personhood, cognition, and agency must not collapse.
- Companions must not become free power.
- Summons must not be flattened.
- Distributed actors must remain scalable.
- Evolution must not become pure upgrade.
- Source-local actor taxonomy must not leak into canon.
- D10 relational doctrine must not be solved inside D08.
