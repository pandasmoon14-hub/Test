## Purpose

D08 defines the actor-state substrate for Astra Ascension. It answers what an actor is, how actors persist, how bodies and forms are represented, how companions and bounded entities are treated, how distributed actors are modeled, how non-player actors can advance or evolve, and how donor actor systems are converted without importing donor taxonomy as canon.

Astra must absorb a corpus of roughly 200–400 donor sources. That corpus will include playable ancestries, bestiaries, monsters, companions, pets, mounts, familiars, summons, swarms, undead templates, construct rules, spirits, AI companions, intelligent items, symbiotes, possession systems, clones, reincarnation rules, avatars, transformations, body cultivation, bloodlines, monster evolution, and source-local creature taxonomies. D08 prevents these constructs from being forced into one humanoid sheet or fragmented into bespoke non-scaling subsystems.

## Core doctrine statement

An Actor is any persistent or system-relevant entity that can hold state, receive consequences, act, be targeted, bond, advance, remember, transform, be harmed, or participate in world-state. Actor is broader than character, creature, monster, companion, summon, AI, spirit, swarm, avatar, anchor-bound entity, possession-adjacent entity, or source-local entity. D08 records actor-state only at the depth required by persistence, relevance, agency, personhood, bond, harm, advancement, source-local pressure, and owner-file impact.

## Embedded risk safeguards

- D08 must not become the universal owner of every system attached to actors.
- Not every actor gets a full character sheet.
- Minor actors remain promotable if they become consequential.
- Body, kinform, ancestry, species, culture, morality, faction, route, and Power Economy remain separated.
- Continuity cannot be body-only, memory-only, or soul-only.
- Personhood, cognition, and agency cannot collapse into one intelligence rating.
- Companions must not become free power or unbounded extra action economy.
- Summons must not be flattened into disposable effects or full characters by default.
- Distributed actors must remain scalable.
- Evolution must not become pure upgrade.
- Source-local actor taxonomy must not leak into Astra canon.
- D10 relational doctrine is reserved for D10.

## Lawful conversion outcome

Every donor actor construct must receive one lawful outcome: direct Astra mapping, normalized Astra mapping, source-local retained construct, quarantined construct pending later doctrine, or escalated doctrine problem. No donor creature type, ancestry package, summon list, companion table, AI assumption, undead morality, vampire weakness profile, familiar rule, construct soullessness premise, or monster evolution ladder becomes Astra canon by default.
