## 4. Required definitions

Actor construct: doctrine-level placeholder for any entity that can hold actionable role posture in Astra without requiring runtime serialization.

Player-grade actor construct: doctrine-language boundary marker for actor classes eligible for player-grade decision pressure without defining runtime permission code.

Capability framework construct: doctrine-language representation of what categories of capability can be attached to actor classes without numeric actor sheets.

Companion/summon/factional/macro permission construct: doctrine-language boundary for whether and how non-primary actors may be treated as autonomous actors.

Escalated actor-ontology problem: unresolved actor-classification conflict forwarded for doctrine escalation rather than patched by canon invention.

## 5. Core doctrine rules

1. Ontology-first rule: actor claims must be routed through explicit actor-category posture before downstream asset/conflict interpretation.
2. Player-grade boundary rule: player-grade status is a doctrine boundary decision, not a donor default import.
3. Non-disposability rule: companions, summons, drones, spirits, and AI actors cannot be silently reduced to disposable property categories.
4. Macro-permission rule: macro-actors (factions, civilizations, worlds, planes) require explicit doctrine permission posture before use.
5. Qualitative-only rule: A11 expresses actor ontology qualitatively at doctrine scope and does not define runtime actor state.
6. No silent import rule: donor race/species/culture/background/lifepath frameworks cannot be silently adopted as Astra identity law.
7. Routing completeness rule: each actor-ontology pressure claim is mapped, source-local retained, quarantined, or escalated.
8. Contradiction visibility rule: unresolved actor-ontology contradictions remain explicit and auditable.

## 6. Conversion mapping rules

- Treat donor creature types, NPC classes, ancestry/lineage/heritage/race/species labels, spirit classes, AI/digital entities, drones/remotes, summons, companions, and faction-scale entities as actor constructs for routing before normalization.
- Classify each claim into actor category, player-grade boundary posture, capability framework posture, and persistence/transformation posture without introducing runtime state machinery.
- If donor pressure requires canon species/ancestry/culture/background lists, fixed bonuses, lifepath tables, career packages, homeworld benefits, or detailed stat-block taxonomies, retain evidence source-local and do not normalize those claims in A11.
- If actor claims collide with A03/A05 continuity/scale boundaries or A06/A07/A08/A10 adjacent authority boundaries, preserve source-local labels and escalate contradiction rather than redefining upstream doctrine.
- If classification cannot be completed without inventing runtime actor identity state or hidden state behavior, quarantine or escalate instead of normalizing.

## 7. Source-local handling

- Donor actor frameworks can be retained as source-local records.
- Source-local records preserve provenance and pressure but do not become Astra actor canon.
- Repeated donor pressure can form canon candidates only through later K-layer review.
- Unclassifiable actor claims are quarantined or escalated, not normalized by invention.
- Donor proper nouns and identity packages remain source-local unless later canon promotion accepts them.
