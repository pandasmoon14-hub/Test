## 4. Required definitions

Travel construct: doctrine-level placeholder for movement claims across local, planetary, orbital, stellar, void, planar, and dimensional contexts without runtime simulation or procedural execution.

Scale transition construct: doctrine-language boundary marker for movement across scale bands, with explicit precondition and consequence posture but without travel-time math, route simulation, or operation procedures.

Exploration construct: doctrine-level classification placeholder for discovery pressure and traversal pressure across ruins, megastructures, and unknown zones without importing donor procedures.

Escalated travel-transition problem: unresolved contradiction across travel/exploration scale posture forwarded for doctrine escalation rather than patched by canon invention.

## 5. Core doctrine rules

1. Classification-first rule: travel and exploration claims must be routed through explicit scale and transition posture before downstream schema or runtime interpretation.
2. Transition-boundary rule: scale changes must remain explicit through precondition and consequence posture, not donor shorthand.
3. Topology-nonownership rule: A14 cannot define cosmology or plane topology and must consume those boundaries from A01.
4. Procedure-nonimport rule: donor route, hexcrawl, encounter, jump-drive, teleportation, and operation procedures cannot be silently adopted as Astra law.
5. Qualitative-only rule: A14 expresses travel doctrine qualitatively at doctrine scope and does not define runtime travel machinery.
6. Routing completeness rule: each travel/exploration pressure claim is mapped, source-local retained, quarantined, or escalated.
7. Contradiction visibility rule: unresolved travel and exploration contradictions remain explicit and auditable.

## 6. Conversion mapping rules

- Treat donor travel, route, exploration, ruin, megastructure, gate, portal, jump, teleport, wilderness, and secret-realm claims as travel/exploration constructs for routing before normalization.
- Classify each claim into travel-scale posture, transition posture, route-stability posture, and exploration-pressure posture without introducing runtime travel state machinery.
- If donor pressure requires route maps, gate-network maps, portal inventories, travel time formulas, fuel/supply rules, navigation procedure, exploration procedure implementation, hexcrawl/random encounter procedure, starship operation procedure, jump-drive rules, teleportation rules, or logistics/economy defaults, retain evidence source-local and do not normalize those claims in A14.
- If travel or exploration claims collide with A01/A05/A12/A13 authority boundaries, preserve source-local labels and escalate contradiction rather than redefining adjacent doctrine.
- If classification cannot be completed without inventing runtime travel/location/route/hidden-information/event-commit behavior, quarantine or escalate instead of normalizing.

## 7. Source-local handling

- Donor jump-drive systems, teleportation systems, FTL systems, gate-network systems, portal systems, wilderness/hexcrawl systems, ruin-exploration systems, megastructure exploration systems, secret-realm traversal systems, and travel-procedure systems can be retained as source-local records.
- Source-local records preserve provenance and pressure but do not become Astra travel doctrine canon.
- Repeated donor pressure can form canon candidates only through later K-layer review.
- Unclassifiable travel and exploration claims are quarantined or escalated, not normalized by invention.
- Donor proper nouns and branded travel systems remain source-local unless later canon promotion accepts them.
- source-local travel, exploration, gate, portal, route, and transition terms do not become accepted lexicon terms through A14.
- source-local wording is boundary-only evidence handling and is never lexical promotion, canon promotion, or default-law adoption.
