## 4. Required definitions

Asset construct: doctrine-level placeholder for any equipment, relic, implant, cybernetic, biotech, or platform-bound capability carrier without runtime serialization.

Implant boundary construct: doctrine-language boundary marker separating embedded actor-linked assets from external or transferable assets without defining installation procedures.

Platform asset construct: doctrine-language boundary for macro-scale assets such as mechs, starships, stations, and dimensional anchors without defining operating procedures.

Escalated asset-system problem: unresolved asset-classification conflict forwarded for doctrine escalation rather than patched by canon invention.

## 5. Core doctrine rules

1. Classification-first rule: asset claims must be routed through explicit asset-category posture before downstream conflict or travel interpretation.
2. Attachment-boundary rule: actor-linked implants/cyberware/biotech must be classified by boundary posture, not donor defaults.
3. Non-silo rule: assets cannot enforce isolated magic-only, tech-only, or cultivation-only interpretation as Astra default law.
4. Platform-boundary rule: platform-scale assets require explicit boundary posture before use in adjacent doctrines.
5. Qualitative-only rule: A12 expresses asset doctrine qualitatively at doctrine scope and does not define runtime asset state.
6. No silent import rule: donor attunement, licensing, tech-level, and acquisition frameworks cannot be silently adopted as Astra law.
7. Routing completeness rule: each asset pressure claim is mapped, source-local retained, quarantined, or escalated.
8. Contradiction visibility rule: unresolved asset contradictions remain explicit and auditable.

## 6. Conversion mapping rules

- Treat donor gear, relic, artifact, implant, cyberware, biotech, mech, starship, drone, construct, station, cultivation tool, living weapon, and dimensional-anchor claims as asset constructs for routing before normalization.
- Classify each claim into taxonomy posture, attachment boundary posture, platform boundary posture, and advancement/bonding posture without introducing runtime state machinery.
- If donor pressure requires specific stats, module lists, pricing/economy rules, acquisition/licensing defaults, attunement limits, technology-level defaults, compatibility math, synergy bonuses, upgrade trees, or procedural installation/repair/crafting rules, retain evidence source-local and do not normalize those claims in A12.
- If asset claims collide with A02/A05/A10/A11 authority boundaries, preserve source-local labels and escalate contradiction rather than redefining adjacent doctrine.
- If classification cannot be completed without inventing runtime inventory, installation, or platform state behavior, quarantine or escalate instead of normalizing.

## 7. Source-local handling

- Donor magic-item systems, relic systems, artifact systems, cyberware systems, biotech systems, implant systems, mech/license systems, starship systems, vehicle systems, tool systems, component systems, living-asset systems, cultivation-artifact systems, and platform-module systems may be retained as source-local records.
- Source-local records preserve provenance and pressure but do not become Astra asset canon.
- Repeated donor pressure can form canon candidates only through later K-layer review.
- Unclassifiable asset claims are quarantined or escalated, not normalized by invention.
- Donor proper nouns and branded asset systems remain source-local unless later canon promotion accepts them.
- Source-local asset, relic, artifact, implant, cyberware, biotech, starship, mech, vehicle, drone, station, tool, and module terms do not become accepted lexicon terms through A12.
