## 4. Required definitions

Faction construct: doctrine-level classification placeholder for collective actor group pressure, hierarchy pressure, and authority pressure without canonical naming or runtime state simulation.

Economy construct: doctrine-level classification placeholder for value, exchange, requisition, scarcity, and logistics pressure without price lists, currency canon, or market simulation.

Institutional advancement claim: doctrine-level progression posture for rank, role, legitimacy, and organizational authority without procedural advancement mechanics.

Escalated institutional contradiction: unresolved faction/society/economy/institution contradiction forwarded for doctrine escalation rather than solved by canon invention.

## 5. Core doctrine rules

1. Classification-first rule: factional, societal, economic, legal, and institutional claims must be routed through explicit doctrine grammar before downstream schema or runtime interpretation.
2. Boundary-preservation rule: A15 consumes scale, actor, asset, conflict, and travel boundaries from A05/A11/A12/A13/A14 and cannot redefine them.
3. Non-procedural rule: A15 defines qualitative doctrine posture only and cannot define law, diplomacy, trade, logistics, colonization, settlement, or domain procedures.
4. Non-simulation rule: A15 does not define currencies, price lists, market simulation, or runtime world-state machinery.
5. Source-local integrity rule: donor faction/economy/institution systems can be retained as source-local evidence without automatic Astra default adoption.
6. Contradiction-visibility rule: unresolved faction/economy/institution contradictions must remain auditable through quarantine or escalation.
7. Institutional-advancement boundary rule: institutional advancement is doctrine posture only and not an exact reputation or advancement engine.

## 6. Conversion mapping rules

- Treat donor faction, society, institution, sect, guild, corporation, empire, trade, requisition, law, reputation, diplomacy, war, and colonization claims as doctrine constructs for routing before normalization.
- Classify each claim into faction/institution posture, economy/logistics posture, and social/political posture without introducing runtime faction/economy/reputation/trade-route state.
- If donor pressure requires named-faction canon, faction statblocks, price lists, currencies, market simulation, exact reputation systems, or procedure engines for law/diplomacy/trade/logistics/colonization/settlement, retain source-local evidence and do not normalize those claims in A15.
- If faction/economy/institution pressure collides with A05/A11/A12/A13/A14 boundaries, preserve source-local labels and escalate contradiction rather than redefining adjacent doctrine.
- If classification cannot be completed without inventing hidden-information behavior or runtime event commits, quarantine or escalate instead of normalizing.

## 7. Source-local handling

- Donor faction systems, donor institution systems, donor reputation systems, donor law systems, donor trade systems, donor logistics systems, donor diplomacy systems, donor war systems, and donor colonization systems can be retained as source-local records.
- Source-local records preserve provenance and pressure but do not become Astra faction/economy/institution doctrine canon.
- Repeated donor pressure can form canon candidates only through later K-layer review.
- Unclassifiable faction, economy, society, and institution claims are quarantined or escalated, not normalized by invention.
- Donor proper nouns, named factions, and named empires remain source-local unless later canon promotion accepts them.
- source-local faction, society, institution, sect, guild, corporation, empire, trade, value, law, reputation, diplomacy, logistics, war, and colonization terms do not become accepted lexicon terms through A15.
- source-local wording is boundary-only evidence handling and is never lexical promotion, canon promotion, or default-law adoption.
