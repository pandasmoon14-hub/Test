## 6. Recovery, training, research, preparation, readiness, proof, and output definitions

For B07 purposes:
- `recovery` is project or posture routing for stabilizing, treating, adapting to, investigating, or seeking owner handling for harm, conditions, fatigue, exposure, corruption, instability, backlash, poison-like affliction, disease-like affliction, trauma-like pressure, form-state disruption, substrate disruption, resource depletion, source-local impairment, or unknown impairment. It is not automatic healing or final recovery math.
- `training` is structured practice, field use, guided instruction, apprenticeship, pressure exposure, corrective work, cross-training, immersion, simulation, or source-local learning that may produce competency pressure, proof candidates, access vectors, risk warnings, or owner handoffs. It is not rank purchase, automatic mastery, or automatic power gain.
- `research` is study, experimentation, reverse-engineering, observation, prototype development, field testing, consultation, simulation, meditation/comprehension, or source-local inquiry that may produce questions, theories, warnings, failed hypotheses, method seeds, lawful-action vectors, prototype candidates, hidden-tag routing, or owner handoffs. - `preparation` is planning, staging, rehearsal, scouting support, stockpiling, route preparation, facility preparation, social preparation, safety preparation, or risk reduction that may route readiness pressure to other owners. It is not a universal bonus.
- `readiness` is a doctrine-facing indication that future action, scene entry, project work, travel, faction engagement, object use, or risk response has relevant preparation pressure. It is not automatic advantage or a final readiness economy.
- `proof` is a candidate or routed evidence event generated by training, field use, research, recovery, preparation, failure, cost, endurance, correction, or transformation. B07 can mark proof pressure or create proof-candidate routing; D04-style advancement owners decide sufficiency.
- `output` is a doctrine-facing result of B07 routing: recovery stabilization, partial recovery, blocked recovery, training progress, proof candidate, method seed, research question, theory, warning, prototype, lawful-action vector, preparation completion, readiness pressure, owner routing, transition note, source-local retained effect, quarantine, or escalation. ## 7. Recovery/training/research/preparation project intake procedure

B07 intake begins only after upstream procedure produces non-object long-task pressure or a recovery/training/research/preparation question that cannot be safely resolved as a single B01/B02 action. Common triggers include:
- B01 identifies extended recovery, study, practice, planning, rehearsal, readiness, consultation, access, or field-practice pressure that exceeds scene procedure.
- B02 identifies a declared action whose feasibility, cost, risk, preparation, training, research, authority access, recovery posture, or proof relevance cannot be safely resolved as one action.
- B03 identifies a tool, manual, relic, prototype, simulation apparatus, training equipment, research instrument, or object-supported practice need, while object construction/repair remains B03/B06-routed.
- B04 identifies custody, access, burden, treatment access, archive access, teacher access, safe storage, secure recovery, or lawful-loss recovery pressure that constrains non-object work.
- B05 identifies payment, patronage, requisition, upkeep, scarcity, obligation, institutional dues, tuition-like value pressure, market access, or value sink attached to teacher access, research access, medical/treatment access, or preparation.
- B06 identifies a project interval whose output is non-object recovery, training, research, preparation, readiness, proof, authority access, method, theory, or warning pressure.
- A source-local donor system presents rest, downtime, healing, training, research, crafting-adjacent study, preparation, or long-task rules that must be quarantined, mapped, or escalated.

Intake procedure:
1. Identify the upstream trigger and preserve the upstream owner boundary.
2. State the project-facing intent in plain doctrine-facing language.
3. Classify the family, scope band, target or mode, and desired-output class.
4. Identify known, unknown, missing, protected-hidden, source-local, and schema-blocked requirements.
5. Identify likely commitments and whether any commitment has already been made under B02, B04, B05, or B06.
6. Preview cost/risk categories without setting final math.
7. Identify output owners before resolving progress.
8. If safe owner coverage exists, set a B06-compatible interval setup note; if not, mark quarantine, escalation, `pending_schema`, `human_review`, or `defer_until_schema_exists`.

## 8. Family and scope classification

B07 classifies the project by intent, family, scope, target/mode, and output before selecting procedure.

Family classification uses the vocabulary in section 6. Use `mixed_project` when multiple family procedures are materially involved. Use `unknown_project` when the intent cannot be safely classified. Use a `source_local_*` family when the source's own recovery/training/research/preparation/rest/downtime system must be retained only as quarantined source-local evidence.

Scope classification uses the B07 scope bands in section 6. Scope is not numeric time/cost law. Scope describes doctrine-facing magnitude and owner pressure:
- `minor`: limited work with low owner surface and likely simple handoff.
- `standard`: ordinary work with clear requirements and at least one output owner.
- `major`: work with multiple requirements, meaningful costs, risk, or multiple owners.
- `extended`: work spanning multiple intervals, locations, contributors, facilities, teachers, archives, field tests, or obligations.
- `transformational`: work that may alter actor state, proof posture, route pressure, Expression/Technique candidates, social/legal status, world state, power pacing, hidden-state access, canon eligibility, or source-local retention.
- `source_local_scope`: scope is retained from donor/source procedure and cannot be safely converted yet.
- `unknown_scope`: insufficient doctrine or schema coverage exists.

If the work is mostly object construction, salvage processing, repair, maintenance, modification, or upgrade, route to B06. If the work is mostly object use, tool readiness, or asset deployment, route to B03. If the work is mostly acquisition, reward, requisition, market access, value flow, upkeep, or value sink, route to B05. If the work is mostly custody, storage, access, burden, or lawful loss, route to B04. If the work is an immediate action or scene transition, route to B01/B02.

## 9. Requirement discovery and protected-hidden handling

Requirement discovery identifies what must be present, known, owned, permitted, safe, practiced, studied, accessed, stabilized, contained, routed, or committed before progress or output can be recognized. A requirement is not automatically visible. A missing requirement does not authorize invention. A protected-hidden requirement may be marked without revealing hidden truth.

Requirement categories may include:
- recovery target identity, severity, stability, treatment environment, caregiver, contamination status, resource state, exposure source, corruption/instability/backlash risk, actor-state owner, and hidden incompatibility;
- training basis, current competency evidence, practice environment, teacher/mentor/manual access, safe repetition conditions, field use opportunity, correction path, apprenticeship obligation, simulation authority, source-local training gate, and proof owner;
- research question, known basis, missing basis, source evidence, archive/manual/dataset access, testable phenomenon, lab/workshop/simulation access, containment, consultation, target custody, reverse-engineering permission, and hidden truth boundary;
- preparation goal, future scene/action/project/travel/faction target, stockpile, route data, scouting support, rehearsal environment, safety plan, staging access, facility state, social permission, and risk-reduction owner;
- time block, contributor, teacher, institution, faction, archive, spirit, relic, domain, source-local authority, facility, tool, value commitment, obligation, permission, or legal/social exposure;
- schema owner, owner-file approval, canon review, runtime review, hidden-state owner, or source-local boundary.

Protected-hidden handling procedure:
1. Mark `protected_hidden` only for the requirement state, not the hidden truth.
2. State the visible symptom, constraint, or uncertainty that justifies routing.
3. Identify the owner file or `pending_schema` destination that can receive the hidden pressure.
4. Do not expose hidden cause, secret tag, hidden threshold, concealed opposition, concealed cure, hidden research answer, or hidden-state reveal rule.
5. If conversion or example text would reveal hidden truth, quarantine or redact the handoff and route to `human_review`.

## 10. Cost/risk preview and commitment procedure

Before a B07 interval begins or advances, preview cost/risk categories in B02/D12 style without defining final math. The preview must distinguish known cost, possible cost, hidden/protected cost, source-local cost, and owner-blocked cost.

Cost/risk categories may include:
- time block, attention, opportunity cost, fatigue, overstrain, injury setback, exposure, contamination, corruption, instability, backlash, resource depletion, emotional/identity pressure, social risk, legal risk, faction attention, institutional obligation, teacher conflict, archive debt, material loss, value commitment, safety setup, test-subject risk, prototype risk, hidden pressure, and source-local retained effect.

Commitment procedure:
1. Confirm the actor or responsible party understands the visible intent and visible cost/risk categories.
2. Identify costs already committed by upstream B02/B04/B05/B06 procedure.
3. Reserve or route commitments to the appropriate owner without creating final ledgers.
4. If the cost/risk is protected-hidden, mark the requirement/cost as `protected_hidden` and route without reveal.
5. If the actor refuses commitment, route to `no_delta_required`, `transition_note`, `training_stalled`, `recovery_blocked`, `preparation_completed` only if appropriate, or abandonment/interruption handling.
6. If commitment would require missing schema, route to `pending_schema`, quarantine, escalation, `human_review`, or `defer_until_schema_exists`.

## 12. Recovery project procedure

Recovery project procedure applies when the work primarily addresses a recovery target listed in section 6 or an actor-state impairment that cannot be resolved by ordinary scene/action procedure.

Recovery routing steps:
1. Identify the recovery target as precisely as visible evidence permits.
2. Determine whether the goal is stabilization, partial recovery, adaptation, treatment access, diagnosis/investigation, containment, prevention of worsening, owner handoff, or source-local retained recovery.
3. Identify requirements such as safe environment, caregiver, time, supplies, facility, value commitment, permission, hidden cause, anti-contamination, rest conditions, treatment risk, or owner-file coverage.
4. Preview visible costs and setbacks: fatigue, overstrain, injury/condition setback, corruption/instability pressure, exposure, resource depletion, social/legal risk, or source-local cost.
5. Commit the visible work interval using B06-compatible interval grammar.
6. Route outputs to harm, condition, actor-state, resource, world, faction, custody, value-flow, source-local, schema, or human-review owners.

Recovery output rules:
- `recovery_stabilized` may indicate reduced immediate worsening pressure, but not final healing math.
- `recovery_partial` may indicate some owner-routed improvement or adaptation, but not condition removal rules.
- `recovery_blocked` may indicate missing requirement, hidden requirement, owner-file block, source-local lock, or schema gap.
- Rest may be a requirement or support condition, but rest is not automatic reset and B07 does not define long-rest or short-rest procedure.
- Downtime may provide an interval context, but downtime is not safe time and may produce active scene triggers, complications, or faction attention.

## 13. Training project procedure

Training project procedure applies when the work primarily addresses practice, field use, instruction, apprenticeship, pressure training, corrective training, cross-training, immersive training, simulation, source-local training, or proof-bearing practice.

Training routing steps:
1. Identify training mode, visible competency/method/route pressure, and actor intent.

## 14. Teacher, mentor, institution, archive, simulation, and source-local authority procedure

Bad access shortcuts are invalid: buying lessons does not create rank; owning a manual does not create mastery; finding an archive does not reveal all truths; gaining a teacher does not bypass proof; entering a simulation does not guarantee safe consequence-free practice.

## 16. Prototype, method-seed, lawful-action-vector, and dangerous-temptation routing

B07 may route preliminary non-object outputs when research/training/preparation identifies a possible method, action vector, prototype, or temptation.

Routing distinctions:

- A `lawful_action_vector_created` output means the desired outcome appears to have a possible lawful path if owners validate costs, requirements, proof, and limits. It is not immediate authorization.
- A `prototype_created` output is a test-facing or doctrine-facing candidate. If it is an object, route object state to B03/B06. If it is a method, route to training/research/route owners. If it affects power economy, instability, actor state, or harm, route to those owners.

- A `dangerous_temptation_marked` output identifies an attractive but unlawful, unsupported, corrupting, unsafe, hidden-truth-bypassing, source-local-only, or owner-blocked path. It is not a legal playable mechanic.

A missing basis does not authorize invention. Unsupported desired outcomes must be classified, routed, quarantined, blocked, or deferred rather than turned into player-facing powers.

## 27. Examples of good and bad B07 usage

Good usage examples:
- A character spends a week recovering from backlash. B07 classifies `recovery` with target `backlash`, marks a protected-hidden requirement for an unknown contaminant, routes visible stabilization to harm/actor-state owners, and does not define healing math.
- A warrior seeks a mentor for pressure training. B07 classifies `teacher_access` plus `pressure_training`, routes payment/obligation to B05, access/permission to faction owners, fatigue/overstrain risk to harm owners, and proof candidate pressure to advancement owners without granting a rank.

Bad usage examples:
- Declaring that two days of rest fully heals all injury and removes corruption. This violates recovery/rest boundaries and final recovery math ownership.

- Completing a research interval and revealing hidden enemy weaknesses automatically. This violates hidden-state and research truth boundaries.

- Giving a universal advantage bonus because the party prepared. This violates preparation/readiness boundaries.
- Importing a donor downtime activity menu as Astra's default downtime system. This violates source-local donor quarantine.
