# Astra Stage 2 - World Activity Engine v0.1

Cross-donor revised from the current Astra distillation layer after RHBF + Shadowrun comparison.

## Purpose

This file defines Astra's baseline **world-operation grammar**.

It does **not** assume one donor's campaign rhythm, ideology, or access structure as universal law. Instead, it records the repeatable operational structures that survived two highly contrasting donors:

- RHBF pressures hub-linked expedition loops, trials, hunting realms, world events, and optional return-to-base support.
- Shadowrun pressures brokered operations, legwork, infiltration, contacts, layered access, fallout, and persistent social-operational consequences.

The Astra-facing question is therefore not merely "what do Ascendants do between creation and late-stage power?" It is:

**What lawful operation patterns must Astra support across fantasy, cyberpunk, hybrid, and future donors without collapsing them into one donor-shaped loop?**

This file answers that question by promoting shared grammar, naming optional profiles explicitly, and keeping donor-local cadence out of baseline law.

---

## I. Consolidation stance

Astra baseline now owns the following:

- repeatable **world-operation loops**,
- explicit **access law**,
- lawful **opportunity structures**,
- active **faction leverage and pressure**,
- persistent **consequence layers**,
- and optional **return/base-operation overlays**.

Astra baseline does **not** require:

- universal token-based travel,
- universal broker-mediated jobs,
- universal stronghold cadence,
- universal anti-technology ideology,
- or one fixed hub model.

Those remain donor-local or optional profile structures unless later donor pressure broadens them further.

---

## II. Core Astra world-operation loop

Astra campaigns should support a repeating operation cycle with these structural steps:

1. **Position** in a world state, hub, settlement, convoy, platform, enclave, route, or field location.
2. **Acquire or confirm access** to an opportunity, site, target, route, claimant-space, or protected layer.
3. **Select or accept an operation structure**.
4. **Approach and prepare** through travel, legwork, scouting, negotiation, staging, provisioning, or networked access.
5. **Execute** the operation through exploration, conflict, infiltration, negotiation, survival, retrieval, defense, research, transport, or mixed procedure.
6. **Resolve gains and losses**.
7. **Return, reposition, or continue forward** into the next world state.
8. **Apply persistent consequences** such as standing shifts, heat, pressure escalation, faction response, world events, access change, or optional base-operation consequences.

This is Astra's baseline operation loop.

### Design rule

A donor may emphasize different slices of the loop:

- RHBF emphasizes hub -> access object -> expedition -> return -> pressure update.
- Shadowrun emphasizes contract -> legwork -> layered operation -> payout/fallout -> standing/heat update.

Astra must support both without treating either as exclusive.

## III. Hubs, positions, and operational anchors

### Astra equivalent: Operational Anchor

RHBF strongly validates the **Nexus Hub** as a neutral or semi-neutral launch point. Shadowrun validates a looser anchor model built around fixers, contacts, safehouses, markets, and neighborhoods rather than one singular sacred hub.

Astra therefore treats the promoted baseline as an **Operational Anchor**.

Operational Anchors may include:

- Nexus Hubs,
- settlements,
- enclaves,
- contract exchanges,
- safehouses,
- orbital or mobile ports,
- convoy bases,
- research stations,
- faction compounds,
- distributed contact webs.

### Nexus Hub remains valid

**Nexus Hub** remains a promoted and useful Astra term for a major region-level anchor with:

- onboarding function,
- information exchange,
- opportunity distribution,
- faction contact density,
- access mediation,
- and route or gate significance.

### Design rule

A major campaign region should have at least one reliable Operational Anchor. Not every region requires one singular neutral city-state hub.

---

## IV. Access law

Access is now promoted as real Astra doctrine.

Astra opportunities, routes, sites, and protected layers should be gated by lawful access structures rather than by vague narrative permission alone.

### Astra access structures may include:

- **Transit Seals**,
- warrants,
- credentials,
- contracts,
- faction standing thresholds,
- route clearances,
- network access rights,
- keyed locations,
- stage or rank permissions,
- bonded rights,
- or other donor-local mechanisms.

### Transit Seals remain optional profile support

RHBF's Transit Seal structure remains valid and important, but it is now an **optional profile**, not universal baseline.

Transit Seals are useful when Astra wants:

- earned world expansion,
- modular site-gating,
- consumable or persistent access keys,
- route discipline,
- and clear acquisition pressure.

### Design rule

Astra should preserve earned access, but not assume every access structure is itemized into a token or seal.

## V. Opportunity structures

Astra baseline should support multiple lawful operation classes.

### Baseline-promoted opportunity structures

#### 1. Ascension Trials

Ascension Trials remain a strong promoted term.

Trials are modular challenge nodes defined by operational fields such as:

- access method,
- urgency,
- location frame,
- claimant or controller,
- objective type,
- environment,
- risk grade,
- and consequence profile.

Batch B owns their operational meaning. Batch C owns their exact record grammar and generator structure.

#### 2. Hunting Realms

Hunting Realms remain a strong promoted term.

They represent repeatable pressure zones or harvest regions where threat, reward, traversal, and extraction matter.

Astra should keep them broad enough to include:

- beast-country,
- ruin fields,
- anomaly corridors,
- toxic zones,
- salvage sectors,
- or similarly repeatable high-risk opportunity spaces.

#### 3. Faction Contracts

Faction Contracts remain promoted and now sit more broadly than RHBF-only faction work.

They cover:

- assignments,
- brokered jobs,
- patron tasks,
- sponsor missions,
- covert tasks,
- procurement,
- escort,
- recovery,
- sabotage,
- protection,
- investigation,
- and mixed operations.

#### 4. Mandates

Mandates remain useful as higher-order pressure opportunities driven by powers larger than ordinary local faction leverage.

They may represent:

- strategic imperatives,
- sector crises,
- anomaly containment,
- war pressure,
- existential warnings,
- doctrinal commands,
- or other supra-local imperatives.

#### 5. Expedition Sites

Expedition Sites remain valid as self-contained modular adventure structures:

- ruins,
- vaults,
- sanctums,
- forge-complexes,
- labs,
- dead cities,
- living biomes,
- wrecks,
- sealed compounds,
- black sites.

### Optional profile structures strongly validated so far

- brokered operation loops,
- network intrusion or access operations,
- remote or delegated operations,
- base-support cycles,
- platform-linked missions,
- consequence-management sessions.

These are lawful, but not yet universalized.

## VI. Faction pressure engine

Astra should preserve factions as active operational pressures, not passive lore wallpaper.

### Promoted faction law

Factions should be defined structurally by:

- **role** in the operation ecology,
- **desire**,
- **pressure style**,
- **available leverage**,
- **reward and access offerings**,
- and **response pattern under provocation or favor**.

### Useful faction roles include:

- patron / sponsor,
- broker / handler,
- gatekeeper / clearance owner,
- rival / suppressor / hunter,
- claimant / occupier / local power,
- contractor / employer,
- knowledge owner,
- market actor,
- enforcer,
- insurgent / saboteur,
- and neutral or semi-neutral mediator.

### Design rule

Do not let faction identity collapse into giant lore dumps. Astra gains more from faction behavior, leverage, and consequence grammar than from donor-specific proper nouns.

---

## VII. Persistent consequence layers

Astra should support consequences that persist across operations.

### Promoted baseline

Operations should be able to alter:

- world pressure,
- faction disposition,
- access status,
- obligation,
- claim state,
- or strategic position.

### Promoted optional-profile consequence layers

Shadowrun strongly reinforces the value of keeping some consequence tracks distinct rather than collapsing them into one reputation score.

Useful distinct layers may include:

- **standing**,
- **heat**,
- **notoriety**,
- **attention**,
- **credibility**,
- **trust**,
- or donor-local equivalents.

These are now lawful optional profiles inside Astra, not universal mandatory tracks.

---

## VIII. Legwork, preparation, and information staging

Astra world procedure should support preparation phases before full execution.

This may include:

- scouting,
- negotiation,
- contact leverage,
- reconnaissance,
- supply acquisition,
- false identity work,
- map or route acquisition,
- target study,
- network approach,
- environmental reading,
- and claimant analysis.

### Design rule

Not every operation begins with immediate entry. Astra should support operations that are shaped materially by prep work.

This does not require all operations to become heists. It only means pre-execution structure is lawful and often valuable.

---

## IX. Network, remote, and delegated operations

Shadowrun validates operation patterns that are neither purely local nor purely bodily.

Astra should therefore explicitly allow optional-profile support for:

- **Network Access Procedure**,
- **Remote Platform Operations**,
- proxy or delegated action,
- platform-linked execution,
- and credential-mediated intrusion or control.

### Boundary rule

Astra should support these procedures without universalizing one donor's exact Matrix cosmology or digital metaphysics.

What Astra needs at baseline is the grammar that:

- some protected spaces are access-layered,
- some operations occur through interfaces and platforms,
- and some operators act through mediated presence rather than direct bodily presence.

The exact metaphysical or technical explanation remains donor-local unless later canon broadens it.

---

## X. Base operations overlay

RHBF strongly validates a return-loop where expedition play feeds a home base, capital, or stronghold. Shadowrun supports adjacent patterns through safehouses, logistics, contacts, upkeep, and operational support, but not the same cadence.

Astra therefore keeps this as an **optional Base Operations Overlay**.

This overlay may include:

- post-operation maintenance,
- unrest or attack checks,
- support asset changes,
- structure or support upgrades,
- staffing or retainer decisions,
- logistics updates,
- faction changes,
- and future opportunity unlocking.

### Design rule

Base operations are lawful and useful, but **not mandatory Astra cadence**. Not every campaign, donor, or region must run a stronghold loop.

Cost math, structure catalogs, and settlement/site records belong elsewhere.

---

## XI. Reward and resource logic

Astra rewards should remain tied to operation structure and world state rather than becoming undifferentiated currency piles.

### Reward families Astra should support

- merit or recognition markers,
- credits / currency / exchange value,
- access-granting assets,
- knowledge,
- bonded or growth-bearing objects,
- components and harvest outputs,
- progression-significant natural treasures,
- support assets,
- contract payouts,
- credentials,
- faction favor,
- and operation-linked unlocks.

### Design rule

Astra rewards should usually do at least one of the following:

- advance the character,
- unlock or broaden access,
- alter future operation posture,
- strengthen support position,
- or change faction/world consequences.

---

## XII. Technology, metaphysics, and ideological pressure

RHBF validates technology-hostility as one donor-local pressure. Shadowrun validates technology-normal and interface-heavy operation ecologies.

Astra should therefore preserve **technology / metaphysics tension** as a lawful world-pressure axis, but explicitly refuse import of anti-technology ideology as universal baseline.

### Promoted Astra stance

Worlds, factions, regions, doctrines, or sites may differ on:

- synthetic ascent,
- augmented embodiment,
- machine dependence,
- ritual vs engineered transcendence,
- networked cognition,
- prohibited synthesis,
- or sacred/material boundaries.

These are local or regional pressures unless canon later states otherwise.

---

## XIII. Framework laws after RHBF + Shadowrun

The following should now be treated as Astra-facing framework law unless later donors break them:

1. Astra needs a repeatable **world-operation loop**.
2. **Access law** must be explicit and mechanically meaningful.
3. Opportunity structures such as **Ascension Trials**, **Hunting Realms**, **Faction Contracts**, **Mandates**, and **Expedition Sites** are lawful operation classes.
4. Factions should act as **mechanical pressure engines**.
5. Operations may generate **persistent consequences** beyond immediate win/loss.
6. Astra should support both **anchor-based expedition loops** and **brokered/prepared operation loops**.
7. Base-support / stronghold-style play is lawful as an **optional overlay**, not universal cadence.
8. Astra must allow operations mediated through **network, remote, credential, or platform-linked access** without universalizing one donor's cosmology.

## XIV. Provisional items requiring further donor pressure

These should remain provisional after RHBF + Shadowrun:

- whether Transit Seals remain a major recurring Astra profile or become only one optional access pattern,
- the final shape of standing / heat / notoriety style consequence layers,
- how broadly remote/platform operation should generalize,
- the exact boundary between contacts/resources as build pressure and as world-operation leverage,
- whether Mandates remain a major recurring Astra term across later donors,
- and how much base-operations support Astra wants as a common campaign mode.
