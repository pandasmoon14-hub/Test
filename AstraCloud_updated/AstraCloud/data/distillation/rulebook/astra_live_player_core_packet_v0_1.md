# Astra Ascension — Player Core Rules Packet v0.1 (Cross-Donor Revised)

## Becoming an Ascendant

An Ascendant is a player-grade actor whose life has crossed a threshold of consequence, capability, or transformation. Some arise through cultivation, ordeal, inheritance, training, augmentation, bonded artifacts, institutional selection, catastrophe, or deliberate pursuit. Others are pulled upward by faction pressure, survival demands, exceptional talent, or access to forces that ordinary people cannot lawfully wield.

Astra does not assume a single origin story for all Ascendants. It only assumes that Ascendants are characters whose actions can materially redirect danger, opportunity, and world pressure.

## Layered Build Law

Astra uses a layered build model rather than a single universal character package chain.

A complete player build may draw from several lawful layers:

- **Body / Origin Layer** — kinform, ancestry expression, lineage, chassis, or other body-origin structure
- **Culture / Background Layer** — upbringing, socialization, pre-ascendant life, affiliations, or formative environment
- **Role / Path Layer** — path, profession, vocation, discipline, route, or equivalent role-defining package
- **Access Layer** — domains, licenses, bonds, attunements, interface states, augment channels, permissions, or other gated capability structures
- **Capability / Asset Layer** — skills, resources, contacts, credentials, starting gear, bonded objects, augmentations, or equivalent build-shaping assets

Not every donor or campaign uses all layers equally. Astra treats them as lawful build owners when the source structure or campaign frame supports them.

## Core Resolution

This live packet uses Astra's current **D20-forward implementation profile** for uncertain actions.

When a character attempts something dangerous, opposed, or difficult, roll:

**D20 + relevant Attribute modifier + Path, Technique, gear, skill, or access modifiers**

If the final result equals or exceeds the target number, the action succeeds.

When two forces oppose one another directly, both sides roll and the higher result prevails. Ties go to the higher governing Attribute unless another rule states otherwise.

Astra's broader doctrine can host other lawful donor-local resolution profiles. This packet describes the current live player-core profile, not the only imaginable Astra resolution architecture.

### Three Check Types

Astra uses three main check types in this live profile.

**Attribute Check**  
Used when raw natural capability matters and no formal training is clearly dominant.

**Skill Check**  
Used when trained competence is the main determining factor.

**Opposed Check**  
Used when two actors directly contest one another.

### Advantage and Disadvantage

When a character has Advantage, roll 2D20 and keep the better result.  
When a character has Disadvantage, roll 2D20 and keep the worse result.

### Critical Outcomes

A natural 1 is a critical failure.  
A natural 20 is a critical success.

In combat, a natural 20 on an attack or offensive Technique is a critical strike:
- the effect hits automatically
- damage is doubled
- any attached critical rider resolves without a resistance attempt unless explicitly stated otherwise

## Attributes

Every Ascendant is defined by six Attributes.

### Vessel
Raw physical force, leverage, carrying power, and crushing impact.

### Core
Durability, physical stability, endurance, and resistance to bodily collapse.

### Flow
Reflexes, evasion, precision, speed, grace, and tactical movement.

### Insight
Reasoning, pattern recognition, investigation, memory, and applied knowledge.

### Resonance
Inner attunement, force sensitivity, mystical or extra-ordinary receptivity, and lawful channeling of power.

### Intent
Presence, command, persuasion, intimidation, leadership, and social pressure.

### Attribute Generation

For the current live profile, distribute **72 points** across the six Attributes.
- no Attribute may begin below **6**
- no Attribute may begin above **18**
- Path or equivalent build layers may modify these after the initial spread

This 72-point spread remains a valid Astra profile, but Astra does not treat one donor's exact character-generation table as universal law.

## Skills and Competence

Skills represent trained competence layered on top of natural capability.

A Skill Check uses:

**D20 + governing Attribute modifier + Skill Rank bonus + situational modifiers**

### Skill Ranks

- **Untrained**: +0  
- **Trained**: +2  
- **Expert**: +4  
- **Ascendant**: +6

### First-Pass Skill List

**Vessel**
- Athletics
- Endurance

**Flow**
- Acrobatics
- Stealth
- Sleight
- Riding

**Insight**
- Analysis
- Lorecraft
- Medicine
- Engineering

**Resonance**
- Awareness
- Survival
- Meditation
- Attunement

**Intent**
- Persuasion
- Deception
- Intimidation
- Leadership

### Skill Use Examples

- Climbing a shattered wall: **Vessel + Athletics**
- Sneaking past sentries: **Flow + Stealth**
- Diagnosing a relic fault: **Insight + Engineering**
- Tracking a beast through broken terrain: **Resonance + Survival**
- Stabilizing an injured ally: **Insight + Medicine**
- Forcing a crowd to back down: **Intent + Intimidation**

If a task depends on training but no perfect skill exists, the Guide chooses the closest match.

### Competence Is Not The Whole Build

Astra distinguishes ordinary trained competence from gated access, installed capability, bonded privilege, route-state permissions, credential authority, and other special build layers.

Not every meaningful capability should be flattened into a Skill. Some donor systems place major pressure on:
- domains or licenses
- bonded objects or relic states
- augment channels or installed modifications
- contacts, credentials, or starting asset access
- interface or resonance-like access states

Those remain lawful build pressures even when they are not ordinary Skills.

## Access and Special Channels

Astra treats **access law** as a real doctrinal layer.

A character may possess lawful access through Paths, Domains, licenses, bonded states, augment channels, interface states, attunements, credentials, lineage privileges, or equivalent donor-local structures. Access law determines what a character may learn, activate, equip, enter, command, or lawfully sustain.

This means:
- capability is not identical to permission
- training is not identical to access
- and a character build may be shaped by both ordinary competence and extraordinary channel structure

## Derived Values

### Vitality
At 1st Degree in the current live profile, an Ascendant begins with Vitality equal to:

**Core + Core modifier**

Each time they gain a new Degree, they increase Vitality by their Core modifier, minimum 0.

### Defense Threshold
Base Defense Threshold equals:

**10 + Flow modifier**

Armor, shields, Techniques, assets, installed systems, or traits can increase this.

### Initiative
Initiative equals the character's **Flow modifier**, modified by gear, Techniques, states, or other lawful effects.

### Presence Pressure
Presence Pressure is the total sum of all six Attributes. It represents the visible and invisible weight of an Ascendant's power.

### Auxiliary Risk / Destiny Economies

Some Astra profiles use additional risk, fortune, or destiny-facing economies.

Examples may include:
- Fate-like reserves
- Edge-like tactical advantage pools
- omen, luck, or intervention tracks
- equivalent donor-local auxiliary currencies

These are **lawful optional profiles**, not universal baseline requirements of every Astra character.

## Resistances

Astra uses three Resistances in the current live profile.

### Core Resistance
Used against poisons, disease, bodily shock, corruption, internal collapse, and forced physical degradation.

### Flow Resistance
Used against blasts, traps, falling hazards, sweeping area effects, and displacement.

### Resonance Resistance
Used against fear, mental domination, illusion, soul assault, hostile interface intrusion, and other mind-, spirit-, or consciousness-affecting forces.

When a hostile effect calls for a Resistance, the defender rolls:

**D20 + appropriate Resistance score**

The target number is either set by the Guide or established by the attacker’s governing Attribute check.

## Character Creation Sequence

Use the following as the current live player-core sequence.

1. Choose name, appearance, and concept.
2. Choose body/origin presentation: kinform, ancestry expression, lineage, or equivalent body-origin layer.
3. Choose culture, background, pre-ascendant life, or equivalent formative layer.
4. Choose Path, role, profession, route, or equivalent primary build layer.
5. Record any special access channel that meaningfully shapes the build.
6. Distribute 72 Attribute points.
7. Apply Path or equivalent layer adjustments.
8. Record starting Resistances.
9. Record Domains, licenses, permissions, or equivalent access tags.
10. Record starting Techniques, abilities, or equivalent active expressions.
11. Record trained Skills.
12. Record starting resources, contacts, credentials, or social-capital pressures when the source structure or campaign frame supports them.
13. Select starting kit, bonded objects, augmentations, or other opening assets.

Astra does not require every build to use the same exact package chain. This sequence is a stable live profile, not an exclusivity claim.

## Progression

Astra supports **broad progression grammar** rather than one universal advancement profile.

At minimum, progression may include:
- broad character advancement,
- specialization or capability growth,
- access expansion,
- asset or bonded-object development,
- and donor-local progression tracks where lawfully relevant.

### Optional Advancement Profile: Tri-Axis / Breakthrough Progression

One strong Astra-supported profile uses four linked tracks:

- **Stage** — broad level of transcendence
- **Degree** — fine progression within a Stage
- **Lineage** — inherited, bonded, or legacy depth
- **Soul** — inner refinement and spiritual capacity

In that profile, an Ascendant may gain Degrees within a Stage through experience, victory, training, or cultivation, while breakthrough into the next Stage may require additional linked thresholds.

This remains a **supported optional profile under review**, not universal law for every donor.

## Temporary Vitality

Some Techniques, relics, systems, or effects grant Temporary Vitality above the normal maximum.
Temporary Vitality remains until lost.
A character cannot hold more Temporary Vitality than double their normal Vitality pool unless another rule states otherwise.

## Current Cross-Donor Consolidation Notes

This revised player-core packet reflects two completed donor tests and therefore adopts the following stance:

- layered build grammar is baseline
- access law is baseline
- contacts/resources/social capital may be lawful build pressure
- auxiliary risk/destiny economies are optional profiles
- tri-axis / breakthrough progression is a supported optional profile, not universal law
- no single donor's exact build table, reserve payload, or slot topology is treated as Astra baseline here
