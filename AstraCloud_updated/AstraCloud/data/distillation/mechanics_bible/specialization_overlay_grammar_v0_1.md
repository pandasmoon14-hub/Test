# Astra Specialization Overlay Grammar v0.1

Derived from cross-donor consolidation after RHBF, Shadowrun, and Lancer.

## Scope of this file

This file owns Astra's **specialization overlay grammar**.

It defines:
- what a specialization overlay is,
- how specialization overlays differ from Paths, competencies, and codified active expressions,
- what kinds of overlay structures Astra currently recognizes,
- how ranked specialization growth should behave,
- what overlay layers may and may not own,
- and what remains optional-profile support or donor-local retention.

It does **not** own:
- full Path doctrine,
- full competency doctrine,
- full Technique doctrine,
- exact talent trees,
- exact feat chains,
- exact donor specialization catalogs,
- exact point-buy tables,
- exact level/rank counts,
- or exact donor-specific prerequisite math.

This is a grammar owner, not a catalog owner.

---

## Why this file exists

The first three donors establish that Astra can no longer behave as though all meaningful differentiation lives in either:
- Path/profession identity,
- ordinary competency,
- or codified active-expression objects.

Shadowrun proves that Astra must support:
- focused differentiation that sharpens a build without becoming a full replacement for skill or role,
- targeted competence emphasis,
- and layered advancement where not every build distinction becomes a spell, technique, or class package.

Lancer proves this even more cleanly through Talents:
- ranked overlays,
- coherent thematic lanes,
- persistent growth through narrow focus,
- and differentiated build identity that sits above broad capability but below total chassis ownership.

RHBF does not negate this. It simply pressures different layers more strongly.

The stable conclusion is:

> Astra needs a specialization-overlay layer that can host ranked, focused, build-shaping differentiation without forcing it into Path, skill, or Technique monoculture.

---

## Core specialization law

A specialization overlay is a **focused advancement layer** that modifies, sharpens, or extends a build without replacing the larger owner layers.

A specialization overlay may:
- reinforce an existing role,
- refine a competency focus,
- alter the use pattern of a codified expression family,
- grant narrow but durable distinctions,
- or provide ranked developmental emphasis.

It should not automatically:
- become the full role identity,
- replace ordinary competence,
- replace access law,
- or absorb all active-expression doctrine.

The stable rule is:

> Specialization overlays are narrower than a Path, more structured than a mere descriptor, and more persistent than one-off option picks.

---

## Overlay is not the same as other build layers

### Overlay is not Path
A Path owns major role identity and opening direction.
A specialization overlay sharpens or redirects a narrower slice of the build.

A build may have both without collapse.

### Overlay is not ordinary competence
Competency answers broad performance questions.
Overlay answers focused differentiation inside or across competencies.

A specialization overlay may bias how a competency family is expressed without becoming the competency layer itself.

### Overlay is not Technique
A specialization overlay may modify how Techniques are learned, selected, readied, interpreted, or emphasized.

It is not itself identical to a Technique object.

### Overlay is not access law
An overlay may require access or interact with access.
It does not replace access as the owner of permission, licensing, clearance, or keyed-state logic.

### Overlay is not equipment or bonded-object ownership
An overlay may support, prefer, or amplify certain objects.
It does not become the object-side owner.

## Recognized specialization overlay families

These are the current Astra-facing overlay families.

### 1. Ranked thematic overlays
These are the clearest cross-donor family.

They own:
- focused thematic advancement,
- repeated progression through a narrow lane,
- and increasingly refined distinction across ranks or tiers.

Lancer strongly validates this family.

This is the current default overlay model Astra should be prepared to host.

---

### 2. Competence-adjacent overlays
These overlays sit close to competency doctrine.

They may:
- deepen a narrow competence family,
- sharpen a preferred execution style,
- or create a recognizable emphasis inside broad trained capability.

Shadowrun pressures this family strongly.

These overlays must remain distinct from raw competence lists.

---

### 3. Active-expression-adjacent overlays
These overlays shape how a codified active-expression family behaves without being identical to the active objects themselves.

They may influence:
- preferred expression type,
- readiness posture,
- enhancement focus,
- interaction patterns,
- or growth trajectory.

These overlays must not swallow full Technique doctrine.

---

### 4. Role-adjacent overlays
These overlays sit near a Path, profession, or archetypal identity layer without replacing it.

They may:
- sharpen a sub-role,
- bias a role toward a specific operational expression,
- or create persistent differentiation between actors with similar major identity layers.

These are lawful but should remain subordinate to full Path ownership.

---

### 5. Platform-side overlays
These overlays apply to platform-bearing donors where the platform itself has build-shaping differentiation layers.

Lancer pressures this family strongly.

They may affect:
- tactical posture,
- platform emphasis,
- subsystem preference,
- mount/install orientation,
- or chassis-side behavioral identity.

These overlays remain distinct from:
- full platform record,
- exact frame math,
- and operator-side role identity.

---

## Overlay structure

A lawful specialization overlay may contain:

- **name**: the overlay identity
- **family**: ranked thematic / competence-adjacent / active-expression-adjacent / role-adjacent / platform-side
- **focus**: what the overlay is actually refining
- **rank or tier state**: whether and how the overlay advances
- **entry conditions**: what enables access to the overlay
- **interaction rules**: what layers it can modify or reinforce
- **exclusions/conflicts**: what it cannot stack with or absorb
- **progression effects**: what changes when it grows
- **handoff boundaries**: what must be owned by other files

Not every donor will use every field, but this structure is broad enough to host multiple overlay families.

---

## Ranked growth law

When an overlay is ranked, the rank progression should follow these rules:

1. **Ranks must remain focused**
   A rank should deepen or refine a lane, not abruptly transform into an entirely different build owner.

2. **Rank growth should be cumulative or directional**
   Later ranks should meaningfully relate to earlier ranks.

3. **Overlay growth must not silently absorb Path ownership**
   Even a strong overlay remains an overlay unless a donor clearly produces a higher-order owner.

4. **Overlay growth must not replace access law**
   A ranked overlay may depend on access, but should not become the permission system itself.

5. **Overlay growth must not collapse into a flat feat pile**
   Astra should preserve coherent overlay families rather than purely disconnected one-off picks where the donor actually presents a lane.

## Overlay interactions

### Path with overlay
A Path may coexist with one or more specialization overlays.
The Path remains the major identity owner.
The overlay refines expression.

### Competence with overlay
An overlay may narrow or bias how broad competence is deployed.
It does not replace competence.

### Technique with overlay
An overlay may shape:
- preferred active-expression posture,
- expression-family emphasis,
- or readiness/use pattern.

It does not replace codified active objects.

### Access with overlay
An overlay may require:
- Domain access,
- license-backed access,
- special channels,
- credential states,
- or similar authorization.

Access remains the owner of permission.

### Operator/platform with overlay
An operator may carry overlays.
A platform may carry overlays.
A linked build may distribute overlays across both records.

These must remain visible and not collapse into one shared bundle unless a donor explicitly requires that.

### Resources/contacts with overlay
A resource-backed or network-backed start may unlock overlays or make them viable earlier.
This does not make the social-capital layer identical to overlay ownership.

## What specialization overlays may lawfully do

A specialization overlay may:
- refine a tactical style,
- deepen a narrow operational identity,
- reinforce a build bias,
- create ranked focus inside a role,
- modify how broad competencies are expressed,
- shape how active-expression families are used,
- or define a platform-side preference lane.

A specialization overlay should not, by default:
- define all core attributes,
- rewrite the actor's entire role identity,
- replace access law,
- replace the full competency layer,
- replace the full active-expression layer,
- or serve as a disguised donor class package.

---

## Optional-profile support

The following are lawful recurring support patterns, but not universal specialization law:

- exact feat-chain style progression,
- exact talent-tree branch counts,
- specialization purchased through isolated point-buy only,
- specialization attached to one donor-specific advancement ladder,
- specialization tied to exact level breakpoints,
- specialization that exists only on platforms,
- specialization that exists only on operators.

Astra should host these profiles without universalizing any one of them.

## What remains donor-local

### RHBF donor-local
RHBF does not currently supply the strongest overlay pressure.
Any exact RHBF class/domain-specific growth packaging remains donor-local unless later canonized elsewhere.

### Shadowrun donor-local
- exact specialization labels and costs,
- exact donor skill-group interactions,
- exact donor advancement math,
- exact overlay relationships to Edge, Essence, or Matrix-specific systems.

### Lancer donor-local
- exact Talent names and trees,
- exact rank counts,
- exact manufacturer or chassis-specific gating,
- exact sitrep or frame-facing interactions,
- exact LL pacing for talents.

## Current Astra-facing summary

Astra now recognizes specialization overlays as a stable build layer that may:
- sit above broad competence,
- sit beside Path doctrine,
- interact with access,
- modify active-expression posture,
- or shape platform-side emphasis.

This layer is now strong enough to own ranked, focused, persistent differentiation across donors without collapsing into either skill lists, Path packages, or Techniques.

The final rule is simple:

> If a donor gives actors coherent, ranked, focused developmental lanes that refine a build without replacing its larger owners, Astra should preserve them as specialization overlays.
