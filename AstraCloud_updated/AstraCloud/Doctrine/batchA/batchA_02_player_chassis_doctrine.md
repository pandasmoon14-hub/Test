## Authority
This file is the authoritative doctrine file for the **Astra player-grade actor chassis**.

It defines:
- what an Astra player-grade actor is,
- what makes such an actor valid for Batch A purposes,
- what high-level chassis interfaces every valid actor may possess,
- what routes may produce such an actor,
- and what downstream Batch A files are allowed to assume.

It does **not** define:
- character creation procedure,
- attribute lists or derived stat formulas,
- skill architecture,
- permission gate mechanics,
- ability object fields,
- resource economies,
- status/condition rules,
- damage taxonomies,
- progression tables,
- ancestry or background catalogs,
- package lists,
- or starting equipment logic.

Those belong to later doctrine files.

---

## 1. Purpose

Batch A requires a stable answer to a foundational question:

**What is an Astra player character before any donor-specific class, ancestry, skill, resource, or progression system is applied?**

This file answers that question.

Its function is ontological and architectural, not procedural. It establishes the minimum doctrine required for later files to describe statistics, competencies, permissions, powers, progression, origins, packages, and legal build order without contradiction.

This file exists to prevent later Astra doctrine from silently inheriting donor assumptions about what a player character must be, how one comes into being, or what systems are automatically attached to that state.

## 3. Doctrinal Definition

An **Astra player-grade actor** is a persistent, advancement-capable entity recognized by Astra doctrine as valid for player-character construction and long-term system interaction.

A valid player-grade actor is not defined by any one donor assumption such as species, class, profession, soul-structure, spiritual cultivation, cybernetic status, social role, or cosmological origin. Instead, it is defined by possessing a chassis capable of:
- meaningful agency,
- repeatable participation in Astra resolution,
- durable accumulation of change,
- lawful interaction with Astra competencies, permissions, abilities, progression structures, and material support layers,
- and coherent continuity across play, conversion, and later canon consolidation.

Astra uses the term **Ascendant** as a broad Astra-native player-facing designation for a being that has crossed into player-grade relevance, but this file does not require that all donor systems use the same in-world explanation for how that threshold is reached.

---

## 4. Threshold of Player-Grade Status

Not every sentient being, creature, construct, spirit, beast, drone, or social actor is automatically a valid Astra player-grade actor.

A player-grade actor must meet the following doctrinal threshold:

### 4.1 Persistent identity
The entity must be stable enough to be represented as a continuing actor rather than a one-scene effect, disposable unit, or purely environmental phenomenon.

### 4.2 Meaningful agency
The entity must be capable of making consequential choices, taking directed action, and participating in outcomes beyond passive existence.

### 4.3 Advancement relevance
The entity must be able to accumulate meaningful change over time, whether through training, awakening, augmentation, inheritance, bonded growth, role mastery, cultivation, adaptation, or analogous donor structures.

### 4.4 Mechanical addressability
The entity must be representable through later Astra framework layers such as statistics, competencies, permissions, abilities, progression interfaces, origin-state layers, and loadout or support structures where applicable.

### 4.5 Play continuity
The entity must be capable of surviving long-term campaign use as a player-facing focal actor, even if the fiction of that continuity differs across donors.

Any entity that fails one or more of these threshold requirements is not automatically invalid in Astra fiction, but it is not automatically valid as a Batch A player chassis.

## 5. Valid Routes into Player-Grade Status

Astra doctrine does not permit a single mandatory origin story for player-grade actors.

A valid Astra player-grade actor may arise through one or more of the following route categories:

### 5.1 Awakened route
The actor crosses a threshold of awareness, power, resonance, consciousness, or internal activation.

### 5.2 Trained route
The actor becomes player-grade through instruction, discipline, apprenticeship, institutional preparation, or repeated directed refinement.

### 5.3 Inherited route
The actor's player-grade status is shaped by lineage, heritage, bloodline, embedded legacy systems, inherited patterns, or analogous donor structures.

### 5.4 Bonded route
The actor becomes player-grade through durable linkage to another being, force, intelligence, relic, symbiote, spirit, construct, system, or pact-like relationship.

### 5.5 Augmented route
The actor becomes player-grade through implants, cybernetics, biotech, external modules, engineered modification, or equivalent enhancement structures.

### 5.6 Chosen or appointed route
The actor is selected, authorized, recognized, licensed, ordained, inducted, or otherwise elevated by an external authority, institution, or metaphysical process.

### 5.7 Transformed route
The actor becomes player-grade through mutation, metamorphosis, uplift, reconstruction, rebirth, incarnation, or other substantial state change.

### 5.8 Manufactured route
The actor is built, grown, printed, assembled, invoked, instantiated, or otherwise deliberately created in a form that satisfies player-grade requirements.

### 5.9 Emergent route
The actor becomes player-grade through unplanned convergence, adaptation, accident, crisis, survival pressure, or spontaneous system formation.

### 5.10 Hybrid route
The actor may arise through any lawful combination of the routes above.

#### Route doctrine note
These route categories define **valid entry models**, not full origin packages. They do not themselves grant mechanics, permissions, abilities, or narrative privileges. Those belong in later files.

## 6. Mandatory Chassis Interfaces

Every valid Astra player-grade actor must be capable of interfacing with the following doctrine layers, even if a given donor system expresses some of them minimally, abstractly, or through unusual terminology.

### 6.1 Identity interface
The actor must possess a coherent self-state or equivalent continuity anchor that allows the system to treat it as one enduring entity.

### 6.2 Resolution interface
The actor must be able to participate in tests, contests, checks, saves, or equivalent action-resolution procedures defined later.

### 6.3 Statistical interface
The actor must be representable through a later stat architecture, whether broad, narrow, numeric, qualitative, or hybrid.

### 6.4 Competency interface
The actor must be capable of possessing learned, practiced, natural, or acquired competencies as defined later by Astra's translation architecture.

### 6.5 Permission interface
The actor must be capable of lawful access, restriction, qualification, attunement, licensing, gating, or equivalent permission logic where relevant.

### 6.6 Active-expression interface
The actor must be capable of manifesting actions, abilities, techniques, powers, maneuvers, modules, or other active expressions if the donor or future Astra package layers support them.

### 6.7 Progression interface
The actor must be capable of receiving durable change over time through one or more advancement lanes.

### 6.8 Origin-state interface
The actor must be capable of carrying persistent identity-shaping factors such as kinform, heritage, culture, background, manufacture-state, bonded-state, or analogous source layers, even if a specific donor system omits some of them.

### 6.9 Material-support interface
The actor must be capable of lawful interaction with gear, tools, relics, implants, companions, vehicles, loadouts, or other support structures where relevant.

#### Interface doctrine note
These interfaces are not claims that every actor must begin with rich content in every layer. They are claims that the chassis must be architecturally compatible with those layers.

## 7. Universal Assumptions for Downstream Files

All later Batch A files may safely assume the following about any valid Astra player-grade actor:

### 7.1 The actor is persistent
It has enough continuity to be tracked over time.

### 7.2 The actor is actionable
It can attempt meaningful actions and be affected by outcomes.

### 7.3 The actor is growth-capable
It can change in lasting ways rather than remaining permanently static.

### 7.4 The actor is framework-addressable
It can receive later Astra structures without contradiction, including statistics, competencies, permissions, abilities, progression layers, and support structures.

### 7.5 The actor is not reducible to one layer
No single layer such as class, ancestry, species, job, soul-state, implant status, culture, or resource engine is sufficient to define the full Astra player chassis.

### 7.6 The actor may be multi-axis
Its development may occur across more than one progression or state-change lane.

### 7.7 The actor may be genre-variable
It may exist in fantasy, sci-fi, cultivation, hybrid, occult, posthuman, planetary, voidfaring, or otherwise unusual donor contexts without requiring a new chassis ontology.

### 7.8 The actor may be non-human or non-humanoid
Player-grade validity is not contingent on human baseline anatomy, appearance, or social normality.

### 7.9 The actor may be biological, synthetic, spiritual, hybrid, or otherwise irregular
Astra does not restrict player-grade validity to one substrate model.

### 7.10 The actor may possess unusual relationships to body, self, mind, spirit, or frame
Split bodies, shared cores, symbiotic overlays, distributed consciousness, alternate embodiment, and comparable donor structures are not automatically invalid, provided the actor still satisfies persistence and agency requirements.

## 8. Non-Universal Assumptions

The following must **not** be treated as default truths of Astra player-grade status unless another doctrine file explicitly adopts them for a narrower purpose:

- that all player-grade actors are born from one cosmological event,
- that all player-grade actors are biological humanoids,
- that all player-grade actors use one universal inner-energy system,
- that all player-grade actors must belong to a class, path, or profession,
- that all player-grade actors possess lineage, bloodline, or ancestry-based power,
- that all player-grade actors are socially important, noble, elite, or politically responsible,
- that all player-grade actors are chosen by the same metaphysical authority,
- that all player-grade actors must begin with a standardized gear loadout,
- that all player-grade actors have the same body requirements, sustenance needs, mortality pattern, or transformation ladder,
- or that any one donor's metaphysical explanation for advancement is automatically Astra law.

This section is protective by design. Its purpose is to stop donor cosmology, donor genre assumptions, and donor package logic from quietly redefining the chassis.

## 9. Cross-Donor Compatibility Rule

The Astra player-grade chassis must be broad enough to support, at minimum:
- fantasy actors,
- science-fiction actors,
- cultivation actors,
- hybrid magic/technology actors,
- class-based actors,
- classless actors,
- profession-centered actors,
- bond-centered actors,
- psionic actors,
- cybernetic and biotech actors,
- relic- or tool-mediated actors,
- beast, construct, spirit, or altered-form actors,
- and donor systems whose player entities are built from unusual subsystem combinations.

If a later file assumes a narrower actor model than this file allows, that narrower assumption must be explicitly scoped and must not retroactively redefine the chassis.

## 10. Anti-Import Guardrails

### 10.1 Donor cosmology guardrail
No donor cosmology becomes Astra default law merely because it explains player advancement in its source text.

### 10.2 Donor terminology guardrail
Donor labels for player-grade entities may be translated into Astra-native terms without importing the donor's full metaphysical baggage.

### 10.3 Donor premise guardrail
A campaign premise, social status assumption, or setting role is not automatically a chassis truth.

### 10.4 Donor resource guardrail
No donor-specific energy pair, fuel model, charge logic, or metaphysical economy is assumed by this file.

### 10.5 Donor embodiment guardrail
No donor-specific transformation ladder, body-state requirement, or life-support rule is universalized here.

### 10.6 Donor hierarchy guardrail
No donor-specific rank, tier, caste, faction, or mission-access structure is assumed by this file.

## 12. Implementation Rule for Later Drafting

When drafting later Batch A files, use the following discipline:

- treat this file as the source of truth for what a valid player-grade actor is,
- do not collapse the chassis into any one donor layer,
- do not assume one mandatory origin myth,
- do not let package, ancestry, class, resource, or progression mechanics redefine the base ontology,
- and when a donor system presents a strange player entity, test it against this file's threshold and interfaces before inventing exceptions.

If a future donor cannot be translated cleanly under this chassis, the solution is not to smuggle donor metaphysics into this file. The solution is to refine the framework explicitly and visibly.
