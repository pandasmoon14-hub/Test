# batchB_07_environment_hazards_weather_regions_and_afflictions.md

## Purpose

This file defines Astra Ascension’s doctrine for **environmental hazards, weather, regions, environmental media, exposure, and environment-origin affliction vectors**.

Its role is to provide Astra with a **universal environmental-pressure grammar** capable of absorbing donor systems built around climate stress, biome pressure, disaster events, cursed lands, void or underwater travel, radiation fields, toxic atmospheres, anomalous regions, planar corruption, deprivation, contamination, and other hostile environmental states without collapsing them into a single donor-shaped survival chapter.

This file is not merely a weather section.
It is the doctrine layer for **hostile, unstable, or operationally meaningful environments as pressure sources**.

In Astra terms, this file governs how:
- environments become mechanically relevant,
- regions are expressed as pressure packages rather than labels,
- weather and environmental states escalate from background description into active pressure,
- environmental media alter movement, perception, protection, sustainment, and viability,
- exposure vectors and hazard triggers are classified,
- affliction vectors originating from the environment are expressed without re-owning their downstream bodily meaning,
- environmental pressure scales from mild nuisance to catastrophic or rule-distorting conditions,
- and environmental procedure hands off cleanly into traversal, survivability, conditions, encounters, platforms, and settlements.

## Core doctrine terms

### Environment
An **environment** is the broader operational context in which beings, carriers, structures, or objects exist and act.
An environment includes not only place, but also medium, climate, pressure state, anomaly overlays, and sustainment assumptions.

### Region
A **region** is a bounded environmental pressure package.
A region is not merely a biome label or map tag.
A region may include baseline climate, terrain state, medium state, weather volatility, endemic hazards, affliction vectors, anomaly overlays, and region-law distortions.

### Environmental medium
An **environmental medium** is the substance, field, or substrate through which entities exist or move.
Examples include breathable atmosphere, thin atmosphere, vacuum, water, toxic gas, corrosive mist, zero-gravity space, dream substrate, void medium, unstable planar substrate, ash cloud, and similar operational states.

### Climate state
A **climate state** is the long-duration environmental baseline of a place.
Climate governs background temperature, moisture, seasonal pressure, prevailing winds, and ordinary expectations of survival and shelter.

### Weather state
A **weather state** is the current short- to mid-duration atmospheric or comparable environmental expression affecting an area.
Weather may be descriptive, operationally meaningful, or catastrophic.

### Ambient state
An **ambient state** is an ongoing environmental condition that applies by presence, duration, or ordinary exposure rather than by singular trigger event.
Examples include oppressive heat, low oxygen, fungal saturation, cursed malaise, corrosive rain-soak, low gravity, or radiological background.

### Episodic hazard
An **episodic hazard** is a discrete environmental event, pulse, surge, burst, collapse, or moment of intensified danger that occurs within an environmental context.
Examples include eruptions, lightning strikes, flash floods, toxic venting, spore bursts, geyser jets, cave-ins, or gravity snaps.

### Persistent region law
A **persistent region law** is a continuing rule distortion imposed by an environment or region that changes how ordinary procedures behave while the subject remains within that environment.
Examples include failed-rest regions, altered healing, weakened resistances, amplified damage, unstable recovery, anti-flight zones, dream logic, or directional inversion.

### Hazard vector
A **hazard vector** is the general channel by which an environment exerts pressure.
Examples include ambient exposure, contact, entry, movement through a medium, periodic pulses, contamination, deprivation, collapse, atmospheric breach, or sustained proximity.

### Exposure vector
An **exposure vector** is the channel by which beings or systems become subject to environmental pressure.
Exposure may arise through presence, inhalation, ingestion, immersion, contact, traversal, breach, contamination transfer, sustained labor, or failed protection.

### Hazard trigger
A **hazard trigger** is the event or condition that activates a discrete environmental pressure instance.
A trigger may be entry, contact, failed traversal, elapsed time, periodic pulse, structural breach, disturbance, interaction, route choice, or threshold crossing.

### Affliction vector
An **affliction vector** is an environmental channel capable of imposing disease, curse, poison, radiation burden, psychic contamination, infestation, deprivation burden, or other downstream adverse states.
This file owns the source-side vector, not the full downstream condition or survivability meaning.

### Protection class
A **protection class** is the type of mitigation that changes how environmental pressure applies.
Protection may be gear-based, trait-based, ritual or ability-based, carrier-based, facility-based, temporary, improvised, or acclimative.

### Shelter
**Shelter** is any protected state that reduces, delays, blocks, or recontextualizes environmental pressure.

### Deprivation
**Deprivation** is environmental pressure arising from unmet survival or operational needs such as breathable air, heat retention, hydration, nutrition, rest viability, radiation shielding, or similar necessities.

### Environmental pressure scale
An **environmental pressure scale** is the severity class of an environmental state.
This scale exists to preserve donor harshness without flattening all environments into the same level of threat.

## Doctrine commitments

### 1. Environmental source and downstream consequence are not the same thing
Astra must preserve the difference between:
- the environment as source,
- the exposure vector by which it acts,
- the trigger that intensifies or activates it,
- and the bodily, structural, or procedural consequences that follow.

This file owns the source-side doctrine.
Adjacent files own condition semantics, survivability meaning, and encounter timing.

### 2. Regions are pressure packages, not labels
A region must be able to carry climate, medium, weather volatility, anomaly state, endemic hazard vectors, affliction vectors, and rule distortions.
Astra must not reduce regions to “forest,” “desert,” “tundra,” or similar descriptive labels alone.

### 3. Environmental pressure has multiple forms
Astra must distinguish:
- ambient state,
- episodic hazard,
- and persistent region law.

These are related but not identical procedure families.

### 4. Medium matters as much as terrain
Hostile environments are often defined not only by location but by what beings are moving through or existing within.
Air, water, vacuum, toxic atmosphere, dream substrate, void medium, and unstable planar matter create different kinds of pressure and different protection assumptions.

### 5. Exposure is not the same as triggering
Some environmental pressures apply merely by ongoing presence.
Others apply only on contact, entry, failed traversal, periodic pulse, breach, disturbance, or other activation conditions.
Astra must preserve this difference.

### 6. Protection must remain plural
Astra must not assume all environmental mitigation is “special gear.”
Protection may come from biology, traits, powers, acclimation, infrastructure, carriers, shelter, treatment, ritual shielding, or temporary emergency response.

### 7. Environmental pressure must scale
Astra must support mild, active, severe, catastrophic, and rule-distorting environmental states without assuming that every environment is always attritional.

### 8. Donor environmental pressure must be preserved
Conversion must preserve donor environmental pressure.
Astra must not flatten harsh survival, contamination, curse-zone, weather-event, or anomaly-heavy environments into decorative scenery.
Astra must also not inflate mild atmospheric settings into constant attrition engines unless the donor actually relies on that pressure.

## Environmental grammar

Every environmental construct should be expressible through the following Astra-native grammar:
- **environmental context**,
- **region or site scope**,
- **environmental medium**,
- **baseline climate state**,
- **current weather state if relevant**,
- **ambient-state pressures**,
- **episodic-hazard pressures**,
- **persistent region-law distortions if present**,
- **exposure vectors**,
- **hazard triggers**,
- **affliction vectors**,
- **pressure scale**,
- **protection classes that apply**,
- **shelter or mitigation interactions**,
- **persistence or escalation logic**,
- **handoff consequences**.

This grammar is the stable Astra-native core.
Donor systems may vary in terminology, genre assumptions, and presentation, but conversion work should map them into this structure whenever possible.

## Region-state doctrine

### Region as pressure package
A region should minimally classify:
- baseline terrain or spatial context,
- environmental medium,
- climate state,
- weather volatility,
- native or endemic hazard vectors,
- common exposure vectors,
- common protection expectations,
- sustainment assumptions,
- anomaly overlays,
- and any persistent region laws.

### Region scope
Region scope may be:
- site-local,
- district or neighborhood,
- valley, forest, swamp, or comparable biome zone,
- wasteland or contaminated tract,
- orbital or void envelope,
- facility sector,
- layer of a trial or mission space,
- or any other bounded environmental unit with stable pressure identity.

### Region identity must not force genre
Astra regions may be natural, artificial, sacred, corrupted, technological, synthetic, simulated, void-born, dream-like, planar, or mixed.
A region doctrine must remain broad enough to absorb fantasy wilderness, urban blight, reactor zones, cursed tombs, alien atmospheres, and planar scarlands under one framework.

## Environmental medium doctrine

### Medium classes
At doctrine level, Astra should support environmental media such as:
- ordinary breathable atmosphere,
- thin or compromised atmosphere,
- hostile atmosphere,
- vacuum,
- water or submerged medium,
- corrosive or chemically active medium,
- particulate or ash-heavy medium,
- toxic or spore-bearing medium,
- zero-g or low-gravity operational medium,
- high-pressure medium,
- dream or psychic substrate,
- void or metaphysical transit medium,
- unstable planar or anomalous substrate,
- and mixed or layered media.

### Medium effects
Environmental media may alter:
- movement and traversal assumptions,
- sustainment needs,
- communication,
- sensory function,
- equipment viability,
- exposure speed,
- protection requirements,
- contamination likelihood,
- and arrival-state risk.

### Medium is not automatic damage
An unusual medium does not automatically imply direct harm.
Some media are merely operationally different.
Others become dangerous only at specific pressure scales, durations, or trigger states.

## Climate and weather doctrine

### Climate state doctrine
Climate represents the long-term environmental baseline of a place.
Climate should guide:
- expected clothing or gear assumptions,
- usual shelter needs,
- normal pace burdens,
- typical sustainment demands,
- and what counts as unusual weather.

### Weather-state tiers
Weather should be expressed in at least three doctrine tiers.

#### Background weather
Background weather is descriptive unless some adjacent pressure makes it operationally relevant.
It informs tone, expectation, visibility flavor, and region identity.

#### Active weather
Active weather alters play meaningfully.
It may affect travel pace, route choice, senses, exposure speed, communication, shelter needs, or procedural difficulty.

#### Catastrophic weather
Catastrophic weather is event-level pressure.
It may threaten survival, split groups, damage carriers or structures, block routes, force emergency shelter, or alter arrival state.

### Weather is not atmosphere alone
Astra weather doctrine may include nontraditional weather analogues when donor systems treat them as region-wide fluctuating environmental states.
Examples include ashfall, mana storms, radiation storms, dream waves, void turbulence, corrosive precipitation, psychic static, or similar event-weather patterns.

## Ambient state, episodic hazard, and persistent region law

### Ambient-state doctrine
Ambient states are by-presence pressures.
They are continuous or semi-continuous conditions that apply through time in place rather than through singular incidents.
Examples include oppressive heat, low oxygen, fungal saturation, background radiation, cursed malaise, or gravity drag.

Ambient states should classify:
- whether they affect all present entities or only some,
- whether duration matters,
- whether protection can fully negate them,
- whether they stack or intensify,
- and whether they merely inconvenience or actively threaten.

### Episodic-hazard doctrine
Episodic hazards are discrete activations within an environmental context.
They should classify:
- what triggers them,
- how often they can occur,
- whether they are predictable,
- whether they can be avoided, delayed, or redirected,
- whether they affect an area, target, route, or structure,
- and whether they leave persistent residue after activation.

### Persistent region-law doctrine
Persistent region laws are continuous rule distortions.
They should classify:
- what ordinary procedures they alter,
- whether the effect is universal or selective,
- whether the effect is suppressed by protection or not,
- whether the distortion is known, hidden, or discoverable,
- and whether leaving the region ends the distortion immediately, gradually, or only after restoration.

Persistent region laws should remain rare or donor-justified.
They are a major pressure category and must not be used as decorative flavor.

## Hazard-vector doctrine

Environmental hazard vectors may include, but are not limited to:
- ambient exposure,
- contact hazard,
- immersion hazard,
- inhalation hazard,
- ingestion hazard,
- traversal hazard,
- periodic pulse hazard,
- collapse or structural failure hazard,
- contamination hazard,
- deprivation hazard,
- atmospheric breach hazard,
- anomaly-field hazard,
- and region-law hazard.

A single environment may use more than one vector at once.
Conversion should preserve that plurality rather than compressing all pressure into one damage tick.

## Exposure-vector doctrine and hazard-trigger doctrine

### Exposure-vector doctrine
Exposure vectors answer **how pressure reaches the subject**.
Environmental exposure may arise through:
- presence over time,
- entering a zone,
- movement through the medium,
- physical contact,
- inhalation,
- ingestion,
- immersion,
- lack of shelter,
- protective failure,
- contamination transfer,
- carrier breach,
- or prolonged labor within the environment.

### Hazard-trigger doctrine
Hazard triggers answer **when an effect activates or intensifies**.
Triggers may include:
- entry,
- contact,
- elapsed time,
- periodic pulse,
- failed traversal,
- route choice,
- disturbance,
- structural breach,
- threshold crossing,
- environmental escalation,
- or deliberate interaction.

### Exposure and trigger must stay separate
A character may be exposed without immediate trigger resolution.
A character may also activate a hazard briefly without remaining in a long-term exposure state.
Astra must preserve both models.

## Environmental pressure-scaling doctrine

Environmental pressure should be classifiable at doctrine level as:
- **mild**,
- **active**,
- **severe**,
- **catastrophic**,
- or **rule-distorting**.

### Mild
Affects comfort, pace, role choice, visibility, resource draw, or minor procedural viability but is not usually immediately dangerous on its own.

### Active
Creates meaningful operational burden, recurring checks, constrained behavior, or moderate sustained risk.

### Severe
Threatens health, continuity, mission success, or structural integrity unless actively mitigated.

### Catastrophic
Can cause mass disruption, route denial, group split, structural failure, or rapid collapse if not addressed.

### Rule-distorting
Changes how ordinary procedures function in the region.
This level should be used only where the donor construct clearly imposes systemic environmental distortion.

## Protection-class doctrine

Environmental mitigation should be classed at least by source.

### Gear protection
Protection provided by clothing, armor support, rebreathers, insulation, filters, suits, masks, charms, seals, or other equipment.

### Trait protection
Protection provided by ancestry, biology, adaptation, mutation, innate resistance, or learned bodily resilience.

### Ritual or ability protection
Protection provided by powers, spells, techniques, wards, blessings, barriers, or transient effect states.

### Carrier or platform protection
Protection provided by vehicles, ships, mechs, caravans, habitats, sealed compartments, field generators, or comparable mobile shelters.

### Facility protection
Protection provided by buildings, bunkers, sanctuaries, field stations, clinics, vaults, shrines, or other stationary infrastructure.

### Temporary mitigation
Short-lived or emergency solutions that delay, soften, or partially block environmental pressure without constituting full protection.

### Acclimation
Protection gained through adaptation over time, prior conditioning, environmental familiarity, inoculation, or procedural preparation.

Protection classes may stack, overlap, substitute, or partially fail depending on donor mapping.
B07 should define the classes, not every possible numerical interaction.

## Shelter and mitigation doctrine

Shelter may be:
- immediate and improvised,
- temporary but stable,
- mobile,
- fixed and hardened,
- partial,
- contingent,
- or illusory.

Mitigation may:
- reduce exposure,
- slow escalation,
- block trigger frequency,
- convert severe pressure to active pressure,
- preserve rest viability,
- enable traversal that would otherwise be impossible,
- or allow only partial occupation of the environment.

Shelter and mitigation should be expressible separately from full immunity.
Many donor systems are built around surviving an environment long enough to achieve an objective rather than nullifying it entirely.

## Environmental affliction-vector doctrine

This file should support environment-origin vectors such as:
- disease vectors,
- toxin or poison vectors,
- radiation or contamination vectors,
- curse or taint vectors,
- infestation vectors,
- deprivation vectors,
- madness, hallucination, or psychic contamination vectors,
- and metamorphic or corruption vectors.

### Source-side ownership only
B07 owns:
- where the vector originates,
- how exposure occurs,
- what protection interacts with it,
- what triggers intensify it,
- and whether it is endemic, episodic, or anomalous.

B07 does **not** own the complete bodily, mental, or structural meaning once the affliction has taken hold.
That meaning is handed off to the relevant downstream files.

## Deprivation and sustainment pressure doctrine

Environmental pressure is not limited to obvious attack-like hazards.
B07 must support deprivation and sustainment pressures such as:
- breathable air failure,
- water scarcity,
- food scarcity,
- heat retention failure,
- overexertion within hostile climates,
- inability to rest effectively,
- contamination of supplies,
- shelter insufficiency,
- and environmental conditions that invalidate normal recovery assumptions.

Deprivation may be ambient, triggered, or region-law based.
It may also be modified by medium, weather, pace, shelter, and acclimation.

## Region-law and anomaly doctrine

Some environments do more than cause ordinary damage or afflictions.
They alter procedural assumptions.
Examples may include:
- recovery behaving differently,
- damage behavior shifting,
- resistance or immunity operating differently,
- navigation misaligning,
- time desynchronizing,
- contamination persisting abnormally,
- or the environment actively reacting to intrusion.

Astra should preserve these as **region-law constructs** rather than flattening them into oversized damage numbers.

Anomaly doctrine should classify:
- whether the anomaly is local or regional,
- whether it is stable or unstable,
- whether it is environmental, metaphysical, technological, or mixed,
- whether it is detectable before entry,
- and whether it changes ordinary procedural assumptions.

## Environmental escalation and persistence doctrine

Environmental pressure may persist or escalate according to:
- duration in place,
- repeated exposure,
- repeated trigger activation,
- worsening weather,
- route depth,
- shelter loss,
- carrier breach,
- contamination build-up,
- or anomaly intensification.

Escalation should remain expressible in multiple lawful forms, including:
- severity steps,
- clocks,
- burden tracks,
- repeated saves/checks,
- throughput or exposure accumulation,
- or donor-native pressure expressions.

Astra must not enforce one universal escalation model.

## Traversal and arrival handoff doctrine

Environmental pressure often overlaps with travel but does not replace it.
This file should therefore define only the environmental side of the handoff.

B07 may determine:
- that a route is hazardous,
- that pace is constrained by environmental state,
- that certain vectors require protection before traversal,
- that arrival state is altered by exposure,
- or that a region law changes what happens upon entry.

B06 remains authoritative for the traversal procedure itself.

## Environmental anti-hallucination rules

### No false precision
If donor material clearly indicates environmental pressure but does not specify exact numbers, intervals, or thresholds, Astra must not invent false precision merely to make the file feel complete.

### Preserve source identity
A fungal grove, cursed valley, vacuum breach, radiation fog, dream sea, and corrosive storm are not interchangeable because they all “deal damage.”
Their source identities matter to conversion and must remain distinct.

### Preserve pressure shape
If a donor environment is mostly atmospheric until a specific escalation point, Astra must not convert it into constant attrition.
If a donor environment is genuinely lethal or rule-distorting, Astra must not soften it into scenery.

## Lawful incomplete mapping

This is mandatory for corpus-scale conversion.
