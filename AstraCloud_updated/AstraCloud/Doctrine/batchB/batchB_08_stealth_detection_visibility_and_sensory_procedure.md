# batchB_08_stealth_detection_visibility_and_sensory_procedure.md

## Purpose

This file defines Astra Ascension’s doctrine for **uncertain perception under pressure**.

Its role is to provide Astra with a **universal concealment-and-perception procedure layer** capable of absorbing donor systems built around stealth, ambush, infiltration, obscurity, invisibility, partial information, sensory disruption, scanning, alarm states, and uncertain target acquisition without collapsing them into a single donor-shaped stealth chapter.

This file is not merely a rogue-facing stealth section.
It is the procedure layer for:
- concealment,
- observation,
- sensory access,
- visibility and obscurity,
- passive notice,
- active search,
- reveal,
- reacquisition,
- awareness degradation,
- targetability under uncertainty,
- identity certainty under uncertainty,
- and alarm or compromise pressure.

In Astra terms, this file governs how:
- entities become difficult to notice,
- entities become partially noticed,
- entities are sensed through one or more channels,
- awareness states change over time,
- obscurity and concealment alter perception quality,
- passive awareness differs from active search,
- search pressure escalates into alert and alarm states,
- false sensory inputs and deceptive inputs are procedurally distinguished from ordinary concealment,
- targets are revealed, reacquired, or lost again,
- and concealment or uncertainty hands off cleanly into encounters, investigation, travel, social procedure, and environmental pressure.

## Core doctrine terms

### Uncertain perception
**Uncertain perception** is any state in which the observing side lacks complete, clean, or reliable sensory access to a target, phenomenon, entity, object, route, or source.

### Awareness state
An **awareness state** is the current procedural degree of notice, localization, and recognition one observer has regarding a target.
Awareness state is always relative.
One observer may fully perceive a target that another observer only partly senses or entirely misses.

### Sensory channel
A **sensory channel** is the procedural route by which a target or phenomenon is perceived.
A channel may be direct, indirect, precise, imprecise, technological, mystical, biological, environmental, or derived from proxy evidence.

### Sensory quality
**Sensory quality** is the reliability, precision, and clarity of a sensory channel under current conditions.

### Concealment vector
A **concealment vector** is the means by which a target resists, degrades, redirects, or complicates perception.
Examples include darkness, cover, obscuring medium, stillness, camouflage, stealth motion, invisibility, deception, scanner spoofing, signal masking, or environmental distortion.

### Obscurity
**Obscurity** is any state that degrades sensory quality without necessarily preventing all awareness.

### Passive awareness
**Passive awareness** is background notice that occurs without explicit search commitment.

### Active search
**Active search** is deliberate effort to detect, localize, identify, verify, or track something that is hidden, uncertain, suspicious, or deceptive.

### Reveal
A **reveal** is any procedural shift that improves awareness of a target, including presence confirmation, location confirmation, identity confirmation, or exposure of a concealment vector.

### Reacquisition
**Reacquisition** is the act of restoring or improving awareness after awareness has been degraded, broken, diverted, or lost.

### Alert state
An **alert state** is the current level of suspicion, watchfulness, search intensity, or procedural readiness on the observing side.

### Alarm pressure
**Alarm pressure** is the rising procedural risk that uncertainty or concealment will escalate into active searching, response mobilization, lockdown, pursuit, or full compromise.

### Targetability
**Targetability** is the degree to which a target can be acted upon reliably under current awareness and sensory conditions.
Targetability is not identical to awareness.

### Identity certainty
**Identity certainty** is the degree to which an observer correctly knows what a perceived target actually is.
A target may be noticed but misidentified, targetable but not recognized, or recognized as present while its exact nature remains uncertain.

## Doctrine commitments

### 1. Awareness is the spine of the file
Astra must not treat stealth as a binary hidden/not-hidden switch.
The primary procedural question is the **awareness relationship** between observer and target.

### 2. Perception is broader than sight
Astra must not assume visual observation is the only meaningful perception model.
The framework must support auditory, olfactory, vibrational, thermal, magical, psychic, technological, and proxy-based sensing without inventing a separate ad hoc rule set each time.

### 3. Concealment, invisibility, and deception are not identical
A target hidden by cover or shadow is not the same as a target made invisible.
An invisible target is not the same as a deceptive, illusory, spoofed, or false-image target.
These are related but distinct concealment families.

### 4. Awareness, targetability, and identity certainty must stay distinct
An observer may:
- notice a target without locating it precisely,
- locate a target without targeting it cleanly,
- target a target without correctly identifying it,
- identify that something is false without fully locating the true source,
- or know something is present while remaining unable to act on it effectively.

### 5. Passive notice and active search are not the same procedure
Astra must preserve the difference between what is noticed in the background and what is revealed through deliberate searching, probing, scanning, testing, or examination.

### 6. Alert pressure is real procedure
Uncertain perception often escalates over time.
Astra must support suspicion, alertness, searching, and full compromise as live procedural states rather than forcing every stealth situation into one roll.

### 7. Shared and conflicting perception must be supported
Teams, sensors, companions, drones, and mixed observers may perceive the same target differently.
Astra must support shared detection, partial relay, and conflicting sensor results.

### 8. Donor pressure must be preserved
Conversion must preserve donor uncertain-perception pressure.
Astra must not normalize stealth-heavy, sensor-heavy, illusion-heavy, paranoia-heavy, or alarm-heavy systems into a flat “roll stealth vs perception once” loop.
Astra must also not inflate lightweight concealment models into overbuilt sensor simulation where the donor does not require it.

## Awareness-state doctrine

### Core rule
Awareness state is the central procedural measure of uncertain perception.
Awareness state tracks what the observer knows, how precisely they know it, and whether that knowledge is reliable enough for action.

### Awareness-state ladder
Astra recognizes at least the following awareness states.

#### Fully observed
The observer has sufficiently reliable awareness of the target to perceive it clearly and interact with it normally through at least one adequate sensory channel.
This does not necessarily mean the target is free of all obscurity or distortion.

#### Partially observed
The observer can localize the target well enough to act with meaningful but imperfect precision.
The target may be visible but obscured, audible but not visually clear, or otherwise imperfectly acquired.

#### Presence-known / location-uncertain
The observer knows or strongly suspects the target is present but lacks clean positional certainty.
The target’s exact position, route, or posture may be ambiguous, approximate, or shifting.

#### Unnoticed
The observer is not yet aware of the target’s presence in a procedurally meaningful sense.

#### Misidentified
The observer detects something real or apparently real, but incorrectly understands what it is.
Misidentification may concern identity, nature, allegiance, threat level, or whether the perceived target is itself genuine.

#### Lost-contact
The observer previously had stronger awareness but has since lost, degraded, or broken contact with the target.
Lost-contact is not the same as never noticing the target.

### Relative awareness
Awareness states are observer-relative.
One actor may fully observe a target that another actor only partly observes or entirely fails to notice.

### Multi-state coexistence
Different awareness dimensions may coexist.
A target may be:
- presence-known but identity-uncertain,
- location-known but targetability-degraded,
- identity-known but visually lost,
- or fully observed by one channel while remaining misleading through another.

## Sensory-channel doctrine

### Core rule
Perception in Astra is channel-based.
Observers detect targets through one or more sensory channels, each with its own quality and vulnerability.

### Channel classes
Astra recognizes at least the following sensory-channel classes.

#### Precise senses
A **precise sense** can support clean localization and ordinary interaction when conditions permit.
Sight is a common example, but not the only one.
Other donor systems may use precise scent-locks, magical sight, thermal imaging, echolocation, tremor precision, or similar channels.

#### Imprecise senses
An **imprecise sense** can indicate presence, direction, or rough location without always providing clean targetability.

#### Indirect or derivative senses
An **indirect or derivative sense** relies on traces, reflections, disturbances, environmental feedback, instruments, overlays, spoor, footprints, residual heat, signal residue, magical resonance, or similar proxy evidence.

#### Blocked senses
A **blocked sense** is unavailable because of obstruction, incapacity, environmental suppression, technological interference, magical nullification, or equivalent denial.

#### Distorted senses
A **distorted sense** remains functional but yields degraded or misleading inputs due to obscurity, interference, refraction, echo, inversion, static, feedback, warping, or comparable effects.

#### Deceptive inputs
A **deceptive input** is a sensory feed actively manipulated to create false impressions, false targets, false identities, false absences, or false certainty.

### Channel coexistence
A target may be fully hidden from one channel and still detectable through another.
A target may be invisible to sight yet precisely tracked by another sense.
A target may be visually present while technologically spoofed, psychically masked, or audibly absent.

### Channel conflict
Multiple channels may disagree.
Astra must support states where one channel reveals presence while another produces false identity or false location.

## Sensory-quality doctrine

### Core rule
Sensory quality expresses how usable a channel is under current conditions.
The same channel may shift in quality as obscurity, range, noise, shielding, interference, or deception changes.

### Sensory quality classes
Astra recognizes at least the following doctrinal quality classes.

#### Clean
The channel is sufficiently clear for reliable use.

#### Degraded
The channel remains useful but with reduced clarity, range, precision, or certainty.

#### Ambiguous
The channel supports only partial or uncertain conclusions.

#### Blocked
The channel currently fails to provide meaningful perception.

#### Distorted
The channel provides usable but procedurally unreliable or warped input.

#### Spoofed or false-fed
The channel is receiving manipulated, fabricated, or redirected information.

## Concealment-vector doctrine

### Core rule
Concealment vectors are the means by which a target resists, degrades, redirects, or weaponizes perception.
Astra must classify concealment by vector family rather than collapsing all concealment into “stealth.”

### Concealment families

#### Stealth vector
The target reduces notice through quiet movement, stillness, timing, route choice, posture, cover use, shadow use, or active concealment behavior.

#### Cover and occlusion vector
The target uses barriers, partial barriers, terrain, architecture, or environmental structure to break or degrade sensory access.

#### Obscuring-medium vector
Fog, smoke, dust, spores, ash, debris, haze, glare, darkness, refractive fields, signal static, or similar states degrade a channel without necessarily removing all perception.
Source-side ownership of these states belongs elsewhere; B08 owns their perceptual meaning.

#### Invisibility or transparency vector
The target is not normally available to one or more perception channels, especially sight-based ones, even if traces or alternate channels remain available.

#### Camouflage and mimicry vector
The target blends with the environment, imitates another object or being, or suppresses distinguishing features.

#### Deception vector
The target produces false inputs, false images, false sounds, false identities, false positions, or false negatives.
This is not identical to concealment by absence of visibility.

#### Signal-mask or sensor-spoof vector
The target defeats scans, magical sensing, tracking arrays, telemetry, or similar nonordinary channels through masking, noise, spoofing, or redirection.

#### Environmental-distortion vector
The space itself bends, reflects, amplifies, dampens, duplicates, or scrambles perception.
Again, source-side ownership belongs elsewhere; B08 owns the sensory consequences.

## Visibility, obscurity, and perceptual quality

### Visibility doctrine
Visibility is one channel-specific expression of sensory quality.
It is important but not universal.
A target may be visually unavailable but otherwise sensed.

### Obscurity doctrine
Obscurity degrades perception without automatically determining awareness state.
A target may be fully observed yet obscured.
A target may be presence-known due to obscurity even though another channel keeps it targetable.

### Visibility and awareness are not identical
Do not collapse “visible” into “fully observed.”
Do not collapse “not visible” into “undetected.”
Astra must preserve the difference between sensory access and broader awareness state.

## Passive awareness vs active search

### Passive awareness doctrine
Passive awareness represents background vigilance, ambient notice, default watchfulness, and ongoing sensory intake without committing specific search effort.

Passive awareness may detect:
- obvious movement,
- inconsistent sound,
- environmental disturbance,
- presence that exceeds concealment quality,
- broad warning signs,
- or failure patterns in the surrounding space.

Passive awareness does not imply active verification, exhaustive examination, or identity certainty.

### Active search doctrine
Active search is deliberate effort to detect, verify, localize, identify, or disprove uncertainty.
Active search may include:
- looking,
- listening,
- scanning,
- probing,
- triangulating,
- sweeping,
- tracking,
- examining,
- testing a suspected illusion,
- or isolating a likely target channel.

Active search may improve awareness state, identity certainty, or targetability.
It may also consume time, create exposure, alter alert state, or trigger countermeasures depending on donor expression.

### Search specialization
Astra must support search procedures focused on:
- presence,
- location,
- identity,
- channel confirmation,
- authenticity,
- source tracing,
- and reacquisition after loss.

## Reveal, reacquire, and counter-concealment doctrine

### Reveal doctrine
A reveal is any procedural event that increases awareness, targetability, or identity certainty.
A reveal may be caused by:
- target movement,
- sound,
- silhouette,
- environmental interaction,
- channel exposure,
- failed concealment,
- active searching,
- alternate-sense confirmation,
- countermeasure use,
- system sweep,
- time pressure,
- or deception collapse.

### Reacquisition doctrine
Reacquisition occurs when awareness was once stronger, then degraded, then is partially or fully restored.
This is especially important for:
- invisible targets,
- fleeing or moving targets,
- targets in changing obscurity,
- lost-contact pursuit,
- smoke or darkness,
- sensor dropouts,
- and false-image environments.

### Counter-concealment doctrine
Astra must support actions, tools, abilities, sensors, and procedures that counter concealment vectors.
Counter-concealment may:
- reveal presence,
- reveal location,
- improve targetability,
- improve identity certainty,
- negate a channel block,
- expose deception,
- or force a target into a less protected awareness state.

### Loss doctrine
Awareness may degrade because of:
- movement,
- concealment renewal,
- channel blockage,
- deception,
- line break,
- environmental change,
- alarm chaos,
- or target withdrawal.

Astra must support awareness improvement and awareness degradation as live procedure, not just a one-time gate.

## Alert-state and alarm-pressure doctrine

### Core rule
Stealth and uncertain perception often produce escalating response states.
Astra must preserve alertness as procedure, not just as flavor.

### Alert-state ladder
Astra recognizes at least the following alert states.

#### Unalert
The observing side is operating under ordinary vigilance without focused suspicion.

#### Suspicious
Something has created concern, irregularity, or partial notice, but active response remains limited.

#### Alert
The observing side is actively watchful and more ready to detect, verify, or respond.

#### Actively searching
The observing side is deliberately allocating effort to detection, verification, localization, or sweep procedures.

#### Fully compromised
The concealment situation has broken in a procedurally meaningful way.
The observing side is mobilized, informed, or committed to response.

### Alarm pressure
Alarm pressure is any cumulative or threshold-based process that raises alert state through noise, time, sensor evidence, missing reports, failed deceptions, partial sightings, environmental disturbances, or procedural suspicion.

### Distributed alertness
Alert states may be held by:
- an individual,
- a group,
- a location,
- a patrol network,
- a settlement,
- a platform,
- a sensor grid,
- or another procedural holder.

## Awareness vs targetability vs identification

### Awareness
Awareness answers: **Do I know something is there, and how well?**

### Targetability
Targetability answers: **Can I act on this target with enough precision for the intended procedure?**

### Identification
Identification answers: **Do I know what this target actually is?**

These three questions must remain distinct.

### Example doctrine consequences
A target may be:
- fully observed but still hard to target cleanly because of obscurity,
- presence-known but location-uncertain,
- targetable by an area action but not by a precise one,
- identified as hostile but not visually fixed,
- seen clearly but misidentified due to deception,
- or targeted through one sense while remaining identity-uncertain through another.

## Shared detection doctrine

### Core rule
Teams do not always perceive targets uniformly.
Astra must support partial, relayed, and uneven awareness.

### Shared detection states
One observer may:
- detect presence,
- another may confirm location,
- another may identify the target,
- and another may still be unaware.

### Relay doctrine
Shared detection may improve team awareness through communication, signal relay, sensor feed sharing, marked targets, guiding instructions, or synchronized channels.
Relay does not automatically equal universal certainty.

## False positives, false negatives, and deceptive certainty

### False positive doctrine
Astra must support cases where an observer believes something is present, real, or threatening when it is not.
This includes illusions, decoys, echoes, spoofed scans, phantom readings, and environmental misreads.

### False negative doctrine
Astra must support cases where an observer believes nothing is present, or believes a channel is clean, when a target remains concealed or masked.

### Deceptive certainty doctrine
A target or environment may create strong but incorrect certainty.
This is more dangerous than simple obscurity and should remain distinct from ordinary concealment.

## Anti-hallucination rules

### 1. Do not collapse uncertainty into binary stealth
If the donor distinguishes awareness grades, sensor quality, concealment vectors, or suspicion states, preserve those distinctions.

### 2. Do not collapse all perception into sight
If the donor uses alternate senses, scanners, proxy evidence, magical perception, or environmental feedback, preserve those channels.

### 3. Do not collapse invisibility into stealth bonus text
Treat invisibility-like effects as a concealment family, not merely a large modifier.

### 4. Do not collapse deception into concealment
False images, scanner ghosts, hallucinations, decoys, and identity misdirection require different procedural handling than simple obscurity.

### 5. Do not collapse awareness into targetability
A target can be known without being cleanly actionable.

### 6. Do not collapse identity certainty into visibility
Seeing something is not identical to knowing what it is.

### 7. Do not let B08 absorb adjacent files
If the problem is mostly routefinding, hand off to B06.
If the problem is mostly clue meaning, hand off to B09.
If the problem is mostly social deception, hand off to B10.
If the problem is mostly environmental source pressure, hand off to B07.
If the problem is mostly encounter timing, hand off to B01.
