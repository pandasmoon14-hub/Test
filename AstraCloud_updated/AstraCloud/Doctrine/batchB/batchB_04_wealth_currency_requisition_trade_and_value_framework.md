# batchB_04_wealth_currency_requisition_trade_and_value_framework.md

## Purpose

This file defines Astra Ascension's doctrine for **wealth, currency, requisition, trade, value, entitlement, and exchange**.

Its role is to provide a **universal economic translation layer** for Astra-native conversion work.
It exists so donor systems with very different economic assumptions can be absorbed into Astra without being flattened into a single cash-market default.

This file is not a coin chapter, not a treasure chapter, and not a shopping procedure chapter.
It is a framework file that defines:
- what kinds of value Astra recognizes,
- what media can express that value,
- how value can move,
- what restrictions govern exchange and access,
- how entitlement and requisition differ from open-market purchase,
- and how donor economic pressure must be preserved during conversion.

## Authority and scope

This file is authoritative for:
- value doctrine,
- wealth and holding doctrine,
- exchange-media doctrine,
- transaction-regime doctrine,
- price and valuation semantics,
- requisition and issue doctrine,
- reward-economy doctrine,
- access-right economic instruments,
- transferability and fungibility fields,
- scarcity and legitimacy classes,
- and lawful incomplete economic mapping.

This file is **not** authoritative for:
- item ontology or item fields,
- crafting, salvage, repair, or modification procedure,
- post-encounter loot generation or reward distribution procedure,
- faction or institutional political doctrine in the broader sense,
- settlement-scale macroeconomic operations,
- platform-scale logistics operations,
- or starting character allotment packages.

## Core design stance

Astra must be able to absorb donor systems where value is expressed through:
- explicit currency,
- multiple denominations,
- barter and commodity exchange,
- favors and obligations,
- requisition points or issue rights,
- personal reward currencies,
- access keys and service permits,
- abstract wealth bands,
- pooled group resources,
- or regime-specific value structures.

Astra must therefore treat economic structure as a doctrine layer rather than as a minor subsection attached to equipment or loot.

## Core doctrine terms

### Value
A recognized form of worth, leverage, utility, or exchange significance.
Value is broader than price.

### Wealth
The set of value-bearing holdings, claims, rights, or reserves controlled by a holder.
Wealth is broader than currency on hand.

### Exchange medium
A vehicle through which value is represented, transferred, redeemed, measured, or stored.

### Currency
A fungible exchange medium primarily used to measure and transfer value.
Not all exchange media are currencies.

### Price
A declared exchange requirement within a particular transaction regime.
Price is not identical to total value, legitimacy, or access eligibility.

### Cost
The amount of value, entitlement, sacrifice, or commitment required to obtain, maintain, use, redeem, or preserve something.

### Valuation
The doctrinal classification of how a thing's worth is expressed.
Valuation may be explicit, abstract, contextual, regime-bound, or provisional.

### Requisition
An acquisition regime where access is granted through authority, clearance, allotment, institutional issue, or command structure rather than ordinary open purchase.

### Entitlement
A recognized claim to receive value, service, equipment, access, or compensation under a specific doctrine or regime.

### Reward
A value-bearing benefit granted in response to accomplishment, compliance, contribution, status, or recognition.
Rewards may or may not be transferable.

### Access-right instrument
A token, permit, key, clearance marker, contractual right, or bounded claim whose primary significance is not commodity value but authorized access to movement, service, location, opportunity, or procedure.

### Transferability
Whether a value medium, claim, or holding can be transferred from one holder to another.

### Fungibility
Whether one unit of a value medium can be treated as equivalent to another unit of the same medium for exchange purposes.

### Market mode
The governing regime through which exchange occurs.

### Transaction regime
The rule structure by which value changes hands, is redeemed, is issued, or is denied.

### Holder
Any individual or collective entity recognized as capable of controlling, carrying, issuing, storing, redeeming, or being credited with wealth.

### Scarcity class
The doctrinal status of how constrained, abundant, rationed, or controlled a value-bearing good or medium is.

### Legitimacy class
The doctrinal status of whether acquisition, exchange, possession, or redemption is lawful, sanctioned, tolerated, restricted, illicit, or forbidden.

## Economic doctrine commitments

### 1. Value is broader than currency
Astra must not assume that all economically meaningful constructs are coins, credits, or market prices.
A donor may encode meaningful value in tokens, favors, obligations, allotments, licenses, contracts, mark systems, requisition channels, stockpiles, or access rights.

### 2. Economic significance is broader than commodity exchange
Some economically meaningful constructs matter because they authorize entry, travel, service, clearance, or redemption rather than because they are ordinary trade goods.
Astra must preserve that difference.

### 3. Price and eligibility are not the same thing
A thing may be affordable yet inaccessible.
A thing may be accessible yet expensive.
Astra must not collapse cost, rank, legality, geography, faction status, and clearance into one field.

### 4. Transferability and fungibility must be explicit
A value medium may be:
- transferable or non-transferable,
- fungible or non-fungible,
- pooled or personal,
- redeemable anywhere or only through specific exchanges,
- and durable, temporary, or one-use.

### 5. Transaction regime is not assumed
Astra must support open-market purchase, barter, issue, reward redemption, requisition, contract compensation, claim-right acquisition, and other regimes without pretending they are the same process.

### 6. Economic pressure must be preserved
Conversion must preserve donor scarcity, generosity, harshness, abstraction level, and gating pressure.
Astra must not flatten unlike donor economies into one mild market model.

## Value-form doctrine

Astra recognizes multiple value forms.
A converted construct may carry one or several of the following value profiles:
- exchange value,
- utility value,
- access value,
- prestige value,
- reward value,
- scarcity value,
- strategic value,
- contractual value,
- institutional value,
- or conversion-input value.

A single construct may have more than one value form at once.
Astra conversion must preserve the donor-significant value forms rather than forcing one universal worth expression.

## Exchange-media doctrine

Astra must support, at minimum, the following exchange-media families:
- standard transferable currencies,
- premium or elevated currencies,
- non-transferable reward currencies,
- commodity goods used as exchange media,
- barter goods,
- supply or ration points,
- requisition credits,
- favor or obligation units,
- service vouchers,
- access-right instruments,
- contract-bound claims,
- and abstract wealth indicators.

Each exchange medium should be classifiable along these field families:
- denomination model,
- transferability,
- fungibility,
- holder scope,
- redemption scope,
- legitimacy class,
- scarcity class,
- and conversion stability.

## Access-right instrument doctrine

Astra must explicitly support economically meaningful instruments whose primary function is **authorized access** rather than ordinary commodity exchange.

These may include:
- travel permits,
- entry tokens,
- mission keys,
- clearance passes,
- redemption proofs,
- escort papers,
- docking rights,
- gate authorizations,
- service privileges,
- or one-use procedural access instruments.

Access-right instruments must not be flattened into ordinary cash equivalents merely because they can be traded, sold, redeemed, or stockpiled.

Every access-right instrument should be classifiable by:
- what it authorizes,
- who may issue it,
- who may redeem it,
- whether it is transferable,
- whether it is single-use or persistent,
- whether it is consumed on use,
- whether it is personal, pooled, or bearer-based,
- and whether its market circulation is lawful, tolerated, restricted, or illicit.

## Transferability and fungibility doctrine

Transferability and fungibility are first-class economic fields.
They are not optional flavor notes.

A value medium or claim may be:
- freely transferable,
- conditionally transferable,
- holder-bound,
- institution-bound,
- location-bound,
- contract-bound,
- non-transferable,
- unit-fungible,
- class-fungible,
- partially fungible,
- or non-fungible.

Astra conversion must preserve these distinctions because they materially alter how value behaves.

## Wealth-holder doctrine

Astra must not assume that all wealth is individual purse wealth.

Recognized holder classes should include at least:
- individual holders,
- paired or linked holders,
- party or shared-pool holders,
- factional holders,
- institutional holders,
- civic or settlement-scale holders,
- platform-scale holders,
- project-specific holders,
- escrow or custodial holders,
- and abstract/non-physical holder states where the donor economy requires them.

This file defines the doctrine of holder classes.
It does not define the operational procedures for every holder type.

## Market-mode doctrine

Astra must support multiple exchange environments.
Market mode is not assumed to be open civilian retail.

Recognized market modes should include at least:
- open market,
- restricted market,
- faction market,
- black market,
- sanctioned exchange booth,
- requisition channel,
- barter network,
- gift/obligation economy,
- contract market,
- auction environment,
- bounty exchange,
- and redemption-only reward market.

A converted economic construct may interact with one or multiple market modes.

## Transaction-regime doctrine

Astra recognizes multiple lawful transaction families.
At minimum, conversion must support:
- purchase,
- sale,
- barter,
- trade-in,
- redemption,
- requisition,
- institutional issue,
- contract compensation,
- bounty payment,
- salary or stipend,
- reward grant,
- claim-based acquisition,
- inheritance or transfer,
- salvage monetization,
- and entitlement fulfillment.

Different transaction families may use different validation rules, legitimacy classes, losses, and records.
Astra must not assume that one universal transaction procedure governs them all.

## Price and valuation semantics

Astra must distinguish among different valuation expressions.
The following are not automatically equivalent:
- listed price,
- market price,
- sale price,
- trade value,
- redemption value,
- requisition cost,
- bounty value,
- contract value,
- replacement value,
- scarcity-adjusted value,
- liquidation value,
- and abstract wealth requirement.

A converted construct may possess one, many, or none of these.
If a donor economy distinguishes them, Astra must preserve the distinction.

## Eligibility, restriction, and legitimacy doctrine

Economic access may be gated by more than price.
Astra must support eligibility constraints such as:
- rank,
- clearance,
- faction alignment,
- geography,
- legality,
- institutional membership,
- reputation or standing,
- mission status,
- holder identity,
- contract binding,
- scarcity controls,
- and temporal windows.

Legitimacy must be treated as a distinct field family.
A transaction or holding may be:
- lawful/open,
- lawful/restricted,
- tolerated/informal,
- shadowed or grey-market,
- illicit,
- forbidden,
- personally bound,
- institutionally bound,
- or regime-conditional.

## Scarcity and pressure doctrine

Scarcity is a doctrine field, not just a narrative mood.
Astra must support at least:
- abundant,
- common,
- controlled,
- scarce,
- rationed,
- luxury,
- crisis-restricted,
- and singular or nearly singular economic constructs.

Conversion must preserve the donor's intended economic pressure.
Do not normalize scarcity-heavy, barter-heavy, favor-gated, requisition-based, or reward-token economies into generic cash shopping.
Do not inflate generous or convenience-oriented donor economies into punitive scarcity simulators.

## Reward-economy doctrine

A reward economy is an economic regime where value is granted primarily through recognition, accomplishment, advancement, service, compliance, or patronage rather than ordinary open-market earning.

Reward media may be:
- transferable,
- non-transferable,
- redeemable only at specific exchanges,
- authority-issued,
- milestone-bound,
- rank-scaled,
- or privilege-bearing.

Astra must preserve donor reward economies when they materially shape progression, access, or prestige.
They must not be lazily rewritten as ordinary cash payouts unless the donor logic genuinely treats them that way.

## Requisition and issue doctrine

Requisition is not discounted shopping.
It is a distinct acquisition regime.

Astra must support requisition and issue models where acquisition is governed by:
- command authority,
- institutional role,
- mission assignment,
- faction standing,
- clearance level,
- logistical availability,
- or regulated allotment.

Issue models may create:
- temporary custody,
- revocable access,
- replacement obligations,
- audit trails,
- restricted transferability,
- and mission-limited use rights.

## Denomination and abstraction doctrine

Astra must not force every donor economy into exact denominations.
It must support economies expressed as:
- explicit denominations,
- tiered denominations,
- abstract wealth bands,
- resource points,
- supply ratings,
- favor scores,
- or mixed denomination-plus-abstraction systems.

When a donor uses abstraction, Astra must preserve that abstraction unless there is a doctrine-grounded reason to convert it into explicit pricing.

## Economic profile and instance distinction

Astra should distinguish between:
- an economic **profile**, meaning the canonical structure of a value medium, exchange regime, or entitlement class,
- and an economic **instance**, meaning a specific current holding, claim, token, balance, debt, grant, or redemption state.

This distinction exists so Astra can correctly represent:
- bound versus unbound value media,
- spent versus unspent entitlements,
- redeemed versus unredeemed claims,
- authorized versus expired access instruments,
- pooled versus assigned credits,
- and lawful versus compromised instances of the same economic class.

## No false-price-precision rule

Astra must not invent fake numeric certainty when donor value is only contextual, abstract, or partially specified.

When donor material does not justify exact pricing, conversion may preserve value by using:
- regime assignment,
- scarcity and legitimacy classification,
- entitlement classification,
- holder and transferability doctrine,
- relative-value relations,
- abstract wealth placement,
- or explicit quarantine pending later doctrine.

The goal is faithful economic translation, not cosmetic numerical completion.

## Economic-preservation invariants

### Invariant 1: Preserve donor economic ecology
Do not collapse materially different economic constructs into one generic money bucket when the donor distinguishes currencies, favors, tokens, rights, credits, barter goods, rewards, or requisition channels.

### Invariant 2: Preserve donor pressure
Do not strip scarcity, access friction, redemption constraints, or transfer rules away from a donor economy merely to make Astra simpler.

### Invariant 3: Preserve donor abstraction level unless doctrine requires otherwise
Do not force exact prices onto intentionally abstract economies.
Do not blur highly explicit economies into shapeless abstraction either.

### Invariant 4: Preserve access-right significance
If a donor construct matters because it authorizes movement, service, mission entry, or institutional access, Astra must preserve that access-right logic rather than reducing it to commodity worth.

### Invariant 5: Preserve holder logic
If a donor system distinguishes personal wealth, shared pools, institutional stock, or non-transferable reward balances, Astra must preserve that distinction.

## Lawful incomplete mapping

When donor economic data is incomplete, ambiguous, or structurally alien, Astra may classify the conversion as:
- **directly mapped**: donor form preserved with minimal transformation,
- **normalized**: donor form translated into an Astra-equivalent regime without loss of core economic pressure,
- **regime-assigned**: donor form assigned to the Astra economic regime that best preserves behavior,
- **placeholder-valued**: construct recognized economically but not given false precision,
- **quarantined**: construct deferred pending later doctrine, additional source context, or corpus-level consolidation.

This doctrine exists to prevent false certainty and premature flattening.

## Drafting constraints for downstream conversion

Any downstream schema, sourcebook consolidation, or conversion output using this file should obey the following constraints:
- never assume all value is cash,
- never assume all transactions are purchases,
- never assume all holders are individuals,
- never assume all access is market access,
- never assume all reward media are transferable,
- and never manufacture exact price doctrine where the donor does not support it.
