# Astra Stage 2 - World Activity Engine v0.1

Derived from RHBF Parts 5-7.

## Purpose

This stage converts RHBF's sector exploration, factions, trials, locations, and world-facing activity loops into an Astra-native activity engine. The goal is not to preserve RHBF's names or cosmology, but to preserve the functional machine that answers a crucial question:

**What do Ascendants actually do between character creation and late-stage power?**

RHBF provides a very strong answer:
- travel through a hub-and-token network,
- pursue modular opportunity nodes,
- navigate factions,
- enter trials,
- harvest dangerous regions,
- respond to world events,
- and build or defend a home power base.

That skeleton is excellent donor material.

## I. Core Astra Activity Loop

Astra Ascendants operate on a repeating strategic loop:

1. Return to a **Nexus Hub**.
2. Acquire or unlock **Transit Seals** to destinations.
3. Choose an opportunity type:
   - Trial
   - Hunting Ground
   - Mandate
   - Faction Contract
   - Expedition Site
   - Capital/Stronghold Management
4. Travel to the site.
5. Resolve exploration, encounters, hazards, negotiation, or combat.
6. Claim resources, favor, marks, relics, and technique growth.
7. Return to hub or stronghold.
8. Trigger world updates, faction shifts, and management consequences.

This is the backbone of Astra's playable campaign rhythm.

## II. Hub Structure

### Astra equivalent: Nexus Hub

RHBF uses Yeross as a de facto sector capital, social melting pot, and launch point for new Ascendants. It is also the main distribution point for opportunities and tokens.

Astra should preserve that function, but not the source-specific identity. The Astra equivalent is a **Nexus Hub**:
- neutral or semi-neutral center,
- contact market,
- mission board / omen hall / contract exchange,
- travel-gate anchor,
- faction touchpoint,
- information clearinghouse,
- onboarding point for new Ascendants.

### Design rule

Every major Astra campaign region should have at least one Nexus Hub.

## III. Travel Structure

### Astra equivalent: Transit Seals

RHBF uses Teleport Tokens as bounded-access travel keys. They gate entry to locations, are earnable through factions or markets, and create modular site-based progression.

Astra should convert this into **Transit Seals**:
- consumable or persistent access keys,
- permissions to enter specific realms/sites/expeditions,
- sometimes earned as rewards,
- sometimes purchased or traded,
- sometimes bound to rank/stage thresholds.

### Design rule

Transit Seals are how Astra prevents world sprawl from becoming mush. Access should feel earned, not automatic.

## IV. Opportunity Types

### 1. Trials

RHBF's trial generation is one of the best donor systems in the corpus. Trials vary by access method, timer, location, claimants, terrain, reward marks, type, and aesthetic. Some require mediation, some defense, some assault, some artifact reconstruction.

Astra equivalent: **Ascension Trials**.

Ascension Trials should be modular challenge nodes defined by:
- access method,
- urgency/timer,
- location frame,
- controlling or contesting powers,
- objective type,
- environmental theme,
- reward grade,
- failure consequence.

#### Core Astra Trial Types
- Breach the Ward
- Summon and Subdue
- Defeat the Endless Wave
- Escape the Labyrinth
- Reforge the Broken Relic
- Maintain Balance in Conflict

These are structurally donor-faithful but Astra-ready.

### 2. Hunting Grounds

RHBF's hunting grounds are repeatable exploration zones reached by travel tokens, constrained by rank/economics, and driven by hex travel plus encounter checks.

Astra equivalent: **Hunting Realms**.

Hunting Realms should be:
- repeatable harvest zones,
- rank-banded,
- resource-rich,
- threat-scaled,
- useful for beast parts, treasures, and training.

### 3. Divine Decrees / Mandates

RHBF uses higher-order calls to action from the heavens and from political/military pressures.

Astra equivalent: **Mandates**.

Mandates are system-level urgencies issued by powers beyond the local faction scale. They are ideal for:
- world-changing escalations,
- anti-heresy campaigns,
- anomaly containment,
- sector war pressure,
- major faction opportunity spikes.

### 4. Faction Contracts

RHBF strongly supports jobs, reputational advancement, and token/reward acquisition through factions.

Astra equivalent: **Faction Contracts**.

Contracts are the main non-cosmic mission type and should drive:
- rewards,
- standing,
- access,
- secrets,
- enemy creation.

### 5. Expedition Sites

RHBF locations like the Forge make it clear that a site can be a self-contained modular adventure with hazards, guardians, rewards, and thematic transformation.

Astra equivalent: **Expedition Sites**.

These should include:
- ruins,
- vaults,
- forge-complexes,
- corpse-cities,
- living forests,
- starfall craters,
- hostile research sanctums.

## V. Faction Engine

RHBF provides a rich faction matrix, including neutral knowledge keepers, neutrality-bound contractors, anti-tech heretics, and structured political powers. It also includes conflict mediation procedures between factional interests.

Astra should preserve this as a **Faction Pressure Engine**.

### Astra faction archetypes
- Hegemonic Clans
- Knowledge Orders
- Neutral Contract Guilds
- Beast/Primal Coalitions
- Heretek or anti-canon technicists
- Capital polities
- Wandering independents
- Trade houses
- Mercenary navies
- Artist/ritual consortiums

### Design rule

Factions should be defined by four things:
- nature,
- desire,
- pressure style,
- available rewards/access.

This is more useful than giant lore dumps.

## VI. World Event Engine

RHBF has strong random-event structures for capital management, sectors, scenes, and surprise developments. These are extremely useful because they create motion without demanding a full plotted campaign.

Astra equivalent: **Pressure Events**.

Pressure Events are grouped by type:
- Encounter
- Physical
- Environment
- Knowledge
- Character
- NPC
- Location
- Faction/Party

This taxonomy is donor-worthy and should be kept.

## VII. Stronghold / Capital Loop

RHBF's Management Phase and capital system are excellent donor mechanics. They create a home-base loop where returning from adventures triggers city events, building choices, defensive concerns, and political consequences.

Astra equivalent: **Stronghold Phase**.

At the end of a major expedition cycle, each Ascendant or party may trigger a Stronghold Phase:
- resolve one Pressure Event,
- handle attacks or unrest,
- build or upgrade one structure,
- gain or lose resources,
- alter faction ties,
- unlock future content.

### Stronghold stats
Minimum useful stronghold metrics:
- Defense
- Prosperity
- Stability
- Influence
- Transit Capacity

### Example structures
- Alchemy Lab -> Refinement Lab
- Barracks -> Retainer Barracks
- Market -> Exchange Hall
- Teleporter center -> Transit Gate Anchor
- Archive -> Memory Vault
- Forge -> Meridian Forge

## VIII. Resource and Reward Logic

RHBF repeatedly uses rewards like marks, gold/currency, shards, treasures, blessings, and site-specific loot. Natural treasures and harvestable beast parts also form a clear resource loop.

Astra equivalent reward families:
- Merit Marks
- Credits or Shards
- Technique Seals
- Domain Fragments
- Natural Treasures
- Blessings
- Relics
- Beast Components
- Stronghold Assets

### Design rule

Astra rewards should always do at least one of four things:
- advance the character,
- unlock access,
- strengthen the stronghold,
- alter faction/world standing.

## IX. Tech-vs-Metaphysics Tension

RHBF's Engineers are a powerful donor example of a technology-centered faction persecuted by the dominant cultivation order. This is useful to Astra because it provides a world-scale pressure axis.

Astra should preserve the function, but not necessarily the absolutism.

Astra equivalent: **Heretek / Ascension-Schism factions**.

These factions should represent:
- engineered transcendence,
- forbidden synthesis,
- anti-cultivation pragmatism,
- dangerous but genuinely effective alternatives.

Unlike RHBF, Astra may ultimately want a more integrated tension rather than total condemnation. For now, keep this as a pressure axis, not final metaphysical law.

## X. Stage 2 Framework Laws

The following should now be treated as Astra framework law unless later testing breaks them:

1. Astra needs a hub-and-expedition activity loop.
2. Travel should be permissioned through earnable access objects or rites.
3. Trials, Hunting Realms, Mandates, Contracts, and Stronghold Phases are distinct opportunity classes.
4. Factions should be mechanically active pressures, not passive lore wallpaper.
5. World motion should come from structured Pressure Events.
6. Site-based modular content should be a first-class Astra design principle.
7. Stronghold growth should be tied directly to expedition play.

## XI. Provisional Items Requiring Review

These should remain provisional:
- exact final name for Merit Marks versus some other reward currency,
- whether Transit Seals are consumable, persistent, or mixed,
- how permissive Astra is toward technology factions,
- whether all campaigns require a stronghold or only some modes,
- final mapping of RHBF's divine/heavenly reward model to Astra's cosmology.
